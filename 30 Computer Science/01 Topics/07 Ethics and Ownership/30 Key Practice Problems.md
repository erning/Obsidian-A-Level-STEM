# Ethics and Ownership Key Practice Problems

These problems cover the core skills of 9618 AS Section 7. Attempt them on paper before reading [the solutions](31%20Key%20Practice%20Solutions.md). Most prompts are scenario-based, so aim to justify your answer against the alternatives rather than just naming a term. No answers are given here on purpose: the point is active recall and full written reasoning.

## A. Professional ethics

1. Define **ethics** for a computing professional, and give two duties it imposes on them.

2. State the full names of **BCS** and **IEEE**, and give three concrete benefits to a professional of joining one of these bodies.

3. A developer is told to release a navigation app on a fixed date, knowing that the rerouting feature gives incorrect directions when the GPS signal is weak. Use stakeholders and harms to weigh acting ethically against acting unethically, and state what the developer should do.

4. A social-media company could sell its users' reading habits to an advertising broker for a large profit. Explain why doing so without informed consent is unethical, and describe the ethical response.

5. Distinguish acting *ethically* from acting *legally*. Give one situation in computing where an action could be legal but still unethical.

## B. Copyright and ownership

6. State what **copyright** protects in computing, and explain why copyright legislation is needed.

7. Explain the difference between the *idea* behind a program and the *expression* of that idea, and state which of the two copyright protects.

8. A student buys a copy of a commercial graphics program. Explain why this purchase does not transfer the copyright to the student, and state two things the student is and is not allowed to do with the software.

## C. Software licences

9. Compare **FSF**, **OSI**, **shareware**, and **commercial** software on:
   (a) cost to the user;
   (b) freedom to view and modify the source code;
   (c) redistribution of the software or modified versions.

10. Explain one similarity and one difference between an **FSF** licence and an **OSI** licence.

11. For each project, choose the most appropriate licence type from FSF, OSI, shareware, and commercial, and justify your choice by naming the matching licence property.
    (a) A charity with no budget that wants to read and adapt the source of a database tool.
    (b) A developer who wants users to try a game for 14 days before paying for the full version.
    (c) A firm selling a proprietary accounting package whose source code must stay closed.
    (d) A startup that wants its web server to be auditable and improved by a community of contributors, while allowing derivatives to be made proprietary.

12. A company takes an **OSI**-licensed library (released under a permissive licence such as MIT), modifies it, and releases the modified version as a closed commercial product. Explain whether this is permitted, and state how the answer would change if the original library had been **FSF**-licensed under the GNU GPL.

## D. AI impact

13. Artificial Intelligence is described as having social, economic, and environmental impact. For each category, give one positive and one negative impact of AI in general.

14. A bank deploys an AI system that scores loan applications automatically. Discuss the **social** and **economic** impact of this application, giving at least one positive and one negative point for each.

15. A logistics company uses AI to optimise its delivery routes across a country. Discuss the **environmental** impact of this application, giving one positive and one negative point.

16. Name four distinct **applications** of AI, and for one of them link it to its social and its environmental impact.
