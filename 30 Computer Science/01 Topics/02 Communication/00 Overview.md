# 02 Communication

## Core Idea

Computers gain value when they are connected. This topic covers how networks are built, how they are classified, how data reaches the right place, and how the internet and the World Wide Web relate to each other.

## Source Alignment

- 9618 AS section 2 Communication
- 2.1 Networks including the internet
- Paper 1

## What to Learn

- Purpose and benefits of networking; LAN and WAN characteristics.
- Client-server and peer-to-peer models; thin-client and thick-client.
- Bus, star, mesh, and hybrid topologies, and how packets move in each.
- Cloud computing: public and private clouds, benefits and drawbacks.
- Wired and wireless media: copper cable, fibre optic, radio (WiFi), microwave, satellite.
- LAN hardware: switch, server, NIC, WNIC, WAP, cables, bridge, repeater.
- Router function; Ethernet and CSMA/CD for collision detection and avoidance.
- Bit streaming: real-time and on-demand; effect of bit rate and broadband speed.
- The internet versus the WWW; internet hardware (modems, PSTN, dedicated lines, cell networks).
- IP addressing: IPv4 and IPv6, subnetting, public/private and static/dynamic addresses.
- URL structure and the role of the Domain Name Service (DNS).

## How to Study

- Draw each topology and trace how a packet travels between two hosts.
- Compare client-server with peer-to-peer, and thin-client with thick-client, for a named scenario.
- Separate the internet (infrastructure) from the WWW (service).

## Practice Directions

- Justify a topology or model for a given situation.
- Describe how a router forwards data across networks.
- Distinguish public/private and static/dynamic IP addresses and their implications.

## Common Traps

- Treating the internet and the WWW as the same thing.
- Confusing a static/dynamic IP with a public/private IP.
- Forgetting how CSMA/CD detects and then avoids collisions.
