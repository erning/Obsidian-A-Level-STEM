# Syllabus and Paper Map

CAIE Computer Science 9618 splits content into AS Level (sections 1-12) and A Level (sections 13-20). Each paper draws from a fixed set of sections, so the paper structure directly shapes the study route.

## Assessment Overview

Calculators must not be used in any paper.

| Paper | Name | Duration | Sections Assessed | Weighting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paper 1 | Theory Fundamentals | 1 h 30 min | 1-8 | 50% AS / 25% A Level |
| Paper 2 | Fundamental Problem-solving and Programming Skills | 2 h | 9-12 | 50% AS / 25% A Level |
| Paper 3 | Advanced Theory | 1 h 30 min | 13-20 | 25% A Level |
| Paper 4 | Practical | 2 h 30 min | 19-20 (except low-level and declarative programming) | 25% A Level |

Paper 2 answers are written in pseudocode, following the [Pseudocode Guide 2027-2029](../../assets/syllabus/9618%20-%20Computer%20Science/CAIE%20Computer%20Science%209618%20Pseudocode%20Guide%202027-2029.pdf). Paper 4 is a computer-based practical: candidates submit complete program code and evidence of testing in Java (console mode), Visual Basic .NET (console mode), or Python (console mode), without internet or email.

## AS-to-A Level Extensions

Several A Level topics deepen an AS topic of the same name:

- 1 Information Representation extends into 13 Data Representation (user-defined types, file organisation, floating-point).
- 5 System Software extends into 16 System Software (process management, virtual memory, translation stages, BNF, RPN).
- 9 Algorithm Design and Problem-solving extends into 19 Computational Thinking and Problem-solving (searching, sorting, ADT operations, recursion, Big O).
- 11 Programming extends into 20 Further Programming (paradigms, file processing, exception handling).

## Examination Routes

There are three routes through the qualification:

- AS Level only: Papers 1 and 2 in one series. Eligible for grades a-e.
- A Level staged over two years: AS (Papers 1 and 2) in Year 1, then Papers 3 and 4 in Year 2, carrying forward AS marks.
- A Level linear: Papers 1, 2, 3, and 4 in one series. Eligible for grades A\*-E.

## Assessment Objectives

| AO | Description | AS % | A Level % |
|---|---|---|---|
| AO1 | Knowledge and understanding of principles and concepts | 30 | 30 |
| AO2 | Application of knowledge and understanding, including analysis in computational terms | 40 | 30 |
| AO3 | Design, program, and evaluate computer systems to solve problems | 30 | 40 |

Papers 1 and 3 are AO1/AO2 weighted; Papers 2 and 4 carry most of the AO3 (problem-solving and programming) weight.
