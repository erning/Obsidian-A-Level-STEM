# Kinematics and Newtonian Motion Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. State the positive
direction before using signs. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ unless a
question states otherwise.

## A. Constant Acceleration and Motion Graphs

1. A particle moves in a straight line with initial velocity
   $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and constant acceleration
   $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$. Find its velocity and displacement after
   $8\ \mathrm{s}$.

2. A car travelling at $24\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ brakes with constant
   acceleration $-3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$. Find the time taken to stop and the
   distance travelled while braking.

3. A particle is projected vertically upwards with speed
   $30\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the greatest height reached and the total
   time before it returns to the point of projection.

4. A velocity-time graph is made from three straight sections through the
   points $(0,0)$, $(4,12)$, $(9,12)$, and $(12,0)$, where time is in seconds
   and velocity is in $\mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the total displacement and
   the acceleration on the first and last sections.

5. A particle has displacement

   $$
   s=t^3-6t^2+9t
   $$

   for $0\le t\le5$. Find the times when it is instantaneously at rest, its
   displacement over the interval, and the total distance travelled.

## B. Calculus with Motion

6. A particle has velocity

   $$
   v=12-3t.
   $$

   Find its displacement and total distance travelled from $t=0$ to $t=5$.

7. A particle has acceleration

   $$
   a=4-2t.
   $$

   Given $v=1$ and $s=3$ when $t=0$, find $v(t)$ and $s(t)$, and find
   $s(4)$.

8. A particle has velocity

   $$
   v=t^2-5t+6
   $$

   for $0\le t\le4$. Find when it changes direction, its displacement, and
   the total distance travelled.

9. A particle has displacement

   $$
   s=2t^2-\frac{t^3}{3}.
   $$

   Find its velocity, acceleration, and maximum displacement for $t\ge0$ while
   it is moving in the positive direction.

10. Two particles move on the same straight line. Their displacements from the
    same origin are

    $$
    s_A=2t^2+3t,\qquad s_B=30+8t-t^2.
    $$

    Find when they meet and the displacement from the origin at that time.

## C. Newton's Laws for One Particle

11. A $6\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle on a smooth horizontal surface is pulled by a
    horizontal force of $42\ \mathrm{N}$. Find its acceleration.

12. An $8\ \mathrm{kg}$ block is pulled along a rough horizontal surface by a
    horizontal force of $50\ \mathrm{N}$. The coefficient of friction is
    $0.25$. Find its acceleration.

13. A $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is pulled vertically upwards by a force of
    $70\ \mathrm{N}$. Find its acceleration and state its direction.

14. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is projected up a smooth plane inclined at
    $30^\circ$ to the horizontal with speed $12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the
    time until it comes to rest and the distance travelled up the plane.

15. A particle slides down a rough plane inclined at $20^\circ$ to the
    horizontal. The coefficient of friction is $0.15$. Find its acceleration
    down the plane.

## D. Connected Particles and Mixed Review

16. Particles of masses $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ and $6\ \mathrm{kg}$ are connected by
    a light inextensible string over a smooth pulley. The $6\ \mathrm{kg}$
    particle moves downwards. Find the acceleration and the tension.

17. Particles of masses $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ and $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ are connected by
    a light string on a smooth horizontal table. A horizontal force of
    $40\ \mathrm{N}$ pulls the $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle away from the
    $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. Find the acceleration and the tension.

18. A car of mass $900\ \mathrm{kg}$ tows a trailer of mass
    $300\ \mathrm{kg}$. The driving force is $1800\ \mathrm{N}$. The
    resistances on the car and trailer are $300\ \mathrm{N}$ and
    $150\ \mathrm{N}$ respectively. Find the acceleration and the tow-bar
    tension.

19. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle on a smooth horizontal table is connected by a
    light string over a smooth pulley to a hanging $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle.
    Find the acceleration and the tension.

20. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ block on a rough horizontal table is connected by a
    light string over a smooth pulley to a hanging $6\ \mathrm{kg}$ mass. The
    coefficient of friction between the block and table is $0.20$. Find the
    acceleration and the tension.
