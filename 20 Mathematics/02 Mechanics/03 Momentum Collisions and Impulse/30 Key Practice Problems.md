# Momentum, Collisions and Impulse Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. State your positive
direction or axes before writing momentum equations.

## A. One-Dimensional Momentum

1. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves right at $3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and a
   $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves left at $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the
   total momentum of the two-particle system.

2. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving at $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ collides with
   a stationary $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. They stick together. Find their
   common velocity.

3. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at $6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   collides with a $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving left at
   $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. They stick together. Find their common velocity
   after impact.

4. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   collides with a stationary $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. After impact the
   $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves left at $1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the
   velocity of the $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle.

5. For the collision in question 2, find the kinetic energy lost.

## B. Impulse

6. A $0.20\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball moving right at $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   rebounds left at $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the impulse on the ball.

7. A constant force of $30\ \mathrm{N}$ acts on a $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle for
   $0.40\ \mathrm{s}$ in the direction of motion. The particle initially moves
   at $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its final velocity.

8. A force $F=20t$ acts on a $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle for
   $0\le t\le3$, where $F$ is in newtons and $t$ in seconds. The particle
   initially moves at $1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in the direction of the force.
   Find its final velocity.

9. A $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving at $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ receives
   an impulse of $3\ \mathrm{N\ s}$ opposite to its motion. Find its final
   velocity.

10. A $0.10\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball hits a wall normally at
    $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and rebounds at $16\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The
    contact time is $0.020\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the average force on the ball.

## C. Restitution in Direct Impact

11. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at $6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
    collides directly with a stationary $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. The
    coefficient of restitution is $0.5$. Find the velocities after impact.

12. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
    collides directly with a $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at
    $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The coefficient of restitution is $0.25$. Find
    the velocities after impact.

13. Two equal masses collide directly. Before impact, their velocities are
    $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and $-1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The collision is
    perfectly elastic. Find their velocities after impact.

14. A particle hits a fixed smooth wall normally at $12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.
    The coefficient of restitution is $0.75$. Find its velocity after impact.

15. A sphere hits a fixed smooth plane. Before impact, its velocity components
    are $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ normal to the plane and
    $24\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ parallel to the plane. The coefficient of
    restitution is $0.8$. Find its speed after impact and the angle it makes
    with the plane.

## D. Oblique Impact and System Boundaries

16. A smooth sphere hits a fixed smooth wall at speed $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.
    Its velocity makes $40^\circ$ with the normal to the wall. The coefficient
    of restitution is $0.6$. Find the speed after impact and the angle the new
    velocity makes with the normal.

17. Sphere $A$ strikes an identical smooth sphere $B$, initially at rest.
    Before impact, the component of $A$'s velocity along the line of centres is
    $6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and its perpendicular component is
    $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The coefficient of restitution is $0.5$. Find the
    speed of $A$ and the speed of $B$ after impact.

18. A smooth sphere $A$ of mass $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ strikes a smooth sphere $B$ of
    mass $3\ \mathrm{kg}$, initially at rest. Along the line of centres,
    $A$'s speed before impact is $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and its
    perpendicular component is $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The coefficient of
    restitution is $0.4$. Find the components of both velocities after impact.

19. Two particles collide directly. Before impact their velocities are
    $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and $1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in the same direction.
    After impact their velocities are $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and
    $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the coefficient of restitution.

20. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving right at $3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and a
    $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moving left at $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ interact.
    During the interaction, an external impulse of $5\ \mathrm{N\ s}$ acts on
    the two-particle system to the right. After the interaction, the
    $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves left at $1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the
    velocity of the $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle.
