# Momentum, Collisions and Impulse Key Practice Solutions

This note gives worked solutions for [[20 Mathematics/02 Mechanics/03 Momentum Collisions and Impulse/30 Key Practice Problems|Momentum, Collisions and Impulse Key Practice Problems]]. Use it to check system choice, signs, restitution equations, and units.

## A. One-Dimensional Momentum

### Problem 1

Take right as positive. The total momentum is

$$
4(3)+2(-5)=12-10=2\ \mathrm{kg\ m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

So the system has momentum

$$
2\ \mathrm{kg\ m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to the right.

### Problem 2

The particles stick together, so they have a common final velocity $v$.
Momentum conservation gives

$$
3(8)+5(0)=(3+5)v.
$$

Thus

$$
v=3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 3

Take right as positive. If the common velocity after impact is $v$, then

$$
2(6)+3(-2)=(2+3)v.
$$

So

$$
6=5v,
$$

and

$$
v=1.2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to the right.

### Problem 4

Take right as positive and let the final velocity of the $3\ \mathrm{kg}$
particle be $v$. Momentum conservation gives

$$
2(10)+3(0)=2(-1)+3v.
$$

Thus

$$
20=-2+3v,
$$

so

$$
v=\frac{22}{3}=7.33\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to the right, to 3 s.f.

### Problem 5

For question 2, initial kinetic energy is

$$
\frac12(3)(8^2)=96\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

Final kinetic energy is

$$
\frac12(8)(3^2)=36\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

The kinetic energy lost is

$$
96-36=60\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

## B. Impulse

### Problem 6

Take right as positive. Then

$$
u=15,\qquad v=-10.
$$

Impulse is change in momentum:

$$
I=m(v-u)=0.20(-10-15)=-5\ \mathrm{N\ s}.
$$

The impulse has magnitude $5\ \mathrm{N\ s}$ and acts to the left.

### Problem 7

The impulse is

$$
I=Ft=30(0.40)=12\ \mathrm{N\ s}.
$$

Use $I=m(v-u)$:

$$
12=3(v-2).
$$

Thus

$$
v=6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 8

Impulse is the integral of force:

$$
I=\int_0^3 20t\,dt.
$$

Therefore

$$
I=\left[10t^2\right]_0^3=90\ \mathrm{N\ s}.
$$

Use $I=m(v-u)$:

$$
90=5(v-1).
$$

Hence

$$
v=19\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 9

Take the original direction of motion as positive. The impulse is
$-3\ \mathrm{N\ s}$. Using $I=m(v-u)$,

$$
-3=0.50(v-8).
$$

Thus

$$
v=2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The particle is still moving in its original direction.

### Problem 10

Take the original direction toward the wall as positive. Then

$$
u=20,\qquad v=-16.
$$

The impulse on the ball is

$$
I=0.10(-16-20)=-3.6\ \mathrm{N\ s}.
$$

Average force is

$$
F=\frac{I}{t}=\frac{-3.6}{0.020}=-180\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

The average force has magnitude

$$
180\ \mathrm{N}
$$

and acts away from the wall.

## C. Restitution in Direct Impact

### Problem 11

Let the final velocities be $v_1$ for the $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle and
$v_2$ for the $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. Momentum conservation gives

$$
2(6)=2v_1+v_2.
$$

Restitution gives

$$
v_2-v_1=0.5(6-0)=3.
$$

Solving,

$$
v_1=3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}},\qquad v_2=6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 12

Let the final velocities be $v_1$ for the $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle and
$v_2$ for the $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle. Momentum conservation gives

$$
1(8)+2(2)=v_1+2v_2.
$$

So

$$
12=v_1+2v_2.
$$

Restitution gives

$$
v_2-v_1=0.25(8-2)=1.5.
$$

Solving,

$$
v_1=3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}},\qquad v_2=4.5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 13

Let the final velocities be $v_1$ and $v_2$. Since the masses are equal,
momentum conservation gives

$$
5+(-1)=v_1+v_2,
$$

so

$$
v_1+v_2=4.
$$

For a perfectly elastic collision, $e=1$, so

$$
v_2-v_1=1(5-(-1))=6.
$$

Solving,

$$
v_1=-1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}},\qquad v_2=5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The equal masses exchange velocities.

### Problem 14

Take motion towards the wall as positive. A fixed wall reverses the normal
component and multiplies its magnitude by $e$. Therefore

$$
v=-0.75(12)=-9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The particle rebounds at

$$
9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

away from the wall.

### Problem 15

The tangential component is unchanged:

$$
v_t=24.
$$

The normal component reverses and has magnitude

$$
v_n=0.8(10)=8.
$$

The speed after impact is

$$
\sqrt{24^2+8^2}=25.3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f. If $\theta$ is the angle with the plane, then

$$
\tan\theta=\frac{8}{24}.
$$

Thus

$$
\theta=18.4^\circ.
$$

## D. Oblique Impact and System Boundaries

### Problem 16

Resolve the initial velocity into components normal and tangential to the
wall:

$$
u_n=15\cos40^\circ,\qquad u_t=15\sin40^\circ.
$$

The tangential component is unchanged:

$$
v_t=15\sin40^\circ=9.64\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The normal component reverses and has magnitude

$$
v_n=0.6(15\cos40^\circ)=6.89\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The speed after impact is

$$
\sqrt{6.89^2+9.64^2}=11.9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f. If $\theta$ is the angle with the normal, then

$$
\tan\theta=\frac{9.64}{6.89}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\theta=54.4^\circ.
$$

### Problem 17

There is no impulse perpendicular to the line of centres, so $A$ keeps
perpendicular component $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and $B$ has perpendicular
component $0$.

Along the line of centres, let the final components be $v_A$ and $v_B$.
Momentum conservation gives

$$
6=v_A+v_B.
$$

Restitution gives

$$
v_B-v_A=0.5(6)=3.
$$

Solving,

$$
v_A=1.5,\qquad v_B=4.5.
$$

The speed of $A$ is

$$
\sqrt{1.5^2+8^2}=8.14\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f. The speed of $B$ is

$$
4.5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 18

There is no impulse perpendicular to the line of centres. Therefore the
perpendicular components after impact are

$$
A:\ 5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}},\qquad B:\ 0.
$$

Along the line of centres, let the final components be $v_A$ and $v_B$.
Momentum conservation gives

$$
2(10)=2v_A+3v_B.
$$

Restitution gives

$$
v_B-v_A=0.4(10)=4.
$$

Using $v_B=v_A+4$ in the momentum equation,

$$
20=2v_A+3(v_A+4).
$$

Thus

$$
v_A=1.6,\qquad v_B=5.6.
$$

So after impact, $A$ has components $1.6$ along the line of centres and $5$
perpendicular to it, while $B$ has components $5.6$ along the line of centres
and $0$ perpendicular to it.

### Problem 19

The speed of approach is

$$
5-1=4.
$$

The speed of separation is

$$
4-2=2.
$$

Therefore

$$
e=\frac{2}{4}=0.5.
$$

### Problem 20

Take right as positive. Initial total momentum is

$$
2(3)+1(-4)=2.
$$

An external impulse of $5\ \mathrm{N\ s}$ acts to the right, so final total
momentum is

$$
2+5=7.
$$

Let the final velocity of the $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle be $v$. The final
total momentum is

$$
2(-1)+v.
$$

Thus

$$
-2+v=7.
$$

So

$$
v=9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to the right.
