# Work, Energy, Power and Elasticity Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Keep every term in joules
when using an energy equation. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ unless a
question states otherwise.

## A. Work and Mechanical Energy

1. A force of $40\ \mathrm{N}$ acts at $60^\circ$ to the direction of motion
   through a displacement of $10\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the work done.

2. A $12\ \mathrm{kg}$ object is lifted vertically by $3\ \mathrm{m}$ at steady
   speed. Find the work done against gravity.

3. A $5\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle speeds up from $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to
   $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its increase in kinetic energy.

4. A particle is released from rest and falls through a vertical height of
   $7.2\ \mathrm{m}$ without resistance. Find its speed after the fall.

5. A $10\ \mathrm{kg}$ block moving at $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ on a horizontal
   surface is brought to rest by a constant frictional force of
   $30\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the stopping distance.

## B. Energy with External Work

6. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is pulled up a smooth plane inclined at
   $30^\circ$ by a force of $50\ \mathrm{N}$ parallel to the plane. Starting
   from rest, it moves $5\ \mathrm{m}$ up the plane. Find its speed.

7. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle slides from rest down a rough plane inclined at
   $20^\circ$ to the horizontal. It travels $10\ \mathrm{m}$ down the plane.
   The coefficient of friction is $0.10$. Find its speed.

8. A variable force $F=6x+4$ acts in the direction of motion from $x=0$ to
   $x=5$, where $F$ is in newtons and $x$ is in metres. Find the work done.

9. A force-displacement graph is a triangle with base $8\ \mathrm{m}$ and
   maximum force $40\ \mathrm{N}$. The force acts on a $4\ \mathrm{kg}$
   particle initially at rest on a smooth horizontal surface. Find the work
   done and the final speed.

10. A particle moves on a smooth track. Its initial speed is
    $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and it later reaches a point $3\ \mathrm{m}$
    lower. Find its speed at the lower point.

## C. Power

11. A machine lifts a $200\ \mathrm{kg}$ load vertically through
    $15\ \mathrm{m}$ in $60\ \mathrm{s}$ at steady speed. Find its average
    power output, ignoring losses.

12. A car travels at $25\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ while its engine supplies a
    driving force of $1200\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the power output.

13. An engine supplies constant power $20\ \mathrm{kW}$ to move a vehicle at
    constant speed against a resistance of $800\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the speed.

14. A car of mass $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ moves on a horizontal road at
    $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The engine power is $30\ \mathrm{kW}$ and the
    resistance is $500\ \mathrm{N}$. Find its instantaneous acceleration.

15. A car of mass $800\ \mathrm{kg}$ accelerates from
    $10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in $5\ \mathrm{s}$.
    Ignoring resistance, find the average power used to increase its kinetic
    energy.

## D. Hooke's Law and Elastic Energy

16. An elastic string has natural length $2\ \mathrm{m}$ and modulus
    $100\ \mathrm{N}$. It is extended by $0.30\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the tension
    and the elastic potential energy.

17. A spring has natural length $0.50\ \mathrm{m}$ and modulus
    $20\ \mathrm{N}$. It is compressed by $0.10\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the
    magnitude of the force and the elastic potential energy.

18. An elastic string has natural length $1.5\ \mathrm{m}$ and modulus
    $60\ \mathrm{N}$. It is stretched to length $2.1\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the
    tension and elastic potential energy.

19. A $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle on a smooth horizontal surface is attached
    to an elastic string of natural length $1\ \mathrm{m}$ and modulus
    $50\ \mathrm{N}$. The string is extended by $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$ and the
    particle is released from rest. Find its speed when the string reaches its
    natural length.

20. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is attached to the lower end of a vertical
    elastic string of natural length $2\ \mathrm{m}$ and modulus
    $40\ \mathrm{N}$. It is released from rest when the string is at its
    natural length. Find the maximum extension.
