# Work, Energy, Power and Elasticity Key Practice Solutions

This note gives worked solutions for [Work, Energy, Power and Elasticity Key Practice Problems](30%20Key%20Practice%20Problems.md). Use it to check energy accounting, units, and the difference between length and extension. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ unless a question states otherwise.

## A. Work and Mechanical Energy

### Problem 1

Use

$$
W=Fd\cos\theta.
$$

Thus

$$
W=40(10)\cos60^\circ=200\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 2

At steady speed, the work done against gravity equals the gain in
gravitational potential energy:

$$
mgh=12(10)(3)=360\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 3

The increase in kinetic energy is

$$
\frac12m(v^2-u^2)
=\frac12(5)(10^2-4^2).
$$

Hence

$$
\Delta KE=210\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 4

Loss of gravitational potential energy equals gain in kinetic energy:

$$
mgh=\frac12mv^2.
$$

Cancel $m$:

$$
10(7.2)=\frac12v^2.
$$

Thus

$$
v=12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 5

Initial kinetic energy is

$$
\frac12(10)(8^2)=320\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

The frictional force does $30d$ joules of work against the motion. At the
stopping point,

$$
30d=320.
$$

Therefore

$$
d=10.7\ \mathrm{m}\quad \text{to 3 s.f.}
$$

## B. Energy with External Work

### Problem 6

The work done by the pulling force is

$$
50(5)=250\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

The vertical height gained is

$$
5\sin30^\circ=2.5\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

So the gain in gravitational potential energy is

$$
4(10)(2.5)=100\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

The remaining work becomes kinetic energy:

$$
\frac12(4)v^2=250-100=150.
$$

Thus

$$
v=8.66\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}\quad \text{to 3 s.f.}
$$

### Problem 7

The work done by gravity down the plane is

$$
3g\sin20^\circ(10).
$$

The frictional force is

$$
0.10(3g\cos20^\circ).
$$

The work done against friction over $10\ \mathrm{m}$ is

$$
0.10(3g\cos20^\circ)(10).
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac12(3)v^2
=3g\sin20^\circ(10)-0.10(3g\cos20^\circ)(10).
$$

So

$$
v=7.04\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}\quad \text{to 3 s.f.}
$$

### Problem 8

Work is the integral of force with respect to displacement:

$$
W=\int_0^5(6x+4)\,dx.
$$

Thus

$$
W=\left[3x^2+4x\right]_0^5=95\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 9

The work done is the area of the triangle:

$$
W=\frac12(8)(40)=160\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

This becomes kinetic energy:

$$
\frac12(4)v^2=160.
$$

Hence

$$
v=8.94\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}\quad \text{to 3 s.f.}
$$

### Problem 10

Mechanical energy is conserved because the track is smooth. The particle drops
$3\ \mathrm{m}$, so

$$
\frac12mv^2=\frac12m(4^2)+mg(3).
$$

Cancel $m$:

$$
\frac12v^2=8+30.
$$

Thus

$$
v=\sqrt{76}=8.72\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}\quad \text{to 3 s.f.}
$$

## C. Power

### Problem 11

The work done against gravity is

$$
mgh=200(10)(15)=30000\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

Average power is

$$
\frac{30000}{60}=500\ \mathrm{W}.
$$

### Problem 12

Use $P=Fv$:

$$
P=1200(25)=30000\ \mathrm{W}=30\ \mathrm{kW}.
$$

### Problem 13

At constant speed, engine force equals resistance. Use $P=Fv$:

$$
20000=800v.
$$

Hence

$$
v=25\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 14

The driving force from the engine is found using $P=Fv$:

$$
30000=F(15).
$$

So

$$
F=2000\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

The resultant force is

$$
2000-500=1500\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

Using Newton's second law,

$$
1500=1000a.
$$

Thus

$$
a=1.5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}.
$$

### Problem 15

The increase in kinetic energy is

$$
\frac12(800)(20^2-10^2)=120000\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

Average power is

$$
\frac{120000}{5}=24000\ \mathrm{W}=24\ \mathrm{kW}.
$$

## D. Hooke's Law and Elastic Energy

### Problem 16

Hooke's law gives

$$
T=\frac{\lambda x}{l}
=\frac{100(0.30)}{2}=15\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

Elastic potential energy is

$$
E=\frac{\lambda x^2}{2l}
=\frac{100(0.30)^2}{2(2)}=2.25\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 17

The magnitude of the elastic force is

$$
F=\frac{\lambda x}{l}
=\frac{20(0.10)}{0.50}=4\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

The elastic potential energy is

$$
E=\frac{20(0.10)^2}{2(0.50)}=0.20\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 18

The extension is

$$
x=2.1-1.5=0.60\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

Tension is

$$
T=\frac{60(0.60)}{1.5}=24\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

Elastic potential energy is

$$
E=\frac{60(0.60)^2}{2(1.5)}=7.2\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

### Problem 19

The initial elastic potential energy is

$$
E=\frac{50(0.40)^2}{2(1)}=4\ \mathrm{J}.
$$

At the natural length this becomes kinetic energy:

$$
\frac12(0.50)v^2=4.
$$

Hence

$$
v=4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 20

Let the maximum extension be $x$. At maximum extension the particle is
instantaneously at rest. Loss of gravitational potential energy equals elastic
potential energy:

$$
mgx=\frac{\lambda x^2}{2l}.
$$

Substitute $m=1$, $g=10$, $\lambda=40$, and $l=2$:

$$
10x=\frac{40x^2}{4}.
$$

So

$$
10x=10x^2.
$$

Ignoring the initial solution $x=0$, the maximum extension is

$$
x=1\ \mathrm{m}.
$$
