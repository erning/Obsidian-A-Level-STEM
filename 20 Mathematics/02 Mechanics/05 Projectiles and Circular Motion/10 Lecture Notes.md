## 1. Core Frame

Projectile and circular-motion models extend motion into two dimensions with clear geometric constraints.

## 3. Method Selection

Use these habits while studying:

- Resolve motion into components before solving.
- Use a diagram to mark launch angle, velocity components, and acceleration.
- Identify the force that supplies centripetal acceleration.

Use these practice routes to turn the ideas into fluency:

- Solve projectile trajectory problems.
- Solve circular-motion force problems.
- Combine energy and circular-motion constraints.

When two methods seem possible, choose the one that exposes the most structure with the least algebra. Then check the answer in another representation whenever possible.

## 6. Common Traps

- Giving horizontal acceleration when air resistance is ignored.
- Using degrees where radians are required.
- Treating centripetal force as an additional physical force.
