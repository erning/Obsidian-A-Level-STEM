# Projectiles and Circular Motion Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Resolve first, then choose
the equation. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ unless a question states
otherwise.

## A. Projectile Components and Trajectories

1. A projectile is launched from horizontal ground at $30\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   at $30^\circ$ above the horizontal. Find the time of flight, range, and
   greatest height.

2. A ball is projected horizontally at $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ from a point
   $45\ \mathrm{m}$ above horizontal ground. Find the time to hit the ground,
   the horizontal range, and the impact speed.

3. A projectile is launched at $30\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ at $30^\circ$ above
   the horizontal. Find its velocity components, speed, and direction after
   $3\ \mathrm{s}$.

4. A projectile is launched at $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ at $45^\circ$. Show
   that its trajectory is $y=x-\dfrac{x^2}{40}$, and find its height when
   $x=20\ \mathrm{m}$.

5. A projectile is fired from the origin with speed $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   and passes through $(30,5)$. Find the possible angles of projection.

6. Find the maximum horizontal range of a projectile launched from ground level
   with speed $25\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and state the angle that gives it.

7. A projectile lands at the same height from which it was launched. Its time
   of flight is $4\ \mathrm{s}$ and its range is $80\ \mathrm{m}$. Find its
   initial speed and angle of projection.

8. A projectile is launched at $30^\circ$ and lands at the same height after
   $5\ \mathrm{s}$. Find its initial speed and range.

## B. Uniform Circular Motion

9. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves in a circle of radius $2\ \mathrm{m}$ at
   speed $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its angular speed and the resultant
   force towards the centre.

10. A particle moves in a circle of radius $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$ with angular
    speed $5\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Find its speed and centripetal
    acceleration.

11. A car of mass $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ moves round a flat circular curve of
    radius $50\ \mathrm{m}$ at $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the frictional
    force needed and the least coefficient of friction.

12. A $0.20\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is attached to a light string of length
    $1.6\ \mathrm{m}$ and moves as a conical pendulum. The string makes
    $60^\circ$ with the vertical. Find the tension and angular speed.

13. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves inside a smooth vertical circle of radius
    $2\ \mathrm{m}$. Its speed at the bottom is $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find
    the normal contact force at the bottom.

14. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves inside a smooth vertical circle of radius
    $2\ \mathrm{m}$. Its speed at the top is $5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the
    normal contact force at the top.

## C. Vertical Circles and Energy

15. A particle attached to a light string of length $1.5\ \mathrm{m}$ has speed
    $8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ at the bottom of a vertical circle. Find its speed
    at the top if there is no energy loss, and decide whether the string can
    remain taut.

16. Find the minimum speed at the bottom for a particle on a string of length
    $1.5\ \mathrm{m}$ to complete a vertical circle.

17. A particle moves inside a smooth vertical circle of radius $2\ \mathrm{m}$.
    It is just in contact at the top. Find its speed at the top and at the
    bottom, assuming no energy loss.

18. A conical pendulum has string length $1\ \mathrm{m}$ and angle
    $60^\circ$ with the vertical. Find its angular speed and period.

## D. Mixed Review

19. A projectile has initial velocity components $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
    horizontally and $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ vertically upwards. Find its
    height when it is $30\ \mathrm{m}$ horizontally from the launch point, and
    decide whether it clears a wall of height $10\ \mathrm{m}$ there.

20. A projectile is fired from the origin and passes through $(40,15)$. Its
    angle of projection $\alpha$ satisfies $\tan\alpha=\dfrac34$. Find its
    initial speed.
