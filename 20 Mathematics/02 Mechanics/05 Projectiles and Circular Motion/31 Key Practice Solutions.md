# Projectiles and Circular Motion Key Practice Solutions

This note gives worked solutions for [[20 Mathematics/02 Mechanics/05 Projectiles and Circular Motion/30 Key Practice Problems|Projectiles and Circular Motion Key Practice Problems]]. Use it to check components, signs, units, and radial force equations. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ unless a question states otherwise.

## A. Projectile Components and Trajectories

### Problem 1

The components of the initial velocity are

$$
u_x=30\cos30^\circ,\qquad u_y=30\sin30^\circ=15.
$$

For landing at the same height,

$$
0=15t-5t^2.
$$

So the non-zero time is

$$
t=3\ \mathrm{s}.
$$

The range is

$$
R=(30\cos30^\circ)(3)=77.9\ \mathrm{m}
$$

to 3 s.f. The greatest height is found from $v_y=0$:

$$
0=15^2-2(10)h,
$$

so

$$
h=11.25\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

### Problem 2

The vertical motion starts with vertical velocity $0$. From a height
$45\ \mathrm{m}$,

$$
45=\frac12gt^2=5t^2.
$$

Thus

$$
t=3\ \mathrm{s}.
$$

The horizontal range is

$$
x=20(3)=60\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

At impact,

$$
v_x=20,\qquad v_y=-gt=-30.
$$

The impact speed is

$$
\sqrt{20^2+30^2}=36.1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f.

### Problem 3

The horizontal component remains

$$
v_x=30\cos30^\circ=26.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f. The vertical component after $3\ \mathrm{s}$ is

$$
v_y=30\sin30^\circ-10(3)=15-30=-15.
$$

Thus

$$
v_y=-15.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The speed is

$$
\sqrt{26.0^2+15.0^2}=30.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The angle below the horizontal is

$$
\tan^{-1}\frac{15.0}{26.0}=30^\circ.
$$

### Problem 4

Use

$$
y=x\tan\theta-\frac{gx^2}{2u^2\cos^2\theta}.
$$

With $u=20$ and $\theta=45^\circ$,

$$
y=x-\frac{10x^2}{2(20^2)(1/2)}
=x-\frac{x^2}{40}.
$$

At $x=20$,

$$
y=20-\frac{20^2}{40}=10\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

### Problem 5

Let $T=\tan\theta$. The trajectory equation can be written

$$
y=xT-\frac{gx^2}{2u^2}(1+T^2).
$$

Substitute $x=30$, $y=5$, $u=20$, and $g=10$:

$$
5=30T-11.25(1+T^2).
$$

This gives

$$
9T^2-24T+13=0.
$$

Therefore

$$
T=\frac{4\pm\sqrt3}{3}.
$$

So

$$
\theta=62.4^\circ\quad \text{or}\quad 37.1^\circ
$$

to 3 s.f.

### Problem 6

For a projectile landing at the same height,

$$
R=\frac{u^2\sin2\theta}{g}.
$$

The maximum occurs when $\sin2\theta=1$, so

$$
\theta=45^\circ.
$$

The maximum range is

$$
R_{\max}=\frac{25^2}{10}=62.5\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

### Problem 7

The horizontal velocity is

$$
u_x=\frac{80}{4}=20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

For a same-height flight, the time to the highest point is $2\ \mathrm{s}$, so

$$
u_y=g(2)=20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

Thus the initial speed is

$$
\sqrt{20^2+20^2}=28.3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f., and the angle is

$$
45^\circ.
$$

### Problem 8

For landing at the same height,

$$
T=\frac{2u\sin30^\circ}{g}.
$$

Since $T=5$,

$$
5=\frac{2u(1/2)}{10}.
$$

Therefore

$$
u=50\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The range is

$$
R=(50\cos30^\circ)(5)=217\ \mathrm{m}
$$

to 3 s.f.

## B. Uniform Circular Motion

### Problem 9

Angular speed is

$$
\omega=\frac{v}{r}=\frac{4}{2}=2\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The resultant force towards the centre is

$$
F=m\frac{v^2}{r}=3\frac{4^2}{2}=24\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

### Problem 10

Use $v=r\omega$:

$$
v=0.80(5)=4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

Centripetal acceleration is

$$
r\omega^2=0.80(5^2)=20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}.
$$

### Problem 11

The frictional force supplies the centripetal force:

$$
F=m\frac{v^2}{r}=1000\frac{20^2}{50}=8000\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

The least coefficient of friction satisfies

$$
\mu mg=8000.
$$

Thus

$$
\mu=\frac{8000}{1000(10)}=0.8.
$$

### Problem 12

Vertical equilibrium gives

$$
T\cos60^\circ=0.20(10)=2.
$$

Thus

$$
T=4\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

For a conical pendulum,

$$
\omega^2=\frac{g}{l\cos\theta}.
$$

So

$$
\omega^2=\frac{10}{1.6(0.5)}=12.5,
$$

and

$$
\omega=3.54\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f.

### Problem 13

At the bottom of the circle, towards the centre is upward:

$$
R-mg=m\frac{v^2}{r}.
$$

Thus

$$
R-10=1\frac{8^2}{2}=32.
$$

So

$$
R=42\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

### Problem 14

At the top of the circle, towards the centre is downward:

$$
R+mg=m\frac{v^2}{r}.
$$

Thus

$$
R+10=1\frac{5^2}{2}=12.5.
$$

So

$$
R=2.5\ \mathrm{N}.
$$

## C. Vertical Circles and Energy

### Problem 15

From bottom to top the particle rises by

$$
2r=3\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

Energy gives

$$
v_t^2=8^2-2(10)(3)=4.
$$

So

$$
v_t=2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

For the string to remain taut at the top, the minimum condition is

$$
v_t^2\ge gr=15.
$$

Here $v_t^2=4$, so the string cannot remain taut.

### Problem 16

For minimum complete motion, the string is just taut at the top:

$$
v_t^2=gr.
$$

Energy from bottom to top gives

$$
v_b^2=v_t^2+4gr=5gr.
$$

With $r=1.5$,

$$
v_b^2=5(10)(1.5)=75.
$$

Therefore

$$
v_b=8.66\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f.

### Problem 17

Just in contact at the top means the normal contact force is zero:

$$
mg=m\frac{v_t^2}{r}.
$$

So

$$
v_t=\sqrt{gr}=\sqrt{20}=4.47\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

From top to bottom the particle drops $4\ \mathrm{m}$, so

$$
v_b^2=v_t^2+2g(4)=20+80=100.
$$

Thus

$$
v_b=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
$$

### Problem 18

For a conical pendulum,

$$
\omega^2=\frac{g}{l\cos\theta}.
$$

With $l=1$ and $\theta=60^\circ$,

$$
\omega^2=\frac{10}{0.5}=20.
$$

So

$$
\omega=4.47\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
$$

The period is

$$
T=\frac{2\pi}{\omega}=1.40\ \mathrm{s}
$$

to 3 s.f.

## D. Mixed Review

### Problem 19

The time to reach $x=30$ is

$$
t=\frac{30}{20}=1.5\ \mathrm{s}.
$$

The height then is

$$
y=15(1.5)-5(1.5^2)=11.25\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

The projectile clears a $10\ \mathrm{m}$ wall by

$$
11.25-10=1.25\ \mathrm{m}.
$$

### Problem 20

Since $\tan\alpha=\frac34$, we have $\cos^2\alpha=\frac{16}{25}$. Use

$$
y=x\tan\alpha-\frac{gx^2}{2u^2\cos^2\alpha}.
$$

Substitute $x=40$, $y=15$, $g=10$, and $\tan\alpha=3/4$:

$$
15=40\left(\frac34\right)
-\frac{10(40^2)}{2u^2(16/25)}.
$$

So

$$
15=30-\frac{12500}{u^2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
u^2=\frac{12500}{15},
$$

and

$$
u=28.9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}
$$

to 3 s.f.
