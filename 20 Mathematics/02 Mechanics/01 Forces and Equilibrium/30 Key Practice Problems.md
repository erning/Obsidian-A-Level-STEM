# Forces and Equilibrium Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw a force diagram before
writing equations. Take $g=9.8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where a mass is given.

## A. Particle Equilibrium and Components

1. A light ring carries a vertical load of $36\ \mathrm{N}$. It is held by a
   string making $50^\circ$ above the horizontal to the right and by a
   horizontal string to the left. Find the tensions in the two strings.

2. Two forces of $28\ \mathrm{N}$ east and $45\ \mathrm{N}$ north act on a
   particle. Find the magnitude and direction of the third force needed for
   equilibrium.

3. A weight of $100\ \mathrm{N}$ is supported by two strings. The left string
   makes $30^\circ$ with the horizontal and the right string makes $60^\circ$
   with the horizontal. Find the two tensions.

4. A particle of mass $6\ \mathrm{kg}$ rests on a smooth plane inclined at
   $20^\circ$ to the horizontal. It is held by a string parallel to the plane.
   Find the string tension and the normal contact force.

5. A block of mass $8\ \mathrm{kg}$ rests on a smooth horizontal table. State
   the normal contact force exerted by the table on the block and the force
   exerted by the block on the table.

## B. Friction and Limiting Equilibrium

6. A crate of mass $10\ \mathrm{kg}$ is on a rough horizontal floor. The
   coefficient of friction is $0.25$. Find the least horizontal force needed
   to make the crate start moving.

7. A box of mass $14\ \mathrm{kg}$ is pulled by a force $P$ at $25^\circ$
   above the horizontal. The coefficient of friction is $0.30$. Find $P$ when
   the box is about to move.

8. A particle rests on a rough plane inclined at $18^\circ$ to the horizontal.
   Find the least coefficient of friction needed for equilibrium.

9. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ block on a rough plane inclined at $25^\circ$ is
   connected by a light string over a smooth pulley to a hanging
   $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ mass. The system is in equilibrium. Find the magnitude and
   direction of the friction on the block, and find the least coefficient of
   friction needed.

10. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle is on a rough plane inclined at $30^\circ$ to
    the horizontal. The coefficient of friction is $0.20$. A force $P$ acts up
    the plane. Find the least value of $P$ needed to prevent the particle from
    sliding down the plane.

## C. Moments and Rigid Bodies

11. A uniform beam of length $5\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $80\ \mathrm{N}$ is
    hinged at one end and supported vertically at the other end. A
    $120\ \mathrm{N}$ load acts $4\ \mathrm{m}$ from the hinge. Find the
    vertical reactions at the support and at the hinge.

12. A uniform rod of length $3\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $60\ \mathrm{N}$ is
    held horizontally by a hinge at one end and a string at the other end. The
    string makes $40^\circ$ above the rod. Find the tension and the hinge
    reaction components.

13. A uniform ladder of length $5\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $200\ \mathrm{N}$
    rests at $60^\circ$ to the horizontal against a smooth vertical wall. The
    floor is rough. Find the normal reaction at the wall, the friction at the
    floor, and the least coefficient of friction at the floor.

14. A uniform beam of length $6\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $90\ \mathrm{N}$ rests
    on supports at its ends. Loads of $150\ \mathrm{N}$ and $120\ \mathrm{N}$
    act $2\ \mathrm{m}$ and $5\ \mathrm{m}$ from the left end respectively.
    Find the two support reactions.

15. A rectangular box has weight $300\ \mathrm{N}$, base width
    $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$, and height $1.50\ \mathrm{m}$. It is pushed
    horizontally at height $1.20\ \mathrm{m}$. The coefficient of friction is
    $0.25$. Decide whether it slides or topples first.

## D. Centre of Mass and Mixed Review

16. A uniform lamina is made from three identical square plates of side
    $1\ \mathrm{m}$. Their centres are at $(0.5,0.5)$, $(1.5,0.5)$, and
    $(0.5,1.5)$. Find the centre of mass.

17. A uniform rod $AB$ has length $4\ \mathrm{m}$ and mass $3\ \mathrm{kg}$.
    A particle of mass $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ is attached at $B$. Find the centre of
    mass of the combined system, measured from $A$.

18. A uniform triangular lamina has vertices $(0,0)$, $(6,0)$, and $(0,3)$.
    Use the centroid formula to find its centre of mass.

19. A uniform horizontal rod $AB$ of length $5\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight
    $80\ \mathrm{N}$ is hinged at $A$. A load of $100\ \mathrm{N}$ acts
    $3\ \mathrm{m}$ from $A$. A string at $B$ makes $30^\circ$ above the rod
    and pulls up and toward $A$. Find the string tension and the hinge
    reaction components.

20. A rectangular block has weight $160\ \mathrm{N}$, base width
    $0.50\ \mathrm{m}$, and height $1.00\ \mathrm{m}$. It is pushed
    horizontally at height $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$. The coefficient of friction is
    $0.60$. Decide whether it slides or topples first, and state the value of
    $P$ at which this first occurs.
