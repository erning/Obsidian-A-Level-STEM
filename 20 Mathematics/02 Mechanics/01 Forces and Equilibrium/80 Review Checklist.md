# Forces and Equilibrium Review Checklist

Use this after the worked examples and practice problems. Revisit [Forces and Equilibrium Lecture Notes](10%20Lecture%20Notes.md) if any item is uncertain.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] A force diagram shows forces acting on one chosen body only.
- [ ] A particle in equilibrium has zero resultant force.
- [ ] Force components must be taken along clearly chosen perpendicular axes.
- [ ] A smooth contact has no friction; a rough contact may have friction.
- [ ] Friction satisfies $F\le \mu R$, and only becomes $F=\mu R$ at limiting equilibrium.
- [ ] Newton's third law pairs act on different bodies.
- [ ] The moment of a force is $Fd$, where $d$ is the perpendicular distance from the point to the force's line of action.
- [ ] A rigid body in equilibrium needs both force balance and moment balance.
- [ ] The weight of a rigid body acts through its centre of mass.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Draw a force diagram for a ring, block, ladder, beam, or lamina.
- [ ] Resolve forces on horizontal, vertical, and inclined axes.
- [ ] Find a third force that balances two given forces.
- [ ] Use $R=mg\cos\theta$ and $mg\sin\theta$ correctly on an inclined plane.
- [ ] Decide the direction of friction from possible motion.
- [ ] Use $F=\mu R$ only when the body is about to slip.
- [ ] Take moments about a point that removes an unwanted unknown.
- [ ] Find support reactions for uniform beams and rods.
- [ ] Compare sliding and toppling thresholds.
- [ ] Find the centre of mass of a simple composite body by replacing parts with particles at their own centres.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Drawing action and reaction forces on the same force diagram.
- [ ] Treating all friction forces as limiting friction.
- [ ] Forgetting that an angled pull changes the normal contact force.
- [ ] Reversing the components of weight on an inclined plane.
- [ ] Using the sloping length instead of the perpendicular distance in a moment.
- [ ] Checking only $\sum F=0$ for a rigid body and forgetting $\sum M=0$.
- [ ] Choosing the wrong edge as the pivot in a toppling problem.
- [ ] Reporting a coefficient of friction with units.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can turn a worded equilibrium situation into a clean force diagram.
- [ ] I can choose axes or a moment point that reduce the algebra.
- [ ] I can explain why friction points in the direction I used.
- [ ] I can solve both particle equilibrium and single rigid-body equilibrium problems.
- [ ] I can check a final answer with signs, units, limiting cases, and physical plausibility.
