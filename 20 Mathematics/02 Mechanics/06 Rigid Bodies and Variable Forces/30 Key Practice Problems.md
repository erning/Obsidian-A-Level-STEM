# Rigid Bodies and Variable Forces Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. For rigid bodies, choose a
moment point deliberately. For variable forces, choose the variable before
writing Newton's second law. Take $g=10\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where needed.

## A. Rigid Bodies and Centre of Mass

1. A uniform beam of length $6\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $120\ \mathrm{N}$ is
   supported at both ends. A $180\ \mathrm{N}$ load acts $2\ \mathrm{m}$ from
   the left end. Find the support reactions.

2. A uniform rod of length $5\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $100\ \mathrm{N}$ is
   hinged at one end and supported vertically at the other. A
   $150\ \mathrm{N}$ load acts $4\ \mathrm{m}$ from the hinge. Find the
   vertical reactions.

3. A uniform lamina consists of a $4$ by $2$ rectangle with lower-left corner
   at the origin and a $2$ by $3$ rectangle placed above its left half. Find
   the centre of mass.

4. A uniform $6$ by $4$ rectangular lamina has a $2$ by $2$ square removed from
   its top-right corner. Find the centre of mass of the remaining lamina.

5. A rectangular block has weight $400\ \mathrm{N}$, base width
   $1.0\ \mathrm{m}$, and height $2.0\ \mathrm{m}$. It is pushed horizontally
   at height $1.5\ \mathrm{m}$. The coefficient of friction is $0.30$. Decide
   whether it slides or topples first.

## B. Work Done by a Variable Force

6. A force $F=5x^2$ acts in the direction of motion from $x=0$ to $x=3$. Find
   the work done.

7. A force $F=20-2x$ acts in the direction of motion from $x=0$ to $x=5$. Find
   the work done.

8. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle initially moving at $2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   receives $75\ \mathrm{J}$ of work from a variable force. Find its final
   speed.

9. A force $F=10+4x$ acts in the direction of motion from $x=0$ to $x=4$. Find
   the work done.

10. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves under force $F=6x$. At $x=1$ its speed
    is $4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its speed at $x=3$.

## C. Variable-Force Differential Equations

11. A $3\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle starts from rest and moves under force
    $F=6t$. Find its velocity at $t=4$.

12. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle has initial velocity
    $3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and moves under force $F=12-4t$. Find $v(t)$ and
    $v(3)$.

13. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves with resistance $3v\ \mathrm{N}$
    opposite to its motion. Its initial speed is $9\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.
    Find $v(t)$.

14. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves under force $F=4x$. At $x=0$, its speed
    is $1\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its speed at $x=3$.

15. A $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle starts from rest at $x=0$ and moves under
    force $F=6-2x$. Find its maximum speed and the next position at which it
    comes to rest.

## D. Mixed Review

16. A rectangular block has weight $300\ \mathrm{N}$, base width
    $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$, and is pushed horizontally at height $1.0\ \mathrm{m}$.
    The coefficient of friction is $0.50$. Decide whether it slides or topples
    first.

17. A uniform rod of length $4\ \mathrm{m}$ and mass $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ has a
    $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle attached at one end and a $3\ \mathrm{kg}$
    particle attached at the other. Find the centre of mass measured from the
    $1\ \mathrm{kg}$ end.

18. A $10\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves with resistance $5v\ \mathrm{N}$
    opposite to its motion. Its initial speed is $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.
    Find the time for its speed to halve.

19. A $4\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle starts from rest and moves along a rough
    horizontal line. A driving force $F=30-2x$ acts from $x=0$ to $x=10$, and a
    constant resistance of $5\ \mathrm{N}$ opposes the motion. Find the speed
    at $x=10$.

20. A $2\ \mathrm{kg}$ particle moves under force $F=\dfrac{16}{x}$ for
    $x>0$. At $x=1$, its speed is $3\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its speed at
    $x=e$.
