# Normal and Poisson Distributions Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Sketch the probability
region before standardising or summing probabilities.

## A. Normal Distribution

1. Let $X\sim N(60,5^2)$. Find $P(X<67)$ and $P(55<X<67)$.

2. Let $X\sim N(100,12^2)$. Find $P(X>118)$.

3. Let $X\sim N(40,6^2)$. Find $k$ such that $P(X<k)=0.25$.

4. Let $X\sim N(\mu,4^2)$ and suppose $P(X<32)=0.1587$. Find $\mu$.

5. A normal random variable $X$ satisfies

   $$
   P(X<18)=0.10,
   \qquad
   P(X<30)=0.90.
   $$

   Find the mean and standard deviation of $X$.

## B. Poisson Distribution

6. Let $X\sim \operatorname{Po}(2.5)$. Find $P(X=3)$ and $P(X\le1)$.

7. Emails arrive at a mean rate of $6$ per hour. Model the number of emails
   by a Poisson distribution. Find the probability of exactly $2$ emails in
   $30$ minutes, and the probability of at least $1$ email in $10$ minutes.

8. Let $X\sim \operatorname{Po}(5)$. Find $P(X>3)$, $E(X)$, and
   $\operatorname{Var}(X)$.

9. Customers arrive independently at an average rate of $4$ per hour. State a
   suitable distribution for the number of customers in $3$ hours, and give
   its mean and variance.

10. Let $X\sim \operatorname{Po}(\lambda)$ and suppose $P(X=0)=0.1353$
    approximately. Find $\lambda$ and then find $P(X=2)$.

## C. Approximations and Continuity Correction

11. Let $X\sim B(120,0.02)$. Use a Poisson approximation to estimate
    $P(X\le2)$.

12. Let $X\sim B(100,0.45)$. Use a normal approximation to estimate
    $P(X<40)$.

13. Let $X\sim \operatorname{Po}(20)$. Use a normal approximation to estimate
    $P(X\ge25)$.

14. Let $X\sim B(400,0.01)$. Choose a suitable approximation and estimate
    $P(X=0)$. State the condition you used.

15. A student wants to approximate $B(20,0.1)$ by a normal distribution.
    Explain whether this is justified by the CAIE condition.

## D. Linear Combinations

16. Let $X\sim N(30,9)$ and $Y=2X+5$. Find the distribution of $Y$ and
    $P(Y<70)$.

17. Independent random variables $X$ and $Y$ have

    $$
    X\sim N(10,4),
    \qquad
    Y\sim N(6,9).
    $$

    Let $T=X+2Y$. Find the distribution of $T$ and $P(T>30)$.

18. Independent random variables $X$ and $Y$ have

    $$
    X\sim \operatorname{Po}(3),
    \qquad
    Y\sim \operatorname{Po}(4).
    $$

    Find the distribution of $X+Y$ and $P(X+Y=5)$.

19. Independent random variables $X$ and $Y$ have
    $X\sim \operatorname{Po}(2)$ and $Y\sim \operatorname{Po}(5)$. Find
    $E(3X-2Y)$ and $\operatorname{Var}(3X-2Y)$. Is $3X-2Y$ Poisson?

20. Independent random variables $X$ and $Y$ have

    $$
    X\sim N(12,4),
    \qquad
    Y\sim N(5,1).
    $$

    Let $W=3X-Y$. Find $P(W>35)$.
