## 3. Method Selection

Use these habits while studying:

- Write the random variable definition in words before using symbols.
- Check total probability equals 1.
- Connect distribution choice to the random mechanism.

Use these practice routes to turn the ideas into fluency:

- Build probability tables.
- Calculate expectation and variance.
- Use binomial and geometric models.

When two methods seem possible, choose the one that exposes the most structure with the least algebra. Then check the answer in another representation whenever possible.

## 6. Common Traps

- Using a distribution without checking assumptions.
- Confusing $P(X=x)$ with $P(X\le x)$.
- Leaving expectation without units or context.
