# Counting and Probability Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Decide first whether order
matters, whether replacement occurs, and whether cases overlap.

## A. Counting

1. From $7$ people, find the number of ordered selections of $3$, and the
   number of unordered selections of $3$.

2. Find the number of distinct arrangements of the letters in BALLOON.

3. Five boys and four girls stand in a line. Find the number of arrangements
   in which all four girls stand together.

4. A committee of $4$ is chosen from $4$ girls and $6$ boys. Find the number
   of committees with exactly $2$ girls.

5. Eight people stand in a line. Find the number of arrangements in which two
   particular people are not next to each other.

6. A four-digit code is made from digits $0$ to $9$. The first digit cannot be
   $0$. Find the number of codes if repetition is allowed, and if repetition
   is not allowed.

## B. Elementary Probability

7. Two fair dice are thrown. Find the probability that the total score is at
   least $10$.

8. A bag contains $4$ red and $5$ blue balls. Three balls are chosen without
   replacement. Find the probability that all three are blue.

9. A bag contains $3$ red and $7$ blue balls. Two balls are drawn with
   replacement. Find the probability of at least one red ball.

10. Events $A$ and $B$ satisfy $P(A)=0.40$, $P(B)=0.35$, and
    $P(A\cap B)=0.12$. Find $P(A\cup B)$, $P((A\cup B)')$, and $P(A\mid B)$.
    Are $A$ and $B$ independent?

## C. Tree Diagrams and Conditional Probability

11. A bag contains $6$ red and $4$ blue balls. Two balls are drawn without
    replacement. Find the probability that the two balls have the same colour.

12. In a group of $100$ students, $45$ study Mathematics, $30$ study Physics,
    and $18$ study both. Find $P(\text{Mathematics}\mid\text{Physics})$ and
    $P(\text{Physics}\mid\text{Mathematics})$.

13. Events $A$ and $B$ are independent with $P(A)=0.30$ and $P(B)=0.50$. Find
    $P(A\cup B)$.

14. Events $A$ and $B$ are mutually exclusive with $P(A)=0.20$ and
    $P(B)=0.45$. Find $P(A\cup B)$ and explain whether they are independent.

15. A fair coin is tossed $4$ times. Find the probability of exactly two heads.

## D. Mixed Review

16. Five cards are selected from a standard deck of $52$ cards. Find the
    probability that exactly two are aces.

17. Find the number of distinct arrangements of the letters in STATISTICS.

18. Seven people stand in a line. Two particular people must have exactly one
    person between them. Find the number of arrangements.

19. A component comes from machine A with probability $0.7$ and from machine B
    with probability $0.3$. Machine A makes defective components with
    probability $0.02$, and machine B with probability $0.05$. Find the
    probability that a defective component came from A.

20. Two fair dice are thrown. Given that the total score is greater than $8$,
    find the probability that at least one die shows $6$.
