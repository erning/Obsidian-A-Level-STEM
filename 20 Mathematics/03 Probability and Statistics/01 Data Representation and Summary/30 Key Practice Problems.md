# Data Representation and Summary Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. State whether values from
grouped data are exact or estimates.

## A. Data Types and Diagrams

1. For each variable, state whether it is qualitative, discrete quantitative,
   or continuous quantitative, and name one suitable display: eye colour,
   number of siblings, and time to run $100\ \mathrm{m}$.

2. The times, in minutes, are
   $12,13,15,16,18,21,22,22,24,27,31,33$. Construct a stem-and-leaf diagram
   and state the median.

3. For the data
   $4,5,7,7,9,10,13,17$, find the mean, median, range, quartiles, and IQR.

4. For the data
   $2,4,4,5,5,7,9$, find the mean and standard deviation.

5. A histogram has classes $0\le x<5$, $5\le x<15$, and $15\le x<30$ with
   frequencies $6$, $20$, and $18$. Find the frequency density for each class.

## B. Grouped Data and Cumulative Frequency

6. Estimate the mean and standard deviation for grouped data with classes
   $0\le x<5$, $5\le x<10$, $10\le x<20$ and frequencies $3$, $7$, $10$.

7. A cumulative frequency table is:

   | Upper boundary | 10 | 20 | 30 | 40 |
   |---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
   | Cumulative frequency | 5 | 17 | 29 | 40 |

   Estimate $Q_1$, the median, and $Q_3$.

8. For $40$ observations, $y=(x-100)/5$, $\sum y=-12$, and
   $\sum y^2=196$. Find the mean and standard deviation of $x$.

9. A data set has $Q_1=32$ and $Q_3=48$. Use the $1.5IQR$ rule to decide
   whether $75$ is an outlier.

10. Data set A has mean $50$ and standard deviation $4$. Data set B has mean
    $48$ and standard deviation $10$. Compare the two sets.

## C. Interpreting Representations

11. Explain one advantage of a back-to-back stem-and-leaf diagram when
    comparing two small data sets.

12. A cumulative frequency table has cumulative frequencies $8$, $20$, $36$,
    and $50$ at upper boundaries $20$, $30$, $40$, and $50$. Estimate the
    proportion of observations greater than $35$.

13. A histogram class has interval $20\le x<35$ and frequency density $1.6$.
    Find its frequency.

14. For $25$ observations, $\sum x=430$ and $\sum x^2=7820$. Find the mean and
    standard deviation.

15. Data set A has $12$ observations with mean $8$. Data set B has $18$
    observations with mean $11$. Find the combined mean.

## D. Mixed Review

16. If $x=3y+7$, $\bar y=4$, and $\sigma_y=2.5$, find $\bar x$ and
    $\sigma_x$.

17. A salary distribution is strongly positively skewed. Which summary pair is
    usually more useful: mean and standard deviation, or median and IQR?
    Explain briefly.

18. A box plot has median closer to $Q_1$ than to $Q_3$, and the upper whisker
    is much longer than the lower whisker. Describe the skew.

19. Explain why a histogram with unequal class widths must use frequency
    density rather than frequency on the vertical axis.

20. Data set A is $6,7,8,9,10$. Data set B is $2,5,8,11,14$. Compare their
    centres and spreads.
