# Sampling, Estimation and Hypothesis Tests Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. In every test, write the
hypotheses before calculating.

## A. Sampling and Estimation

1. A supermarket wants to estimate the mean waiting time of all customers. It
   records the waiting times of the first $30$ customers after opening on a
   Monday. Identify the population, parameter, sample, statistic, and one
   reason the sampling method may be unsatisfactory.

2. A population has mean $40$ and standard deviation $10$. A random sample of
   size $25$ is taken. Find $E(\bar X)$, $\operatorname{Var}(\bar X)$, the
   standard error, and $P(\bar X>43)$ if the population is normal.

3. A random sample gives the observations

   $$
   7,\quad 9,\quad 10,\quad 6,\quad 8.
   $$

   Find the sample mean and the unbiased estimate of the population variance.

4. A random sample of size $100$ has mean $18.4$. The population standard
   deviation is known to be $5$. Find a $95\%$ confidence interval for the
   population mean.

5. In a random sample of $120$ items, $72$ are acceptable. Find an approximate
   $90\%$ confidence interval for the population proportion of acceptable
   items.

## B. 9709 Hypothesis Tests

6. A process has known standard deviation $8$. A random sample of size $64$
   has mean $52.1$. Test at the $5\%$ level whether the population mean is
   greater than $50$.

7. A population has known standard deviation $12$. A random sample of size
   $36$ has mean $95$. Test at the $5\%$ level whether the population mean is
   different from $100$.

8. A manufacturer claims that the defect probability is $0.4$. In $15$
   independent items, $3$ are defective. Test at the $5\%$ level whether the
   defect probability is less than $0.4$.

9. A count is modelled as Poisson. Test

   $$
   H_0:\lambda=2,
   \qquad
   H_1:\lambda>2
   $$

   at the $5\%$ level when the observed count is $6$.

10. A coin is tested for bias towards heads. In $200$ tosses, $116$ heads are
    observed. Use a normal approximation to test at the $5\%$ level whether
    the probability of heads is greater than $\frac12$.

11. A large sample of size $100$ has mean $15.7$ and sample standard deviation
    $4$. Test at the $5\%$ level whether the population mean is greater than
    $15$.

## C. Type I and Type II Errors

12. For

    $$
    H_0:p=0.2,
    \qquad
    H_1:p>0.2,
    $$

    a test based on $X\sim B(10,p)$ rejects $H_0$ when $X\ge5$. Find the
    probability of a Type I error, and the probability of a Type II error when
    the true value is $p=0.4$.

13. For

    $$
    H_0:\lambda=3,
    \qquad
    H_1:\lambda<3,
    $$

    a Poisson test rejects $H_0$ when $X=0$. Find the probability of a Type I
    error, and the probability of a Type II error when the true value is
    $\lambda=1$.

14. A normal test uses

    $$
    H_0:\mu=100,
    \qquad
    H_1:\mu>100,
    $$

    with known standard deviation $10$ and sample size $25$. It rejects $H_0$
    when $\bar X>104$. Find the probability of a Type I error, and the
    probability of a Type II error when the true mean is $106$.

15. Let $X\sim B(12,0.5)$ under $H_0$. For a one-tailed test with
    $H_1:p>0.5$, find the smallest critical region of the form $X\ge c$ with
    significance level at most $5\%$. State the actual significance level.

## D. 9231 Inference Extensions

16. A random sample of size $9$ from a normal population has mean $102.4$ and
    sample standard deviation $4.5$. Test at the $5\%$ level whether the
    population mean is greater than $100$.

17. A random sample of size $12$ from a normal population has mean $31.5$ and
    sample standard deviation $2.4$. Find a $95\%$ confidence interval for the
    population mean.

18. In a paired experiment, the differences after minus before have sample
    size $8$, mean $1.6$, and sample standard deviation $2.0$. Test at the
    $5\%$ level whether the mean difference is positive.

19. Two independent normal samples are assumed to have a common population
    variance. The first has $n_1=6$ and $s_1^2=4.0$; the second has $n_2=8$
    and $s_2^2=6.5$. Calculate the pooled estimate of the population variance.

20. Two independent large samples have

    $$
    \bar x_A=82,\quad n_A=50,\quad \sigma_A=10,
    $$

    and

    $$
    \bar x_B=78,\quad n_B=60,\quad \sigma_B=12.
    $$

    Test at the $5\%$ level whether population $A$ has a larger mean than
    population $B$.
