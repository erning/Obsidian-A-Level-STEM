# Textbook Chapter Map

Sources include the CAIE 9709 and 9231 syllabuses and the coursebooks under `assets/course-books/`. This map gives a reading route through the relevant source chapters.

## 9709 Pure Mathematics

- Quadratics and Functions: [[assets/course-books/9709 - Mathematics/9709 - Pure Mathematics 1.pdf|9709 Pure Mathematics 1]] Chapters 1-2.
- Coordinate Geometry: 9709 Pure Mathematics 1 Chapter 3.
- Circular Measure and Trigonometry: 9709 Pure Mathematics 1 Chapters 4-5.
- Series: 9709 Pure Mathematics 1 Chapter 6.
- Differentiation: 9709 Pure Mathematics 1 Chapters 7-8.
- Integration: 9709 Pure Mathematics 1 Chapter 9.
- Algebra, Logarithmic and Exponential Functions, Trigonometry, Differentiation, Integration, Numerical Solution of Equations, Vectors, Differential Equations, and Complex Numbers: [[assets/course-books/9709 - Mathematics/9709 - Pure Mathematics 2 and 3.pdf|9709 Pure Mathematics 2 and 3]] Chapters 1-11.

## 9709 Mechanics

- Forces and equilibrium, kinematics of motion in a straight line, momentum, Newton's laws of motion, and energy, work and power: [[assets/course-books/9709 - Mathematics/9709 - Mechanics.pdf|9709 Mechanics]].

## 9709 Probability and Statistics

- Representation of data, permutations and combinations, probability, discrete random variables, and the normal distribution: [[assets/course-books/9709 - Mathematics/9709 - Probability and Statistics 1.pdf|9709 Probability and Statistics 1]].
- The Poisson distribution, linear combinations of random variables, continuous random variables, sampling and estimation, and hypothesis tests: [[assets/course-books/9709 - Mathematics/9709 - Probability and Statistics 2.pdf|9709 Probability and Statistics 2]].

## 9231 Further Mathematics

- Further Pure Mathematics 1: roots of polynomial equations, rational functions and graphs, summation of series, matrices, polar coordinates, vectors, and proof by induction.
- Further Pure Mathematics 2: hyperbolic functions, matrices, differentiation, integration, complex numbers, and differential equations.
- Further Mechanics: motion of a projectile, equilibrium of a rigid body, circular motion, Hooke's law, linear motion under a variable force, and momentum.
- Further Probability and Statistics: continuous random variables, inference using normal and t-distributions, chi-squared tests, non-parametric tests, and probability generating functions.

## Reading Method

- Read the syllabus heading first so the coursebook section has a clear purpose.
- Work through selected examples by hand and write why the method applies.
- Record definitions, method conditions, graph meanings, and traps; do not copy whole textbook explanations.
- Before each 9231 extension, review the matching 9709 foundation.
