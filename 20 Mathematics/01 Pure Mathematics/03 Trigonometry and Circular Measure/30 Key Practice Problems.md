# Trigonometry and Circular Measure Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Keep angle units visible,
and state the interval when solving a trigonometric equation.

## A. Circular Measure

1. Convert $150^\circ$ to radians, and convert $\frac{7\pi}{6}$ radians to
   degrees.

2. A sector has radius $6\text{ cm}$ and angle $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians. Find
   its arc length and sector area.

3. A sector has radius $8\text{ cm}$ and arc length $10\text{ cm}$. Find the
   angle in radians and the sector area.

4. A circle has radius $5\text{ cm}$ and central angle $1.2$ radians. Find the
   area of the minor segment between the chord and the arc.

5. Two radii of length $10\text{ cm}$ enclose an angle of $40^\circ$. Find the
   sector area and the area of the triangle formed by the two radii and the
   chord.

6. A sector has area $24\text{ cm}^2$ and radius $4\text{ cm}$. Find its angle
   in radians and its arc length.

## B. Exact Values, Inverses, and Graphs

7. Find the exact values of

   $$
   \sin\frac{7\pi}{6},\qquad
   \cos\frac{5\pi}{6},\qquad
   \tan\frac{3\pi}{4}.
   $$

8. Find the principal values of

   $$
   \sin^{-1}\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right),\qquad
   \cos^{-1}\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right),\qquad
   \tan^{-1}(-1).
   $$

9. For

   $$
   y=2\sin x-1,
   $$

   state the period and range.

10. For

    $$
    y=1-\cos 2x,
    $$

    state the period, range, and the values of $x$ where the graph has a
    minimum for $0\le x\le2\pi$.

11. Find the period and vertical asymptotes of

    $$
    y=\tan\left(x-\frac{\pi}{4}\right).
    $$

## C. Trigonometric Equations and Identities

12. Solve

    $$
    \sin x=\frac{\sqrt3}{2},\qquad 0\le x<2\pi.
    $$

13. Solve

    $$
    \cos x=-\frac{1}{2},\qquad 0\le x<2\pi.
    $$

14. Solve

    $$
    \tan x=-1,\qquad -\pi<x\le\pi.
    $$

15. Solve

    $$
    2\sin^2x-\sin x=0,\qquad 0\le x<2\pi.
    $$

16. Solve

    $$
    2\cos^2x-3\cos x+1=0,\qquad 0\le x<2\pi.
    $$

17. Simplify

    $$
    \frac{1-\cos^2x}{1-\sin^2x}
    $$

    and state the restriction needed for the simplified form.

18. Prove the identity

    $$
    \frac{\sec^2x-1}{\tan x}=\tan x
    $$

    where both sides are defined.

## D. Compound Angles, Reciprocal Functions, and Auxiliary Angles

19. Find the exact value of $\sin75^\circ$.

20. Given $\sin A=\frac{3}{5}$ and $A$ is in quadrant II, find $\cos A$,
    $\sin 2A$, and $\cos 2A$.

21. Solve

    $$
    \sin 2x=\cos x,\qquad 0\le x<2\pi.
    $$

22. Solve

    $$
    \tan\theta+\cot\theta=4,\qquad 0<\theta<\pi.
    $$

23. Simplify

    $$
    \sec^2x-\tan^2x+\operatorname{cosec}^2x-\cot^2x.
    $$

24. Express

    $$
    3\sin\theta+4\cos\theta
    $$

    in the form $R\sin(\theta+\alpha)$, where $R>0$ and
    $0<\alpha<\frac{\pi}{2}$. State the maximum and minimum values.

25. Solve

    $$
    3\sin\theta+4\cos\theta=0,\qquad 0\le\theta<2\pi.
    $$

26. State the general solution of

    $$
    \tan\left(\theta-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)=\sqrt3.
    $$
