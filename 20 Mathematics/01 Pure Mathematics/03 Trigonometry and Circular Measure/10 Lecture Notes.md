## 3. Method Selection

Use these habits while studying:

- Use the unit circle for signs, symmetry, and periodicity.
- Write angle units explicitly and use radians when calculus is involved.
- Verify identities by transforming one side into the other.

Use these practice routes to turn the ideas into fluency:

- Solve trigonometric equations over specified intervals.
- Use identities to simplify expressions.
- Apply arc length and sector area formulae.

When two methods seem possible, choose the one that exposes the most structure with the least algebra. Then check the answer in another representation whenever possible.

- Dropping solution families in trigonometric equations.
