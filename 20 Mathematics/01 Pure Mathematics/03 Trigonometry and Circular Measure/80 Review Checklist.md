# Trigonometry and Circular Measure Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. Any unchecked item is a cue to
return to the lecture notes or worked examples.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] An angle in radians is defined by arc length divided by radius.
- [ ] The formulae $s=r\theta$ and $A=\frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ require $\theta$
      in radians.
- [ ] Segment area is often sector area minus triangle area.
- [ ] On the unit circle, $\cos\theta$ is the $x$-coordinate and
      $\sin\theta$ is the $y$-coordinate.
- [ ] $\tan\theta=\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$, where $\cos\theta\ne0$.
- [ ] The signs of sine, cosine, and tangent follow from the quadrant.
- [ ] The exact values for $30^\circ$, $45^\circ$, and $60^\circ$ should be
      automatic.
- [ ] $\sin^{-1}x$, $\cos^{-1}x$, and $\tan^{-1}x$ mean principal inverse
      values, not reciprocals.
- [ ] $y=\sin x$ and $y=\cos x$ have period $2\pi$; $y=\tan x$ has period
      $\pi$.
- [ ] Reciprocal functions are
      $\operatorname{cosec}\theta=\frac{1}{\sin\theta}$,
      $\sec\theta=\frac{1}{\cos\theta}$, and
      $\cot\theta=\frac{1}{\tan\theta}$.
- [ ] Pythagorean identities include $\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1$,
      $1+\tan^2\theta=\sec^2\theta$, and
      $1+\cot^2\theta=\operatorname{cosec}^2\theta$.
- [ ] Compound-angle and double-angle formulae are tools for exact values,
      simplification, and equations.
- [ ] $a\sin\theta+b\cos\theta$ can be rewritten as one shifted sine or cosine.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Convert between degrees and radians.
- [ ] Find arc length, sector area, triangle area, and segment area.
- [ ] Use exact values and related angles in any quadrant.
- [ ] Read period, range, intercepts, maxima, minima, and asymptotes from a
      trigonometric graph.
- [ ] Solve trigonometric equations over a specified interval.
- [ ] Find all solutions by using quadrant signs and periods.
- [ ] Avoid losing solutions when a trigonometric factor could be zero.
- [ ] Prove identities by transforming one side into the other.
- [ ] Simplify expressions using reciprocal and Pythagorean identities.
- [ ] Use compound-angle and double-angle formulae in exact evaluation.
- [ ] Express $a\sin\theta+b\cos\theta$ in auxiliary-angle form.
- [ ] Use auxiliary-angle form to find maximum, minimum, or solutions.
- [ ] State general solutions when the question asks for them.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Using $s=r\theta$ or $A=\frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ with degrees.
- [ ] Treating $\sin^{-1}x$ as $\frac{1}{\sin x}$.
- [ ] Keeping only the calculator's principal value for an equation.
- [ ] Forgetting the period of $\tan x$ is $\pi$.
- [ ] Missing extra solutions when the argument is $2x$ or $x-\alpha$.
- [ ] Dividing by $\sin x$, $\cos x$, or $\tan x$ without checking zero cases.
- [ ] Applying $1+\tan^2x=\sec^2x$ where $\cos x=0$.
- [ ] Using the wrong sign for a related angle in quadrant II, III, or IV.
- [ ] Reversing signs in compound-angle formulae.
- [ ] Forgetting to shift the interval when solving with an auxiliary angle.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can explain why radians make circular formulae simple.
- [ ] I can combine sector and triangle areas in circular measure problems.
- [ ] I can use the unit circle to control exact values, signs, and periods.
- [ ] I can sketch and interpret sine, cosine, tangent, and simple transformed
      graphs.
- [ ] I can solve trigonometric equations over finite intervals and as general
      solutions.
- [ ] I can prove and use identities without assuming the result.
- [ ] I can work with reciprocal functions and their undefined values.
- [ ] I can rewrite and use $a\sin\theta+b\cos\theta$ confidently.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [[20 Mathematics/01 Pure Mathematics/02 Coordinate Geometry and Graphs/00 Overview|Coordinate Geometry and Graphs]],
