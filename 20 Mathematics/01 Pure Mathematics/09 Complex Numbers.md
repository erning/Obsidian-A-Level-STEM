# 09 Complex Numbers

## Core Idea

Complex numbers extend algebra into a plane where multiplication naturally includes rotation and scaling.

## Source Alignment

- 9709 3.9 Complex numbers
- 9231 2.5 Complex numbers
- Coursebook route: 9709 Pure Mathematics 2 and 3 Chapter 11; 9231 Further Mathematics complex numbers content.

## What to Learn

- Imaginary unit, complex numbers, complex conjugate, modulus, and argument.
- Argand diagram and loci.
- Solving equations with complex roots.
- Polar and exponential forms where appropriate.
- de Moivre-style reasoning and roots in the complex plane.

## How to Study

- Draw every complex-number problem on the complex plane.
- Interpret multiplication as scaling and rotation.
- Keep principal arguments and argument intervals explicit.

## Practice Directions

- Convert between Cartesian and polar form.
- Solve polynomial equations with complex roots.
- Describe loci geometrically and algebraically.

## Common Traps

- Confusing modulus with real part.
- Forgetting conjugate roots for real polynomial coefficients.
- Using the wrong branch of argument.
