# Complex Numbers Review Checklist

Use this after the worked examples and practice problems. Revisit [Complex Numbers Lecture Notes](10%20Lecture%20Notes.md) if any item is uncertain.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] A complex number $z=x+iy$ has real part $x$ and imaginary part $y$, not $iy$.
- [ ] The conjugate $z^*=x-iy$ reflects $z$ in the real axis and is used to divide in Cartesian form.
- [ ] The modulus $|z|$ is distance from the origin; the argument $\arg z$ is an angle from the positive real axis.
- [ ] The principal argument must be chosen from the interval requested by the question.
- [ ] Polar and exponential forms are best for multiplication, division, powers, roots, and rotations.
- [ ] In a real-coefficient polynomial, non-real roots occur in conjugate pairs.
- [ ] Simple complex loci are distance or direction statements in the Argand diagram.
- [ ] 9709 includes the two square roots of a complex number; 9231 extends this to de Moivre's theorem, roots of unity, rational powers, and trigonometric series.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Add, subtract, multiply, and divide complex numbers in Cartesian form.
- [ ] Find the conjugate, modulus, and principal argument of a complex number.
- [ ] Convert between $x+iy$, $r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)$, and $re^{i\theta}$.
- [ ] Use polar form to multiply, divide, and interpret transformations as scaling and rotation.
- [ ] Find square roots by writing $(x+iy)^2=a+bi$ and equating real and imaginary parts.
- [ ] Use conjugate roots to factor real cubic or quartic equations.
- [ ] Sketch and describe loci such as $|z-a|=r$, $|z-a|<r$, $|z-a|=|z-b|$, and $\arg(z-a)=\alpha$.
- [ ] Use de Moivre's theorem to evaluate powers and derive multiple-angle identities.
- [ ] Express powers such as $\cos^4\theta$ in terms of multiple angles.
- [ ] Find and use the $n$th roots of unity.
- [ ] Find all $n$th roots of a non-zero complex number by adding $2k\pi$ to the argument.
- [ ] Turn trigonometric sums into geometric series using $e^{i\theta}$.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Using $\tan^{-1}(y/x)$ without checking the quadrant.
- [ ] Giving an argument outside the interval requested by the question.
- [ ] Treating $\arg0$ as defined.
- [ ] Forgetting to multiply by the conjugate when dividing in Cartesian form.
- [ ] Finding only one square root or only one $n$th root.
- [ ] Forgetting the conjugate root in a real polynomial.
- [ ] Sketching an argument locus as a full line when it should be a ray or sector.
- [ ] Mixing degrees and radians in polar or exponential form.
- [ ] Treating the 9231 roots of unity and trigonometric-series methods as part of the 9709 core.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can choose Cartesian form or polar form before starting the calculation.
- [ ] I can explain each locus as a distance or direction statement before expanding.
- [ ] I can list all roots and justify why the list is complete.
- [ ] I can check a complex-root answer by substitution or by its geometry on the Argand diagram.
- [ ] I can separate the 9709 complex-number skills from the 9231 de Moivre extensions.
