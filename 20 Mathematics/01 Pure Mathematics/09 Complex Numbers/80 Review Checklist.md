# Complex Numbers Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. Any unchecked item is a cue to return to the lecture notes or worked examples.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Imaginary unit, complex numbers, complex conjugate, modulus, and argument.
- [ ] Argand diagram and loci.
- [ ] Solving equations with complex roots.
- [ ] Polar and exponential forms where appropriate.
- [ ] de Moivre-style reasoning and roots in the complex plane.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Convert between Cartesian and polar form.
- [ ] Solve polynomial equations with complex roots.
- [ ] Describe loci geometrically and algebraically.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Confusing modulus with real part.
- [ ] Forgetting conjugate roots for real polynomial coefficients.
- [ ] Using the wrong branch of argument.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can explain the purpose of Complex Numbers without reading the notes.
- [ ] I can choose a method before seeing a worked solution.
- [ ] I can check my answer using units, signs, domains, graph features, limiting cases, or context.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to at least one neighbouring topic.
