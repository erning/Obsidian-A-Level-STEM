## 1. Core Frame

## 3. Method Selection

Use these habits while studying:

- Draw every complex-number problem on the complex plane.
- Interpret multiplication as scaling and rotation.
- Keep principal arguments and argument intervals explicit.

Use these practice routes to turn the ideas into fluency:

- Convert between Cartesian and polar form.
- Solve polynomial equations with complex roots.
- Describe loci geometrically and algebraically.

When two methods seem possible, choose the one that exposes the most structure with the least algebra. Then check the answer in another representation whenever possible.

## 6. Common Traps

- Confusing modulus with real part.
- Forgetting conjugate roots for real polynomial coefficients.
- Using the wrong branch of argument.
