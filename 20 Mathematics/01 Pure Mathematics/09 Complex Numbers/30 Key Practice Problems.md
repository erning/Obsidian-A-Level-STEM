# Complex Numbers Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw an Argand diagram
whenever a problem mentions modulus, argument, polar form, or a locus.

## A. 9709 Core Complex Number Skills

1. For

   $$
   z=3-4i,
   $$

   find $\operatorname{Re}z$, $\operatorname{Im}z$, $z^*$, $|z|$, and the
   principal argument of $z$.

2. Let

   $$
   z=2+3i,\qquad w=4-i.
   $$

   Find $z+w$, $zw$, and $\dfrac{z}{w}$ in Cartesian form.

3. Solve

   $$
   z^2-4z+13=0.
   $$

4. It is given that $1+2i$ is a root of

   $$
   z^3-5z^2+11z-15=0.
   $$

   Find all roots.

5. Find the square roots of

   $$
   5+12i.
   $$

6. Write

   $$
   -1+i\sqrt3
   $$

   in modulus-argument form and in exponential form.

7. Write

   $$
   4e^{-\pi i/6}
   $$

   in Cartesian form.

8. Let

   $$
   z_1=2e^{5\pi i/6},
   \qquad
   z_2=3e^{-2\pi i/3}.
   $$

   Find $z_1z_2$ and $\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}$ using principal arguments.

9. Find the image of $1+i$ after multiplication by

   $$
   2e^{\pi i/3}.
   $$

   Describe the geometrical effect as well as giving the Cartesian result.

10. Describe the locus

    $$
    |z-(2+i)|=3.
    $$

11. Describe the locus

    $$
    |z-(1+2i)|=|z-(5-2i)|
    $$

    geometrically and by a Cartesian equation.

12. Describe the locus

    $$
    \arg(z-(1+i))=\frac{\pi}{4}.
    $$

13. Find where the circle

    $$
    |z-(2+i)|=3
    $$

    meets the real axis.

## B. 9231 de Moivre and Roots Extensions

14. Use de Moivre's theorem to evaluate

    $$
    (1+i)^8.
    $$

15. Use de Moivre's theorem to express $\cos3\theta$ and $\sin3\theta$ in
    powers of $\cos\theta$ and $\sin\theta$.

16. Express $\cos^4\theta$ in terms of $\cos2\theta$ and $\cos4\theta$.

17. Find all roots of

    $$
    z^5=1.
    $$

    Hence state the sum of the five roots.

18. Solve

    $$
    z^3=8i.
    $$

19. For $0<\theta<2\pi$, show that

    $$
    \sum_{r=0}^{n-1}e^{ir\theta}
    =e^{i(n-1)\theta/2}
    \frac{\sin(n\theta/2)}{\sin(\theta/2)}.
    $$

    Hence write formulae for

    $$
    \sum_{r=0}^{n-1}\cos r\theta
    \quad\text{and}\quad
    \sum_{r=0}^{n-1}\sin r\theta.
    $$

20. Solve

    $$
    z^4+z^3+z^2+z+1=0
    $$

    using roots of unity.
