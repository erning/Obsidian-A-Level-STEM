# Sequences, Series and Binomial Expansions Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Before calculating, decide
whether the question asks for a term, a finite sum, an infinite sum, a
coefficient, or a validity condition.

## A. Arithmetic and Geometric Progressions

1. An arithmetic progression has first term $7$ and common difference $3$.
   Find $u_{20}$ and $S_{20}$.

2. An arithmetic progression has $u_5=18$ and $u_{12}=46$. Find the first
   term, the common difference, and $S_{20}$.

3. The numbers $x-2$, $2x+1$, and $5x-1$ are consecutive terms of an
   arithmetic progression. Find $x$.

4. A geometric progression has first term $5$ and common ratio $3$. Find
   $u_6$ and $S_6$.

5. A geometric progression has $u_2=6$ and $u_5=162$. Find the first term,
   the common ratio, and $S_5$.

6. The numbers $x$, $6$, and $x+9$ are consecutive terms of a geometric
   progression. Find the possible values of $x$.

7. A geometric progression has first term $12$ and common ratio $-\frac{1}{3}$.
   Find its sum to infinity.

8. A convergent geometric progression has first term $5$ and sum to infinity
   $20$. Find its common ratio and its fourth term.

## B. Positive-Integer Binomial Expansions

9. Find the coefficient of $x^3$ in

   $$
   (1+2x)^5.
   $$

10. Find the coefficient of $x^2$ in

    $$
    (3-2x)^6.
    $$

11. Find the constant term in

    $$
    \left(2x-\frac{1}{x}\right)^6.
    $$

12. Find the coefficient of $x^4$ in

    $$
    (1+x)^8(1-2x).
    $$

13. Find the term independent of $x$ in

    $$
    \left(x^2+\frac{2}{x}\right)^9.
    $$

## C. Non-Integer Binomial Expansions and Partial Fractions

14. Expand

    $$
    (1-2x)^{-1}
    $$

    up to and including the term in $x^3$, and state the validity condition.

15. Expand

    $$
    (1+3x)^{\frac{1}{2}}
    $$

    up to and including the term in $x^3$, and state the validity condition.

16. Expand

    $$
    (4+x)^{-2}
    $$

    up to and including the term in $x^2$, and state the validity condition.

17. Use the expansion of $(1+3x)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ with $x=0.1$ to approximate
    $\sqrt{1.3}$.

18. Decompose

    $$
    \frac{1}{r(r+1)}
    $$

    into partial fractions.

19. Decompose

    $$
    \frac{2}{(r+1)(r+3)}
    $$

    into partial fractions.

20. Expand

    $$
    \frac{1}{(1-x)(2+x)}
    $$

    up to and including the term in $x^2$, and state the validity condition.

## D. Standard Sums and Method of Differences

21. Find

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{n}(3r^2-2r+1).
    $$

22. Find

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{10}(2r+5).
    $$

23. Find

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{n}r(r+1).
    $$

24. Use method of differences to find

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{n}\frac{1}{r(r+1)}
    $$

    and its sum to infinity.

25. Use method of differences to find

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{n}\frac{2}{(r+1)(r+3)}
    $$

    and its sum to infinity.

26. Evaluate

    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{n}\left((r+1)^3-r^3\right).
    $$

27. A sequence of partial sums is given by

    $$
    S_n=5-\frac{3}{n+2}.
    $$

    Find $u_n=S_n-S_{n-1}$ for $n\ge2$, and find the sum to infinity.

28. Find the sum to infinity of

    $$
    10-5+\frac{5}{2}-\frac{5}{4}+\cdots.
    $$
