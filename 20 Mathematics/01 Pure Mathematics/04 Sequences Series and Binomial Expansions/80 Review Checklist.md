# Sequences, Series and Binomial Expansions Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. Any unchecked item is a cue to
return to the lecture notes or worked examples.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] A sequence lists terms; a series adds terms.
- [ ] $u_n$ is a term, while $S_n$ is the sum of the first $n$ terms.
- [ ] An arithmetic progression has constant difference $d$.
- [ ] A geometric progression has constant ratio $r$.
- [ ] Three numbers $a$, $b$, $c$ are in arithmetic progression when
      $2b=a+c$.
- [ ] Three numbers $a$, $b$, $c$ are in geometric progression when $b^2=ac$.
- [ ] A geometric series has a sum to infinity only when $|r|<1$.
- [ ] The binomial coefficient is
      $\binom{n}{r}=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$ for positive integer $n$.
- [ ] In $(a+b)^n$, the term with $b^r$ is the $(r+1)$th term.
- [ ] Non-integer binomial expansions are infinite and need a validity
      condition.
- [ ] Standard sums for $\sum r$, $\sum r^2$, and $\sum r^3$ support polynomial
      summation.
- [ ] Method of differences works when terms can be written as cancellations
      such as $f(r)-f(r+1)$.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Decide whether a progression is arithmetic, geometric, or neither.
- [ ] Find $u_n$ and $S_n$ for arithmetic progressions.
- [ ] Find $u_n$, $S_n$, and $S_\infty$ for geometric progressions.
- [ ] Solve AP or GP problems from two given terms.
- [ ] Use the AP condition $2b=a+c$ and the GP condition $b^2=ac$.
- [ ] Find a required coefficient without expanding a whole binomial.
- [ ] Track powers of $x$ when binomial terms include negative powers.
- [ ] Expand $(1+x)^n$ for non-integer $n$ to a required degree.
- [ ] Rewrite expressions such as $(a+x)^n$ into $(1+u)^n$ form first.
- [ ] State and use validity conditions for non-integer binomial expansions.
- [ ] Use partial fractions before summing or expanding a rational expression.
- [ ] Apply standard sums to polynomial expressions in $r$.
- [ ] Write enough terms of a telescoping series to see boundary terms.
- [ ] Find a sum to infinity from a formula for $S_n$ when the limit exists.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Confusing $u_n$ with $S_n$.
- [ ] Using the wrong formula for an arithmetic or geometric sum.
- [ ] Using an infinite geometric sum when $|r|\ge1$.
- [ ] Treating $r=1$ as valid in the usual finite GP sum formula.
- [ ] Forgetting the $(r+1)$th term shift in binomial expansions.
- [ ] Losing a sign from a negative binomial term such as $(-2x)^r$.
- [ ] Expanding a whole binomial when only one coefficient is needed.
- [ ] Omitting the validity condition for a non-integer binomial expansion.
- [ ] Forgetting to factor constants before using $(1+x)^n$.
- [ ] Cancelling a telescoping series too early and losing boundary terms.
- [ ] Assuming a sum to infinity exists without taking a limit.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can distinguish terms, finite sums, and infinite sums in the question
      wording.
- [ ] I can handle AP and GP questions from definitions, formulae, or given
      terms.
- [ ] I can find binomial coefficients and constant terms efficiently.
- [ ] I can expand non-integer powers and state the correct validity interval.
- [ ] I can use standard sums for polynomial series.
- [ ] I can use partial fractions to create telescoping sums.
- [ ] I can identify the boundary terms that survive in method of differences.
