# Algebra and Functions Key Practice Solutions

These solutions correspond to [Key Practice Problems](30%20Key%20Practice%20Problems.md).

## A. Quadratics

1. $3x^2-18x+20=3(x^2-6x)+20=3[(x-3)^2-9]+20$.

   $$
   3x^2-18x+20=3(x-3)^2-7.
   $$

   Minimum value: $-7$.

2. Discriminant condition:

   $$
   (k-2)^2-4k>0.
   $$

   So

   $$
   k^2-8k+4>0.
   $$

   The roots are $4\pm 2\sqrt{3}$, hence

   $$
   k<4-2\sqrt{3}
   \quad \text{or} \quad
   k>4+2\sqrt{3}.
   $$

3. Factorise:

   $$
   2x^2-5x-3=(2x+1)(x-3).
   $$

   Since the quadratic opens upwards,

   $$
   -\frac{1}{2}\le x\le 3.
   $$

4. Put $u=x^2$:

   $$
   u^2-10u+9=0=(u-1)(u-9).
   $$

   So $x^2=1$ or $x^2=9$, giving

   $$
   x=\pm 1,\ \pm 3.
   $$

5. Intersections satisfy

   $$
   2x+c=x^2-4x+1.
   $$

   Thus

   $$
   x^2-6x+(1-c)=0.
   $$

   Tangency gives

   $$
   36-4(1-c)=0.
   $$

   Hence $32+4c=0$, so

   $$
   c=-8.
   $$

## B. Functions

6. Since $fg(x)=f(g(x))$,

   $$
   fg(x)=2(x^2+1)-5=2x^2-3.
   $$

   Since $gf(x)=g(f(x))$,

   $$
   gf(x)=(2x-5)^2+1=4x^2-20x+26.
   $$

7. Require $3x-2\ge 0$, so

   $$
   x\ge \frac{2}{3}.
   $$

8. Let

   $$
   y=\frac{2x+1}{x-3}.
   $$

   Then

   $$
   yx-3y=2x+1,
   $$

   so

   $$
   x(y-2)=3y+1.
   $$

   Therefore

   $$
   f^{-1}(x)=\frac{3x+1}{x-2}.
   $$

   Excluded value: $x=2$.

9. Complete the square:

   $$
   f(x)=x^2-4x+7=(x-2)^2+3.
   $$

   With $x\ge 2$, the range is $f(x)\ge 3$.

   Let $y=(x-2)^2+3$. Since $x\ge 2$,

   $$
   x=2+\sqrt{y-3}.
   $$

   Thus

   $$
   f^{-1}(x)=2+\sqrt{x-3}, \qquad x\ge 3.
   $$

10. If $(2,5)$ lies on $y=f(x)$, then $f(2)=5$.

    For $y=3f(x-1)-4$, set $x-1=2$, so $x=3$.

    The new $y$ value is $3(5)-4=11$. The point is

    $$
    (3,11).
    $$

## C. Modulus and Polynomial Algebra

11. Use $|A|=|B|\iff A^2=B^2$:

    $$
    (3x+2)^2=(x-6)^2.
    $$

    Difference of squares gives

    $$
    (3x+2-(x-6))(3x+2+x-6)=0.
    $$

    So

    $$
    (2x+8)(4x-4)=0,
    $$

    giving

    $$
    x=-4 \quad \text{or} \quad x=1.
    $$

12. $|x-4|\ge 6$ means distance from $4$ is at least $6$:

    $$
    x\le -2 \quad \text{or} \quad x\ge 10.
    $$

13. Since $x-2$ is a factor, $f(2)=0$:

    $$
    8+4a-14+6=0.
    $$

    Thus $4a=0$, so

    $$
    a=0.
    $$

14. By the remainder theorem, the remainder on division by $x+2$ is $f(-2)$:

    $$
    2(-2)^4-(-2)^3+3(-2)-5=32+8-6-5=29.
    $$

15. Synthetic or long division gives

    $$
    x^3+2x^2-5x+1=(x-1)(x^2+3x-2)-1.
    $$

    Quotient: $x^2+3x-2$. Remainder: $-1$.

## D. Further Algebra and Rational Functions

16. For

    $$
    x^3-5x^2+3x+2=0,
    $$

    we have

    $$
    \alpha+\beta+\gamma=5
    $$

    and

    $$
    \alpha\beta+\beta\gamma+\gamma\alpha=3.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    \alpha^2+\beta^2+\gamma^2
    =5^2-2(3)=19.
    $$

17. Let the new root be $y=x+1$, so $x=y-1$.

    Substitute into the original equation:

    $$
    (y-1)^3-2(y-1)^2-5(y-1)+6=0.
    $$

    Expanding:

    $$
    y^3-3y^2+3y-1-2y^2+4y-2-5y+5+6=0.
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    y^3-5y^2+2y+8=0.
    $$

    The required equation is

    $$
    x^3-5x^2+2x+8=0.
    $$

18. The vertical asymptote is

    $$
    x=2.
    $$

    Divide:

    $$
    x^2-3x+4=(x-2)(x-1)+2.
    $$

    So

    $$
    y=x-1+\frac{2}{x-2}.
    $$

    The oblique asymptote is

    $$
    y=x-1.
    $$

19. Write

    $$
    \frac{7x-1}{(x+1)(2x-3)}
    =
    \frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{2x-3}.
    $$

    Then

    $$
    7x-1=A(2x-3)+B(x+1).
    $$

    Set $x=-1$: $-8=-5A$, so $A=\frac{8}{5}$.

    Set $x=\frac{3}{2}$:

    $$
    \frac{19}{2}=\frac{5}{2}B,
    $$

    so $B=\frac{19}{5}$.

    Therefore

    $$
    \frac{7x-1}{(x+1)(2x-3)}
    =
    \frac{8}{5(x+1)}+\frac{19}{5(2x-3)}.
    $$

20. Rearrange

    $$
    y=\frac{x}{x^2+4}
    $$

    to get

    $$
    yx^2-x+4y=0.
    $$

    For real $x$,

    $$
    (-1)^2-4(y)(4y)\ge 0.
    $$

    So

    $$
    1-16y^2\ge 0.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    -\frac{1}{4}\le y\le \frac{1}{4}.
    $$

## E. Mixed Review

21. Move everything to one side:

    $$
    \frac{x+2}{x-1}-2>0.
    $$

    So

    $$
    \frac{x+2-2x+2}{x-1}>0,
    $$

    hence

    $$
    \frac{4-x}{x-1}>0.
    $$

    Critical values are $x=1$ and $x=4$. Sign testing gives

    $$
    1<x<4.
    $$

22. No real roots means discriminant $<0$:

    $$
    m^2-4(m+3)<0.
    $$

    So

    $$
    m^2-4m-12<0.
    $$

    Factorise:

    $$
    (m-6)(m+2)<0.
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    -2<m<6.
    $$

23. Let

    $$
    y=\frac{4-x}{2x+1}.
    $$

    Then

    $$
    2xy+y=4-x.
    $$

    So

    $$
    x(2y+1)=4-y.
    $$

    Thus

    $$
    f^{-1}(x)=\frac{4-x}{2x+1}.
    $$

    This function is its own inverse, with $x\ne -\frac{1}{2}$.

24. Use the remainder theorem:

    $$
    f(1)=1+p+q=4,
    $$

    so

    $$
    p+q=3.
    $$

    Also

    $$
    f(-2)=-8-2p+q=-2,
    $$

    so

    $$
    -2p+q=6.
    $$

    Subtracting gives $-3p=3$, so $p=-1$. Then $q=4$.

25. For

    $$
    y=\frac{x^2+1}{x-1},
    $$

    the vertical asymptote is $x=1$.

    Divide:

    $$
    x^2+1=(x-1)(x+1)+2.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    y=x+1+\frac{2}{x-1}.
    $$

    The oblique asymptote is $y=x+1$.

    The $y$-intercept is

    $$
    y=\frac{1}{-1}=-1,
    $$

    so $(0,-1)$.

    There are no $x$-intercepts because $x^2+1=0$ has no real solution.
