# Logarithms, Exponentials and Numerical Methods Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. For logarithmic equations,
write the domain restrictions before applying logarithm laws. For numerical
methods, keep enough working precision and round only at the end.

## A. Logarithms and Exponentials

1. Simplify

   $$
   \ln8+\ln4-\ln2.
   $$

2. Solve

   $$
   \ln(x-1)+\ln(x+2)=\ln12.
   $$

3. Solve

   $$
   2e^{3x}=7.
   $$

4. Solve

   $$
   5^{x+1}=3^{2x}.
   $$

5. Solve the inequality

   $$
   e^{2x-1}>4.
   $$

6. Solve the inequality

   $$
   \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^x\le9.
   $$

7. State the domain of

   $$
   \ln(x-2)+\ln(5-x).
   $$

8. Solve

   $$
   \ln(x-2)+\ln(5-x)=\ln2.
   $$

## B. Modelling and Linear Form

9. A graph of $\ln y$ against $\ln x$ has gradient $\frac{5}{2}$ and intercept
   $\ln3$. Write the relationship in the form $y=kx^n$.

10. A graph of $\ln y$ against $x$ has gradient $-0.2$ and intercept $\ln80$.
    Write the relationship in the form $y=ke^{bx}$.

11. A relationship $y=kx^n$ passes through $(2,12)$ and $(8,96)$. Find $k$
    and $n$.

12. A relationship $y=ka^x$ passes through $(0,5)$ and $(3,40)$. Find $k$ and
    $a$.

13. A quantity is modelled by

    $$
    N=100e^{-0.07t}.
    $$

    Find the time when $N=40$.

14. A quantity follows

    $$
    P=250e^{kt}.
    $$

    It doubles in $8$ years. Find $k$ and $P$ after $12$ years.

## C. Numerical Solution of Equations

15. Show that

    $$
    f(x)=x^3-x-1
    $$

    has a root between $1$ and $2$.

16. Show that the same root lies between $1.3$ and $1.4$.

17. Use

    $$
    x_{n+1}=(x_n+1)^{\frac{1}{3}},\qquad x_0=1.3,
    $$

    to find $x_1$, $x_2$, and $x_3$.

18. Explain why a fixed point of

    $$
    x=(x+1)^{\frac{1}{3}}
    $$

    is a root of $x^3-x-1=0$.

19. Show that

    $$
    e^{-x}=x
    $$

    has a root between $0$ and $1$.

20. Use

    $$
    x_{n+1}=e^{-x_n},\qquad x_0=0.5,
    $$

    to find $x_1$, $x_2$, $x_3$, and $x_4$.

21. Solve

    $$
    2^x=10.
    $$

22. Use Newton iteration twice from $x_0=1.3$ for

    $$
    f(x)=x^3-x-1.
    $$

23. Give one reason why an iteration may fail to converge.

24. Explain why taking logarithms of

    $$
    x^2-4
    $$

    requires a domain check first.
