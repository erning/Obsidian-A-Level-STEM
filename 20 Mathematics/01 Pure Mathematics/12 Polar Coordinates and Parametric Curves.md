# 12 Polar Coordinates and Parametric Curves

## Core Idea

Polar and parametric forms describe curves by changing the coordinate language or introducing a parameter.

## Source Alignment

- 9231 1.5 Polar coordinates
- 9709 parametric differentiation content
- Coursebook route: 9231 Further Mathematics Coursebook polar coordinate sections; 9709 Pure Mathematics 2 and 3 parametric differentiation sections.

## What to Learn

- Relations between Cartesian and polar coordinates.
- Sketching simple polar curves and identifying symmetry, intersections, and least or greatest radius.
- Area in polar coordinates using $\frac{1}{2}\int r^2\,d\theta$.
- Parametric curves, derivatives, tangents, and graph features.
- Choosing the representation that makes a curve easier to understand.

## How to Study

- Plot sample points before trusting an unfamiliar curve.
- Track parameter or angle intervals carefully.
- Convert forms only when conversion helps the problem.

## Practice Directions

- Convert between Cartesian and polar equations.
- Sketch polar and parametric curves.
- Find tangents and enclosed areas.

## Common Traps

- Using the wrong angle interval.
- Forgetting that one point can have multiple polar descriptions.
- Treating parameter $t$ as if it must be time.
