# Coordinate Geometry and Graphs Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. If a calculation becomes long,
return to the diagram and identify the geometric condition being translated.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Gradient is change in $y$ divided by change in $x$.
- [ ] Distance comes from Pythagoras' theorem.
- [ ] The midpoint formula averages the coordinates.
- [ ] Parallel non-vertical lines have equal gradients.
- [ ] Perpendicular non-vertical lines have gradients whose product is $-1$.
- [ ] Vertical lines have equation $x=a$ and no finite gradient.
- [ ] A circle with centre $(a,b)$ and radius $r$ has equation
      $(x-a)^2+(y-b)^2=r^2$.
- [ ] Expanded circle equations are read by completing the square.
- [ ] A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of
      contact.
- [ ] Intersections of graphs are simultaneous solutions of their equations.
- [ ] Tangency often means a quadratic has discriminant $0$.
- [ ] $y=f(x)+a$ translates the graph vertically.
- [ ] $y=f(x-a)$ translates the graph $a$ units to the right.
- [ ] $y=af(x)$ stretches parallel to the $y$-axis.
- [ ] $y=f(ax)$ stretches parallel to the $x$-axis with scale factor
      $\frac{1}{a}$.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Find gradient, distance, and midpoint between two points.
- [ ] Find the equation of a line from two points or from one point and a
      gradient.
- [ ] Use parallel and perpendicular gradient relationships.
- [ ] Solve simultaneous line equations.
- [ ] Convert a circle between centre-radius and expanded form.
- [ ] Find a circle from a diameter.
- [ ] Find a tangent to a circle using the radius.
- [ ] Solve line-circle intersections by substitution.
- [ ] Use the discriminant to classify intersections and tangency.
- [ ] Track points through transformations of $y=f(x)$.
- [ ] Update domain and range after graph transformations.
- [ ] Relate a one-one function and its inverse by reflection in $y=x$.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Treating gradient as the angle rather than $\tan\theta$.
- [ ] Applying $m_1m_2=-1$ to a vertical line.
- [ ] Forgetting that the negative reciprocal reverses both sign and fraction.
- [ ] Expanding a circle equation and losing the centre.
- [ ] Completing the square without balancing the added constants.
- [ ] Saying a tangent just touches without using perpendicular radius or
      discriminant $0$.
- [ ] Substituting into a circle and forgetting to find both coordinates.
- [ ] Translating $f(x+a)$ in the wrong horizontal direction.
- [ ] Transforming a graph but leaving the old domain or range unchanged.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can move between geometric statements and algebraic equations.
- [ ] I can solve line and circle problems using gradients, distances, and
      midpoints.
- [ ] I can complete the square to recover a circle's centre and radius.
- [ ] I can solve and classify graph intersections.
- [ ] I can find tangents to circles by geometry or discriminants.
- [ ] I can transform $y=f(x)$ and update points, domain, and range.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [Algebra and Functions](../01%20Algebra%20and%20Functions/00%20Overview.md)
      and [Polar Coordinates and Parametric Curves](../12%20Polar%20Coordinates%20and%20Parametric%20Curves/00%20Overview.md).
