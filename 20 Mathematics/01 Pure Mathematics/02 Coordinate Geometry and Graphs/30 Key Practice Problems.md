# Coordinate Geometry and Graphs Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw a quick sketch before
solving, and check each answer by substituting a point or matching the geometry.

## A. Lines and Points

1. For $A(2,-1)$ and $B(8,3)$, find the gradient of $AB$, the distance $AB$,
   and the midpoint of $AB$.

2. Find the equation of the line through $A(2,-1)$ and $B(8,3)$ in general
   form.

3. Find the equation of the line parallel to $AB$ passing through $(0,5)$.

4. Find the equation of the line perpendicular to $AB$ passing through
   $B(8,3)$.

5. State the equations of the vertical line through $(4,-2)$ and the horizontal
   line through $(4,-2)$.

6. Find the intersection point of

   $$
   2x-3y=7
   $$

   and

   $$
   x+y=5.
   $$

## B. Circles, Tangents, and Intersections

7. State the centre and radius of

   $$
   (x-3)^2+(y+2)^2=25.
   $$

8. Find the centre and radius of

   $$
   x^2+y^2-6x+4y-12=0.
   $$

9. A circle has diameter endpoints $A(1,2)$ and $B(7,10)$. Find its equation.

10. A circle has centre $C(3,-2)$ and a tangent at $T(6,2)$. Find the equation
    of the tangent.

11. Find the intersection points of

    $$
    y=x+1
    $$

    and

    $$
    x^2+y^2=25.
    $$

12. Find the values of $k$ for which the line $y=x+k$ is tangent to the circle
    $x^2+y^2=18$.

13. Find the values of $k$ for which the line $y=x+k$ intersects the curve
    $y=x^2-4$ in two distinct points, touches it, or does not meet it.

14. The line $y=mx+1$ is tangent to the circle $x^2+y^2=1$. Find $m$.

## C. Graph Transformations

15. A point $(2,5)$ lies on $y=f(x)$. State the corresponding point on each
    graph:

    $$
    y=f(x)+4,\quad y=f(x-3),\quad y=2f(x),\quad y=f(2x).
    $$

16. The function $f$ has domain $1\le x\le4$ and range $-2\le y\le3$. Find the
    domain and range of $y=f(x-2)+1$.

17. Using the same domain and range as question 16, find the domain and range
    of $y=2f(x)$.

18. Describe the transformation from $y=f(x)$ to $y=f(-x)$.

19. Describe the transformation from $y=f(x)$ to $y=-f(x)$.

20. The graph of $y=f(x)$ has an $x$-intercept at $x=3$. Find the
    corresponding $x$-intercept of $y=f(x-2)$.

21. The graph of $y=f(x)$ has a maximum point at $(1,4)$. Find the
    corresponding maximum point of $y=3f(x+2)-5$.

22. A one-one function has point $(3,7)$ on its graph. State a point on the
    graph of its inverse.

23. The line $y=x$ is used when sketching a function and its inverse. Explain
    its role.

24. A student says the graph of $y=f(x+2)$ is the graph of $y=f(x)$ translated
    $2$ units to the right. Correct the statement.
