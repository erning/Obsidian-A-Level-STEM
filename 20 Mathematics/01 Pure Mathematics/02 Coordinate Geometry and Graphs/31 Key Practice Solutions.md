# Coordinate Geometry and Graphs Key Practice Solutions

Use these solutions to check method choice and algebra for [[20 Mathematics/01 Pure Mathematics/02 Coordinate Geometry and Graphs/30 Key Practice Problems|Key Practice Problems]].

## A. Lines and Points

1. The gradient is

   $$
   m=\frac{3-(-1)}{8-2}=\frac{2}{3}.
   $$

   The distance is

   $$
   AB=\sqrt{(8-2)^2+(3-(-1))^2}
   =\sqrt{52}=2\sqrt{13}.
   $$

   The midpoint is

   $$
   \left(\frac{2+8}{2},\frac{-1+3}{2}\right)=(5,1).
   $$

2. Use point-gradient form:

   $$
   y+1=\frac{2}{3}(x-2).
   $$

   Hence

   $$
   3y+3=2x-4,
   $$

   so

   $$
   2x-3y-7=0.
   $$

3. A parallel line has the same gradient $\frac{2}{3}$. Through $(0,5)$:

   $$
   y=\frac{2}{3}x+5.
   $$

4. A perpendicular gradient is $-\frac{3}{2}$. Through $B(8,3)$:

   $$
   y-3=-\frac{3}{2}(x-8).
   $$

   Equivalently,

   $$
   3x+2y-30=0.
   $$

5. The vertical line is

   $$
   x=4.
   $$

   The horizontal line is

   $$
   y=-2.
   $$

6. From $x+y=5$, $y=5-x$. Substitute:

   $$
   2x-3(5-x)=7.
   $$

   Thus

   $$
   5x=22,
   $$

   so

   $$
   x=\frac{22}{5},\qquad y=\frac{3}{5}.
   $$

   The intersection is

   $$
   \left(\frac{22}{5},\frac{3}{5}\right).
   $$

## B. Circles, Tangents, and Intersections

7. The centre is $(3,-2)$ and the radius is $5$.

8. Complete the square:

   $$
   x^2-6x+y^2+4y=12,
   $$

   $$
   (x-3)^2-9+(y+2)^2-4=12.
   $$

   Hence

   $$
   (x-3)^2+(y+2)^2=25.
   $$

   The centre is $(3,-2)$ and the radius is $5$.

9. The centre is the midpoint:

   $$
   \left(\frac{1+7}{2},\frac{2+10}{2}\right)=(4,6).
   $$

   The diameter length is

   $$
   \sqrt{(7-1)^2+(10-2)^2}=10,
   $$

   so the radius is $5$. The circle is

   $$
   (x-4)^2+(y-6)^2=25.
   $$

10. The radius gradient from $C(3,-2)$ to $T(6,2)$ is

    $$
    \frac{2-(-2)}{6-3}=\frac{4}{3}.
    $$

    The tangent gradient is $-\frac{3}{4}$. Through $T(6,2)$:

    $$
    y-2=-\frac{3}{4}(x-6),
    $$

    or

    $$
    3x+4y-26=0.
    $$

11. Substitute $y=x+1$:

    $$
    x^2+(x+1)^2=25.
    $$

    This gives

    $$
    x^2+x-12=0,
    $$

    so

    $$
    x=3\quad\text{or}\quad x=-4.
    $$

    The points are

    $$
    (3,4)\quad\text{and}\quad(-4,-3).
    $$

12. Substitute $y=x+k$ into $x^2+y^2=18$:

    $$
    2x^2+2kx+k^2-18=0.
    $$

    Tangency means discriminant $0$:

    $$
    (2k)^2-4(2)(k^2-18)=0.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    k=\pm6.
    $$

13. Intersections satisfy

    $$
    x+k=x^2-4,
    $$

    so

    $$
    x^2-x-(k+4)=0.
    $$

    The discriminant is

    $$
    \Delta=1+4(k+4)=4k+17.
    $$

    Therefore:

    - two distinct intersections if $k>-\frac{17}{4}$;
    - tangent if $k=-\frac{17}{4}$;
    - no real intersection if $k<-\frac{17}{4}$.

14. Substitute $y=mx+1$ into $x^2+y^2=1$:

    $$
    x^2+(mx+1)^2=1.
    $$

    This becomes

    $$
    (1+m^2)x^2+2mx=0.
    $$

    For tangency, the quadratic must have a repeated root:

    $$
    (2m)^2=0.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    m=0.
    $$

    The tangent is $y=1$.

## C. Graph Transformations

15. The corresponding points are:

    $$
    y=f(x)+4:\ (2,9),
    $$

    $$
    y=f(x-3):\ (5,5),
    $$

    $$
    y=2f(x):\ (2,10),
    $$

    $$
    y=f(2x):\ (1,5).
    $$

16. The graph is translated $2$ units right and $1$ unit up. The domain is

    $$
    3\le x\le6,
    $$

    and the range is

    $$
    -1\le y\le4.
    $$

17. The domain is unchanged:

    $$
    1\le x\le4.
    $$

    The range is stretched by scale factor $2$ parallel to the $y$-axis:

    $$
    -4\le y\le6.
    $$

18. $y=f(-x)$ is a reflection of $y=f(x)$ in the $y$-axis.

19. $y=-f(x)$ is a reflection of $y=f(x)$ in the $x$-axis.

20. The graph of $y=f(x-2)$ is translated $2$ units to the right, so the
    intercept moves from $x=3$ to

    $$
    x=5.
    $$

21. The transformation $f(x+2)$ moves the point $2$ units left:

    $$
    (1,4)\mapsto(-1,4).
    $$

    Then $3f(x+2)-5$ gives

    $$
    (-1,3(4)-5)=(-1,7).
    $$

22. The inverse graph swaps coordinates, so $(7,3)$ lies on the inverse.

23. The graphs of a one-one function and its inverse are reflections of each
    other in the line $y=x$.

24. The graph of $y=f(x+2)$ is translated $2$ units to the left, not to the
    right. Horizontal transformations act on the input.
