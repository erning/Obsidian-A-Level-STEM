# Learning Checklist

This is a high-level checklist for CAIE Chemistry 9701. A checked item means you can explain the idea, use it in calculation or interpretation, connect it to practical evidence, and link it to nearby topics in [[40 Chemistry/01 Topics/00 Overview|the topic index]].

## Foundations

- [ ] Use atomic structure, isotopes, electronic configuration, ionisation energy, and orbital language to explain chemical behaviour.
- [ ] Use amount of substance, formulae, equations, empirical formula, molecular formula, gas volume, solution concentration, and yield calculations with correct units.
- [ ] Explain ionic, covalent, coordinate, metallic, and intermolecular bonding in terms of electrostatic attraction, orbital overlap, shape, polarity, and structure.
- [ ] Connect physical properties of gases, liquids, and solids to structure, bonding, intermolecular forces, and particle models.

## Physical Chemistry

- [ ] Use enthalpy changes, Hess cycles, bond energies, lattice energy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy to describe energy transfer and feasibility.
- [ ] Use oxidation numbers, redox equations, electrolysis, standard electrode potentials, cell potentials, and the Nernst equation where required.
- [ ] Use dynamic equilibrium, equilibrium constants, acids and bases, pH, buffers, solubility product, partition coefficients, and Le Chatelier's principle.
- [ ] Use collision theory, Boltzmann distributions, activation energy, catalysts, rate equations, orders of reaction, half-life, and mechanisms.

## Inorganic Chemistry

- [ ] Explain periodic trends across Period 3 using structure, bonding, electronegativity, oxidation number, and acid-base behaviour.
- [ ] Describe and explain Group 2 trends, reactions, solubilities, and thermal stabilities.
- [ ] Describe and explain Group 17 trends, redox behaviour, halide-ion reactions, and chlorine chemistry.
- [ ] Explain nitrogen and sulfur chemistry using bonding, oxidation states, atmospheric chemistry, acid rain, and industrial processes where relevant.
- [ ] Explain transition-element chemistry using d orbitals, variable oxidation states, complex ions, ligand exchange, colour, stereoisomerism, and stability constants.

## Organic Chemistry

- [ ] Use organic nomenclature, formula types, functional groups, isomerism, reaction types, and mechanism language accurately.
- [ ] Connect alkanes, alkenes, arenes, halogen compounds, hydroxy compounds, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids and derivatives, and nitrogen compounds through reagents, conditions, observations, and products.
- [ ] Draw and explain free-radical substitution, electrophilic addition, nucleophilic substitution, nucleophilic addition, electrophilic substitution, and addition-elimination mechanisms where required.
- [ ] Plan multi-step synthetic routes using functional-group interconversions, chemoselectivity, reaction conditions, and analytical evidence.
- [ ] Explain addition and condensation polymerisation, repeat units, monomers, disposal, degradation, and hydrolysis.

## Analytical And Practical Thinking

- [ ] Interpret qualitative observations, test results, spectra, chromatograms, mass spectra, infrared data, and NMR data as evidence for structure or composition.
- [ ] Design and evaluate practical methods using variables, controls, safety, apparatus choice, measurements, uncertainties, graph processing, and anomalous results.
- [ ] Keep equations, units, significant figures, state symbols, charges, oxidation numbers, and practical observations consistent.
- [ ] Link every calculation or mechanism back to the chemical model that justifies it.
