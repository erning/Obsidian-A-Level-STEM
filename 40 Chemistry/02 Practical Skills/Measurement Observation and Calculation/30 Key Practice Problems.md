# Measurement, Observation and Calculation - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. A burette gives titres of $23.40$, $23.45$, $23.50$ and $24.80\ \mathrm{cm^3}$. Identify the anomalous titre and calculate the mean titre to use.

2. A temperature rose from $21.5\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to $35.5\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ using a thermometer calibrated every $1\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Calculate the temperature change and its percentage error.

3. $25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of hydrochloric acid was titrated against $0.100\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ sodium hydroxide; the mean titre was $23.45\ \mathrm{cm^3}$. The reaction is $1:1$. Calculate the concentration of the acid and give your answer to an appropriate number of significant figures.

4. A student records two masses in the same column as $0.06\ \mathrm{g}$ and $0.1\ \mathrm{g}$. Explain the error and give the corrected form.

5. State the precision to record when using: (a) a burette; (b) a thermometer calibrated every $1\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$; (c) a balance reading to $0.01\ \mathrm{g}$; (d) a measuring cylinder calibrated every $1\ \mathrm{cm^3}$.

6. Distinguish a systematic error from a random error, giving one example of each from a calorimetry experiment.

7. On a graph of rate against concentration, two points on the line of best fit are $(0.10,\ 0.0048)$ and $(0.50,\ 0.0240)$. Calculate the gradient, and state its units if rate is in $\mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}\ s^{-1}}$ and concentration in $\mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$.

8. Give two reasons why a large triangle is used to find the gradient of a straight-line graph.

9. Write suitable table headings (with units) for a rates experiment in which the time for a cross to disappear is measured at five different thiosulfate concentrations.

10. Explain why "human error" is not an acceptable description, and state what should be written instead.

11. A calculated concentration is written as $0.13048\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$, but the titre was $23.45\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ and the standard alkali was $0.100\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$. Explain why this number of significant figures is not justified, and give an acceptable form.

12. Describe three features of a correctly drawn line of best fit.
