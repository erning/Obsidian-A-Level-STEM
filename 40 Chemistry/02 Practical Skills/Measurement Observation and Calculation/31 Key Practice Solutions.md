# Measurement, Observation and Calculation - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. $24.80\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ is anomalous (far from the others). The concordant titres are $23.40$, $23.45$ and $23.50\ \mathrm{cm^3}$, all within $0.10\ \mathrm{cm^3}$.

   $$\text{mean} = \frac{23.40 + 23.45 + 23.50}{3} = 23.45\ \mathrm{cm^3}$$

2. $\Delta T = 35.5 - 21.5 = 14.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Each reading has uncertainty $\pm 0.5\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$, so the change has uncertainty $\pm(0.5 + 0.5) = \pm 1.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$.

   $$\text{percentage error} = \frac{1.0}{14.0} \times 100 = 7.14\%$$

3. Moles of alkali:

   $$n(\ce{NaOH}) = \frac{0.100 \times 23.45}{1000} = 2.345 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}$$

   $1:1$ reaction, so $n(\ce{HCl}) = 2.345 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}$ in $25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3} = 0.0250\ \mathrm{dm^3}$:

   $$[\ce{HCl}] = \frac{2.345 \times 10^{-3}}{0.0250} = 0.0938\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$$

   The least precise input ($0.100$ and $25.0$, three significant figures) limits the answer, so $0.0938\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ (three significant figures) is appropriate; $0.09380$ (four) is also acceptable.

4. The two masses are not recorded to the same precision. $0.06\ \mathrm{g}$ is to the nearest $0.01\ \mathrm{g}$ but $0.1\ \mathrm{g}$ is to the nearest $0.1\ \mathrm{g}$. Corrected: write $0.10\ \mathrm{g}$ so both are to the nearest $0.01\ \mathrm{g}$.

5. (a) Burette: nearest $0.05\ \mathrm{cm^3}$. (b) Thermometer: nearest $0.5\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. (c) Balance: nearest $0.01\ \mathrm{g}$. (d) Measuring cylinder: nearest $0.5\ \mathrm{cm^3}$.

6. A systematic error shifts every reading the same way, such as a thermometer reading $1\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ high throughout a calorimetry run; it is reduced by calibration and using the same instrument for all readings. A random error varies between readings, such as fluctuations in room temperature; it is reduced by repeats and averaging.

7. 

   $$m = \frac{0.0240 - 0.0048}{0.50 - 0.10} = \frac{0.0192}{0.40} = 0.0480$$

   Units: rate divided by concentration, so $\mathrm{s^{-1}}$.

8. Reading uncertainty is a fixed amount in each coordinate. Spread over a large distance, it is a small percentage of the gradient; over a small distance, it dominates. A large triangle also reduces the effect of any single misread point.

9. For example: $[\ce{Na2S2O3}]$ / mol dm⁻³  |  volume of water / cm³  |  time $t$ / s  |  $1/t$ / s⁻¹.

10. "Human error" names neither the instrument nor the limitation. Instead, name the specific error and whether it is systematic or random, for example "a zero error on the balance (systematic)" or "judging the end point of the disappearing cross varies between runs (random)".

11. The titre ($23.45$, four significant figures) and the alkali ($0.100$, three) do not justify five significant figures. Acceptable forms are $0.131\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ (three) or $0.1305\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ (four).

12. Any three: points are evenly scattered on either side along the whole length; it is a single straight line or smooth curve (not joining every point); anomalous points are identified and not hidden; it passes through or near the majority of points.
