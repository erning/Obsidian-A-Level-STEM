# Planning, Analysis and Evaluation

Paper 5 is a written practical paper. It does not require laboratory facilities in the exam, but the skills depend on real laboratory experience: planning a workable investigation, handling data, drawing conclusions, and evaluating the evidence.

## Syllabus Route

The Paper 5 skill areas are planning; analysis; drawing conclusions; and evaluation. Planning includes defining the problem and method. Analysis, conclusion and evaluation include dealing with data, conclusion, and evaluation.

## Planning

- Define the aim, prediction, or hypothesis.
- Identify independent, dependent, and controlled variables.
- Choose apparatus and explain why its range and precision are suitable.
- Describe the arrangement of apparatus using clear words or a labelled diagram.
- State the readings to take and the table headings to prepare.
- Describe how repeats, control experiments, or additional readings will improve reliability or validity.
- Include safety precautions that match the actual risks.

## Analysis

- Decide what calculations are needed before using the data.
- Use mean, percentage, percentage error, rate, concentration, amount of substance, molar mass, or gas volume calculations where appropriate.
- Plot a graph when it reveals a pattern or helps find a gradient, intercept, or unknown value.
- For a straight line, use $y = mx + c$ and interpret $m$ and $c$ in the chemical context.

## Conclusion

- State the main pattern in the data.
- Decide whether the data support the prediction.
- Link the conclusion to chemical reasoning, not just to numerical agreement.
- Say whether measurement error could account for the difference between expected and experimental values.

## Evaluation

- Identify anomalous values and suggest plausible causes.
- Judge whether repeats and the range of data are adequate.
- Explain weaknesses in the procedure.
- Suggest realistic improvements, including changes to concentration, conditions, apparatus, or measurement method.
- Comment on reliability, validity, and confidence in the conclusion.
