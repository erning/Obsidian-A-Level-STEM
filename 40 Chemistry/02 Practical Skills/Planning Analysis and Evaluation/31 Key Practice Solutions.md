# Planning, Analysis and Evaluation - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. Prediction: as temperature rises, the time for the cross to disappear decreases and $1/t$ increases. Independent: temperature, set with a water bath (for example $25$, $35$, $45$, $55$, $65\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$). Dependent: time for the cross to disappear. Controls: volumes and concentrations of thiosulfate and acid (fixed, measured with a pipette); total volume; the same flask and cross. Table headings: temperature / $^\circ\mathrm{C}$, time $t$ / s, $1/t$ / s⁻¹. Safety: sulfur dioxide is an irritant, so use a fume hood or good ventilation and small volumes; hot water can scald, so use a test-tube holder; wear eye protection.

2. For a first-order reaction, $\ln[\ce{A}]$ decreases linearly with time. Plot $\ln[\ce{A}]$ on the $y$-axis against $t$ on the $x$-axis. The values $\ln(0.500) = -0.693$, $\ln(0.397) = -0.924$, $\ln(0.315) = -1.155$, $\ln(0.250) = -1.386$ fall by about $0.231$ each $20\ \mathrm{s}$, giving a straight line. A straight-line $\ln[\ce{A}]$-against-$t$ plot with a constant gradient confirms first order.

3. The main source of error is heat loss to the surroundings during the reaction, a systematic error that makes $\Delta T$ too small. Improvements: add a lid and use more concentrated reagents to give a larger, faster temperature rise so the same heat loss is a smaller fraction; extrapolate the cooling curve back to the moment of mixing.

4. Reliability is about agreement between repeats: closely grouped repeats are reliable. Validity is about whether the data actually test the prediction: data are valid only if the independent variable is the only significant cause of the change in the dependent variable. Reliable data can still be invalid if a control variable was not controlled.

5. The gradient $m$ is found from two points on the line of best fit that are more than half the line length apart: $m = (y_2 - y_1)/(x_2 - x_1)$. The intercept $c$ is where the line meets the $y$-axis. Extra readings spread across the range, especially at the ends, improve the gradient's reliability.

6. Linearising means choosing quantities to plot so that a suspected non-linear relationship becomes a straight line, which is easier to test. Example: if rate is expected to be proportional to concentration, plot rate against concentration for a straight line through the origin; if time is inversely related to concentration, plot $1/t$ against concentration.

7. Any three: a description of the main trend or shape of the data; a statement of whether the data support the prediction; a scientific explanation linking the pattern to chemistry; a judgement on whether measurement error could account for any discrepancy; a further prediction or suggested improvement.

8. Plot volume of $\ce{CO2}$ on the $y$-axis against mass of carbonate on the $x$-axis. The reaction stoichiometry fixes the mole ratio of carbonate to $\ce{CO2}$, so the volume of gas is directly proportional to the mass of carbonate, giving a straight line through the origin whose gradient is related to the molar mass.

9. Carbon dioxide is slightly soluble in water, so some gas dissolves before it is collected, making the volume too low. Reading the level of water against a scale is also less precise than a gas syringe, and the method needs careful levelling to equalise pressure.

10. "Prove" is too strong: experimental data always carry uncertainty, so they cannot logically prove a prediction. "Support" is correct: the data are consistent with the prediction within the limits of experimental error.

11. Record temperature against time before mixing to establish a baseline, then after the maximum to show the cooling trend. Plot temperature against time and extrapolate the cooling line straight back to the moment of mixing; the value at that point is the temperature change corrected for heat lost during the reaction.

12. A wider range shows the full shape of the relationship and makes the trend clearer against random scatter, and it makes the line of best fit more reliable because the gradient depends on well-separated points.
