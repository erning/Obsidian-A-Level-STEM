# Planning, Analysis and Evaluation - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. Plan an experiment to test how temperature affects the rate of the sodium thiosulfate-acid reaction. Give the prediction, the independent and dependent variables, three control variables with methods, the results-table headings, and the safety precautions.

2. The concentration of a reactant $\ce{A}$ was measured against time: at $t = 0$, $[\ce{A}] = 0.500$; at $20\ \mathrm{s}$, $0.397$; at $40\ \mathrm{s}$, $0.315$; at $60\ \mathrm{s}$, $0.250\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$. The reaction is suspected to be first order. Explain what to plot to obtain a straight line and how the plot confirms the order.

3. An enthalpy experiment gave a measured $\Delta T = 8.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$, but the expected value from literature data was about $10.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Identify the main source of error and suggest two realistic improvements.

4. Explain the difference between the reliability of a set of data and its validity.

5. A graph of $y$ against $x$ is a straight line through the origin. State how to find $m$ and $c$ from the graph, and state what extra readings would improve the reliability of the gradient.

6. Explain what is meant by linearising data, and give one example of choosing quantities to plot so that a suspected relationship becomes a straight line.

7. State three things a conclusion should include beyond restating the numbers.

8. An experiment measures the volume of carbon dioxide produced from different masses of carbonate. Suggest the axes that would give a straight line through the origin, and explain why.

9. A student measures the volume of carbon dioxide using an inverted measuring cylinder full of water. Identify two weaknesses of this method for carbon dioxide.

10. Explain the difference between saying "the data prove the prediction" and "the data support the prediction".

11. Describe how to use a cooling curve to correct for heat loss in a thermometric experiment.

12. State two reasons why a wider range of values of the independent variable improves an investigation.
