# Qualitative Analysis - Review Checklist

## Recall

- [ ] I can state the good-habit rules: caution, small quantity, specified amount, record "no change", test solubility in excess, identify gases.
- [ ] I can recall the cation results with $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and $\ce{NH3(aq)}$, including solubility in excess.
- [ ] I can recall the anion tests (carbonate, sulfate, sulfite, chloride, bromide, iodide, nitrate).
- [ ] I can recall the gas tests for ammonia, carbon dioxide, hydrogen and oxygen.
- [ ] I can recall the four organic analysis tests and their positive results.

## Skills

- [ ] I can record an observation before drawing a conclusion.
- [ ] I can use solubility in excess to separate $\ce{Al^{3+}}$ from $\ce{Zn^{2+}}$.
- [ ] I can identify a halide from the silver halide colour and its solubility in ammonia.
- [ ] I can distinguish a sulfate from a sulfite.
- [ ] I can order the organic tests: 2,4-DNPH, then Fehling's/Tollens', then tri-iodomethane.
- [ ] I can confirm a gas by its specific test.

## Common Errors

- [ ] I do not write a conclusion before recording the observation.
- [ ] I do not forget to test solubility in excess when a precipitate forms.
- [ ] I do not omit "no change" observations.
- [ ] I do not add silver nitrate without first acidifying.
- [ ] I do not name a gas without its matching test result.
- [ ] I do not use organic tests out of order.

## Self-Checks

- [ ] Given a sequence of observations, I can shortlist and then identify the cation.
- [ ] Given two anions with similar tests, I can choose the distinguishing test.
- [ ] Given organic test results, I can deduce the functional groups in order.
- [ ] Given effervescence, I can confirm the gas and write its test.

## Connections

- [ ] I can link safe handling of unknowns to *Experimental Design and Safety*.
- [ ] I can link observation discipline to *Measurement, Observation and Calculation*.
- [ ] I can link the organic tests to the carbonyl and hydroxy compound topics.

## Ready When

- [ ] I can work through an observational problem methodically, recording every result.
- [ ] I can separate cations and anions that give similar initial results.
- [ ] I can justify each conclusion from observations rather than guessing.
