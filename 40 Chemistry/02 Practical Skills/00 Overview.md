## How to Use

- Treat these notes as a practical-skills index, not as a substitute for laboratory practice.
- Keep raw observations separate from inferences: "white precipitate" is an observation; "chloride ion present" is a conclusion.
- Link worked examples and topic-specific experiments back here as they are created.
- Use [Mathematical Tools for Chemistry](../04%20Reference/Mathematical%20Tools%20for%20Chemistry.md) and [Chemical Formulae and Equations](../04%20Reference/Chemical%20Formulae%20and%20Equations.md) when practical work becomes calculation-heavy.

## Core Practical Habits

- Work safely and use only appropriate quantities of reagents.
- Record "no change" when that is what was observed.
- Use headings and units consistently in tables.
- Keep calculated values to an appropriate number of significant figures.
- Identify the largest source of uncertainty before suggesting improvements.
- Make evaluations specific to the method, apparatus, and data.
