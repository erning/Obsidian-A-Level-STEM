# Experimental Design and Safety

Experimental design turns a chemical question into a safe, workable procedure. In Chemistry 9701, this matters both in laboratory practical work and in written planning questions.

## Syllabus Route

Paper 5 expects candidates to identify a safe and efficient procedure, choose suitable apparatus, understand risks, and define independent, dependent, and controlled variables. Paper 3 also expects candidates to follow instructions safely and make sensible decisions about measurements and observations.

## Design Checklist

- State the aim as a testable prediction or hypothesis.
- Identify the independent variable and explain how it will be changed.
- Identify the dependent variable and explain exactly how it will be measured.
- List the key control variables and how they will be kept constant.
- Choose apparatus with a suitable range and precision.
- Plan enough readings to show a pattern, including repeats where reliability matters.
- Prepare a results table before collecting data.

## Safety

- Treat unknown substances with caution.
- Use small, appropriate quantities of chemicals.
- Choose controls that reduce risk as well as improve validity.
- Use a fume hood for hazardous gases and avoid naked flames with flammable materials.
- Wear eye protection and use chemically resistant gloves when the procedure requires them.
- Heat solids in suitable glassware and use a test-tube holder where appropriate.

## Common Weaknesses

- Naming hazards without saying how the risk is reduced.
- Saying "repeat for accuracy" without explaining what will be averaged or checked.
- Giving apparatus names but no method for using them.
- Changing more than one variable at the same time.
- Suggesting a safety precaution that does not match the chemical risk.
