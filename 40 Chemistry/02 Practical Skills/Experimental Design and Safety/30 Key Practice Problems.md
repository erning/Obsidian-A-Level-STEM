# Experimental Design and Safety - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. In an investigation of how sodium thiosulfate concentration affects the rate of its reaction with acid, identify the independent variable, the dependent variable and three key control variables. For each control variable, state how it is kept constant.

2. A student plans to measure the enthalpy change of neutralisation in a plastic cup. State two precautions that reduce heat loss and explain how each one works.

3. Explain why weighing by difference gives a more reliable mass than weighing the solid directly on a watch glass.

4. State what is meant by a control experiment, and give one example in an investigation that tests whether a catalyst increases the rate of a reaction.

5. An experiment must transfer exactly $25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of acid and deliver a variable volume of alkali. Name the apparatus for each and state the precision each should be read to.

6. A plan uses $2.0\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ sodium hydroxide. State the hazard and two precautions that match it.

7. Explain why a titration is repeated until the titres are concordant, and state what "concordant" means in $\mathrm{cm^3}$.

8. Give two reasons, other than cost, for using small quantities of reagents in a school investigation.

9. State three control variables for an experiment investigating how temperature affects the rate of the reaction between marble chips and dilute hydrochloric acid.

10. A student's plan says only "keep everything else the same". Explain why this is an inadequate description of the control variables, and rewrite it properly for one control variable.

11. Describe how a water bath is used to ensure the reactants are at the intended temperature before mixing in a rates experiment.

12. State two pieces of standard laboratory practice that improve the reliability of a titration result.
