# 18 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. Use acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ and heat under reflux.

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CH2OH + 2[O] -> CH3CH2COOH + H2O}
   $$

2. Use acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ and heat under reflux.

   $$
   \ce{CH3CHO + [O] -> CH3COOH}
   $$

3. The final organic product is propanoic acid. In alkaline hydrolysis, the nitrile first forms a propanoate salt or propanoate ion. Acidification supplies $\ce{H+}$ to form the carboxylic acid:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2COO- + H+ -> CH3CH2COOH}
   $$

4. With magnesium, the products are a salt and hydrogen:

   $$
   \ce{2CH3COOH + Mg -> (CH3COO)2Mg + H2}
   $$

   With sodium hydroxide, the products are a salt and water:

   $$
   \ce{CH3COOH + NaOH -> CH3COONa + H2O}
   $$

   With sodium carbonate, the products are a salt, water, and carbon dioxide:

   $$
   \ce{2CH3COOH + Na2CO3 -> 2CH3COONa + H2O + CO2}
   $$

5. Ethanol supplies the ethyl group and methanoic acid supplies the methanoate part, so the ester is ethyl methanoate. The catalyst is concentrated sulfuric acid.

   $$
   \ce{HCOOH + CH3CH2OH <=> HCOOCH2CH3 + H2O}
   $$

6. The immediate organic products are sodium ethanoate and methanol:

   $$
   \ce{CH3COOCH3 + NaOH -> CH3COONa + CH3OH}
   $$

   After acidification, sodium ethanoate is converted into ethanoic acid. The organic products are ethanoic acid and methanol.

7. First substitute chlorine with cyanide:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2Cl ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CN}
   $$

   Then hydrolyse the nitrile:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CN ->[dilute\ acid,\ heat] CH3CH2COOH}
   $$

   A route using dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification for the second step is also suitable.

8. The carbonate reaction suggests a carboxylic acid. One possible structure with formula $\ce{C4H8O2}$ is butanoic acid, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2COOH}$. Another acceptable structure is methylpropanoic acid, depending on the level of structural isomerism required.

9. The ester must contain the propanoate part from propanoic acid and the methyl group from methanol. It is methyl propanoate:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2COOCH3}
   $$

10. Reflux keeps volatile reactants and intermediates in the reaction mixture so the primary alcohol can be fully oxidised to the carboxylic acid. Distillation removes the aldehyde as it forms, limiting further oxidation when the aldehyde is the target product.
