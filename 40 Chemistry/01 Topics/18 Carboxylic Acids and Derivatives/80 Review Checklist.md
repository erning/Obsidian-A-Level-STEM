# 18 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives - Review Checklist

## Recall

- I can identify the carboxyl group, $\ce{-COOH}$, and the ester group, $\ce{-COO-}$.
- I can state that carboxylic acids are produced by oxidation of primary alcohols and aldehydes using acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ under reflux.
- I can state that nitriles hydrolyse with dilute acid, or with dilute alkali followed by acidification, to produce carboxylic acids.
- I can state that esters hydrolyse with dilute acid or with dilute alkali and heat.
- I can state that esterification uses an alcohol, a carboxylic acid, and concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ as catalyst.
- I can state that carboxylic acids are reduced to primary alcohols using $\ce{LiAlH4}$.

## Equations and Products

- I can write equations for carboxylic acids reacting with reactive metals, alkalis, and carbonates.
- I can predict the salt formed when a named carboxylic acid reacts with a named metal, alkali, or carbonate.
- I can split an ester into its acid-derived and alcohol-derived parts.
- I can give different products for acidic and alkaline ester hydrolysis.
- I can include acidification after alkaline hydrolysis when the final product should be a carboxylic acid.

## Synthesis Skills

- I can plan oxidation routes from primary alcohols or aldehydes to carboxylic acids.
- I can plan chain-lengthening routes using halogenoalkane $\to$ nitrile $\to$ carboxylic acid.
- I can plan ester synthesis from a given alcohol and carboxylic acid.
- I can name reagents and conditions for every step of a short route.

## Analysis Skills

- I can connect effervescence with carbonate to $\ce{CO2}$ formation and carboxylic acid behaviour.
- I can use broad infrared $\ce{O-H}$ and $\ce{C=O}$ evidence to support a carboxylic acid.
- I can use ester $\ce{C=O}$ and $\ce{C-O}$ evidence together with hydrolysis products to support an ester.
- I can avoid treating one spectral absorption as complete proof.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Do not use distillation when the intended oxidation product is a carboxylic acid.
- Do not forget that alkaline hydrolysis gives a carboxylate salt before acidification.
- Do not reverse the alcohol-derived and acid-derived parts of an ester name.
- Do not use $\ce{NaBH4}$ for reduction of carboxylic acids.
- Do not omit conditions such as heat, reflux, ethanol, concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$, or acidification.

## Ready for the Next Topic When

- I can move confidently between alcohols, aldehydes, nitriles, carboxylic acids, and esters.
- I can explain each reaction as oxidation, hydrolysis, acid-base reaction, condensation, or reduction.
- I can use carboxylic acids and esters as reliable steps in AS organic synthesis routes.
