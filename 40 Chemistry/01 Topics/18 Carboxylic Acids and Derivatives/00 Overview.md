# 18 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives

## Core Idea

Carboxylic acids and esters extend carbonyl chemistry into acid-base reactions, esterification, hydrolysis and organic synthesis routes.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 18 Carboxylic acids and derivatives.
- 18.1 Carboxylic acids.
- 18.2 Esters.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Recall production of carboxylic acids by oxidation, nitrile hydrolysis and ester hydrolysis.
- Describe carboxylic acid reactions with reactive metals, alkalis, carbonates, alcohols and reducing agents.
- Describe ester formation from alcohols and carboxylic acids with concentrated sulfuric acid as catalyst.
- Describe hydrolysis of esters by dilute acid and by dilute alkali with heat.
- Use carboxylic acids and esters as links in organic synthesis routes.

## How to Study

- Place carboxylic acids after alcohols, aldehydes and nitriles in the reaction map.
- Separate acid-base reactions from nucleophilic substitution or condensation language.
- For ester questions, always identify which part came from the alcohol and which came from the acid.

## Practice Directions

- Predict products from carboxylic acid reactions with metals, alkalis and carbonates.
- Write esterification and hydrolysis equations.
- Choose reagents to convert between alcohols, aldehydes, acids, esters and nitriles.

- Forgetting that ester hydrolysis conditions change the products.
- Missing carbon dioxide as evidence for reaction with carbonates.
- Reducing carboxylic acids with the wrong reducing agent.
