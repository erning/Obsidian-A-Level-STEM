# 18 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. State the reagent and condition needed to convert propan-1-ol into propanoic acid. Write an equation using $[O]$.

2. Ethanal is converted into ethanoic acid. State a suitable oxidising agent and condition, and write an equation using $[O]$.

3. Propanenitrile is hydrolysed using dilute alkali and heat, followed by acidification. Name the final organic product and explain why acidification is needed.

4. Ethanoic acid reacts separately with magnesium, sodium hydroxide, and sodium carbonate. For each reaction, state the product type and write an equation.

5. Name the ester formed from ethanol and methanoic acid. Write the equation and state the catalyst.

6. Methyl ethanoate is heated with dilute sodium hydroxide. Give the immediate organic products. Then give the organic products after acidification.

7. Devise a route from 1-chloroethane to propanoic acid. Include reagents and conditions for each step.

8. A compound has formula $\ce{C4H8O2}$. It reacts with sodium carbonate to produce a gas that gives a white precipitate with limewater. Suggest a possible functional group and one possible structural formula.

9. A compound has an ester infrared pattern and is hydrolysed to methanol and propanoic acid. Name the ester and write its condensed structural formula.

10. Explain why reflux is used to oxidise a primary alcohol to a carboxylic acid, while distillation is used when the aldehyde is the target product.
