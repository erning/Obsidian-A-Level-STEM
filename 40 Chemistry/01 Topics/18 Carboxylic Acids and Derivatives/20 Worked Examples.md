# 18 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Complete Oxidation of a Primary Alcohol

**Question.** State the reagent and condition needed to convert butan-1-ol into butanoic acid. Write an equation using $[O]$.

**Working.** A primary alcohol can be oxidised first to an aldehyde and then to a carboxylic acid. To reach the acid, the mixture must be heated under reflux with acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2OH + 2[O] -> CH3CH2CH2COOH + H2O}
$$

**Answer.** Acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$, heat under reflux.

**Check.** Reflux is the clue for full oxidation. Distillation would be used if the aldehyde were the intended product.

## Example 2: Distinguishing Acid and Alkaline Ester Hydrolysis

**Question.** Ethyl propanoate is hydrolysed by heating with aqueous sodium hydroxide. Give the organic products after the mixture is acidified.

**Working.** First split the ester at the $\ce{C-O}$ bond next to the carbonyl:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2COOCH2CH3}
$$

The acid side is propanoate. The alcohol side is ethanol.

With $\ce{NaOH}$, the immediate organic salt is sodium propanoate:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2COOCH2CH3 + NaOH -> CH3CH2COONa + CH3CH2OH}
$$

Acidification converts the propanoate salt into propanoic acid:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2COO- + H+ -> CH3CH2COOH}
$$

**Answer.** Propanoic acid and ethanol.

**Check.** If the question had not included acidification, sodium propanoate would be the carboxylate product.

## Example 3: Naming an Ester from Its Reactants

**Question.** Methanol reacts with butanoic acid in the presence of concentrated sulfuric acid. Name and draw the ester product in condensed structural form.

**Working.** The alcohol gives the alkyl group, methyl. The acid gives the carboxylate part, butanoate.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2COOH + CH3OH <=> CH3CH2CH2COOCH3 + H2O}
$$

**Answer.** Methyl butanoate, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2COOCH3}$.

**Check.** The name is alkyl first, carboxylate second.

## Example 4: Producing an Acid from a Halogenoalkane

**Question.** Devise a two-step route from 1-bromopropane to butanoic acid.

**Working.** To increase the carbon chain by one carbon, substitute bromine with cyanide:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2CN}
$$

The nitrile has four carbons in total. Hydrolyse the nitrile with dilute acid, or with dilute alkali followed by acidification:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2CN ->[dilute\ acid,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2COOH}
$$

**Answer.** 1-bromopropane $\to$ butanenitrile using $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat; butanenitrile $\to$ butanoic acid by hydrolysis using dilute acid and heat, or dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification.

**Check.** The carbon count changes from three to four at the cyanide step.

## Example 5: Reaction Evidence for a Carboxylic Acid

**Question.** A liquid reacts with sodium carbonate to give effervescence. The gas gives a white precipitate with limewater. What functional group is suggested, and what salt forms if the liquid is ethanoic acid reacting with sodium carbonate?

**Working.** Carbonates react with acids to give carbon dioxide. A carboxylic acid is the AS organic functional group that gives this acid-carbonate reaction.

$$
\ce{2CH3COOH + Na2CO3 -> 2CH3COONa + H2O + CO2}
$$

**Answer.** A carboxylic acid group is suggested. The salt is sodium ethanoate, $\ce{CH3COONa}$.

**Check.** Limewater confirms $\ce{CO2}$, not hydrogen.

## Example 6: Reducing a Carboxylic Acid

**Question.** Give the reagent for converting propanoic acid into propan-1-ol. Why is $\ce{NaBH4}$ not the best answer?

**Working.** AS requires $\ce{LiAlH4}$ for reduction of carboxylic acids to primary alcohols.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2COOH + 4[H] -> CH3CH2CH2OH + H2O}
$$

$\ce{NaBH4}$ is used for reducing aldehydes and ketones to alcohols, not for this carboxylic acid transformation in the syllabus.

**Answer.** Use $\ce{LiAlH4}$. $\ce{NaBH4}$ is not the syllabus reagent for reducing carboxylic acids.

**Check.** The carbon skeleton is unchanged; only $\ce{-COOH}$ becomes $\ce{-CH2OH}$.
