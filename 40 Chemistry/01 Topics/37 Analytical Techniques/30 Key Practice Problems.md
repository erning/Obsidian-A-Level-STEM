# 37 Analytical Techniques - Key Practice Problems

1. Define stationary phase, mobile phase, baseline, solvent front and $R_f$ value for TLC.

2. A TLC spot moves 4.5 cm and the solvent front moves 9.0 cm. Calculate the $R_f$ value.

3. Explain why two compounds can have different $R_f$ values on the same TLC plate.

4. Define stationary phase, mobile phase and retention time for gas / liquid chromatography.

5. A gas / liquid chromatogram has peak areas 25, 15 and 10. Calculate the percentage composition.

6. Explain why a compound with stronger interaction with the stationary phase has a longer retention time.

7. Predict the number of carbon-13 NMR peaks in ethane, propanone and ethanol.

8. Explain why carbon-13 NMR peak count is not the same as the number of carbon atoms.

9. For $\ce{CH3CH2OH}$, predict the integration ratio for the main proton environments, ignoring exchange effects in splitting.

10. Use the $n+1$ rule to predict the splitting of the $\ce{CH3}$ and $\ce{CH2}$ protons in an ethyl group.

11. State why TMS is used in NMR.

12. State why deuterated solvents such as $\ce{CDCl3}$ are used in proton NMR.

13. Describe how $\ce{D2O}$ exchange identifies $\ce{O-H}$ and $\ce{N-H}$ protons.

14. Explain how TLC, gas / liquid chromatography and NMR could each help after an organic synthesis.
