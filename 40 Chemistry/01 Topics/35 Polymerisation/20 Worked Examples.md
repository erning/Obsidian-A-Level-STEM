# 35 Polymerisation - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Polyester from a Diol and a Dicarboxylic Acid

Deduce the repeat unit from ethane-1,2-diol, $\ce{HOCH2CH2OH}$, and benzene-1,4-dicarboxylic acid, $\ce{HOOCC6H4COOH}$.

The diol supplies $\ce{-OCH2CH2O-}$. The dicarboxylic acid supplies $\ce{-COC6H4CO-}$. Joining through ester links gives:

$$
\ce{[-OCH2CH2OOC-C6H4-CO-]_{n}}
$$

Water is eliminated when each ester link forms.

Check: the repeat unit contains ester links and continuation bonds through both ends.

## Example 2: Polyester from a Hydroxycarboxylic Acid

Draw the repeat unit from $\ce{HOCH2COOH}$.

The same molecule contains $\ce{-OH}$ and $\ce{-COOH}$, so it self-condenses. Remove $\ce{H}$ from the alcohol and $\ce{OH}$ from the carboxyl group to form an ester link.

Repeat unit:

$$
\ce{[-OCH2CO-]_{n}}
$$

Check: one monomer can form a condensation polymer if it has two suitable functional groups.

## Example 3: Polyamide from a Diamine and a Dioyl Chloride

Deduce the repeat unit from $\ce{H2N(CH2)6NH2}$ and $\ce{ClOC(CH2)4COCl}$.

The diamine supplies $\ce{-NH(CH2)6NH-}$. The dioyl chloride supplies $\ce{-CO(CH2)4CO-}$. Joining gives amide links and eliminates $\ce{HCl}$.

Repeat unit:

$$
\ce{[-NH(CH2)6NHCO(CH2)4CO-]_{n}}
$$

Check: diamine plus dioyl chloride forms a polyamide, not a polyester.

## Example 4: Identifying Monomers from a Polymer Section

A polymer section contains the repeating pattern $\ce{-OCH2CH2OOCCH2CO-}$. Identify likely monomers.

The $\ce{-OCH2CH2O-}$ part comes from ethane-1,2-diol, $\ce{HOCH2CH2OH}$. The $\ce{-OCCH2CO-}$ carbonyl part corresponds to propanedioic acid, $\ce{HOOCCH2COOH}$.

Likely monomers:

$$
\ce{HOCH2CH2OH}
$$

and

$$
\ce{HOOCCH2COOH}
$$

Check: restore $\ce{-OH}$ groups at the cut ester links.

## Example 5: Predicting Polymerisation Type

Predict the type of polymerisation for chloroethene, $\ce{CH2=CHCl}$, and for $\ce{H2NCH2COOH}$.

Chloroethene contains a carbon-carbon double bond, so it undergoes addition polymerisation.

$\ce{H2NCH2COOH}$ contains both amine and carboxylic acid groups, so it can undergo condensation polymerisation to form a polyamide, eliminating water.

Check: decide from functional groups, not from molecule size.

## Example 6: Degradability

Explain why a polyester is generally more biodegradable by hydrolysis than poly(ethene).

A polyester contains ester links in the chain. Ester links can be broken by acidic or alkaline hydrolysis. Poly(ethene) has a chemically inert carbon-carbon backbone and lacks hydrolysable functional groups, so it is difficult to biodegrade.

Check: hydrolysis requires a bond type that water can break under the conditions.
