# 31 Halogen Compounds

## Core Idea

A Level halogen compounds compare halogenoarenes with halogenoalkanes. The central idea is that the carbon-halogen bond behaves differently when attached directly to an aromatic ring.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 31 Halogen compounds
- 31.1 Halogen compounds

## What to Learn

- Recall how halogenoarenes are made by substitution of arenes with chlorine or bromine in the presence of a suitable catalyst.
- Use benzene and methylbenzene examples to name and locate halogenoarene products.
- Explain the difference in reactivity between a halogenoalkane and a halogenoarene.

## How to Study

- Compare chloroethane and chlorobenzene explicitly; the contrast is the point of the topic.
- Link reduced halogenoarene reactivity to the aromatic system rather than memorising it as an isolated fact.
- Keep aryl halides and alkyl halides separate when choosing mechanisms.

## Practice Directions

- Predict halogenoarene products from arene substitution reactions.
- Explain why chlorobenzene is much less reactive than chloroethane in nucleophilic substitution.
- Decide when a halogen is on the aromatic ring and when it is on a side-chain.

## Common Traps

- Applying $S_N1$ or $S_N2$ halogenoalkane mechanisms directly to halogenoarenes.
- Confusing a halogenoarene with an aryl-substituted halogenoalkane.
- Missing positional isomers when methylbenzene is halogenated on the ring.
- Explaining reactivity differences without referring to bonding in the aromatic ring.
