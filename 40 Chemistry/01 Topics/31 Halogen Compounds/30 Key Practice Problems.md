# 31 Halogen Compounds - Key Practice Problems

1. Define halogenoarene and give one example.

2. Write the equation and conditions for converting benzene into bromobenzene.

3. Write the equation and conditions for converting benzene into chlorobenzene.

4. Methylbenzene reacts with $\ce{Cl2}$ and $\ce{AlCl3}$. State the main ring positions of substitution.

5. Methylbenzene reacts with $\ce{Cl2}$ in ultraviolet light. State the organic product and explain why it is not a halogenoarene.

6. Explain why chloroethane is more reactive than chlorobenzene towards nucleophilic substitution.

7. State one reason why an $S_N2$ pathway is not suitable for chlorobenzene.

8. State one reason why an $S_N1$ pathway is not suitable for chlorobenzene.

9. Explain how Topic 31 links back to AS halogenoalkane chemistry.

10. Explain how Topic 31 links forward to organic synthesis.
