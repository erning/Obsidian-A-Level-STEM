# Group 17 - Review Checklist

## Recall

- [ ] I know the colours and states of $\ce{Cl2}$, $\ce{Br2}$ and $\ce{I2}$.
- [ ] I can explain decreasing volatility down Group 17 using instantaneous dipole-induced dipole forces.
- [ ] I can describe the decreasing $\ce{X-X}$ bond strength down the group.
- [ ] I can state that halogen oxidising strength decreases down the group.
- [ ] I can state that halide ion reducing strength increases down the group.
- [ ] I can describe the reactions of halogens with hydrogen.
- [ ] I can explain the decreasing thermal stability of $\ce{HCl}$, $\ce{HBr}$ and $\ce{HI}$.

## Equations

- [ ] I can write ionic equations for halogen displacement reactions.
- [ ] I can write $\ce{Ag+ + X- -> AgX}$ precipitation equations for $\ce{Cl-}$, $\ce{Br-}$ and $\ce{I-}$.
- [ ] I can write equations for chloride, bromide and iodide ions with concentrated sulfuric acid.
- [ ] I can write the cold sodium hydroxide disproportionation equation for chlorine.
- [ ] I can write the hot sodium hydroxide disproportionation equation for chlorine.
- [ ] I can write equations showing formation of $\ce{HOCl}$ and $\ce{ClO-}$ in water purification.

## Observations

- [ ] I know $\ce{AgCl}$ is white and dissolves in dilute aqueous ammonia.
- [ ] I know $\ce{AgBr}$ is cream and dissolves in concentrated aqueous ammonia.
- [ ] I know $\ce{AgI}$ is yellow and is insoluble in concentrated aqueous ammonia.
- [ ] I can recognise brown or orange-brown bromine and purple or black iodine.
- [ ] I can link $\ce{SO2}$, sulfur and $\ce{H2S}$ products to the reducing strength of iodide.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] I do not confuse $\ce{Cl2}$ as an oxidising agent with $\ce{Cl-}$ as a reducing agent.
- [ ] I do not reverse the halogen and halide redox trends.
- [ ] I do not explain volatility by breaking covalent bonds inside $\ce{X2}$ molecules.
- [ ] I do not use the silver-ammonia complex formula unless specifically required.
- [ ] I do not forget that chlorine disproportionation means chlorine is both oxidised and reduced.

## Ready to Move On

- [ ] I can predict whether a halogen displacement reaction occurs.
- [ ] I can identify an unknown halide from silver nitrate, ammonia and concentrated sulfuric acid observations.
- [ ] I can use oxidation numbers fluently in Group 17 redox equations.
