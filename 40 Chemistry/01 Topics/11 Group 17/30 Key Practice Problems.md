# Group 17 - Key Practice Problems

Keep answers out of this file during active recall. Use [[40 Chemistry/01 Topics/11 Group 17/31 Key Practice Solutions|31 Key Practice Solutions]] only after attempting the questions.

## Questions

1. State the colours and physical states at room temperature of chlorine, bromine and iodine.

2. Explain why volatility decreases from chlorine to iodine.

3. Describe and explain the trend in $\ce{X-X}$ bond strength from $\ce{Cl2}$ to $\ce{I2}$.

4. State the trend in oxidising strength of chlorine, bromine and iodine. Use it to predict the reaction, if any, between chlorine and iodide ions.

5. State the trend in reducing strength of chloride, bromide and iodide ions. Explain why iodide ions are the strongest reducing agents.

6. Write equations for the reactions of chlorine, bromine and iodine with hydrogen. Comment on the trend in reactivity.

7. Explain the trend in thermal stability of $\ce{HCl}$, $\ce{HBr}$ and $\ce{HI}$.

8. A solution gives a yellow precipitate with aqueous silver ions that is insoluble in concentrated aqueous ammonia. Identify the halide ion and write the precipitation equation.

9. Write equations for the reaction of sodium chloride with concentrated sulfuric acid. State the observation.

10. Write equations for the reaction of sodium iodide with concentrated sulfuric acid to form iodine and hydrogen sulfide.

11. Write the equations for chlorine reacting with cold aqueous sodium hydroxide and hot aqueous sodium hydroxide. Use oxidation numbers to identify disproportionation.

12. Explain how chlorine acts in water purification, including equations that show formation of $\ce{HOCl}$ and $\ce{ClO-}$.
