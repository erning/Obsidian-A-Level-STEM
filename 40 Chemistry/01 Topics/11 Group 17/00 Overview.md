# 11 Group 17

## Core Idea

Group 17 chemistry compares halogens and halide ions through volatility, bond strength, redox behaviour and characteristic reactions. Chlorine, bromine and iodine show increasing intermolecular forces down the group, decreasing oxidising strength as elements, and increasing reducing strength as halide ions.

## What to Learn

- Describe colours and volatility trends for chlorine, bromine and iodine.
- Explain volatility using instantaneous dipole-induced dipole forces.
- Describe and explain the trend in halogen-halogen bond strength.
- Compare halogens as oxidising agents and halide ions as reducing agents.
- Describe reactions of halogens with hydrogen and thermal stability of hydrogen halides.
- Use silver ions, aqueous ammonia and concentrated sulfuric acid reactions to identify halide ions.
- Interpret chlorine reactions with cold and hot aqueous sodium hydroxide as disproportionation.
- Explain chlorine in water purification, including $\ce{HOCl}$ and $\ce{ClO-}$.

## How to Study

- Keep two opposite redox trend lines separate: $\ce{Cl2 > Br2 > I2}$ as oxidising agents, but $\ce{I- > Br- > Cl-}$ as reducing agents.
- Use oxidation numbers to make displacement and disproportionation visible.
- Learn test observations with the reagent sequence, not as isolated colours.
- Connect volatility to molecular size and instantaneous dipole-induced dipole forces.

## Common Traps

- Reversing the oxidising strength trend for halogens.
- Confusing halogen molecules with halide ions.
- Forgetting that concentrated sulfuric acid is an oxidising agent with bromide and iodide ions.
- Treating chlorine water purification as simple sterilisation without identifying the active species.
