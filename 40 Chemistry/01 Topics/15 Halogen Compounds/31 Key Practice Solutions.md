# 15 Halogen Compounds - Key Practice Solutions

1. Classifications:
   - $\ce{CH3CH2Cl}$: primary.
   - $\ce{CH3CHClCH2CH3}$: secondary.
   - $\ce{(CH3)3CCl}$: tertiary.

2. Use free-radical substitution: react ethane with $\ce{Cl2}$ in ultraviolet light.

$$
\ce{CH3CH3 + Cl2 ->[uv] CH3CH2Cl + HCl}
$$

3. React propene with $\ce{HBr(g)}$ at room temperature.

$$
\ce{CH3CH=CH2 + HBr -> CH3CHBrCH3}
$$

4. Possible reagent options include $\ce{HCl(g)}$, or $\ce{KCl}$ with concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ or concentrated $\ce{H3PO4}$. Chlorinating reagents such as $\ce{PCl3}$ with heat, $\ce{PCl5}$ or $\ce{SOCl2}$ can also be used.

5. Products from 1-bromobutane:
   - With $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and heat: butan-1-ol, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2OH}$.
   - With $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat: pentanenitrile, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2CN}$.
   - With $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol under pressure: butylamine, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2NH2}$.

6. Elimination gives propene:

$$
\ce{CH3CHBrCH3 + OH- -> CH3CH=CH2 + H2O + Br-}
$$

7. In the $S_N2$ mechanism, a lone pair on $\ce{OH-}$ attacks the electron-deficient carbon bonded to bromine. One curly arrow starts at the hydroxide lone pair and points to the carbon. A second curly arrow starts at the $\ce{C-Br}$ bond and points to bromine. The products are ethanol and $\ce{Br-}$.

8. In the $S_N1$ mechanism, the $\ce{C-Br}$ bond breaks first by heterolytic fission to form a tertiary carbocation and $\ce{Br-}$:

$$
\ce{(CH3)3CBr -> (CH3)3C+ + Br-}
$$

Then hydroxide attacks the carbocation:

$$
\ce{(CH3)3C+ + OH- -> (CH3)3COH}
$$

9. The $\ce{C-I}$ bond is weaker than the $\ce{C-Cl}$ bond, so it breaks more readily during hydrolysis. Iodide ions are released faster, giving a precipitate with $\ce{Ag+}$ sooner.

10. A yellow precipitate is $\ce{AgI}$, so the halide ion is $\ce{I-}$.

$$
\ce{Ag+ + I- -> AgI(s)}
$$
