# 15 Halogen Compounds - Key Practice Problems

1. Classify each halogenoalkane as primary, secondary or tertiary:
   - $\ce{CH3CH2Cl}$
   - $\ce{CH3CHClCH2CH3}$
   - $\ce{(CH3)3CCl}$

2. State one route and the required conditions for making chloroethane from ethane.

3. State reagents and conditions for making 2-bromopropane from propene.

4. State two reagent options for converting ethanol to chloroethane.

5. Give the organic product when 1-bromobutane reacts with:
   - $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and heat
   - $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat
   - $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol, heated under pressure

6. Give the product when 2-bromopropane reacts with $\ce{NaOH}$ in ethanol and heat.

7. Describe the $S_N2$ mechanism for bromoethane reacting with hydroxide ions. State where each curly arrow starts.

8. Describe the $S_N1$ mechanism for 2-bromo-2-methylpropane reacting with hydroxide ions.

9. Explain why iodoalkanes react faster than chloroalkanes with aqueous silver nitrate in ethanol.

10. A halogenoalkane gives a yellow precipitate with aqueous silver nitrate in ethanol. Identify the halide ion and write the ionic equation for precipitate formation.
