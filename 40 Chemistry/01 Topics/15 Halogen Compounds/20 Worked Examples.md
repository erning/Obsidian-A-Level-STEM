# 15 Halogen Compounds - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Classifying Halogenoalkanes

Classify $\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br}$, $\ce{CH3CHBrCH3}$ and $\ce{(CH3)3CBr}$.

In $\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br}$, the carbon bonded to bromine is attached to one other carbon, so it is primary.

In $\ce{CH3CHBrCH3}$, the carbon bonded to bromine is attached to two other carbons, so it is secondary.

In $\ce{(CH3)3CBr}$, the carbon bonded to bromine is attached to three other carbons, so it is tertiary.

Check: classify the carbon bearing the halogen, not the whole molecule by longest chain.

## Example 2: Making a Halogenoalkane from an Alkene

Propene reacts with hydrogen bromide at room temperature. Predict the major product.

The reaction is electrophilic addition. The major product comes from the more stable secondary carbocation intermediate, so bromine ends up on carbon 2:

$$
\ce{CH3CH=CH2 + HBr -> CH3CHBrCH3}
$$

The product is 2-bromopropane.

Check: the reagent and conditions match alkene addition, not alkane radical substitution.

## Example 3: Chain Extension with Cyanide

Give the product when 1-bromopropane reacts with $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat.

The nucleophile is $\ce{CN-}$. It substitutes for bromide:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br + CN- -> CH3CH2CH2CN + Br-}
$$

The product is butanenitrile because the nitrile carbon is counted in the main chain.

Check: this reaction increases the carbon chain by one carbon.

## Example 4: Reaction with Ammonia

Write an equation for bromoethane reacting with ammonia in ethanol under pressure.

Ammonia substitutes for bromide to give ethylamine. A second ammonia molecule neutralises $\ce{HBr}$:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br + 2NH3 -> CH3CH2NH2 + NH4Br}
$$

Check: the organic product is a primary amine, not an alcohol.

## Example 5: Choosing Substitution or Elimination

Bromoethane is heated with sodium hydroxide. Predict the product when the solvent is water, and when the solvent is ethanol.

With $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and heat, substitution is favoured:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br + OH- -> CH3CH2OH + Br-}
$$

With $\ce{NaOH}$ in ethanol and heat, elimination is favoured:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br + OH- -> CH2=CH2 + H2O + Br-}
$$

Check: the same base/nucleophile can lead to different products because the solvent changes the favoured pathway.

## Example 6: Choosing $S_N1$ or $S_N2$

Suggest the likely mechanism for hydrolysis of 1-bromobutane and 2-bromo-2-methylpropane.

1-bromobutane is primary, so it tends to react by $S_N2$. The nucleophile can attack with little steric hindrance.

2-bromo-2-methylpropane is tertiary, so it tends to react by $S_N1$. The tertiary carbocation is stabilised by three alkyl groups.

Check: primary favours $S_N2$, tertiary favours $S_N1$, secondary may show a mixture.

## Example 7: Silver Nitrate Test

A halogenoalkane gives a cream precipitate when warmed with aqueous silver nitrate in ethanol. Identify the halogen and write the ionic equation.

A cream precipitate is $\ce{AgBr}$, so the halogen is bromine.

$$
\ce{Ag+ + Br- -> AgBr(s)}
$$

Check: white suggests chloride, cream suggests bromide, yellow suggests iodide.
