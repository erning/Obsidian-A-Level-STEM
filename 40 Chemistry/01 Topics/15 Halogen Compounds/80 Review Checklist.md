# 15 Halogen Compounds - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Define halogenoalkane and recognise the $\ce{R-X}$ structure.
- Classify primary, secondary and tertiary halogenoalkanes.
- Recall production routes from alkanes, alkenes and alcohols.
- State reagents and conditions for substitution with $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$, $\ce{KCN}$ and $\ce{NH3}$.
- State conditions for elimination using $\ce{NaOH}$ in ethanol and heat.
- Recall silver nitrate precipitate colours for chloride, bromide and iodide.

## Skills

- Predict alcohol, nitrile, amine and alkene products from halogenoalkanes.
- Explain why cyanide substitution extends the carbon chain.
- Draw or describe $S_N2$ with curly arrows from nucleophile lone pair and carbon-halogen bond.
- Draw or describe $S_N1$ through carbocation formation and nucleophile attack.
- Use primary, secondary and tertiary classification to predict likely mechanism.
- Explain reactivity differences using carbon-halogen bond strength.

## Common Errors

- Swapping the products of aqueous hydroxide and ethanolic hydroxide.
- Forgetting heat for several halogenoalkane reactions.
- Counting the nitrile carbon incorrectly when naming the product.
- Describing $S_N1$ for a primary halogenoalkane without justification.
- Saying silver nitrate tests the halogenoalkane directly, instead of recognising that halide ions form during hydrolysis.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can choose substitution or elimination from reagent and solvent.
- You can explain why halogenoalkanes are useful intermediates in organic synthesis.
- You can connect halogenoalkanes to hydrocarbons, alcohols, nitriles and amines using reagent-condition-product maps.
