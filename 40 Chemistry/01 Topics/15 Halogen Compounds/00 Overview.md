# 15 Halogen Compounds

## Core Idea

Halogen compounds focus on halogenoalkanes as useful reaction intermediates, especially through nucleophilic substitution, elimination and carbon-halogen bond reactivity.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 15 Halogen compounds.
- 15.1 Halogenoalkanes.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Recall production routes for halogenoalkanes from alkanes, alkenes and alcohols.
- Classify halogenoalkanes as primary, secondary or tertiary.
- Describe nucleophilic substitution with aqueous hydroxide, cyanide and ammonia.
- Describe elimination with ethanolic sodium hydroxide.
- Compare $S_N1$ and $S_N2$ mechanisms and explain reactivity using carbon-halogen bond strength.

## How to Study

- For each reagent, decide whether substitution or elimination is favoured.
- Track what the nucleophile contributes to the product.
- Compare primary, secondary and tertiary halogenoalkanes using mechanism and structure, not just labels.

## Practice Directions

- Predict products from halogenoalkane reactions with hydroxide, cyanide and ammonia.
- Draw $S_N1$ and $S_N2$ mechanisms for suitable examples.
- Interpret silver nitrate observations for carbon-halogen bond reactivity.

## Common Traps

- Using aqueous and ethanolic hydroxide interchangeably.
- Forgetting that cyanide extends the carbon chain by one carbon.
- Mixing up primary, secondary and tertiary classifications.
