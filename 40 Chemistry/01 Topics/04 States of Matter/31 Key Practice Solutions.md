# States of Matter - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. Gas molecules move randomly and collide with the walls of the container. Each collision exerts a force on the wall. Pressure is force per unit area, so the total effect of many molecular collisions produces gas pressure.

2. Ideal gases have zero particle volume and no intermolecular forces of attraction.

3. Convert units:

   $$
   V = 2.40\ \mathrm{dm^3} = 2.40 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^3}
   $$

   $$
   p = 100\ \mathrm{kPa} = 100000\ \mathrm{Pa}
   $$

   Use:

   $$
   n = \frac{pV}{RT}
   $$

   $$
   n = \frac{100000 \times 2.40 \times 10^{-3}}{8.31 \times 298}
   $$

   $$
   n = 0.0969\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

4. Convert units:

   $$
   V = 400\ \mathrm{cm^3} = 4.00 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{m^3}
   $$

   $$
   p = 120\ \mathrm{kPa} = 120000\ \mathrm{Pa}
   $$

   Calculate moles:

   $$
   n = \frac{pV}{RT} = \frac{120000 \times 4.00 \times 10^{-4}}{8.31 \times 300}
   $$

   $$
   n = 0.0193\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   Molar mass:

   $$
   M = \frac{0.960}{0.0193} = 49.9\ \mathrm{g\ mol^{-1}}
   $$

   For a molecular gas, $M_r = 49.9$.

5. At low pressure, gas particles are far apart, so the volume of the particles is small compared with the volume of the container. At high temperature, particles have greater kinetic energy, so intermolecular attractions are less significant. The ideal assumptions are therefore closer to true.

6. $\ce{NaCl}$ has a giant ionic lattice of $\ce{Na+}$ and $\ce{Cl-}$ ions. Strong electrostatic attractions act between oppositely charged ions throughout the lattice. A large amount of energy is required to overcome these attractions, so $\ce{NaCl}$ has a high melting point.

7. Solid $\ce{MgO}$ contains ions, but they are fixed in the ionic lattice and cannot move. Molten $\ce{MgO}$ has mobile $\ce{Mg^{2+}}$ and $\ce{O^{2-}}$ ions, so charge can flow and it conducts electricity.

8. Iodine is a simple molecular substance. During melting, only intermolecular forces between $\ce{I2}$ molecules are overcome. Sodium chloride is a giant ionic structure, and strong ionic bonds throughout the lattice must be overcome. Less energy is needed for iodine, so it has a much lower melting point.

9. Diamond is a giant molecular structure where each carbon atom forms four covalent bonds in a three-dimensional network. It has no delocalised electrons and does not conduct electricity. Graphite has layers; each carbon forms three covalent bonds and has one delocalised electron. These electrons can move along the layers, so graphite conducts electricity.

10. Copper has a giant metallic structure. Delocalised electrons can move through the lattice of positive copper ions. These mobile electrons carry charge, so solid copper conducts electricity.

11. A low melting point and no electrical conductivity suggest a simple molecular structure. The low melting point is due to relatively weak intermolecular forces between molecules. Lack of conductivity suggests no mobile ions or delocalised electrons. Insolubility in water is consistent with a non-polar simple molecular substance.

12. A very high melting point and no conductivity suggests a giant molecular structure without mobile electrons, such as diamond or silicon(IV) oxide, $\ce{SiO2}$. Strong covalent bonds throughout the structure require much energy to overcome, and there are no mobile charged particles.
