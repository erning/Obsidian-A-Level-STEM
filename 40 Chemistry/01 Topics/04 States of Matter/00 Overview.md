# 04 States of Matter

## Core Idea

States of matter connects the particle model of gases with the structures of solids, so that pressure, volume, amount, temperature and physical properties can be explained from particles and bonding.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Physical chemistry: 4 States of matter.
- 4.1 The gaseous state: ideal and real gases and $pV = nRT$.
- 4.2 Bonding and structure.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Explain gas pressure in terms of molecular collisions with container walls.
- Use the ideal gas equation $pV = nRT$ in calculations, including determination of relative molecular mass.
- Understand the assumptions of ideal gases and why real gases can differ.
- Describe simple lattice structures for giant ionic, simple molecular, giant molecular and giant metallic solids.
- Deduce bonding and structure from physical properties such as melting point, boiling point, conductivity and solubility.

## How to Study

- Keep gas quantities in consistent SI units before using $pV = nRT$.
- Link each solid structure to the particles present and the forces holding them.
- For property explanations, name both the structure and the attraction that must be overcome.

## Practice Directions

- Solve ideal gas equation problems with pressure, volume, temperature and amount.
- Classify substances from given physical property data.
- Explain conductivity and solubility by referring to mobile charged particles and interactions with water.

## Common Traps

- Using Celsius instead of kelvin in gas calculations.
- Treating all solids as molecular.
- Explaining conductivity without checking whether charged particles are mobile.
