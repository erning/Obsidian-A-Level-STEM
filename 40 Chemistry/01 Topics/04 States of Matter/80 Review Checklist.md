# States of Matter - Review Checklist

## Recall

- [ ] I can explain gas pressure in terms of collisions between gas molecules and container walls.
- [ ] I can state that ideal gases have zero particle volume and no intermolecular forces of attraction.
- [ ] I can state and rearrange $pV = nRT$.
- [ ] I know the SI units for pressure, volume, amount of substance and temperature in ideal gas calculations.
- [ ] I can describe giant ionic, simple molecular, giant molecular and giant metallic lattice structures.
- [ ] I can recall the required examples: $\ce{NaCl}$, $\ce{MgO}$, iodine, $\ce{C60}$, ice, $\ce{SiO2}$, graphite, diamond and copper.

## Skills

- [ ] I can convert $\mathrm{kPa}$ to $\mathrm{Pa}$, $\mathrm{cm^3}$ or $\mathrm{dm^3}$ to $\mathrm{m^3}$, and Celsius to kelvin.
- [ ] I can use $pV = nRT$ to calculate $p$, $V$, $n$ or $T$.
- [ ] I can determine $M_r$ from gas mass, volume, pressure and temperature.
- [ ] I can explain how real gases differ from the ideal gas assumptions.
- [ ] I can describe, interpret and predict melting point, boiling point, conductivity and solubility from structure and bonding.
- [ ] I can deduce structure and bonding from physical property data.

## Common Errors

- [ ] I do not use Celsius in $pV = nRT$.
- [ ] I do not use $\mathrm{cm^3}$ directly when $R$ is in SI units.
- [ ] I do not say solid ionic compounds conduct just because they contain ions.
- [ ] I check whether charged particles are mobile before explaining conductivity.
- [ ] I do not confuse simple molecular $\ce{C60}$ with a giant molecular carbon structure.
- [ ] I explain melting of simple molecular substances by overcoming intermolecular forces, not breaking covalent bonds within molecules.

## Self-Checks

- [ ] Given gas data, I can write all quantities in SI units before substituting.
- [ ] Given conductivity data, I can identify the mobile charged particle, if one exists.
- [ ] Given a melting point and conductivity pattern, I can distinguish giant ionic, simple molecular, giant molecular and metallic structures.
- [ ] Given a named solid from the syllabus, I can state its structure type and one key property.

## Connections

- [ ] I can connect mole calculations from stoichiometry to gas calculations.
- [ ] I can connect bonding types and intermolecular forces to solid structures.
- [ ] I can connect graphite conductivity to delocalised electrons.
- [ ] I can connect ionic conductivity to mobile ions in molten or aqueous states.

## Ready When

- [ ] I can complete an ideal gas calculation without unit mistakes.
- [ ] I can explain a solid property using particles, structure and attractions.
- [ ] I can deduce a structure from unfamiliar property data.
- [ ] I can compare two structures without using memorised labels alone.
