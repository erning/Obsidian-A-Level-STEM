# Electrochemistry - Key Practice Problems

1. Define oxidation and reduction in terms of electron transfer.

2. Define oxidation and reduction in terms of oxidation number.

3. Calculate the oxidation number of the underlined element in each species: sulfur in $\ce{SO2}$, sulfur in $\ce{SO4^{2-}}$, manganese in $\ce{MnO4-}$, nitrogen in $\ce{NH4+}$.

4. For the reaction below, identify what is oxidised, what is reduced, the oxidising agent and the reducing agent.

$$
\ce{Mg + 2H+ -> Mg^{2+} + H2}
$$

5. Explain why the following reaction is not a redox reaction.

$$
\ce{Ag+(aq) + Cl-(aq) -> AgCl(s)}
$$

6. Show that the following reaction is disproportionation.

$$
\ce{2H2O2 -> 2H2O + O2}
$$

7. Balance the equation:

$$
\ce{I- + Cl2 -> I2 + Cl-}
$$

8. Balance the acidified dichromate(VI) reaction:

$$
\ce{Cr2O7^{2-} + Fe^{2+} + H+ -> Cr^{3+} + Fe^{3+} + H2O}
$$

9. Give the formula of copper(II) chloride and name $\ce{FeO}$ using a Roman numeral.

10. The syllabus places detailed electrolysis and standard electrode potentials in A Level Topic 24. Explain, in one or two sentences, why AS redox half-equations are still a useful foundation for those later topics.
