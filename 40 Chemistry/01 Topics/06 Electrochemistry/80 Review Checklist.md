# Electrochemistry - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Oxidation is loss of electrons or increase in oxidation number.
- Reduction is gain of electrons or decrease in oxidation number.
- Redox means oxidation and reduction occur together.
- An oxidising agent is reduced.
- A reducing agent is oxidised.
- Disproportionation means the same element is both oxidised and reduced.
- Roman numerals in names show oxidation number.

## Skills

- Assign oxidation numbers in compounds and ions.
- Identify oxidation number increases and decreases.
- Write simple oxidation and reduction half-equations.
- Use oxidation number changes to balance redox equations.
- Identify oxidising and reducing agents.
- Recognise disproportionation reactions.
- Use Roman numerals correctly in inorganic names.

## Common Errors To Avoid

- Treating an ion's total charge as the oxidation number of every atom in the ion.
- Reversing oxidising agent and reducing agent.
- Forgetting that uncombined elements have oxidation number 0.
- Ignoring coefficients when balancing electron transfer.
- Calling electrolysis Topic 6 AS content; detailed electrolysis is Topic 24 in the verified syllabus.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can calculate oxidation numbers without guessing from names.
- You can explain redox using both electron transfer and oxidation number.
- You can balance a redox equation and check total charge.
- You can recognise disproportionation from a full equation.
