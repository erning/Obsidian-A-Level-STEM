# 06 Electrochemistry

## Core Idea

AS electrochemistry is the language of redox. Reactions are understood through electron transfer and changes in oxidation number. These ideas later support electrolysis and electrode potentials, but the verified AS Topic 6 syllabus scope is redox processes.

## What to Learn

- Calculate oxidation numbers of elements in compounds and ions.
- Use oxidation number changes to identify oxidation, reduction and redox reactions.
- Explain redox, oxidation, reduction and disproportionation in terms of electron transfer and oxidation number.
- Use oxidation number changes to help balance chemical equations.
- Identify oxidising agents and reducing agents.
- Use Roman numerals to indicate the magnitude of an oxidation number in names.

## How to Study

Assign oxidation numbers before naming redox roles. The species whose oxidation number increases is oxidised and acts as a reducing agent. The species whose oxidation number decreases is reduced and acts as an oxidising agent.

Use both definitions of redox. Electron transfer is often easiest in half-equations; oxidation number change is often easiest in full equations, molecular equations and inorganic naming.

## Practice Directions

- Calculate oxidation numbers in neutral compounds and complex ions.
- Decide which element is oxidised and which is reduced.
- Write simple oxidation and reduction half-equations.
- Balance redox equations using oxidation number changes.
- Identify disproportionation, where the same element is both oxidised and reduced.
- Name compounds using Roman numerals where oxidation number must be clear.

## Common Traps

- Saying "gain of oxygen" when the question needs oxidation number evidence.
- Forgetting that an oxidising agent is reduced.
- Treating the charge on an ion as the oxidation number of every atom inside the ion.
- Changing charges in an equation without checking that atoms and total charge still balance.
- Missing disproportionation because the same element appears in two products.
