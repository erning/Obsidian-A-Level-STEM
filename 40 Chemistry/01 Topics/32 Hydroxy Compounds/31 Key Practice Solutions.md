# 32 Hydroxy Compounds - Key Practice Solutions

1. Equation:

$$
\ce{CH3COCl + CH3CH2OH -> CH3COOCH2CH3 + HCl}
$$

2. The by-product is hydrogen chloride, $\ce{HCl}$.

3. React phenylamine with $\ce{HNO2}$, or $\ce{NaNO2}$ and dilute acid, below $10\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to form a diazonium salt. Then warm the diazonium salt with water to form phenol.

4. With sodium hydroxide:

$$
\ce{C6H5OH + NaOH -> C6H5O-Na+ + H2O}
$$

With sodium:

$$
\ce{2C6H5OH + 2Na -> 2C6H5O-Na+ + H2}
$$

5. Phenol is more acidic than water and ethanol because phenoxide ion is stabilised by delocalisation into the aromatic ring. Ethoxide lacks this stabilisation and is destabilised by the electron-donating ethyl group. The order is phenol > water > ethanol.

6. Phenol reacts with dilute $\ce{HNO3(aq)}$ at room temperature to give a mixture of 2-nitrophenol and 4-nitrophenol.

7. Phenol reacts with $\ce{Br2(aq)}$ to form 2,4,6-tribromophenol.

8. The $\ce{-OH}$ group donates electron density into the aromatic ring, making it more reactive towards electrophiles than benzene. It also directs substitution to the 2-, 4- and 6-positions.

9. The $\ce{-OH}$ group directs to the 2-, 4- and 6-positions.

10. The key group is the azo group, $\ce{-N=N-}$. Phenol reacts with diazonium salts in $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ to form azo compounds.
