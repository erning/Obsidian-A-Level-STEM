# 32 Hydroxy Compounds

## Core Idea

A Level hydroxy compounds extend alcohol chemistry to reactions with acyl chlorides and introduce phenol as an aromatic hydroxy compound with distinctive acidity and ring reactivity.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 32 Hydroxy compounds
- 32.1 Alcohols
- 32.2 Phenol

## What to Learn

- Describe the reaction of alcohols with acyl chlorides to form esters.
- Recall how phenol can be produced through diazonium salt chemistry.
- Describe phenol reactions with bases, sodium, diazonium salts, dilute nitric acid, and bromine water.
- Explain the acidity of phenol and compare the acidities of water, phenol, and ethanol.
- Use the directing effect of the hydroxy group in phenolic compounds.

## How to Study

- Keep alcohol and phenol chemistry side by side, noting where the aromatic ring changes behaviour.
- Link phenol acidity to stabilisation of the phenoxide ion.
- Learn phenol nitration and bromination conditions separately from benzene conditions.
- Use positions 2, 4, and 6 consistently when predicting phenol substitution products.

## Practice Directions

- Predict ester products from alcohol or phenol reactions with acyl chlorides.
- Explain the relative acidities of ethanol, water, and phenol.
- Predict phenol products from bromination, nitration, and coupling reactions.
- Use phenol chemistry in short synthetic routes.

## Common Traps

- Treating phenol as if it were just another alcohol.
- Forgetting that phenol is acidic enough to react with alkali but ethanol is not.
- Using benzene nitration or bromination conditions for phenol.
- Ignoring the 2-, 4-, and 6-directing effect of the phenolic hydroxy group.
