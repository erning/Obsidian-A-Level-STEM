# 32 Hydroxy Compounds - Key Practice Problems

1. Write an equation for the reaction of ethanol with ethanoyl chloride.

2. State the by-product formed when an alcohol reacts with an acyl chloride.

3. Describe the two-stage conversion of phenylamine into phenol, including the temperature condition for the diazonium salt.

4. Write equations for phenol reacting with $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and with sodium metal.

5. Explain the relative acidities of phenol, water and ethanol.

6. State the reagents, conditions and products for nitration of phenol.

7. State the reagent and product for bromination of phenol.

8. Explain why phenol undergoes nitration and bromination under milder conditions than benzene.

9. State the ring positions directed by the $\ce{-OH}$ group in phenol.

10. Identify the key group in an azo compound and state how phenol can be used to form one.
