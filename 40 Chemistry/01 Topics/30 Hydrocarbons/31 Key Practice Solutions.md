# 30 Hydrocarbons - Key Practice Solutions

1. Addition would disrupt the delocalised aromatic $\pi$ system and remove aromatic stabilisation. Electrophilic substitution replaces a ring hydrogen and then restores the aromatic system.

2. Use $\ce{Br2}$ with $\ce{AlBr3}$ catalyst. Equation:

$$
\ce{C6H6 + Br2 ->[AlBr3] C6H5Br + HBr}
$$

3. Use concentrated $\ce{HNO3}$ and concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ at $25$ to $60\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$. The electrophile is $\ce{NO2+}$.

4. Use $\ce{CH3Cl}$, $\ce{AlCl3}$ and heat.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + CH3Cl ->[AlCl3][heat] C6H5CH3 + HCl}
$$

5. Use $\ce{CH3COCl}$, $\ce{AlCl3}$ and heat.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + CH3COCl ->[AlCl3][heat] C6H5COCH3 + HCl}
$$

6. The product is benzoic acid, $\ce{C6H5COOH}$.

7. Ring chlorination: $\ce{Cl2}$ with $\ce{AlCl3}$. Side-chain chlorination: $\ce{Cl2}$ with ultraviolet light.

8. $\ce{-OH}$, $\ce{-CH3}$ and $\ce{-NH2}$ direct mainly to 2-/4-positions. $\ce{-NO2}$, $\ce{-COOH}$ and $\ce{-COR}$ direct mainly to the 3-position.

9. $\ce{-COOH}$ is a 3-directing group, so nitration of benzoic acid gives mainly 3-nitrobenzoic acid.

10. Hydrogenation:

$$
\ce{C6H6 + 3H2 ->[Pt/Ni][heat] C6H12}
$$

Use Pt or Ni catalyst with heat.
