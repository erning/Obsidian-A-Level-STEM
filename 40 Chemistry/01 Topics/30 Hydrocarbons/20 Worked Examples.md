# 30 Hydrocarbons - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Nitration Conditions and Product

State the reagents, conditions and organic product for nitration of benzene.

Use concentrated $\ce{HNO3}$ and concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ at $25$ to $60\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$. The product is nitrobenzene.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + HNO3 ->[conc. H2SO4][25-60 ^\circ C] C6H5NO2 + H2O}
$$

Check: the reaction is electrophilic substitution, and the electrophile is $\ce{NO2+}$.

## Example 2: Bromination Mechanism Outline

Describe the electrophilic substitution mechanism for bromination of benzene.

The catalyst $\ce{AlBr3}$ helps generate a bromine electrophile. The benzene $\pi$ system attacks the electrophile to form a positively charged intermediate. A proton is removed from the carbon that bonded to bromine, and aromaticity is restored.

Overall:

$$
\ce{C6H6 + Br2 ->[AlBr3] C6H5Br + HBr}
$$

Check: the final product is substituted benzene, not an addition product.

## Example 3: Ring or Side-Chain Halogenation

Predict the main organic product when methylbenzene reacts with chlorine under ultraviolet light.

Ultraviolet light gives free-radical substitution in the alkyl side-chain, as in AS alkane chemistry. One side-chain hydrogen is replaced by chlorine.

Product: $\ce{C6H5CH2Cl}$.

$$
\ce{C6H5CH3 + Cl2 ->[uv] C6H5CH2Cl + HCl}
$$

Check: if $\ce{AlCl3}$ were present instead, ring substitution would be expected.

## Example 4: Directing Effect

Predict the main ring positions attacked when phenol undergoes electrophilic substitution.

The $\ce{-OH}$ group directs electrophilic substitution to the 2-, 4- and 6-positions. Therefore phenol tends to form 2-substituted and 4-substituted products first, and under stronger or excess reagent conditions can form 2,4,6-substituted products.

Check: $\ce{-OH}$ is one of the CAIE 2,4-directing groups.

## Example 5: Side-Chain Oxidation

Give the reagents and product for converting methylbenzene into benzoic acid.

Use hot alkaline $\ce{KMnO4}$, then add dilute acid. The side-chain is oxidised to $\ce{-COOH}$.

$$
\ce{C6H5CH3 ->[hot alkaline KMnO4][then dilute acid] C6H5COOH}
$$

Check: this reaction changes the side-chain, not the aromatic ring.

## Example 6: Friedel-Crafts Acylation

Write the equation for the acylation of benzene using ethanoyl chloride.

Use $\ce{CH3COCl}$, $\ce{AlCl3}$ and heat. The acyl group $\ce{CH3CO-}$ enters the ring.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + CH3COCl ->[AlCl3][heat] C6H5COCH3 + HCl}
$$

Check: the product contains $\ce{-COCH3}$ attached to the ring, not $\ce{-CH2CH3}$.
