# 30 Hydrocarbons - Key Practice Problems

1. Explain why benzene usually undergoes electrophilic substitution rather than electrophilic addition.

2. State the reagents and conditions for bromination of benzene and write the overall equation.

3. State the reagents, temperature range and electrophile for nitration of benzene.

4. Give the reagents and conditions for converting benzene into methylbenzene by Friedel-Crafts alkylation.

5. Give the reagents and conditions for converting benzene into $\ce{C6H5COCH3}$.

6. Predict the product of treating methylbenzene with hot alkaline $\ce{KMnO4}$ followed by dilute acid.

7. For methylbenzene, state the conditions that favour ring chlorination and the conditions that favour side-chain chlorination.

8. For each substituent, state whether it directs electrophilic substitution mainly to the 2-/4-positions or the 3-position: $\ce{-OH}$, $\ce{-NO2}$, $\ce{-CH3}$, $\ce{-COOH}$, $\ce{-NH2}$, $\ce{-COR}$.

9. Predict the main product position when benzoic acid is nitrated.

10. Write an equation for hydrogenation of benzene and state the catalyst.
