# 33 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives

## Core Idea

A Level carboxylic acid chemistry centres on benzoic acid, acyl chlorides, esters, acidity, oxidation, and addition-elimination reactions. Acyl chlorides are especially important because their high reactivity opens routes to esters and amides.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.

- 33.2 Esters
- 33.3 Acyl chlorides

## What to Learn

- Recall how benzoic acid is produced from an alkylbenzene side-chain.
- Describe formation of acyl chlorides from carboxylic acids.
- Recognise carboxylic acids that can be further oxidised.
- Compare acidities of carboxylic acids, phenols, alcohols, and chlorine-substituted carboxylic acids.
- Describe acyl chloride reactions and their addition-elimination mechanism.

## How to Study

- Treat acyl chlorides as a hub: water, alcohols, phenol, ammonia, and amines all give different products.
- Keep reagent, condition, product, and by-product together for each reaction.
- Use electron-withdrawing effects to explain acidity trends.
- Compare hydrolysis of acyl chlorides, alkyl chlorides, and halogenoarenes carefully.

## Practice Directions

- Choose reagents to convert carboxylic acids into acyl chlorides.
- Predict products of acyl chloride reactions with water, alcohols, phenol, ammonia, and amines.
- Write addition-elimination mechanisms for acyl chloride reactions.
- Explain relative acidity using structure and substituent effects.

## Common Traps

- Confusing ester formation from acyl chlorides with acid-catalysed esterification.
- Forgetting HCl as a by-product in acyl chloride reactions.
- Treating addition-elimination as the same mechanism as halogenoalkane substitution.
- Explaining acid strength trends without considering substituent effects.
