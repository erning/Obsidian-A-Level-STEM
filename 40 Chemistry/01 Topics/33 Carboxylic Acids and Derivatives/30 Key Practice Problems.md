# 33 Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives - Key Practice Problems

1. State the reagents and conditions for converting methylbenzene into benzoic acid.

2. Give three reagents that can convert a carboxylic acid into an acyl chloride.

3. Write an equation for converting ethanoic acid into ethanoyl chloride using $\ce{SOCl2}$.

4. State the oxidising reagents that can oxidise methanoic acid, and give the products.

5. State the reagent that oxidises ethanedioic acid and give the product.

6. Explain the relative acidities of carboxylic acids, phenols and alcohols.

7. Explain why trichloroethanoic acid is stronger than chloroethanoic acid.

8. Write equations for ethanoyl chloride reacting with water, ethanol, ammonia and methylamine.

9. Describe the addition-elimination mechanism for acyl chloride reactions.

10. Explain the relative ease of hydrolysis of ethanoyl chloride, chloroethane and chlorobenzene.
