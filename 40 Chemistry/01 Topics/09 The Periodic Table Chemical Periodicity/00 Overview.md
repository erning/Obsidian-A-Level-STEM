# 09 The Periodic Table: Chemical Periodicity

## Core Idea

Chemical periodicity links repeated patterns in electronic structure to trends in physical properties, reactions, bonding and acid-base behaviour. In this AS topic, Period 3 is the main example: the elements move from metallic structures through giant covalent silicon to simple molecular or atomic substances, and their oxides and chlorides change from mainly ionic to mainly covalent.

## What to Learn

- Describe Period 3 trends in atomic radius, ionic radius, melting point and electrical conductivity.
- Explain melting point and electrical conductivity using structure and bonding.
- Describe reactions of selected Period 3 elements with oxygen, chlorine and water.
- State and explain oxidation numbers in the named Period 3 oxides and chlorides.
- Describe reactions of the specified oxides and chlorides with water, including likely pH.
- Explain acid-base and amphoteric behaviour of named oxides and hydroxides.
- Use periodicity to predict properties and identify unknown elements from data.

## How to Study

- Keep the Period 3 sequence visible: $\ce{Na, Mg, Al, Si, P, S, Cl, Ar}$.
- For every physical trend, name the structure first, then explain the forces or charge carriers.
- For every chemical trend, connect bonding and electronegativity to the observed reaction or pH.
- Learn equations as evidence for explanations, not as isolated facts.

## Common Traps

- Describing a trend without explaining the cause.
- Treating Period 3 as if all elements have the same structure.
- Forgetting that ionic radius has separate cation and anion sequences.
- Confusing oxide acidity with the pH of unrelated solutions.
