# The Periodic Table: Chemical Periodicity - Key Practice Problems

Keep answers out of this file during active recall. Use [31 Key Practice Solutions](31%20Key%20Practice%20Solutions.md) only after attempting the questions.

## Questions

1. Describe and explain the trend in atomic radius from sodium to chlorine in Period 3.

2. Explain why $\ce{Na+}$, $\ce{Mg^{2+}}$ and $\ce{Al^{3+}}$ decrease in ionic radius in that order.

3. Explain the melting point trend from $\ce{Na}$ to $\ce{Si}$, then from $\ce{Si}$ to $\ce{Ar}$.

4. Describe the electrical conductivity of $\ce{Na}$, $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Al}$, $\ce{Si}$, $\ce{P}$, $\ce{S}$, $\ce{Cl}$ and $\ce{Ar}$ in terms of mobile charged particles.

5. Write balanced equations for the reactions of $\ce{Na}$, $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Al}$, $\ce{P}$ and $\ce{S}$ with oxygen to form the syllabus-specified products.

6. Write balanced equations for the reactions of $\ce{Na}$, $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Al}$, $\ce{Si}$ and $\ce{P}$ with chlorine to form the syllabus-specified products.

7. Write equations for the reactions of sodium with cold water, magnesium with cold water and magnesium with steam. Include expected alkalinity where appropriate.

8. State the oxidation number of the Period 3 element in each compound: $\ce{Na2O}$, $\ce{MgO}$, $\ce{Al2O3}$, $\ce{P4O10}$, $\ce{SO2}$, $\ce{SO3}$, $\ce{SiCl4}$ and $\ce{PCl5}$.

9. For $\ce{Na2O}$, $\ce{MgO}$, $\ce{Al2O3}$, $\ce{P4O10}$, $\ce{SO2}$ and $\ce{SO3}$, classify the oxide as basic, amphoteric or acidic. Include one equation for each type of behaviour.

10. Predict the reaction with water and likely pH for $\ce{NaCl}$, $\ce{MgCl2}$, $\ce{AlCl3}$, $\ce{SiCl4}$ and $\ce{PCl5}$.

11. An unknown Period 3 oxide reacts with both hydrochloric acid and aqueous sodium hydroxide. Suggest the oxide and write equations for both reactions.

12. An unknown Period 3 element forms a covalent chloride that fumes in moist air and gives a strongly acidic solution. Explain what this suggests about the element's position in Period 3.
