# 17 Carbonyl Compounds - Key Practice Solutions

1. Classifications:
   - $\ce{CH3CHO}$: aldehyde.
   - $\ce{CH3COCH3}$: ketone.
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CHO}$: aldehyde.
   - $\ce{CH3COCH2CH3}$: ketone.

2. Oxidise ethanol using acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ or acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ and distillation.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2OH + [O] -> CH3CHO + H2O}
$$

3. Butan-2-ol oxidises to butanone:

$$
\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH2CH3 + [O] -> CH3COCH2CH3 + H2O}
$$

4. Reduction products:
   - Propanal, $\ce{CH3CH2CHO}$, gives propan-1-ol, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2OH}$.
   - Propanone, $\ce{CH3COCH3}$, gives propan-2-ol, $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3}$.

5. Ethanal forms 2-hydroxypropanenitrile:

$$
\ce{CH3CHO + HCN -> CH3CH(OH)CN}
$$

6. $\ce{CN-}$ attacks the electron-deficient carbonyl carbon. The first curly arrow starts at a lone pair on carbon in $\ce{CN-}$ and points to the carbonyl carbon. The second curly arrow starts at the $\ce{C=O}$ $\pi$ bond and points to oxygen. An alkoxide intermediate forms, then it is protonated by $\ce{HCN}$ to form the hydroxynitrile.

7. 2,4-DNPH gives an orange precipitate with aldehydes and ketones.

8. Observations:
   - With Fehling's reagent, ethanal gives a brick-red precipitate; propanone gives no change.
   - With Tollens' reagent, ethanal gives a silver mirror or grey silver precipitate; propanone gives no change.

9. Aldehydes are readily oxidised to carboxylic acids and reduce Fehling's and Tollens' reagents. Ketones are not oxidised under these simple conditions, so they do not reduce the reagents.

10. Positive tri-iodomethane tests:
    - Ethanal: positive, because it can be written $\ce{CH3CO-H}$.
    - Propanone: positive, because it contains $\ce{CH3CO-R}$.
    - Pentan-3-one: negative, because it does not contain $\ce{CH3CO-R}$.
    - Pentan-2-one: positive, because it contains $\ce{CH3CO-R}$.
