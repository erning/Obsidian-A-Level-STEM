# 17 Carbonyl Compounds

## Core Idea

Carbonyl compounds introduce aldehydes and ketones, their preparation from alcohols, their reduction, nucleophilic addition and diagnostic tests.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 17 Carbonyl compounds.
- 17.1 Aldehydes and ketones.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions; qualitative tests can support practical contexts.

## What to Learn

- Recall production of aldehydes and ketones by oxidation of primary and secondary alcohols.
- Describe reduction of aldehydes and ketones to alcohols.
- Describe nucleophilic addition of hydrogen cyanide to form hydroxynitriles.
- Use 2,4-DNPH to detect carbonyl compounds.
- Distinguish aldehydes and ketones using Fehling's and Tollens' reagents, ease of oxidation and specified tri-iodomethane reactions.

## How to Study

- Draw the carbonyl carbon as the reaction centre and mark its polarity.
- Compare aldehydes and ketones by structure before comparing test results.
- Treat tests as evidence chains: reagent, observation and conclusion.

## Practice Directions

- Predict products of carbonyl reduction and cyanide addition.
- Draw the nucleophilic addition mechanism for simple aldehydes and ketones.
- Interpret diagnostic test observations for unknown carbonyl compounds.

## Common Traps

- Assuming aldehydes and ketones give the same oxidation test results.
- Forgetting that 2,4-DNPH detects a carbonyl group but does not by itself distinguish aldehyde from ketone.
- Drawing cyanide addition without showing the electron-rich nucleophile attacking the carbonyl carbon.
