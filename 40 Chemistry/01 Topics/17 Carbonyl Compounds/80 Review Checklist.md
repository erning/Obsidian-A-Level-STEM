# 17 Carbonyl Compounds - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Recognise the carbonyl group, $\ce{C=O}$.
- Distinguish aldehydes, $\ce{RCHO}$, from ketones, $\ce{RCO R'}$.
- Recall oxidation routes from primary and secondary alcohols.
- Recall reduction reagents $\ce{NaBH4}$ and $\ce{LiAlH4}$.
- Recall $\ce{HCN}$ addition conditions: $\ce{HCN}$, $\ce{KCN}$ catalyst and heat.
- State tests and observations for 2,4-DNPH, Fehling's reagent, Tollens' reagent and alkaline $\ce{I2(aq)}$.

## Skills

- Predict aldehydes from primary alcohol oxidation with distillation.
- Predict ketones from secondary alcohol oxidation.
- Predict alcohols from aldehyde or ketone reduction.
- Draw or describe nucleophilic addition of $\ce{CN-}$ to a carbonyl compound.
- Interpret carbonyl test observations as an evidence chain.
- Identify the $\ce{CH3CO-R}$ group for tri-iodomethane reactions.

## Common Errors

- Using reflux when the intended product from a primary alcohol is an aldehyde.
- Assuming 2,4-DNPH distinguishes aldehydes from ketones.
- Making $\ce{CN-}$ attack oxygen instead of the carbonyl carbon.
- Forgetting that ethanal gives a positive tri-iodomethane test.
- Calling ketones easy to oxidise under Fehling's or Tollens' conditions.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can move both ways between alcohols and carbonyl compounds.
- You can explain carbonyl reactivity from the polar $\ce{C=O}$ bond.
- You can identify an unknown as aldehyde or ketone from test observations.
- You can use hydroxynitrile formation as a carbon-carbon bond-forming reaction.
