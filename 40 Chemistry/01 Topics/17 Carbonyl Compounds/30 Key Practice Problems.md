# 17 Carbonyl Compounds - Key Practice Problems

1. Classify each compound as an aldehyde or ketone:
   - $\ce{CH3CHO}$
   - $\ce{CH3COCH3}$
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CHO}$
   - $\ce{CH3COCH2CH3}$

2. State reagents and conditions for preparing ethanal from ethanol.

3. State the product when butan-2-ol is oxidised with acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$.

4. Give the alcohol formed when each compound is reduced with $\ce{NaBH4}$:
   - propanal
   - propanone

5. Give the product of ethanal reacting with $\ce{HCN}$ using $\ce{KCN}$ as catalyst and heat.

6. Describe the nucleophilic addition mechanism of $\ce{HCN}$ with a carbonyl compound, including where the first two curly arrows start.

7. State the observation with 2,4-DNPH reagent for an aldehyde or ketone.

8. State the observations when ethanal and propanone are separately warmed with:
   - Fehling's reagent
   - Tollens' reagent

9. Explain why Fehling's and Tollens' reagents distinguish aldehydes from ketones.

10. Identify which compounds give a positive tri-iodomethane test:
    - ethanal
    - propanone
    - pentan-3-one
    - pentan-2-one
