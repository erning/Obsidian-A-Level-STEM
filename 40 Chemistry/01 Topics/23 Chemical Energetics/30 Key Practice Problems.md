# 23 Chemical Energetics - Key Practice Problems

Answers are intentionally excluded. Use [[40 Chemistry/01 Topics/23 Chemical Energetics/31 Key Practice Solutions|Key Practice Solutions]] after attempting the questions.

## 1. Lattice Energy From A Born-Haber Cycle

For $\ce{LiF(s)}$, use the following data in $\text{kJ mol}^{-1}$:

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---:|
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{f}}(\ce{LiF})$ | -617 |
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{at}}(\ce{Li})$ | +161 |
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{at}}(\ce{F})$ | +79 |
| $\text{IE}_1(\ce{Li})$ | +520 |
| $\text{EA}_1(\ce{F})$ | -328 |

Calculate $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{latt}}$ for:

$$
\ce{Li+(g) + F-(g) -> LiF(s)}
$$

## 2. Born-Haber Cycle With A 2+ Cation

For $\ce{MgCl2(s)}$, explain which enthalpy steps are required in a Born-Haber cycle from $\ce{Mg(s)}$ and $\ce{Cl2(g)}$. Then state why both $\text{IE}_1$ and $\text{IE}_2$ of magnesium must be included.

## 3. Enthalpy Change Of Solution

For $\ce{NaBr(s)}$:

| Quantity | Value / $\text{kJ mol}^{-1}$ |
|---|---:|
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{latt}}(\ce{NaBr})$ | -742 |
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{hyd}}(\ce{Na+})$ | -406 |
| $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{hyd}}(\ce{Br-})$ | -336 |

Calculate $\Delta H^\circ_{\text{sol}}$ for:

$$
\ce{NaBr(s) -> Na+(aq) + Br-(aq)}
$$

## 4. Interpreting Hydration Enthalpy

Explain why $\ce{Al^{3+}}$ has a more exothermic hydration enthalpy than $\ce{Na+}$. Your answer should use charge, radius, and charge density.

## 5. Predicting Entropy Signs

Predict the sign of $\Delta S$ for each process and briefly explain your choice.

(a)

$$
\ce{H2O(l) -> H2O(g)}
$$

(b)

$$
\ce{2SO2(g) + O2(g) -> 2SO3(g)}
$$

(c)

$$
\ce{NH4NO3(s) -> NH4+(aq) + NO3-(aq)}
$$

## 6. Calculating Entropy Change

Use the data to calculate $\Delta S^\circ$ for:

$$
\ce{2CO(g) + O2(g) -> 2CO2(g)}
$$

| Species | $S^\circ$ / $\text{J mol}^{-1}\text{ K}^{-1}$ |
|---|---:|
| $\ce{CO(g)}$ | 198 |
| $\ce{O2(g)}$ | 205 |
| $\ce{CO2(g)}$ | 214 |

## 7. Gibbs Free Energy At A Given Temperature

For a reaction, $\Delta H^\circ = -74.8\ \text{kJ mol}^{-1}$ and $\Delta S^\circ = -80.7\ \text{J mol}^{-1}\text{ K}^{-1}$. Calculate $\Delta G^\circ$ at $298\ \text{K}$ and state whether the reaction is feasible under standard conditions.

## 8. Temperature And Feasibility

A process has $\Delta H^\circ = +112\ \text{kJ mol}^{-1}$ and $\Delta S^\circ = +250\ \text{J mol}^{-1}\text{ K}^{-1}$.

(a) Calculate the temperature at which $\Delta G^\circ = 0$.

(b) State whether the process is more likely to be feasible above or below this temperature.
