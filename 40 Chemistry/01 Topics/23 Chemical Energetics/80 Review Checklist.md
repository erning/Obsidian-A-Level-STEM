# 23 Chemical Energetics - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Define enthalpy change of atomisation, lattice energy, first electron affinity, enthalpy change of hydration, and enthalpy change of solution.
- State the syllabus convention for lattice energy: gaseous ions forming one mole of solid lattice.
- Describe the factors affecting first electron affinity: nuclear charge, radius, shielding, and electron-electron repulsion.
- Define entropy as the number of possible arrangements of particles and their energy in a system.
- State and use $\Delta G^\circ = \Delta H^\circ - T\Delta S^\circ$.

## Perform

- Construct Born-Haber cycles for ionic solids with +1, +2, -1, and -2 ions.
- Include all required ionisation energies and electron affinities.
- Calculate lattice energy from formation enthalpy and the other cycle data.
- Construct solution cycles using lattice energy and hydration enthalpies.
- Calculate $\Delta S^\circ$ from standard entropy data.
- Convert entropy units correctly before calculating $\Delta G^\circ$.
- Predict how temperature affects feasibility when given $\Delta H^\circ$ and $\Delta S^\circ$.

## Explain

- Larger ionic charges and smaller ionic radii increase the numerical magnitude of lattice energy.
- Higher charge density makes hydration enthalpy more exothermic.
- More gaseous particles, changes from solid to liquid to gas, and higher temperature usually increase entropy.
- Negative $\Delta G^\circ$ means thermodynamic feasibility under stated conditions, not a high reaction rate.

## Avoid

- Do not mix lattice formation and lattice dissociation signs.
- Do not omit state symbols from cycle equations.
- Do not forget the second electron affinity for forming a 2- ion.
- Do not mix $\text{J mol}^{-1}\text{ K}^{-1}$ and $\text{kJ mol}^{-1}$ in Gibbs calculations.
- Do not claim a reaction is fast because it is feasible.

## Ready To Move On

You are ready when you can draw a Born-Haber cycle without prompts, explain each arrow in words, calculate a missing enthalpy term, and use entropy and Gibbs data to make a justified feasibility prediction.
