# 23 Chemical Energetics

## Core Idea

A Level chemical energetics extends AS enthalpy work into ionic energy cycles, entropy, and Gibbs free energy. The topic asks whether energy changes can be represented quantitatively, and whether a reaction or process is thermodynamically feasible.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 23 Chemical energetics
- 23.1 Lattice energy and Born-Haber cycles
- 23.2 Enthalpies of solution and hydration
- 23.3 Entropy change, $\Delta S$
- 23.4 Gibbs free energy change, $\Delta G$

## What to Learn

- Use enthalpy terms for atomisation, lattice energy, solution, and hydration.
- Construct and use Born-Haber cycles and solution energy cycles.
- Explain how ionic charge and ionic radius affect lattice energy and enthalpy change of hydration.
- Predict and calculate entropy changes using standard entropy data.
- Use $\Delta G^\circ = \Delta H^\circ - T\Delta S^\circ$ to judge feasibility and the effect of temperature.

## How to Study

- Draw every energy cycle before calculating; label states, signs, and units.
- Keep enthalpy data in kJ mol^-1 and entropy data in J mol^-1 K^-1 until the final substitution.
- Connect numerical answers to particle-level ideas: charge density, ionic radius, disorder, and number of gaseous particles.
- Treat feasibility as a thermodynamic statement, not as a prediction that a reaction is fast.

## Practice Directions

- Complete Born-Haber cycles from incomplete enthalpy data.
- Use lattice energy, hydration enthalpy, and solution enthalpy in the same cycle.
- Calculate $\Delta S^\circ$ and $\Delta G^\circ$ from data tables.
- Predict how changing temperature changes feasibility for a named reaction.

## Common Traps

- Using the wrong sign convention for lattice energy.
- Mixing kJ mol^-1 and J mol^-1 K^-1 in the Gibbs equation.
- Forgetting state symbols when constructing cycles.
- Treating a negative $\Delta G$ as proof that the reaction has a high rate.
