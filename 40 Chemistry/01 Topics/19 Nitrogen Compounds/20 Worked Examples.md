# 19 Nitrogen Compounds - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Making a Primary Amine

**Question.** State the reagent and conditions for converting 1-bromopropane into propylamine. Write an equation.

**Working.** Primary amines are produced by reaction of a halogenoalkane with ammonia in ethanol heated under pressure.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br + 2NH3 -> CH3CH2CH2NH2 + NH4Br}
$$

**Answer.** Use $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol, heat under pressure. The product is propylamine, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2NH2}$.

**Check.** The carbon chain length is unchanged because ammonia introduces nitrogen but not carbon.

## Example 2: Making a Nitrile and Counting Carbons

**Question.** Bromoethane reacts with potassium cyanide in ethanol and heat. Name the organic product.

**Working.** Cyanide substitutes for bromine:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br + KCN -> CH3CH2CN + KBr}
$$

The product has three carbon atoms because the nitrile carbon is counted.

**Answer.** Propanenitrile.

**Check.** Do not call $\ce{CH3CH2CN}$ ethanenitrile; the $\ce{CN}$ carbon is part of the chain.

## Example 3: Nitrile Hydrolysis to a Carboxylic Acid

**Question.** Propanenitrile is heated with dilute sodium hydroxide and then acidified. Give the final organic product.

**Working.** Hydrolysis of a nitrile gives a carboxylate under alkaline conditions. Acidification converts the carboxylate into the carboxylic acid.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CN ->[dilute\ alkali,\ heat] CH3CH2COO- ->[H+] CH3CH2COOH}
$$

**Answer.** Propanoic acid.

**Check.** The carbon count does not change during hydrolysis; propanenitrile has three carbons and gives propanoic acid with three carbons.

## Example 4: Forming a Hydroxynitrile from an Aldehyde

**Question.** Ethanal reacts with $\ce{HCN}$ in the presence of $\ce{KCN}$ and heat. Draw the organic product in condensed structural form.

**Working.** $\ce{HCN}$ adds across the carbonyl group. The carbonyl oxygen becomes $\ce{-OH}$, and $\ce{CN}$ attaches to the former carbonyl carbon.

$$
\ce{CH3CHO + HCN -> CH3CH(OH)CN}
$$

**Answer.** $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CN}$.

**Check.** The product contains both $\ce{-OH}$ and $\ce{-C#N}$.

## Example 5: Choosing a Synthetic Route

**Question.** Suggest a two-step route from 1-chlorobutane to pentanoic acid.

**Working.** The target has one more carbon than the starting halogenoalkane. Use cyanide substitution, then hydrolyse the nitrile.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2Cl ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2CH2CN}
$$

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2CN ->[dilute\ acid,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2CH2COOH}
$$

**Answer.** Step 1: $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat to form pentanenitrile. Step 2: hydrolysis with dilute acid and heat, or dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification, to form pentanoic acid.

**Check.** The nitrile intermediate and the acid product both have five carbons.

## Example 6: Interpreting Infrared Evidence

**Question.** A compound has a strong absorption around $2220\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ in its infrared spectrum. It was made from a halogenoalkane and $\ce{KCN}$. What functional group is strongly suggested?

**Working.** The syllabus data section gives $\ce{C#N}$ absorption around $2200-2250\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$. The preparation route from a halogenoalkane and $\ce{KCN}$ also points to a nitrile.

**Answer.** A nitrile group, $\ce{-C#N}$.

**Check.** The route evidence and the infrared evidence agree.
