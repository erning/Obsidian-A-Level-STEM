# 19 Nitrogen Compounds - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. Use $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol, heated under pressure.

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2Cl + 2NH3 -> CH3CH2NH2 + NH4Cl}
   $$

2. The product is butanenitrile:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CH2Br + KCN -> CH3CH2CH2CN + KBr}
   $$

   The starting halogenoalkane has three carbons. The $\ce{CN}$ group adds one carbon, so the nitrile has four carbons.

3. First make the nitrile:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2Br ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CN}
   $$

   Then hydrolyse the nitrile:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CN ->[dilute\ acid,\ heat] CH3CH2COOH}
   $$

   Dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification is also acceptable for the hydrolysis step.

4. The product is $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CN}$. It contains a hydroxy group, $\ce{-OH}$, and a nitrile group, $\ce{-C#N}$.

5. The product is $\ce{(CH3)2C(OH)CN}$.

   $$
   \ce{CH3COCH3 + HCN -> (CH3)2C(OH)CN}
   $$

6. The final organic product is butanoic acid. Alkaline hydrolysis first forms a carboxylate ion or salt. Acidification supplies $\ce{H+}$ to give the carboxylic acid:

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CH2COO- + H+ -> CH3CH2CH2COOH}
   $$

7. The absorption near $2220\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ suggests a nitrile group, $\ce{-C#N}$. The likely reagent was $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol with heat.

8. $\ce{NH3}$ introduces nitrogen but no carbon, so the carbon skeleton is unchanged. $\ce{KCN}$ introduces a cyanide group whose carbon becomes part of the organic molecule, so the carbon chain increases by one.

9. Use $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol, heated under pressure. The reaction type is nucleophilic substitution.

   $$
   \ce{CH3CH2CH2Cl + 2NH3 -> CH3CH2CH2NH2 + NH4Cl}
   $$

10. You should know how primary amines are produced from halogenoalkanes using $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol heated under pressure, recognise the $\ce{-NH2}$ group in the product, and use that transformation in AS synthesis routes.
