# 19 Nitrogen Compounds

## Core Idea

AS nitrogen compounds focus on primary amines, nitriles and hydroxynitriles as products and intermediates in organic synthesis.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 19 Nitrogen compounds.
- 19.1 Primary amines.
- 19.2 Nitriles and hydroxynitriles.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Recall production of primary amines from halogenoalkanes and ammonia in ethanol under pressure.
- Recall production of nitriles from halogenoalkanes and potassium cyanide in ethanol with heat.
- Recall production of hydroxynitriles from aldehydes or ketones with hydrogen cyanide and cyanide catalyst.
- Describe hydrolysis of nitriles to carboxylic acids after acidification where required.
- Use nitrogen-containing compounds in multi-step organic routes.

## How to Study

- Track whether a reaction adds nitrogen, adds carbon, or both.
- Keep amines, nitriles and hydroxynitriles in separate boxes on the reaction map.
- Link each nitrogen compound back to its usual precursor.

## Practice Directions

- Predict products from halogenoalkanes with ammonia or cyanide.
- Convert nitriles into carboxylic acids using suitable conditions.
- Identify routes that introduce a nitrile or hydroxynitrile functional group.

## Common Traps

- Forgetting that cyanide substitution increases the carbon chain length.
- Treating amine classification as an AS requirement when the syllabus limits this topic to primary amines.
- Confusing nitriles with amines because both contain nitrogen.
