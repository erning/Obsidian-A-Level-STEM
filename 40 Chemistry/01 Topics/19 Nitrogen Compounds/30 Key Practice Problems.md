# 19 Nitrogen Compounds - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. State the reagent and conditions needed to convert chloroethane into ethylamine. Write an equation.

2. 1-Bromopropane reacts with $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat. Name the organic product and explain the carbon count.

3. Write a two-step route from 1-bromoethane to propanoic acid. Include reagents and conditions.

4. Ethanal reacts with $\ce{HCN}$ in the presence of $\ce{KCN}$ and heat. Draw the organic product and name the two functional groups present.

5. Propanone reacts with $\ce{HCN}$ in the presence of $\ce{KCN}$ and heat. Draw the organic product in condensed structural form.

6. Butanenitrile is hydrolysed with dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification. Name the final organic product and explain why acidification is required.

7. A compound has an infrared absorption near $2220\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and was produced from a halogenoalkane by nucleophilic substitution. Suggest the functional group present and the reagent likely used.

8. Explain why using $\ce{NH3}$ and using $\ce{KCN}$ with a halogenoalkane give different changes to the carbon skeleton.

9. A synthesis requires converting $\ce{CH3CH2CH2Cl}$ into $\ce{CH3CH2CH2NH2}$. Give the reagent, conditions, and reaction type.

10. The syllabus says classification of amines will not be tested at AS Level. What should you still know about primary amines in this topic?
