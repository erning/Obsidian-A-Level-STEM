# 19 Nitrogen Compounds - Review Checklist

## Recall

- I can state that primary amines are produced from halogenoalkanes and $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol heated under pressure.
- I can state that nitriles are produced from halogenoalkanes and $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol with heat.
- I can state that hydroxynitriles are produced from aldehydes or ketones with $\ce{HCN}$, $\ce{KCN}$ as catalyst, and heat.
- I can state that nitriles hydrolyse with dilute acid, or with dilute alkali followed by acidification, to produce carboxylic acids.
- I know that classification of amines will not be tested at AS Level.

## Structures and Carbon Counting

- I can recognise $\ce{-NH2}$ as the primary amine group used in this topic.
- I can recognise $\ce{-C#N}$ as the nitrile group.
- I can count the nitrile carbon as part of the carbon chain.
- I can explain why $\ce{KCN}$ substitution increases the carbon chain length by one.
- I can draw simple hydroxynitriles from aldehydes and ketones.

## Reagents and Conditions

- I can distinguish $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol heated under pressure from $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat.
- I can include dilute acid or dilute alkali followed by acidification for nitrile hydrolysis.
- I can include $\ce{HCN}$, $\ce{KCN}$ catalyst, and heat for hydroxynitrile formation.

## Synthesis Skills

- I can plan halogenoalkane $\to$ primary amine routes.
- I can plan halogenoalkane $\to$ nitrile $\to$ carboxylic acid routes.
- I can plan aldehyde or ketone $\to$ hydroxynitrile routes.
- I can explain whether a step changes carbon count.

## Analysis Skills

- I can use an infrared absorption around $2200-2250\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ as evidence for a nitrile group.
- I can use $\ce{N-H}$ absorption as possible evidence for an amine group when supported by structure and route.
- I can combine infrared evidence with the reaction pathway instead of using one clue in isolation.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Do not forget the extra carbon introduced by $\ce{CN-}$.
- Do not leave out ethanol and heat for $\ce{KCN}$ substitution.
- Do not leave out pressure for the ammonia route to primary amines.
- Do not forget acidification after alkaline nitrile hydrolysis.
- Do not confuse nitriles with amines simply because both contain nitrogen.

## Ready for the Next Topic When

- I can use nitrogen compounds as deliberate intermediates in AS organic synthesis.
- I can explain why nitriles connect halogenoalkanes to longer carboxylic acids.
- I can interpret basic nitrile and amine spectral evidence in simple structure questions.
