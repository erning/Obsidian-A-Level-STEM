# Atoms, Molecules and Stoichiometry - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. Define the unified atomic mass unit.

2. Calculate $M_r$ or relative formula mass for:
   - $\ce{H2SO4}$
   - $\ce{Ca(OH)2}$
   - $\ce{Al2(SO4)3}$

3. Magnesium has isotopes $^{24}\ce{Mg}$, $^{25}\ce{Mg}$ and $^{26}\ce{Mg}$ with abundances $79.0\%$, $10.0\%$ and $11.0\%$. Calculate $A_r$.

4. Write formulae for:
   - ammonium sulfate
   - zinc nitrate
   - calcium phosphate
   - copper(II) carbonate

5. Balance the equation and add state symbols for aqueous barium chloride reacting with aqueous sodium sulfate to form solid barium sulfate and aqueous sodium chloride.

6. Write the ionic equation for the precipitation reaction in Problem 5.

7. A compound contains $52.2\%$ carbon, $13.0\%$ hydrogen and $34.8\%$ oxygen by mass. Find its empirical formula.

8. A compound has empirical formula $\ce{CH2}$ and $M_r = 70.0$. Find its molecular formula.

9. $4.95\ \mathrm{g}$ of hydrated copper(II) sulfate, $\ce{CuSO4} \cdot x\ce{H2O}$, leaves $3.19\ \mathrm{g}$ of anhydrous $\ce{CuSO4}$ after heating. Find $x$.

10. Calculate the mass of $\ce{CO2}$ formed when $5.00\ \mathrm{g}$ of $\ce{CaCO3}$ reacts completely with acid.

11. $25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of $0.200\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ $\ce{H2SO4}$ is neutralised by $\ce{NaOH}$:

   $$
   \ce{H2SO4(aq) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2SO4(aq) + 2H2O(l)}
   $$

   Calculate the moles of $\ce{NaOH}$ required.

12. $30.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of ethene burns completely:

   $$
   \ce{C2H4(g) + 3O2(g) -> 2CO2(g) + 2H2O(g)}
   $$

   Calculate the volumes of oxygen used and carbon dioxide formed at the same temperature and pressure.

13. $0.250\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{Mg}$ reacts with $0.180\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{O2}$:

   $$
   \ce{2Mg + O2 -> 2MgO}
   $$

   Identify the limiting reagent and calculate the amount of $\ce{MgO}$ formed.
