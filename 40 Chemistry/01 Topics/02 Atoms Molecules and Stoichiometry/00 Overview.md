# 02 Atoms, Molecules and Stoichiometry

## Core Idea

Stoichiometry turns chemical formulas and equations into measurable amounts. The mole links particle count, mass, gas volume and solution concentration so that reactions can be treated quantitatively.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.

- 2.1 Relative masses of atoms and molecules.
- 2.2 The mole and the Avogadro constant.
- 2.3 Formulas.
- 2.4 Reacting masses and volumes (of solutions and gases).
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions; calculations also support Paper 3 practical work.

## What to Learn

- Use relative atomic mass, relative isotopic mass, relative molecular mass and relative formula mass.
- Use the mole and the Avogadro constant as the bridge between particles and amount of substance.
- Write formulae and balanced equations, including ionic equations and state symbols.
- Determine empirical and molecular formulae from data.
- Calculate reacting masses, gas volumes, solution concentrations, percentage yield, limiting reagent and excess reagent.

## How to Study

- Start every calculation by writing the balanced equation and identifying the mole ratio.
- Keep units visible, especially for concentration, volume and amount of substance.
- Round only at the end, using the significant figures justified by the data.

## Practice Directions

- Convert between mass, moles, particles, gas volume and concentration.
- Balance molecular and ionic equations with correct state symbols.
- Solve limiting reagent and percentage yield problems from experimental-style data.

## Common Traps

- Using formula ratios from an unbalanced equation.
- Mixing $cm^3$ and $dm^3$ in concentration calculations.
- Reporting more significant figures than the data supports.
