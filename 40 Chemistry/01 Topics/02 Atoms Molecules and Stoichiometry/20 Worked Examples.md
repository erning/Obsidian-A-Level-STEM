# Atoms, Molecules and Stoichiometry - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Relative Atomic Mass from Isotopic Abundance

Chlorine has two isotopes: $^{35}\ce{Cl}$ with abundance $75.8\%$ and $^{37}\ce{Cl}$ with abundance $24.2\%$. Calculate $A_r$.

**Working**

Use a weighted mean:

$$
A_r = \frac{35(75.8) + 37(24.2)}{100}
$$

$$
A_r = \frac{2653 + 895.4}{100} = 35.484
$$

**Answer:** $A_r = 35.5$ to three significant figures.

## Example 2: Formula from Ion Charges

Write the formula of iron(III) sulfate.

**Working**

Iron(III) means $\ce{Fe^{3+}}$. Sulfate is $\ce{SO4^{2-}}$.

The lowest common multiple of $3$ and $2$ is $6$, so two $\ce{Fe^{3+}}$ ions balance three $\ce{SO4^{2-}}$ ions.

**Answer:** $\ce{Fe2(SO4)3}$.

**Check:** total positive charge $= 2 \times 3+ = 6+$; total negative charge $= 3 \times 2- = 6-$.

## Example 3: Empirical and Molecular Formula

A compound contains $40.0\%$ carbon, $6.7\%$ hydrogen and $53.3\%$ oxygen by mass. Its $M_r$ is $180$. Find its empirical and molecular formulae.

**Working**

Assume $100.0\ \mathrm{g}$:

| Element | Mass / g | Moles |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| C | 40.0 | $\frac{40.0}{12.0} = 3.33$ |
| H | 6.7 | $\frac{6.7}{1.0} = 6.7$ |
| O | 53.3 | $\frac{53.3}{16.0} = 3.33$ |

Divide by the smallest:

$$
\ce{C}:\ce{H}:\ce{O} = 1:2:1
$$

Empirical formula is $\ce{CH2O}$.

Empirical formula mass:

$$
12.0 + 2(1.0) + 16.0 = 30.0
$$

Multiplier:

$$
\frac{180}{30.0} = 6
$$

**Answer:** empirical formula $\ce{CH2O}$; molecular formula $\ce{C6H12O6}$.

## Example 4: Water of Crystallisation

A hydrated salt $\ce{Na2CO3} \cdot x\ce{H2O}$ has a molar mass of $286.0\ \mathrm{g\ mol^{-1}}$. Find $x$.

**Working**

Relative formula mass of $\ce{Na2CO3}$:

$$
2(23.0) + 12.0 + 3(16.0) = 106.0
$$

Mass due to water:

$$
286.0 - 106.0 = 180.0
$$

Each $\ce{H2O}$ has $M_r = 18.0$, so:

$$
x = \frac{180.0}{18.0} = 10
$$

**Answer:** $\ce{Na2CO3*10H2O}$.

## Example 5: Reacting Mass and Percentage Yield

Calcium carbonate decomposes on heating:

$$
\ce{CaCO3(s) -> CaO(s) + CO2(g)}
$$

$10.0\ \mathrm{g}$ of $\ce{CaCO3}$ is heated. The actual mass of $\ce{CaO}$ obtained is $4.80\ \mathrm{g}$. Calculate the percentage yield.

**Working**

$$
M_r(\ce{CaCO3}) = 40.1 + 12.0 + 3(16.0) = 100.1
$$

$$
n(\ce{CaCO3}) = \frac{10.0}{100.1} = 0.0999\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

The mole ratio $\ce{CaCO3}:\ce{CaO}$ is $1:1$, so:

$$
n(\ce{CaO}) = 0.0999\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

$$
M_r(\ce{CaO}) = 40.1 + 16.0 = 56.1
$$

$$
\text{theoretical mass of } \ce{CaO} = 0.0999 \times 56.1 = 5.60\ \mathrm{g}
$$

$$
\text{percentage yield} = \frac{4.80}{5.60} \times 100 = 85.7\%
$$

**Answer:** $85.7\%$.

## Example 6: Solution Concentration

$25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of $0.100\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ $\ce{NaOH}$ reacts with hydrochloric acid:

$$
\ce{HCl(aq) + NaOH(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)}
$$

Calculate the amount of $\ce{HCl}$ that reacted.

**Working**

Convert volume:

$$
25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3} = 0.0250\ \mathrm{dm^3}
$$

Calculate moles of $\ce{NaOH}$:

$$
n = cV = 0.100 \times 0.0250 = 0.00250\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

The mole ratio $\ce{HCl}:\ce{NaOH}$ is $1:1$.

**Answer:** $0.00250\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{HCl}$ reacted.

## Example 7: Gas Volume Ratio

Propane burns completely:

$$
\ce{C3H8(g) + 5O2(g) -> 3CO2(g) + 4H2O(g)}
$$

What volume of oxygen is needed to burn $20.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of propane at the same temperature and pressure?

**Working**

For gases at the same temperature and pressure, volume ratio equals mole ratio.

The equation shows:

$$
\ce{C3H8}:\ce{O2} = 1:5
$$

$$
20.0\ \mathrm{cm^3} \times 5 = 100\ \mathrm{cm^3}
$$

**Answer:** $100\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of oxygen.

## Example 8: Limiting Reagent

$0.100\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{Al}$ reacts with $0.120\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{Cl2}$:

$$
\ce{2Al + 3Cl2 -> 2AlCl3}
$$

Identify the limiting reagent and calculate moles of $\ce{AlCl3}$ formed.

**Working**

Compare moles divided by coefficients:

$$
\ce{Al}: \frac{0.100}{2} = 0.0500
$$

$$
\ce{Cl2}: \frac{0.120}{3} = 0.0400
$$

The smaller value is for $\ce{Cl2}$, so $\ce{Cl2}$ is limiting.

Use the ratio $\ce{3Cl2 -> 2AlCl3}$:

$$
n(\ce{AlCl3}) = 0.120 \times \frac{2}{3} = 0.0800\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

**Answer:** limiting reagent is $\ce{Cl2}$; $0.0800\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{AlCl3}$ forms.
