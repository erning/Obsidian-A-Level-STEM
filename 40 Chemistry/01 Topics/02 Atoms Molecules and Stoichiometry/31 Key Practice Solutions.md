# Atoms, Molecules and Stoichiometry - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. The unified atomic mass unit is one twelfth of the mass of one carbon-12 atom.

2. $\ce{H2SO4}$:

   $$
   M_r = 2(1.0) + 32.1 + 4(16.0) = 98.1
   $$

   $\ce{Ca(OH)2}$:

   $$
   M_r = 40.1 + 2(16.0 + 1.0) = 74.1
   $$

   $\ce{Al2(SO4)3}$:

   $$
   M_r = 2(27.0) + 3(32.1 + 4(16.0)) = 342.3
   $$

3. Weighted mean:

   $$
   A_r = \frac{24(79.0) + 25(10.0) + 26(11.0)}{100}
   $$

   $$
   A_r = \frac{1896 + 250 + 286}{100} = 24.32
   $$

   To three significant figures, $A_r = 24.3$.

4. Ammonium sulfate: $\ce{(NH4)2SO4}$.

   Zinc nitrate: $\ce{Zn(NO3)2}$.

   Calcium phosphate: $\ce{Ca3(PO4)2}$.

   Copper(II) carbonate: $\ce{CuCO3}$.

5. Barium chloride and sodium sulfate are aqueous; barium sulfate is an insoluble solid:

   $$
   \ce{BaCl2(aq) + Na2SO4(aq) -> BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}
   $$

6. Remove spectator ions $\ce{Na+}$ and $\ce{Cl-}$:

   $$
   \ce{Ba^{2+}(aq) + SO4^{2-}(aq) -> BaSO4(s)}
   $$

7. Assume $100.0\ \mathrm{g}$:

   $$
   n(\ce{C}) = \frac{52.2}{12.0} = 4.35
   $$

   $$
   n(\ce{H}) = \frac{13.0}{1.0} = 13.0
   $$

   $$
   n(\ce{O}) = \frac{34.8}{16.0} = 2.175
   $$

   Divide by $2.175$:

   $$
   \ce{C}:\ce{H}:\ce{O} = 2:6:1
   $$

   Empirical formula: $\ce{C2H6O}$.

8. Empirical formula mass of $\ce{CH2}$:

   $$
   12.0 + 2(1.0) = 14.0
   $$

   Multiplier:

   $$
   \frac{70.0}{14.0} = 5
   $$

   Molecular formula: $\ce{C5H10}$.

9. Mass of water lost:

   $$
   4.95 - 3.19 = 1.76\ \mathrm{g}
   $$

   Moles of anhydrous $\ce{CuSO4}$:

   $$
   M_r(\ce{CuSO4}) = 63.5 + 32.1 + 4(16.0) = 159.6
   $$

   $$
   n(\ce{CuSO4}) = \frac{3.19}{159.6} = 0.0200\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   Moles of water:

   $$
   n(\ce{H2O}) = \frac{1.76}{18.0} = 0.0978\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   Ratio:

   $$
   x = \frac{0.0978}{0.0200} = 4.89 \approx 5
   $$

   Formula: $\ce{CuSO4*5H2O}$.

10. Equation:

   $$
   \ce{CaCO3 + 2HCl -> CaCl2 + CO2 + H2O}
   $$

   Moles of $\ce{CaCO3}$:

   $$
   n = \frac{5.00}{100.1} = 0.04995\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   Mole ratio $\ce{CaCO3}:\ce{CO2}$ is $1:1$, so:

   $$
   n(\ce{CO2}) = 0.04995\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   $$
   m(\ce{CO2}) = 0.04995 \times 44.0 = 2.20\ \mathrm{g}
   $$

11. Convert volume:

   $$
   25.0\ \mathrm{cm^3} = 0.0250\ \mathrm{dm^3}
   $$

   Moles of $\ce{H2SO4}$:

   $$
   n = cV = 0.200 \times 0.0250 = 0.00500\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

   Ratio $\ce{H2SO4}:\ce{NaOH}$ is $1:2$:

   $$
   n(\ce{NaOH}) = 2(0.00500) = 0.0100\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$

12. From the equation:

   $$
   \ce{C2H4}:\ce{O2}:\ce{CO2} = 1:3:2
   $$

   Oxygen used:

   $$
   30.0 \times 3 = 90.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}
   $$

   Carbon dioxide formed:

   $$
   30.0 \times 2 = 60.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}
   $$

13. Compare moles divided by coefficients:

   $$
   \ce{Mg}: \frac{0.250}{2} = 0.125
   $$

   $$
   \ce{O2}: \frac{0.180}{1} = 0.180
   $$

   Magnesium gives the smaller value, so $\ce{Mg}$ is limiting.

   Ratio $\ce{Mg}:\ce{MgO}$ is $2:2$, so:

   $$
   n(\ce{MgO}) = 0.250\ \mathrm{mol}
   $$
