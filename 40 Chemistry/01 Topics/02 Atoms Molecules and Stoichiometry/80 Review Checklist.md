# Atoms, Molecules and Stoichiometry - Review Checklist

## Recall

- [ ] I can define the unified atomic mass unit.
- [ ] I can define relative atomic mass, relative isotopic mass, relative molecular mass and relative formula mass.
- [ ] I can define mole in terms of the Avogadro constant.
- [ ] I can recall the formulae and charges of nitrate, carbonate, sulfate, hydroxide, ammonium, zinc, silver, hydrogencarbonate and phosphate ions.
- [ ] I can define empirical formula and molecular formula.
- [ ] I can explain anhydrous, hydrated and water of crystallisation.

## Skills

- [ ] I can calculate $A_r$ from isotopic abundances.
- [ ] I can calculate $M_r$ and relative formula mass from a formula.
- [ ] I can convert between particles, moles and mass.
- [ ] I can write ionic formulae from charges and oxidation numbers.
- [ ] I can balance full equations and write ionic equations without spectator ions.
- [ ] I can calculate empirical and molecular formulae from data.
- [ ] I can solve reacting mass, percentage yield, solution concentration, gas volume and limiting reagent problems.
- [ ] I can deduce stoichiometric relationships from calculation data.

## Common Errors

- [ ] I do not use coefficients from an unbalanced equation.
- [ ] I do not treat equation ratios as mass ratios.
- [ ] I convert $\mathrm{cm^3}$ to $\mathrm{dm^3}$ before using $c = \frac{n}{V}$.
- [ ] I keep state symbols and charges consistent in ionic equations.
- [ ] I do not include spectator ions in net ionic equations.
- [ ] I round only at the end and keep significant figures justified by the question.

## Self-Checks

- [ ] Given a formula, I can identify the particles counted by one mole of that substance.
- [ ] Given a mass and a balanced equation, I can find the mass of another reactant or product.
- [ ] Given titration data, I can move from volume to moles to concentration.
- [ ] Given moles of two reactants, I can identify the limiting reagent.
- [ ] Given hydrate mass data, I can calculate the water of crystallisation.

## Connections

- [ ] I can connect isotope abundance to relative atomic mass.
- [ ] I can connect ionic charge to formula writing and equation balancing.
- [ ] I can connect mole ratios to gas calculations and ideal gas calculations.
- [ ] I can connect stoichiometry to practical work such as titration and yield.

## Ready When

- [ ] I can set up a calculation from a balanced equation without guessing.
- [ ] I can explain every unit conversion in a solution calculation.
- [ ] I can identify the limiting reagent before calculating product mass.
- [ ] I can judge whether my final significant figures are reasonable.
