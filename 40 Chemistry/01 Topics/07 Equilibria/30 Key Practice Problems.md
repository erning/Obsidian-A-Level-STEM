# Equilibria - Key Practice Problems

1. Define dynamic equilibrium, including the rate condition, concentration condition and system condition.

2. State Le Chatelier's principle.

3. For the equilibrium below, the forward reaction is exothermic.

$$
\ce{2SO2(g) + O2(g) <=> 2SO3(g)}
$$

Predict the effect on equilibrium position and on $K_p$ of increasing temperature.

4. For the equilibrium in Problem 3, predict the effect of increasing pressure and adding a catalyst.

5. Write the $K_c$ expression for:

$$
\ce{CH3COOH(aq) + C2H5OH(aq) <=> CH3COOC2H5(aq) + H2O(l)}
$$

6. At equilibrium for:

$$
\ce{PCl5(g) <=> PCl3(g) + Cl2(g)}
$$

the concentrations are $[\ce{PCl5}] = 0.200\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$, $[\ce{PCl3}] = 0.100\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$ and $[\ce{Cl2}] = 0.100\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$. Calculate $K_c$.

7. For:

$$
\ce{N2(g) + 3H2(g) <=> 2NH3(g)}
$$

write the $K_p$ expression.

8. A gas mixture at equilibrium contains $0.20\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{N2}$, $0.60\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{H2}$ and $0.20\ \mathrm{mol}$ of $\ce{NH3}$. The total pressure is $500\ \mathrm{kPa}$. Calculate the partial pressure of each gas.

9. Explain why the Haber process uses a compromise temperature and an iron catalyst.

10. Define Brønsted-Lowry acid and Brønsted-Lowry base. Identify the acid and base in:

$$
\ce{NH3(aq) + H2O(l) <=> NH4+(aq) + OH-(aq)}
$$

11. Explain the difference between a strong acid and a concentrated acid.

12. A titration curve for a weak acid with a strong alkali has an equivalence point above pH 7. Explain how an indicator should be selected when transition-range data are given.
