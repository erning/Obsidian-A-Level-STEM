# Equilibria - Key Practice Solutions

1. Dynamic equilibrium is established in a closed system when the forward and reverse reactions continue at equal rates and the concentrations of reactants and products remain constant.

2. Le Chatelier's principle states that if a change is made to a system at dynamic equilibrium, the position of equilibrium moves to minimise this change.

3. The forward reaction is exothermic, so increasing temperature favours the endothermic reverse reaction. The equilibrium position shifts left, giving less $\ce{SO3}$. Since temperature changes the equilibrium constant, $K_p$ decreases for the exothermic forward reaction.

4. Increasing pressure favours the side with fewer gas molecules. The left side has 3 moles of gas and the right side has 2 moles, so equilibrium shifts right. Adding a catalyst does not change equilibrium position or $K_p$; it only increases the rate at which equilibrium is reached.

5. The expression is:

$$
K_c = \frac{[\ce{CH3COOC2H5}][\ce{H2O}]}{[\ce{CH3COOH}][\ce{C2H5OH}]}
$$

If water is treated as the solvent or as a pure liquid in a specific context, it may be omitted, but for the homogeneous esterification expression as written here it is commonly shown.

6. The expression is:

$$
K_c = \frac{[\ce{PCl3}][\ce{Cl2}]}{[\ce{PCl5}]}
$$

Substitute:

$$
K_c = \frac{(0.100)(0.100)}{0.200} = 0.0500
$$

7. The expression is:

$$
K_p = \frac{p_{\ce{NH3}}^2}{p_{\ce{N2}}p_{\ce{H2}}^3}
$$

8. Total amount:

$$
0.20 + 0.60 + 0.20 = 1.00\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

Mole fractions are 0.20, 0.60 and 0.20 respectively. Therefore:

$$
p_{\ce{N2}} = 0.20 \times 500 = 100\ \mathrm{kPa}
$$

$$
p_{\ce{H2}} = 0.60 \times 500 = 300\ \mathrm{kPa}
$$

$$
p_{\ce{NH3}} = 0.20 \times 500 = 100\ \mathrm{kPa}
$$

9. The forward reaction in the Haber process is exothermic, so a low temperature would improve equilibrium yield but would make the reaction too slow. A compromise temperature, commonly about $450\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$, gives an acceptable rate and yield. High pressure, commonly about $200\ \mathrm{atm}$, improves yield but is limited by cost and safety. The iron catalyst increases the rate of reaching equilibrium without changing the equilibrium position.

10. A Brønsted-Lowry acid is a proton donor. A Brønsted-Lowry base is a proton acceptor.

In:

$$
\ce{NH3(aq) + H2O(l) <=> NH4+(aq) + OH-(aq)}
$$

$\ce{H2O}$ donates a proton to $\ce{NH3}$, so water acts as the acid. $\ce{NH3}$ accepts a proton, so ammonia acts as the base.

11. Strong describes extent of dissociation. A strong acid is fully dissociated in aqueous solution. Concentrated describes amount of acid per unit volume. A concentrated acid has a high concentration, but it may still be weak if it is only partially dissociated.

12. The indicator should have a transition range that lies within the steep vertical part of the titration curve near the end point. For a weak acid with a strong alkali, the equivalence point is above pH 7, so an indicator changing in the alkaline range is usually suitable if its transition range falls within that steep section.
