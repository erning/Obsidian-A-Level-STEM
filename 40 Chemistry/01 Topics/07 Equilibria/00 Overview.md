# 07 Equilibria

## Core Idea

Equilibria describes reversible systems in which forward and reverse reactions continue at equal rates in a closed system. AS work combines qualitative shifts, equilibrium constants, industrial conditions and Brønsted-Lowry acid-base behaviour.

## What to Learn

- Describe reversible reactions and dynamic equilibrium in a closed system.
- State and apply Le Chatelier's principle.
- Predict qualitative effects of changes in concentration, pressure, temperature and catalyst on equilibrium position.
- Deduce and use $K_c$ expressions.
- Use mole fraction and partial pressure.
- Deduce and use $K_p$ expressions, without needing the relationship between $K_p$ and $K_c$.
- Calculate equilibrium quantities from suitable data, without solving quadratic equations.
- State whether temperature, concentration, pressure or catalyst affects the value of an equilibrium constant.
- Explain the Haber process and Contact process as applications of dynamic equilibrium and Le Chatelier's principle.
- Use Brønsted-Lowry acid-base ideas, including strong and weak acids and bases, neutralisation, salts, titration curves and indicators.

## How to Study

Keep three ideas separate: rate, position of equilibrium and equilibrium constant. At dynamic equilibrium, the forward and reverse rates are equal. The position of equilibrium describes the relative amounts of reactants and products. The equilibrium constant has a fixed value at a given temperature.

For $K_c$ and $K_p$, write the expression from the balanced equation before substituting values. Coefficients become powers.

For Le Chatelier questions, state the change, identify how the system can minimise it, then describe the direction of shift. A catalyst changes the rate of reaching equilibrium, not the position or the equilibrium constant.

## Practice Directions

- Explain dynamic equilibrium using equal rates and constant concentrations.
- Predict equilibrium shifts using Le Chatelier's principle.
- Construct $K_c$ and $K_p$ expressions from equations.
- Calculate mole fractions, partial pressures and equilibrium amounts.
- Decide which changes alter $K_c$ or $K_p$.
- Explain compromise conditions in the Haber process and Contact process.
- Compare strong and weak acids using pH, reaction rate with reactive metals and conductivity.
- Sketch titration curves and choose suitable indicators from transition ranges.

## Common Traps

- Saying a catalyst changes the position of equilibrium.
- Treating strong acid as the same idea as concentrated acid.
- Omitting powers in $K_c$ or $K_p$ expressions from equation coefficients.
- Forgetting that only temperature changes the value of the equilibrium constant.
- Using total pressure instead of partial pressure in a $K_p$ expression.
