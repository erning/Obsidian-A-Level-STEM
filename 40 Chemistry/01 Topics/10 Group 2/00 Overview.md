# 10 Group 2

## Core Idea

Group 2 chemistry studies the alkaline earth metals from magnesium to barium and the systematic behaviour of their oxides, hydroxides, carbonates, nitrates and sulfates. Down the group, the metals generally become more reactive, hydroxides become more soluble, sulfates become less soluble, and carbonates and nitrates become more thermally stable.

## What to Learn

- Describe and write equations for Group 2 metals reacting with oxygen, water and dilute hydrochloric and sulfuric acids.
- Describe and write equations for Group 2 oxides, hydroxides and carbonates reacting with water and dilute acids.
- Describe thermal decomposition of Group 2 nitrates and carbonates, including the thermal stability trend.
- Predict trends in physical and chemical properties down the group.
- State the variation in solubilities of Group 2 hydroxides and sulfates.

## How to Study

- Use $\ce{M}$ as a general Group 2 metal, then practise with $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Ca}$, $\ce{Sr}$ and $\ce{Ba}$.
- Learn each reaction as a pattern, then add observations and exceptions in reactivity or solubility.
- Keep the two solubility trends separate: hydroxides become more soluble; sulfates become less soluble.
- Link reactivity to the increasing ease of forming $\ce{M^{2+}}$ ions down the group.

## Common Traps

- Mixing up solubility trends for hydroxides and sulfates.
- Assuming magnesium reacts with cold water as vigorously as calcium or barium.
- Forgetting that Group 2 metals form $\ce{M^{2+}}$ ions.
- Writing word descriptions without balanced equations.
