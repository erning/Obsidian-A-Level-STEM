# Group 2 - Review Checklist

## Recall

- [ ] I can list $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Ca}$, $\ce{Sr}$ and $\ce{Ba}$ as the AS Group 2 metals.
- [ ] I know Group 2 metals form $\ce{M^{2+}}$ ions.
- [ ] I can explain why reactivity increases down Group 2.
- [ ] I can state that hydroxide solubility increases down the group.
- [ ] I can state that sulfate solubility decreases down the group.
- [ ] I can state that nitrate and carbonate thermal stability increases down the group.

## Equations

- [ ] I can write $\ce{2M + O2 -> 2MO}$ for reaction with oxygen.
- [ ] I can write $\ce{M + 2H2O -> M(OH)2 + H2}$ for reaction with cold water where appropriate.
- [ ] I can write $\ce{Mg + H2O -> MgO + H2}$ for magnesium with steam.
- [ ] I can write equations for metals with dilute $\ce{HCl}$ and dilute $\ce{H2SO4}$.
- [ ] I can write equations for oxides and hydroxides reacting with acids.
- [ ] I can write equations for carbonates reacting with acids.
- [ ] I can write thermal decomposition equations for $\ce{MCO3}$ and $\ce{M(NO3)2}$.

## Observations and Tests

- [ ] I know hydrogen gives a squeaky pop with a lighted splint.
- [ ] I know carbon dioxide turns limewater milky.
- [ ] I know oxygen relights a glowing splint.
- [ ] I know nitrogen dioxide is a brown gas.
- [ ] I know $\ce{BaSO4}$ is a white insoluble precipitate.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] I do not reverse the hydroxide and sulfate solubility trends.
- [ ] I do not forget the $\ce{2+}$ charge when writing Group 2 formulae.
- [ ] I do not write $\ce{MNO3}$ for a Group 2 nitrate; it is $\ce{M(NO3)2}$.
- [ ] I do not write hydrogen as a product when a carbonate reacts with acid.
- [ ] I do not assume all Group 2 sulfates are equally soluble.

## Ready to Move On

- [ ] I can use one general equation and adapt it to any named Group 2 metal.
- [ ] I can predict observations for reactions with water, acids and heat.
- [ ] I can use solubility and thermal stability trends to compare two named Group 2 compounds.
