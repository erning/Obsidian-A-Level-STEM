# Chemical Energetics - Key Practice Problems

1. Define exothermic reaction and endothermic reaction in terms of $\Delta H$ and energy transfer.

2. A reaction pathway diagram has reactants at $80\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, products at $135\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, and a peak at $190\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$. Calculate $\Delta H$ and $E_A$, and state whether the reaction is exothermic or endothermic.

3. State the standard conditions used in CAIE Chemistry 9701 for standard enthalpy changes, and explain what the symbol $^\ominus$ indicates.

4. Write equations that represent the standard enthalpy change of formation of $\ce{MgO(s)}$ and the standard enthalpy change of combustion of $\ce{C2H5OH(l)}$.

5. In a calorimetry experiment, $50.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of $1.00\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$ hydrochloric acid reacts with excess magnesium. The temperature of the solution rises by $18.2\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Assume the solution has mass $50.0\ \mathrm{g}$ and $c = 4.18\ \mathrm{J\,g^{-1}\,K^{-1}}$. Calculate the enthalpy change per mole of $\ce{HCl}$ reacting.

6. Estimate $\Delta H_r$ for:

$$
\ce{CH4(g) + Cl2(g) -> CH3Cl(g) + HCl(g)}
$$

Use bond energies: $\ce{C-H} = 412\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, $\ce{Cl-Cl} = 243\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, $\ce{C-Cl} = 338\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, $\ce{H-Cl} = 431\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$.

7. Use the following formation enthalpies to calculate $\Delta H_r^\ominus$ for:

$$
\ce{CO(g) + 1/2O2(g) -> CO2(g)}
$$

$\Delta H_f^\ominus(\ce{CO(g)}) = -111\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$ and $\Delta H_f^\ominus(\ce{CO2(g)}) = -394\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$.

8. Use the following combustion enthalpies to calculate $\Delta H_r^\ominus$ for:

$$
\ce{C(s, graphite) + 2H2(g) -> CH4(g)}
$$

$\Delta H_c^\ominus(\ce{C(s, graphite)}) = -394\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, $\Delta H_c^\ominus(\ce{H2(g)}) = -286\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, and $\Delta H_c^\ominus(\ce{CH4(g)}) = -891\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$.

9. Explain why bond energy calculations may differ from experimentally measured enthalpy changes.

10. A student burns a fuel under a copper can of water and obtains a value of $-520\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$. The data book value is $-690\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$. Suggest two experimental reasons for the difference.
