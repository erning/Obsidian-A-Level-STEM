# 34 Nitrogen Compounds - Key Practice Problems

1. State reagents and conditions for converting a halogenoalkane into a primary amine.

2. State reagents and conditions for converting a halogenoalkane and a primary amine into a secondary amine.

3. Give two reagents that can reduce a nitrile to a primary amine.

4. Write a general equation for reducing an amide with $\ce{LiAlH4}$.

5. Explain the basicity order ethylamine > ammonia > phenylamine.

6. Explain why amides are much weaker bases than amines.

7. Give a two-stage route from benzene to phenylamine.

8. State the product when phenylamine reacts with bromine water at room temperature.

9. Describe the formation of a diazonium salt from phenylamine, including temperature.

10. State how a diazonium salt can be converted into phenol.

11. Identify the azo group and describe how phenol forms an azo compound with benzenediazonium chloride.

12. Write equations for ethanoyl chloride reacting with ammonia and with methylamine.

13. State the conditions for amide hydrolysis and the reagent for reducing amides to amines.

14. Explain what a zwitterion is using 2-aminoethanoic acid as an example.

15. Predict the movement of an amino acid in electrophoresis when the pH is below, equal to, and above its isoelectric point.
