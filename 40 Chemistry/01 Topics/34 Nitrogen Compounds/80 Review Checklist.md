# 34 Nitrogen Compounds - Review Checklist

## Source Scope

- [ ] I can state that this topic follows CAIE Chemistry 9701 Topic 34.
- [ ] I can explain how the topic extends AS nitrile and amine chemistry.

## Amines

- [ ] I know how primary amines are made from halogenoalkanes and ammonia.
- [ ] I know how secondary amines are made from halogenoalkanes and primary amines.
- [ ] I know how nitriles and amides are reduced to amines.
- [ ] I can explain basicity of aqueous amines.
- [ ] I can compare ethylamine, ammonia, phenylamine and amides.

## Phenylamine and Azo Compounds

- [ ] I can prepare phenylamine from benzene via nitrobenzene.
- [ ] I remember hot $\ce{Sn}$ and concentrated $\ce{HCl}$ followed by $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$.
- [ ] I can describe phenylamine with bromine water.
- [ ] I can form a diazonium salt below $10\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$.
- [ ] I can convert a diazonium salt into phenol by warming with water.
- [ ] I can identify the azo group and describe azo coupling with phenol.

## Amides

- [ ] I can make amides from acyl chlorides and ammonia or primary amines.
- [ ] I can describe amide hydrolysis with aqueous acid or alkali.
- [ ] I can reduce amides to amines using $\ce{LiAlH4}$.
- [ ] I can explain why amides are much weaker bases than amines.

## Amino Acids

- [ ] I can describe acid-base behaviour and zwitterion formation.
- [ ] I can define isoelectric point.
- [ ] I can draw peptide bond formation as a condensation reaction.
- [ ] I can predict electrophoresis movement at different pH values.

## Ready to Move On

- [ ] I can use nitrogen reactions in organic synthesis routes.
- [ ] I can connect amino acids and amides to polyamide formation.
