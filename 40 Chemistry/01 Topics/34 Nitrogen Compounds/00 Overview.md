# 34 Nitrogen Compounds

## Core Idea

A Level nitrogen chemistry brings together amines, phenylamine, diazonium salts, azo compounds, amides, amino acids, zwitterions, peptide bonds, and electrophoresis. The topic is rich because nitrogen compounds can act as bases, nucleophiles, intermediates, and biological building blocks.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.

- 34.2 Phenylamine and azo compounds
- 34.3 Amides
- 34.4 Amino acids

## What to Learn

- Recall routes to primary and secondary amines from halogenoalkanes, amides, and nitriles.
- Describe amine or ammonia reactions with acyl chlorides to form amides.
- Explain basicity trends for amines, phenylamine, ammonia, and amides.
- Describe phenylamine preparation, diazonium salt formation, and azo coupling.
- Use amino acid acid-base behaviour, zwitterions, isoelectric point, peptide bonds, and electrophoresis.

## How to Study

- Separate amines from amides; similar names hide very different basicity.
- Track lone-pair availability when explaining base strength.
- Learn diazonium salt conditions carefully because temperature matters.
- For amino acids, draw the charge form at the stated pH before predicting movement in electrophoresis.

## Practice Directions

- Choose routes to amines from halogenoalkanes, nitriles, or amides.
- Compare basicities using structure and electron-pair availability.
- Predict products from phenylamine diazotisation and azo coupling.
- Draw amino acid forms at different pH values and interpret electrophoresis results.

## Common Traps

- Assuming amides are as basic as amines.
- Forgetting that phenylamine is less basic than many aliphatic amines.
- Missing the low-temperature condition for diazonium salt formation.
- Predicting amino acid charge without first comparing pH with the relevant acid-base form.
