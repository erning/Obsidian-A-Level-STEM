# 22 Analytical Techniques - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. State the infrared absorption evidence that would support the presence of a carboxylic acid group.

2. A compound has an infrared absorption at $2220\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$. Which functional group is suggested?

3. A compound has a molecular ion peak at $m/e = 74$. What is its relative molecular mass?

4. An element has isotopes of mass 10 and 11 with relative abundances 20.0% and 80.0%. Calculate its relative atomic mass.

5. In a mass spectrum, $M^+$ has relative abundance 100 and $[M+1]^+$ has relative abundance 5.5. Deduce the number of carbon atoms.

6. A compound has $M$ and $M+2$ peaks in a ratio of about $3:1$. Which halogen is suggested?

7. A compound has $M$ and $M+2$ peaks in a ratio of about $1:1$. Which halogen is suggested?

8. Suggest a possible fragment ion with $m/e = 29$.

9. A liquid gives an orange precipitate with 2,4-DNPH reagent and a silver mirror with Tollens' reagent. What functional group is present?

10. An unknown gives a yellow precipitate with alkaline aqueous iodine. What structural features can this support in AS organic analysis?

11. An unknown produces a gas that turns damp red litmus paper blue. Identify the gas.

12. In a TLC experiment, a spot moves $2.4\ \mathrm{cm}$ while the solvent front moves $6.0\ \mathrm{cm}$. Calculate the $R_f$ value.

13. A compound has $M^+$ at $m/e = 88$, a strong $\ce{C=O}$ absorption in the ester range, and hydrolyses to ethanol and ethanoic acid. Suggest its structure.

14. Explain why qualitative analysis notes require candidates to record "no change" observations.
