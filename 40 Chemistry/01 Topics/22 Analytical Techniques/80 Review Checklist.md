# 22 Analytical Techniques - Review Checklist

## Infrared Spectroscopy

- I can use the syllabus Data section to identify characteristic infrared absorptions.
- I can recognise carboxylic acid evidence from broad $\ce{O-H}$ at $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ plus $\ce{C=O}$.
- I can recognise alcohol evidence from hydroxy $\ce{O-H}$ at $3200-3600\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ when the rest of the data fit.
- I can recognise nitrile evidence from $\ce{C#N}$ at $2200-2250\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$.
- I can use ester $\ce{C=O}$ and $\ce{C-O}$ evidence together.
- I do not use one absorption as complete proof of a full structure.

## Mass Spectrometry

- I can identify the molecular ion peak and use it to find relative molecular mass.
- I can distinguish molecular ion peaks from fragment peaks.
- I can calculate relative atomic mass from isotope abundance data.
- I can suggest simple fragment ions from $m/e$ values.
- I can use the $[M+1]^+$ formula to estimate the number of carbon atoms.
- I can recognise chlorine from an approximate $3:1$ $M:M+2$ pattern.
- I can recognise bromine from an approximate $1:1$ $M:M+2$ pattern.

## Qualitative Analysis

- I can record observations before conclusions.
- I can include negative observations such as "no change".
- I can identify $\ce{NH3}$, $\ce{CO2}$, $\ce{H2}$, and $\ce{O2}$ using the standard gas tests.
- I can use Fehling's reagent and Tollens' reagent as aldehyde evidence.
- I can use alkaline aqueous iodine as evidence for $\ce{CH3CO}$ or $\ce{CH3CH(OH)}$ groups.
- I can use acidified potassium manganate(VII) decolourisation as evidence that a compound can be oxidised.

## Chromatography Data

- I know chromatography is included here as supporting analysis literacy; formal TLC and gas / liquid chromatography outcomes are A Level topic 37.
- I can calculate $R_f$ if spot and solvent-front distances are supplied.
- I can use one spot or one peak as evidence of purity under stated conditions.
- I can use matching spots or retention times only when conditions and standards are comparable.

## Structure Deduction

- I can combine molecular mass, isotope pattern, infrared absorptions, fragments, and qualitative tests.
- I can reject a proposed structure if any piece of evidence contradicts it.
- I can explain why two structures may remain possible until another test separates them.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Do not confuse $M^+$ with the base peak.
- Do not ignore $[M+1]^+$ and $[M+2]^+$ peaks when they are provided.
- Do not use chromatography as absolute proof without comparison conditions.
- Do not write qualitative conclusions without observations.
- Do not assume all $\ce{O-H}$ absorptions mean the same functional group.

## Ready for Organic Synthesis When

- I can use analytical data to confirm a product after a synthetic route.
- I can identify when a reaction product has the expected functional group.
- I can combine spectroscopy and qualitative tests into a coherent evidence-based structure proposal.
