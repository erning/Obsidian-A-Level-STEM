# 22 Analytical Techniques - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Identifying a Carboxylic Acid by Infrared Spectroscopy

**Question.** A compound has a broad infrared absorption at $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and a strong absorption around $1710\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$. What functional group is suggested?

**Working.** The broad $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ absorption is characteristic of carboxyl $\ce{O-H}$. The absorption around $1710\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ is consistent with $\ce{C=O}$ in a carboxyl or carbonyl group.

**Answer.** A carboxylic acid group, $\ce{-COOH}$.

**Check.** The $\ce{O-H}$ and $\ce{C=O}$ evidence support each other.

## Example 2: Distinguishing an Alcohol from a Carboxylic Acid

**Question.** Compound A has a broad absorption at $3200-3600\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and no $\ce{C=O}$ absorption. Compound B has a broad absorption at $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and a $\ce{C=O}$ absorption. Which is more likely to be an alcohol?

**Working.** Hydroxy $\ce{O-H}$ in alcohols appears at $3200-3600\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$. Carboxyl $\ce{O-H}$ appears at $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and is accompanied by $\ce{C=O}$.

**Answer.** Compound A is more likely to be an alcohol. Compound B is more likely to be a carboxylic acid.

**Check.** The absence of $\ce{C=O}$ is important for compound A.

## Example 3: Relative Atomic Mass from Isotope Abundances

**Question.** An element has isotopes of mass 35 and 37 with relative abundances 75.0% and 25.0%. Calculate its relative atomic mass.

**Working.**

$$
A_r = \frac{(35 \times 75.0) + (37 \times 25.0)}{100}
$$

$$
A_r = \frac{2625 + 925}{100} = 35.5
$$

**Answer.** $A_r = 35.5$.

**Check.** The answer is closer to 35 than 37 because the mass-35 isotope is more abundant.

## Example 4: Carbon Count from the $[M+1]^+$ Peak

**Question.** In a mass spectrum, $M^+$ has relative abundance 80.0 and $[M+1]^+$ has relative abundance 3.52. Deduce the number of carbon atoms.

**Working.**

$$
n =
\frac{100 \times 3.52}{1.1 \times 80.0}
$$

$$
n = \frac{352}{88.0} = 4
$$

**Answer.** The compound contains four carbon atoms.

**Check.** A non-integer answer in real data should usually be rounded to the nearest sensible whole number.

## Example 5: Chlorine or Bromine from $[M+2]^+$

**Question.** A compound has molecular ion peaks at $m/e = 122$ and $m/e = 124$ with relative intensities approximately $1:1$. What halogen is suggested?

**Working.** A $1:1$ $M:M+2$ pattern suggests one bromine atom. A chlorine atom would give an approximate $3:1$ pattern.

**Answer.** Bromine is suggested.

**Check.** The two peaks are two mass units apart, which is the key isotope pattern.

## Example 6: Molecular Ion and Fragment

**Question.** An organic compound has a molecular ion peak at $m/e = 58$ and a fragment peak at $m/e = 43$. Suggest one possible fragment ion at $m/e = 43$.

**Working.** Simple fragment ions with $m/e = 43$ include $\ce{C3H7+}$ and $\ce{CH3CO+}$. The correct choice depends on the rest of the spectrum and the functional group evidence.

**Answer.** One possible fragment is $\ce{C3H7+}$. If carbonyl evidence is present, $\ce{CH3CO+}$ may also be plausible.

**Check.** Fragment identification is evidence, not a complete structure by itself.

## Example 7: Qualitative Analysis Observation

**Question.** An unknown solution gives effervescence when dilute acid is added. The gas gives a white precipitate with limewater. What gas is present, and what ion or functional group might this support?

**Working.** A gas that gives a white precipitate with limewater is carbon dioxide.

**Answer.** The gas is $\ce{CO2}$. In inorganic qualitative analysis this supports carbonate, $\ce{CO3^{2-}}$. In organic analysis, effervescence with carbonate can support a carboxylic acid group.

**Check.** Always record the observation before the conclusion.

## Example 8: Chromatography Data Provided in a Question

**Question.** In a thin-layer chromatography experiment, a spot moves $3.0\ \mathrm{cm}$ and the solvent front moves $7.5\ \mathrm{cm}$. Calculate the $R_f$ value.

**Working.**

$$
R_f = \frac{3.0}{7.5} = 0.40
$$

**Answer.** $R_f = 0.40$.

**Check.** $R_f$ has no units because it is a ratio of distances.

## Example 9: Combining Evidence

**Question.** A compound has $M^+$ at $m/e = 60$. Its infrared spectrum shows a broad absorption at $2500-3000\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$ and a $\ce{C=O}$ absorption. It reacts with sodium carbonate to produce a gas that gives a white precipitate with limewater. Suggest a possible structure.

**Working.** The infrared evidence and carbonate reaction suggest a carboxylic acid. A carboxylic acid with relative molecular mass 60 is ethanoic acid:

$$
\ce{CH3COOH}
$$

Relative molecular mass: $2(12) + 4(1) + 2(16) = 60$.

**Answer.** Ethanoic acid, $\ce{CH3COOH}$.

**Check.** Molecular mass, infrared evidence, and chemical test all agree.
