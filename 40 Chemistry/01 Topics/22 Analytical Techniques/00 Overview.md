# 22 Analytical Techniques

## Core Idea

Analytical techniques use evidence from spectra, qualitative observations and separation data to identify functional groups, molecular mass, isotopic patterns, simple fragments and possible structures.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Analysis: 22 Analytical techniques.
- 22.1 Infrared spectroscopy.
- 22.2 Mass spectrometry.
- Supporting AS practical analysis: qualitative analysis notes and organic analysis tests from the practical assessment section.
- Chromatography is included in the generated notes as analysis context when data are provided; the formal thin-layer and gas / liquid chromatography learning outcomes are in A Level topic 37.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions; data interpretation supports practical thinking.

## What to Learn

- Analyse an infrared spectrum of a simple molecule to identify functional groups.
- Analyse mass spectra using m/e values and isotopic abundances.
- Calculate relative atomic mass from isotope abundance data.
- Deduce molecular mass from the molecular ion peak.
- Suggest simple fragment identities and use $[M+1]^+$ and $[M+2]^+$ patterns where required.
- Record and interpret qualitative observations carefully, including negative results.
- Use chromatography data cautiously as supporting separation evidence when a question provides it.

## How to Study

- Treat each spectrum as evidence, not as a picture to memorise.
- Start with the strongest functional group clues, then test whether the molecular mass and fragments fit.
- Keep the data section close while learning characteristic infrared absorptions.

## Practice Directions

- Identify functional groups from infrared absorption data.
- Interpret molecular ion and fragment peaks in simple mass spectra.
- Use isotope patterns to recognise chlorine and bromine where relevant.

## Common Traps

- Using one infrared absorption as proof without checking the rest of the evidence.
- Confusing molecular ion peaks with fragment peaks.
- Forgetting that the syllabus does not require knowledge of how the mass spectrometer works at AS level.
