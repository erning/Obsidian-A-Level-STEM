# 29 An Introduction to A Level Organic Chemistry - Key Practice Solutions

1. $\ce{C6H5Cl}$ is a halogenoarene. $\ce{C6H5OH}$ is phenol. $\ce{CH3CH2COCl}$ is an acyl chloride. $\ce{CH3CONH2}$ is an amide. $\ce{H2NCH2COOH}$ is an amino acid.

2. In phenol, $\ce{-OH}$ is directly attached to an aromatic ring. The ring changes the acidity and directs electrophilic substitution at the 2-, 4- and 6-positions. Alcohols do not have this aromatic $\pi$ system attached directly to the $\ce{-OH}$ group.

3. The compound is 3-nitrobenzoic acid. The carboxyl group defines benzoic acid and fixes carbon 1; the nitro group is on carbon 3.

4. Benzene is planar. Each carbon atom is $sp^2$ hybridised and forms three $\sigma$ bonds. Each carbon also has a p orbital; the six p orbitals overlap to form a delocalised $\pi$ system above and below the ring.

5. Addition would destroy the delocalised $\pi$ system and remove aromatic stabilisation. Electrophilic substitution replaces a ring hydrogen but restores the aromatic system, so it is favoured.

6. Nitration of benzene is electrophilic substitution. Reaction of an acyl chloride with water is addition-elimination.

7. $\ce{CH3CHBrCH2CH3}$ can show optical isomerism. Carbon 2 is attached to four different groups: $\ce{H}$, $\ce{Br}$, $\ce{CH3}$ and $\ce{CH2CH3}$.

8. An optically active substance rotates plane-polarised light. A racemic mixture contains equal amounts of two enantiomers. The two enantiomers rotate plane-polarised light by equal amounts in opposite directions, so the rotations cancel.

9. Biological targets such as enzymes and receptors are chiral, so two enantiomers of a drug can have different biological activity. A synthesis may therefore need to separate a racemic mixture or use a chiral catalyst to make one enantiomer preferentially.

10. A molecule containing both $\ce{-NH2}$ and $\ce{-COOH}$ groups is an amino acid. It becomes important in Topic 34 Nitrogen Compounds, especially in zwitterions, peptide bonds and electrophoresis.
