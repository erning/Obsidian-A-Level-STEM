# 29 An Introduction to A Level Organic Chemistry - Key Practice Problems

1. Identify the functional group in each compound: $\ce{C6H5Cl}$, $\ce{C6H5OH}$, $\ce{CH3CH2COCl}$, $\ce{CH3CONH2}$ and $\ce{H2NCH2COOH}$.

2. Explain why phenol should not be treated simply as an alcohol in A Level organic chemistry.

3. Name an aromatic compound with a benzene ring bearing $\ce{-COOH}$ at position 1 and $\ce{-NO2}$ at position 3.

4. Describe the bonding and shape of benzene using $sp^2$ hybridisation, $\sigma$ bonds and a delocalised $\pi$ system.

5. Explain why benzene tends to undergo electrophilic substitution rather than addition.

6. Classify each reaction as electrophilic substitution or addition-elimination:
   - $\ce{C6H6 + NO2+ -> C6H5NO2 + H+}$
   - $\ce{CH3COCl + H2O -> CH3COOH + HCl}$

7. For $\ce{CH3CHBrCH2CH3}$, decide whether the molecule can show optical isomerism and justify your answer.

8. Define optically active and racemic mixture, then explain why a racemic mixture does not rotate plane-polarised light overall.

9. Explain why chirality is relevant when preparing drug molecules.

10. A molecule contains both $\ce{-NH2}$ and $\ce{-COOH}$ groups. Identify the class of compound and state one later topic where this class becomes important.
