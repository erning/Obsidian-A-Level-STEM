# 20 Polymerisation

## Core Idea

AS polymerisation focuses on addition polymerisation, where alkene monomers join to form long-chain poly(alkene)s.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 20 Polymerisation.
- 20.1 Addition polymerisation.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Describe addition polymerisation using poly(ethene) and poly(chloroethene), PVC.
- Deduce the repeat unit from a given alkene monomer.
- Identify monomer or monomers from a section of an addition polymer.
- Recognise disposal difficulties of poly(alkene)s, including non-biodegradability and harmful combustion products.

## How to Study

- Mark the carbon-carbon double bond before drawing the repeat unit.
- Use brackets and the continuation bonds carefully when drawing polymer structures.
- Connect polymer disposal issues to chemical structure and combustion products.

## Practice Directions

- Draw repeat units from alkene monomers.
- Work backwards from polymer sections to monomers.
- Explain why addition polymers can be difficult to dispose of responsibly.

## Common Traps

- Leaving the carbon-carbon double bond in an addition polymer repeat unit.
- Drawing the repeat unit without open continuation bonds.
- Confusing addition polymerisation with condensation polymerisation.
