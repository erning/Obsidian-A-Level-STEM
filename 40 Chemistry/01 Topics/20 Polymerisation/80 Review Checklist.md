# 20 Polymerisation - Review Checklist

## Recall

- I can describe addition polymerisation as the joining of many alkene monomers by opening the $\ce{C=C}$ bond.
- I know poly(ethene) is formed from ethene.
- I know poly(chloroethene), PVC, is formed from chloroethene.
- I know addition polymerisation does not eliminate a small molecule.

## Drawing Skills

- I can identify the double bond in an alkene monomer.
- I can draw the repeat unit by changing $\ce{C=C}$ to $\ce{C-C}$.
- I can keep side groups attached to the correct carbon.
- I can use brackets, continuation bonds, and $n$ correctly.
- I can draw repeat units for ethene, chloroethene, propene, and similar substituted alkenes.

## Reverse Problems

- I can identify the monomer from a given repeat unit.
- I can restore the double bond between the two backbone carbons.
- I can recognise when a given polymer section may contain more than one monomer type.

## Disposal Issues

- I can state non-biodegradability as a disposal difficulty of poly(alkene)s.
- I can state harmful combustion products as a disposal difficulty.
- I can explain why PVC can give harmful chlorine-containing combustion products.
- I can link persistence to strong carbon-carbon chains and lack of easily hydrolysed groups.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Do not leave $\ce{C=C}$ in an addition polymer repeat unit.
- Do not omit brackets, continuation bonds, or $n$.
- Do not move side groups when drawing repeat units.
- Do not confuse addition polymerisation with condensation polymerisation.
- Do not give vague disposal comments without naming the required issues.

## Ready for the Next Topic When

- I can move both ways between alkene monomers and addition polymer repeat units.
- I can explain polymer disposal issues using syllabus wording.
- I can use polymerisation as one route in the broader AS organic reaction map.
