# 20 Polymerisation - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. A monomer is a small molecule that joins with many similar molecules to form a polymer. A repeat unit is the smallest structural section repeated along the polymer chain. A polymer is a long-chain molecule made from many repeat units.

2. The polymerisation equation is:

   $$
   \ce{n CH2=CH2 -> [-CH2-CH2-]}_n
   $$

   The repeat unit is $\ce{-CH2-CH2-}$.

3. The polymerisation equation is:

   $$
   \ce{n CH2=CHCl -> [-CH2-CHCl-]}_n
   $$

   The polymer is poly(chloroethene), commonly called PVC.

4. The repeat unit is:

   $$
   \ce{n CH2=CHCH3 -> [-CH2-CH(CH3)-]}_n
   $$

5. Restore the double bond between the two backbone carbons:

   $$
   \ce{CH2=CHCH3}
   $$

   The monomer is propene.

6. Restore the double bond between the two backbone carbons:

   $$
   \ce{CH2=C(CH3)2}
   $$

7. The $\ce{C=C}$ double bond opens during addition polymerisation. This allows each monomer to form new single bonds to neighbouring monomer units in the polymer chain.

8. Poly(alkene)s are often non-biodegradable, so they persist in the environment. They can also produce harmful combustion products when burned.

9. PVC contains chlorine. When burned, it can form acidic and harmful chlorine-containing gases such as hydrogen chloride, especially under poor combustion conditions.

10. In addition polymerisation the alkene double bond opens and becomes single bonds in the polymer chain. The repeat unit of poly(ethene) should be $\ce{-CH2-CH2-}$, not $\ce{-CH=CH-}$.
