# 24 Electrochemistry

## Core Idea

A Level electrochemistry turns redox ideas into quantitative predictions. It covers electrolysis, electrode potentials, cell potentials, concentration effects, and the thermodynamic link between electrical work and Gibbs free energy.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 24 Electrochemistry
- 24.1 Electrolysis
- 24.2 Standard electrode potentials, standard cell potentials and the Nernst equation

## What to Learn

- Predict products of electrolysis from electrolyte state, redox series position, and concentration.
- Use $Q = It$ and the Faraday constant to calculate amounts liberated during electrolysis.
- Describe the standard hydrogen electrode and how standard electrode potentials are measured.
- Combine electrode potentials to find a standard cell potential and predict feasibility.
- Use half-equations, the Nernst equation, and $\Delta G^\circ = -nE^\circ_{\text{cell}}F$ in suitable contexts.

## How to Study

- Write oxidation and reduction half-equations before assigning electrodes.
- Track electron flow separately from ion movement.
- State when conditions are standard and when concentration changes must be considered.
- Link sign, polarity, feasibility, and direction of electron flow in one consistent diagram.

## Practice Directions

- Calculate masses or gas volumes produced during electrolysis.
- Build simple cell diagrams from two half-cells.
- Calculate $E^\circ_{\text{cell}}$ and use it to predict redox feasibility.
- Use concentration changes qualitatively and, where required, quantitatively with the Nernst equation.

## Common Traps

- Switching anode and cathode meanings between electrolysis and cells without checking oxidation and reduction.
- Reversing a half-equation but forgetting to reverse the sign of its electrode potential.
- Counting charge in coulombs but failing to convert to moles of electrons.
- Applying standard electrode potentials to non-standard concentrations without comment.
