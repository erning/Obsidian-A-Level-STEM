# Nitrogen and Sulfur - Key Practice Problems

Keep answers out of this file during active recall. Use [31 Key Practice Solutions](31%20Key%20Practice%20Solutions.md) only after attempting the questions.

## Questions

1. Explain why nitrogen gas is relatively unreactive at room temperature.

2. Define a Bronsted-Lowry base and use an equation to show that ammonia acts as a base in water.

3. Explain how the ammonium ion forms from ammonia and a proton. Include the shape of the ammonium ion.

4. Write an equation for ammonia gas reacting with hydrogen chloride gas. State the observation.

5. Write ionic and full equations for ammonia being displaced from ammonium chloride by sodium hydroxide.

6. State two natural and two man-made sources or occurrences of oxides of nitrogen.

7. Write equations showing how $\ce{NO}$ and $\ce{NO2}$ can form in high-temperature combustion.

8. Write an equation for catalytic removal of $\ce{NO}$ using carbon monoxide. Identify which element is oxidised and which is reduced.

9. Explain how $\ce{NO}$ and $\ce{NO2}$ are involved in formation of PAN in photochemical smog.

10. Write an equation showing the direct formation of nitric acid from nitrogen dioxide in the atmosphere.

11. Write equations showing how sulfur dioxide can form sulfuric acid in acid rain.

12. Explain, with equations, how $\ce{NO}$ and $\ce{NO2}$ catalyse the oxidation of atmospheric sulfur dioxide.
