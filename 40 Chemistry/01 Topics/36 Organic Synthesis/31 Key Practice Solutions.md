# 36 Organic Synthesis - Key Practice Solutions

1. Nitrate benzene using concentrated $\ce{HNO3}$ and concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ at $25$ to $60\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + HNO3 ->[conc. H2SO4][25-60 ^\circ C] C6H5NO2 + H2O}
$$

2. First nitrate benzene to nitrobenzene. Then reduce nitrobenzene with hot $\ce{Sn}$ and concentrated $\ce{HCl}$, followed by $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$.

3. React phenylamine with $\ce{HNO2}$, or $\ce{NaNO2}$ and dilute acid, below $10\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to form a diazonium salt. Warm with water to give phenol.

4. Use hot alkaline $\ce{KMnO4}$ followed by dilute acid.

$$
\ce{C6H5CH3 ->[hot alkaline KMnO4][then dilute acid] C6H5COOH}
$$

5. Convert benzoic acid to benzoyl chloride using $\ce{SOCl2}$, $\ce{PCl5}$, or $\ce{PCl3}$ with heat. Then react benzoyl chloride with ethanol at room temperature.

$$
\ce{C6H5COCl + CH3CH2OH -> C6H5COOCH2CH3 + HCl}
$$

6. Substitute bromine with cyanide using ethanolic $\ce{KCN}$ and heat:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CN}
$$

Then reduce the nitrile with $\ce{LiAlH4}$ or $\ce{H2/Ni}$:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CN ->[LiAlH4] CH3CH2CH2NH2}
$$

The cyanide step adds one carbon.

7. Ethanoyl chloride reacts with phenol to form phenyl ethanoate and $\ce{HCl}$.

$$
\ce{CH3COCl + C6H5OH -> CH3COOC6H5 + HCl}
$$

8. The $\ce{-COCl}$ group is likely to react with ammonia at room temperature, forming an amide. The aromatic ring is not the main reacting site under these conditions.

9. First step: nucleophilic substitution by cyanide, using ethanolic $\ce{KCN}$ and heat. Second step: reduction of the nitrile to a primary amine, using $\ce{LiAlH4}$ or $\ce{H2/Ni}$.

10. Topic 37 techniques can check product identity and purity. TLC can compare starting material and product spots, gas / liquid chromatography can estimate mixture composition, and NMR can confirm carbon and proton environments in the product.
