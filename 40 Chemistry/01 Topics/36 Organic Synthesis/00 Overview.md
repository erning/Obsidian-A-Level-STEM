# 36 Organic Synthesis

## Core Idea

Organic synthesis brings the A Level organic course together. The aim is to identify functional groups, predict reactions, devise multi-step routes, and analyse the reagents, reaction types, and possible by-products in a given route.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 36 Organic synthesis
- 36.1 Organic synthesis

## What to Learn

- Identify organic functional groups using reactions from the syllabus.
- Predict properties and reactions of molecules containing several functional groups.
- Devise multi-step synthetic routes using known syllabus reactions.
- Analyse a synthetic route by naming each reaction type, reagent, condition, and possible by-product.

## How to Study

- Make a reaction map organised by functional group, not by topic number alone.
- Work backwards from the target molecule to plausible precursors.
- Check carbon skeleton changes separately from functional-group changes.
- Keep conditions visible; a route is incomplete without them.

## Practice Directions

- Build two-step and three-step routes between named organic compounds.
- Identify functional groups in unfamiliar molecules and predict likely reactions.
- Fill missing reagents or intermediates in a synthetic sequence.
- Critique a proposed route for selectivity, by-products, or unsupported reaction steps.

## Common Traps

- Proposing a route that changes the functional group but not the carbon skeleton correctly.
- Omitting reagents, conditions, or by-products.
- Using reactions outside the syllabus without justification.
- Ignoring competing functional groups in molecules with several reactive sites.
