# 36 Organic Synthesis - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Benzene to Phenylamine

Devise a route from benzene to phenylamine.

Step 1: nitration of benzene.

$$
\ce{C6H6 + HNO3 ->[conc. H2SO4][25-60 ^\circ C] C6H5NO2 + H2O}
$$

Step 2: reduction of nitrobenzene.

$$
\ce{C6H5NO2 ->[Sn/concentrated HCl][then NaOH(aq)] C6H5NH2}
$$

Check: this route uses electrophilic substitution followed by reduction.

## Example 2: Methylbenzene to Phenyl Benzoate

Suggest a route from methylbenzene to phenyl benzoate, assuming phenol is available.

Step 1: oxidise the side-chain to benzoic acid.

$$
\ce{C6H5CH3 ->[hot alkaline KMnO4][then dilute acid] C6H5COOH}
$$

Step 2: convert benzoic acid to benzoyl chloride.

$$
\ce{C6H5COOH ->[SOCl2] C6H5COCl}
$$

Step 3: react benzoyl chloride with phenol.

$$
\ce{C6H5COCl + C6H5OH -> C6H5COOC6H5 + HCl}
$$

Check: the final step is addition-elimination at the acyl chloride, forming an ester.

## Example 3: Bromoethane to Propylamine

Devise a route from bromoethane to propylamine.

The target has one more carbon than bromoethane, so use a nitrile intermediate.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CN}
$$

Then reduce:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CN ->[LiAlH4] CH3CH2CH2NH2}
$$

Check: cyanide substitution adds one carbon.

## Example 4: Choosing the Reactive Functional Group

Predict the main organic product when $\ce{C6H5COCl}$ reacts with ethanol at room temperature.

The acyl chloride group reacts rapidly with ethanol to form an ester. The aromatic ring remains unchanged.

$$
\ce{C6H5COCl + CH3CH2OH -> C6H5COOCH2CH3 + HCl}
$$

Check: identify the most reactive functional group before choosing the reaction.

## Example 5: Analysing a Route

A route converts benzene to nitrobenzene, then to phenylamine, then to a diazonium salt, then to phenol. Name the reaction type for each step.

Benzene to nitrobenzene: electrophilic substitution.

Nitrobenzene to phenylamine: reduction.

Phenylamine to diazonium salt: diazotisation.

Diazonium salt to phenol: substitution by water on warming, with nitrogen gas released.

Check: route analysis asks for reaction type as well as reagents.

## Example 6: By-Product Awareness

In the reaction of ethanoyl chloride with methylamine, identify the organic product and the small-molecule by-product.

The organic product is an amide:

$$
\ce{CH3COCl + CH3NH2 -> CH3CONHCH3 + HCl}
$$

The small-molecule by-product is $\ce{HCl}$.

Check: acyl chloride reactions commonly eliminate $\ce{HCl}$.
