# 36 Organic Synthesis - Key Practice Problems

1. Devise a route from benzene to nitrobenzene, giving reagents and conditions.

2. Devise a route from benzene to phenylamine.

3. Devise a route from phenylamine to phenol.

4. Devise a route from methylbenzene to benzoic acid.

5. Devise a route from benzoic acid to ethyl benzoate.

6. Devise a route from bromoethane to propylamine and explain the carbon-count change.

7. Predict the product when ethanoyl chloride reacts with phenol, and name the by-product.

8. A molecule contains $\ce{-COCl}$ and an aromatic ring. Explain which part is likely to react with ammonia at room temperature.

9. For the route $\ce{CH3CH2Br -> CH3CH2CN -> CH3CH2CH2NH2}$, name the reaction type and reagent for each step.

10. Explain how analytical techniques from Topic 37 could support a synthesis route after the product is made.
