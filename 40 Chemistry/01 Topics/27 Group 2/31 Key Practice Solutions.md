# 27 Group 2 - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. The general equation is:

$$
\ce{MCO3(s) -> MO(s) + CO2(g)}
$$

2. The general equation is:

$$
\ce{2M(NO3)2(s) -> 2MO(s) + 4NO2(g) + O2(g)}
$$

3. The order of increasing thermal stability is:

$$
\ce{MgCO3} < \ce{CaCO3} < \ce{SrCO3} < \ce{BaCO3}
$$

Down Group 2, $\ce{M^{2+}}$ ionic radius increases and charge density decreases. The cation polarises the large carbonate ion, $\ce{CO3^{2-}}$, less strongly. The carbonate ion is less destabilised, so a higher temperature is needed for decomposition.

4. $\ce{Mg^{2+}}$ has a smaller ionic radius and higher charge density than $\ce{Ba^{2+}}$. It polarises the nitrate ion, $\ce{NO3-}$, more strongly, so the nitrate ion is more destabilised and decomposes more easily on heating.

5. A complete explanation is: down Group 2, the $\ce{M^{2+}}$ ion becomes larger, so its charge density decreases. It polarises the large carbonate or nitrate anion less strongly. The anion is less distorted and less destabilised, so the compound is more thermally stable.

6. The solubility of Group 2 hydroxides increases down the group:

$$
\ce{Mg(OH)2} < \ce{Ca(OH)2} < \ce{Sr(OH)2} < \ce{Ba(OH)2}
$$

where the order is from least soluble to most soluble.

7. Down Group 2, the magnitude of the lattice energy decreases and the hydration enthalpy of $\ce{M^{2+}}$ becomes less exothermic. For hydroxides, the decrease in lattice energy is more important than the decrease in hydration enthalpy, so $\Delta H^\circ_\mathrm{sol}$ becomes more favourable and solubility increases.

8. The solubility of Group 2 sulfates decreases down the group:

$$
\ce{MgSO4} > \ce{CaSO4} > \ce{SrSO4} > \ce{BaSO4}
$$

where the order is from most soluble to least soluble.

9. Down Group 2, the hydration enthalpy of $\ce{M^{2+}}$ becomes less exothermic and the lattice energy decreases in magnitude. For sulfates, the decrease in hydration enthalpy is more important than the decrease in lattice energy, so $\Delta H^\circ_\mathrm{sol}$ becomes less favourable and solubility decreases.

10. $\ce{BaSO4}$ is near the bottom of the Group 2 sulfate series, and sulfate solubility decreases down Group 2. The $\ce{Ba^{2+}}$ ion has relatively low hydration enthalpy compared with smaller Group 2 ions. Hydration does not compensate enough for separating the lattice, so $\ce{BaSO4}$ is very sparingly soluble and precipitates.

11. The completed table is:

| Statement | Hydroxides, sulfates, or both? |
|---|---|
| Solubility increases down Group 2. | Hydroxides |
| Solubility decreases down Group 2. | Sulfates |
| Hydration enthalpy becomes less exothermic down Group 2. | Both |
| Lattice energy decreases in magnitude down Group 2. | Both |

12. Compare lattice energy and enthalpy change of hydration. The sign of $\Delta H^\circ_\mathrm{sol}$ describes the overall balance, but a full explanation must say why that balance changes down Group 2. CAIE expects the explanation in terms of the relative magnitudes of hydration enthalpy and lattice energy.
