# 27 Group 2 - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. Write the general equation for the thermal decomposition of a Group 2 carbonate, $\ce{MCO3}$.

2. Write the general equation for the thermal decomposition of a Group 2 nitrate, $\ce{M(NO3)2}$.

3. Arrange $\ce{MgCO3}$, $\ce{CaCO3}$, $\ce{SrCO3}$, and $\ce{BaCO3}$ in order of increasing thermal stability. Explain your order.

4. Explain why $\ce{Mg(NO3)2}$ decomposes more easily than $\ce{Ba(NO3)2}$.

5. A student writes: "Thermal stability increases down Group 2 because the atoms get bigger." Rewrite this as a complete A Level explanation.

6. State the solubility trend down Group 2 for the hydroxides $\ce{M(OH)2}$.

7. Explain the solubility trend of Group 2 hydroxides using lattice energy and enthalpy change of hydration.

8. State the solubility trend down Group 2 for the sulfates $\ce{MSO4}$.

9. Explain the solubility trend of Group 2 sulfates using lattice energy and enthalpy change of hydration.

10. A data table shows that $\ce{BaSO4}$ forms a dense white precipitate when solutions containing $\ce{Ba^{2+}}$ and $\ce{SO4^{2-}}$ are mixed. Use the Group 2 sulfate trend to explain this observation.

11. For each statement, identify whether it applies to Group 2 hydroxides, Group 2 sulfates, or both.

| Statement | Hydroxides, sulfates, or both? |
|---|---|
| Solubility increases down Group 2. |  |
| Solubility decreases down Group 2. |  |
| Hydration enthalpy becomes less exothermic down Group 2. |  |
| Lattice energy decreases in magnitude down Group 2. |  |

12. A question gives a set of $\Delta H^\circ_\mathrm{sol}$ values and asks for a qualitative explanation of a solubility trend. What two energy terms should you compare, and why is the sign of $\Delta H^\circ_\mathrm{sol}$ not enough by itself for a full explanation?
