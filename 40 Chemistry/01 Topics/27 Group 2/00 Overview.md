# 27 Group 2

## Core Idea

A Level Group 2 chemistry extends AS trend work by explaining thermal stability and solubility through ionic radius, polarisation, lattice energy, and hydration enthalpy.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.

- No local Chemistry coursebook has been verified for this topic, so this topic does not claim coursebook alignment.

## What to Learn

- Describe and explain trends in the thermal stability of Group 2 nitrates and carbonates.
- Use ionic radius and polarisation to explain changes in thermal stability.
- Describe solubility trends for Group 2 hydroxides and sulfates.
- Explain solubility and enthalpy change of solution using lattice energy and enthalpy change of hydration.

## How to Study

- Keep the direction magnesium to barium explicit in every trend.
- Pair each observation with the particle-level explanation that causes it.
- Use energy-cycle language from chemical energetics when discussing solution enthalpy.
- Compare hydroxides and sulfates separately; their solubility trends are not interchangeable.

## Practice Directions

- Explain thermal decomposition trends for nitrates and carbonates.
- Compare solubility trends for hydroxides and sulfates.
- Use lattice energy and hydration enthalpy qualitatively to account for solution behaviour.
- Write concise trend explanations using radius, charge density, and polarisation.

## Common Traps

- Mixing up the solubility trends of hydroxides and sulfates.
- Explaining thermal stability without mentioning polarisation of the anion.
- Treating larger ionic radius as having the same effect in lattice energy, hydration enthalpy, and polarisation.
- Giving only the trend and not the reason for the trend.
