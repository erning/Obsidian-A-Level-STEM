# 16 Hydroxy Compounds

## Core Idea

Hydroxy compounds at AS focus on alcohols: how they are made, classified and converted into halogenoalkanes, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids, alkenes and esters.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 16 Hydroxy compounds.
- 16.1 Alcohols.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions; practical observations may include oxidation and characteristic tests.

## What to Learn

- Recall production routes for alcohols from alkenes, halogenoalkanes, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids and esters.
- Describe alcohol reactions: combustion, substitution, sodium, oxidation, dehydration and ester formation.
- Classify alcohols as primary, secondary or tertiary.
- Use characteristic oxidation observations to distinguish alcohol classes.
- Deduce the presence of a methyl alcohol group from the tri-iodomethane reaction where required.

## How to Study

- Make alcohols a hub in the organic reaction map.
- Learn oxidation conditions with the apparatus idea: distillation for aldehydes and reflux for carboxylic acids.
- Keep classification tied to the carbon bearing the hydroxyl group.

## Practice Directions

- Predict products from alcohol oxidation, dehydration and substitution.
- Choose reagents and conditions for preparing an alcohol.
- Identify primary, secondary and tertiary alcohols from structural formulae.

## Common Traps

- Oxidising tertiary alcohols under AS conditions.
- Forgetting the difference between distillation and reflux in primary alcohol oxidation.
- Calling the whole molecule a hydroxyl group instead of identifying the functional group.
