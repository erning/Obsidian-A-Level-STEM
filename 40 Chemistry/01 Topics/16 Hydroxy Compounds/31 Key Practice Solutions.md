# 16 Hydroxy Compounds - Key Practice Solutions

1. Classifications:
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CH2OH}$: primary.
   - $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH2CH3}$: secondary.
   - $\ce{(CH3)2C(OH)CH2CH3}$: tertiary, because the $\ce{-OH}$ carbon is bonded to three carbon groups.

2. Use steam and $\ce{H3PO4}$ catalyst.

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 + H2O -> CH3CH2OH}
$$

3. Use cold dilute acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$.

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 + [O] + H2O -> HOCH2CH2OH}
$$

4. The product is ethanol.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br + OH- -> CH3CH2OH + Br-}
$$

5. Reduction products:
   - Ethanal gives ethanol, $\ce{CH3CH2OH}$.
   - Propanone gives propan-2-ol, $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3}$.
   - Ethanoic acid gives ethanol, $\ce{CH3CH2OH}$.

6. Sodium reaction:

$$
\ce{2CH3CH2OH + 2Na -> 2CH3CH2ONa + H2}
$$

7. Butan-1-ol oxidation:
   - Under distillation: butanal, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CHO}$.
   - Under reflux: butanoic acid, $\ce{CH3CH2CH2COOH}$.

8. There is no orange to green colour change with acidified dichromate(VI), because a tertiary alcohol is not oxidised under AS conditions.

9. Ethene is formed:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2OH -> CH2=CH2 + H2O}
$$

10. The ester is propyl ethanoate, $\ce{CH3COOCH2CH2CH3}$.

11. Ethanol and butan-2-ol give a positive tri-iodomethane test because they contain the $\ce{CH3CH(OH)-R}$ pattern, taking $\ce{R=H}$ for ethanol. Propan-1-ol does not.
