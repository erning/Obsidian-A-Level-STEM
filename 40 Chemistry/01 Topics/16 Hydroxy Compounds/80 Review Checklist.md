# 16 Hydroxy Compounds - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Recognise the hydroxyl group in alcohols.
- Classify primary, secondary and tertiary alcohols, including molecules with more than one $\ce{-OH}$ group.
- Recall alcohol preparation routes from alkenes, halogenoalkanes, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids and esters.
- State reagents for converting alcohols to halogenoalkanes.
- State oxidation reagents and the orange to green dichromate(VI) observation.
- State dehydration conditions and esterification conditions.
- Recall the tri-iodomethane test for $\ce{CH3CH(OH)-R}$.

## Skills

- Predict oxidation products using alcohol class and apparatus.
- Choose distillation to obtain aldehydes from primary alcohols.
- Choose reflux to obtain carboxylic acids from primary alcohols.
- Predict ketones from secondary alcohol oxidation.
- Explain why tertiary alcohols resist oxidation under AS conditions.
- Write equations for sodium reactions and esterification.
- Use alkaline $\ce{I2(aq)}$ observations to identify the required alcohol group.

## Common Errors

- Classifying alcohols by total chain length instead of the $\ce{-OH}$ carbon.
- Forgetting that primary alcohols can oxidise further than aldehydes.
- Using reflux when the intended product is an aldehyde.
- Treating every alcohol as tri-iodomethane-positive.
- Confusing dehydration to alkenes with oxidation to carbonyl compounds.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can use alcohols as a central conversion point between alkenes, halogenoalkanes, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids and esters.
- You can explain observations for oxidation and tri-iodomethane tests.
- You can choose reagents and conditions without mixing up substitution, oxidation, dehydration and esterification.
