# 16 Hydroxy Compounds - Worked Examples

## Example 1: Classifying Alcohols

Classify $\ce{CH3CH2OH}$, $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3}$ and $\ce{(CH3)3COH}$.

In ethanol, the $\ce{-OH}$ carbon is attached to one other carbon, so it is primary.

In propan-2-ol, the $\ce{-OH}$ carbon is attached to two other carbons, so it is secondary.

In 2-methylpropan-2-ol, the $\ce{-OH}$ carbon is attached to three other carbons, so it is tertiary.

Check: the classification uses the carbon bearing $\ce{-OH}$, not the number of carbon atoms in the whole molecule.

## Example 2: Preparing Ethanol from Ethene

State reagents and conditions for converting ethene to ethanol.

Use steam with a phosphoric acid catalyst:

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 + H2O -> CH3CH2OH}
$$

Conditions: $\ce{H2O(g)}$ and $\ce{H3PO4}$ catalyst.

Check: this is electrophilic addition of steam to an alkene, not hydrolysis.

## Example 3: Oxidising a Primary Alcohol

Predict products when propan-1-ol is heated with acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$ under distillation and under reflux.

With distillation, the aldehyde is removed as it forms:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2OH + [O] -> CH3CH2CHO + H2O}
$$

The product is propanal.

With reflux, further oxidation occurs:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2OH + 2[O] -> CH3CH2COOH + H2O}
$$

The product is propanoic acid.

Check: the oxidising agent is the same; the apparatus controls how far oxidation proceeds.

## Example 4: Oxidising Secondary and Tertiary Alcohols

Compare propan-2-ol and 2-methylpropan-2-ol with acidified $\ce{K2Cr2O7}$.

Propan-2-ol is secondary, so it oxidises to propanone:

$$
\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3 + [O] -> CH3COCH3 + H2O}
$$

The orange dichromate(VI) solution turns green.

2-methylpropan-2-ol is tertiary, so it is not oxidised under AS conditions. There is no orange to green colour change.

Check: tertiary alcohols lack a hydrogen atom on the carbon bearing $\ce{-OH}$.

## Example 5: Substitution to a Halogenoalkane

Give the product when ethanol reacts with $\ce{PCl5}$.

The hydroxyl group is substituted by chlorine:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2OH + PCl5 -> CH3CH2Cl + POCl3 + HCl}
$$

The organic product is chloroethane.

Check: this is not oxidation; the carbon skeleton is unchanged.

## Example 6: Dehydration

Butan-2-ol is heated with concentrated acid. Give one alkene product.

Dehydration removes water and forms a carbon-carbon double bond. One possible product is but-2-ene:

$$
\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH2CH3 -> CH3CH=CHCH3 + H2O}
$$

But-1-ene may also form depending on which adjacent hydrogen is removed.

Check: dehydration is elimination.

## Example 7: Esterification

Name the ester formed from ethanol and ethanoic acid.

The alcohol part gives ethyl. The carboxylic acid part gives ethanoate.

$$
\ce{CH3COOH + CH3CH2OH <=> CH3COOCH2CH3 + H2O}
$$

The ester is ethyl ethanoate.

Check: in ester names, the alkyl group from the alcohol is named first.

## Example 8: Tri-Iodomethane Test

Which alcohol gives a positive tri-iodomethane test: propan-1-ol or propan-2-ol?

The required group is $\ce{CH3CH(OH)-R}$. Propan-2-ol is $\ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3}$, so it contains this group and gives a yellow precipitate of $\ce{CHI3}$ with alkaline $\ce{I2(aq)}$.

Propan-1-ol is $\ce{CH3CH2CH2OH}$ and does not contain the required group.

Check: the positive result identifies the local group, not simply any alcohol.
