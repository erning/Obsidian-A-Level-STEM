# Reaction Kinetics - Key Practice Problems

1. Define rate of reaction.

2. Define frequency of collisions, effective collision and non-effective collision.

3. The volume of gas produced in a reaction increases from $5.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ at $10\ \mathrm{s}$ to $29.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ at $70\ \mathrm{s}$. Calculate the average rate over this interval.

4. A reactant concentration falls from $0.800\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$ to $0.500\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}$ in $120\ \mathrm{s}$. Calculate the average rate of disappearance of the reactant.

5. Explain why increasing the concentration of a reactant in solution usually increases reaction rate.

6. Explain why increasing the pressure of gaseous reactants usually increases reaction rate.

7. Define activation energy, $E_A$.

8. Use the Boltzmann distribution to explain why increasing temperature increases reaction rate.

9. A reaction has reactants at $40\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, products at $95\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$, and a peak at $150\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$. Calculate $\Delta H$ and $E_A$, and state whether the reaction is exothermic or endothermic.

10. Explain how a catalyst increases rate in terms of mechanism, activation energy and the Boltzmann distribution.

11. State whether each catalyst is homogeneous or heterogeneous:
    - $\ce{V2O5(s)}$ in the Contact process with gaseous reactants.
    - $\ce{H+(aq)}$ catalysing an aqueous ester reaction.

12. A catalyst is added to a reversible reaction at equilibrium. State and explain the effect on the rate of reaching equilibrium, the equilibrium position and the value of $K$.
