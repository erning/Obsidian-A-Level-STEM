# Reaction Kinetics - Key Practice Solutions

1. Rate of reaction is the change in amount, concentration, mass or volume of a reactant or product per unit time.

2. Frequency of collisions is how often reacting particles collide per unit time. An effective collision leads to reaction. A non-effective collision does not lead to reaction.

3. Change in gas volume:

$$
29.0 - 5.0 = 24.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}
$$

Change in time:

$$
70 - 10 = 60\ \mathrm{s}
$$

Average rate:

$$
\frac{24.0}{60} = 0.400\ \mathrm{cm^3\,s^{-1}}
$$

4. Change in concentration:

$$
0.800 - 0.500 = 0.300\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}}
$$

Average rate of disappearance:

$$
\frac{0.300}{120} = 2.50 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol\,dm^{-3}\,s^{-1}}
$$

5. Increasing concentration gives more reacting particles per unit volume. Collisions occur more frequently, so the frequency of effective collisions increases. The reaction rate increases.

6. Increasing pressure for gases places more particles in a given volume. Gas particles are closer together and collide more frequently, so the frequency of effective collisions increases. The reaction rate increases.

7. Activation energy, $E_A$, is the minimum energy required for a collision to be effective.

8. At higher temperature, particles have greater kinetic energy and collide slightly more frequently. More importantly, the Boltzmann distribution becomes lower and broader, with a larger area to the right of $E_A$. A greater fraction of particles has energy at least equal to $E_A$, so the frequency of effective collisions increases and the rate increases.

9. Enthalpy change:

$$
\Delta H = 95 - 40 = +55\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}
$$

Activation energy:

$$
E_A = 150 - 40 = 110\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}
$$

The reaction is endothermic because products are at higher enthalpy than reactants.

10. A catalyst provides a different reaction mechanism with lower activation energy. On a Boltzmann distribution, this means the $E_A$ line is effectively moved to lower energy, so a larger fraction of particles has enough energy to react. The frequency of effective collisions increases, so rate increases.

11. $\ce{V2O5(s)}$ with gaseous reactants is a heterogeneous catalyst because it is in a different physical state. $\ce{H+(aq)}$ in an aqueous reaction is a homogeneous catalyst because it is in the same phase as the reacting mixture.

12. The catalyst increases the rates of the forward and reverse reactions, so equilibrium is reached faster. It does not change the equilibrium position and does not change $K$, because it lowers activation energy for both directions and does not change the relative enthalpies of reactants and products.
