# 21 Organic Synthesis - Worked Examples

## Example 1: One-Step Functional Group Change

**Question.** Convert bromoethane into ethanol. Give the reagent, condition, and reaction type.

**Working.** A halogenoalkane can undergo nucleophilic substitution with aqueous hydroxide.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2Br ->[NaOH(aq),\ heat] CH3CH2OH}
$$

**Answer.** Use $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and heat. The reaction type is nucleophilic substitution.

**Check.** If ethanolic $\ce{NaOH}$ were used instead, elimination to ethene would compete or dominate.

## Example 2: Chain Extension to a Carboxylic Acid

**Question.** Devise a route from 1-bromopropane to butanoic acid.

**Working.** The target has one more carbon than the starting material, so use cyanide substitution followed by nitrile hydrolysis.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br ->[KCN,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2CN}
$$

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CH2CN ->[dilute\ acid,\ heat] CH3CH2CH2COOH}
$$

**Answer.** Step 1: $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat, nucleophilic substitution, to form butanenitrile. Step 2: dilute acid and heat, hydrolysis, to form butanoic acid. Dilute alkali and heat followed by acidification is also acceptable for step 2.

**Check.** The cyanide step is where the carbon count increases.

## Example 3: Making an Ester from an Alkene

**Question.** Suggest a route from ethene to ethyl ethanoate using AS reactions.

**Working.** Ethyl ethanoate needs ethanol and ethanoic acid. Ethene can be hydrated to ethanol. Part of the ethanol can then be oxidised to ethanoic acid. Finally, ethanol and ethanoic acid form the ester.

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 ->[steam,\ H3PO4] CH3CH2OH}
$$

$$
\ce{CH3CH2OH ->[acidified\ K2Cr2O7,\ reflux] CH3COOH}
$$

$$
\ce{CH3COOH + CH3CH2OH <=>[conc.\ H2SO4] CH3COOCH2CH3 + H2O}
$$

**Answer.** Hydrate ethene to ethanol; oxidise some ethanol under reflux to ethanoic acid; esterify ethanol with ethanoic acid using concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ catalyst.

**Check.** The oxidation step must use reflux because the target is the carboxylic acid, not ethanal.

## Example 4: Choosing Conditions for Competing Reactions

**Question.** A student wants to convert 2-bromopropane into propene. Should they use aqueous or ethanolic sodium hydroxide?

**Working.** Aqueous $\ce{NaOH}$ favours nucleophilic substitution to form propan-2-ol. Ethanolic $\ce{NaOH}$ with heat favours elimination to form the alkene.

$$
\ce{CH3CHBrCH3 ->[NaOH,\ ethanol,\ heat] CH3CH=CH2}
$$

**Answer.** Use $\ce{NaOH}$ in ethanol and heat. The reaction type is elimination.

**Check.** The desired product has a $\ce{C=C}$ bond, so elimination is required.

## Example 5: Identifying Functional Groups from Tests

**Question.** Compound X gives an orange precipitate with 2,4-DNPH reagent. It gives a silver mirror with Tollens' reagent. What functional group is present?

**Working.** 2,4-DNPH detects carbonyl compounds, meaning aldehydes or ketones. Tollens' reagent distinguishes aldehydes from ketones: aldehydes give a silver mirror or black precipitate.

**Answer.** X contains an aldehyde group.

**Check.** The two tests together are stronger than either test alone.

## Example 6: Analysing a Given Route

**Question.** A route converts propan-1-ol to propene using concentrated sulfuric acid and heat, then converts propene to 2-bromopropane using hydrogen bromide at room temperature. State the reaction type for each step and one possible issue in the second step.

**Working.** Alcohol to alkene is dehydration, an elimination reaction. Alkene to halogenoalkane with $\ce{HBr}$ is electrophilic addition. Propene is unsymmetrical, so addition could in principle give positional products; the major product is explained by carbocation stability and Markovnikov addition.

**Answer.** Step 1: elimination / dehydration. Step 2: electrophilic addition. A possible issue is formation of more than one positional product, though 2-bromopropane is expected as the major product.

**Check.** Route analysis includes reaction type and possible by-products, as required by the topic.

## Example 7: Preserving a Functional Group

**Question.** Suggest a reagent to reduce propanal to propan-1-ol without planning an oxidation route. What reaction type is this?

**Working.** Aldehydes are reduced to primary alcohols using $\ce{NaBH4}$ or $\ce{LiAlH4}$.

$$
\ce{CH3CH2CHO + 2[H] -> CH3CH2CH2OH}
$$

**Answer.** Use $\ce{NaBH4}$ or $\ce{LiAlH4}$. The reaction type is reduction.

**Check.** $\ce{NaBH4}$ is suitable for aldehydes and ketones; $\ce{LiAlH4}$ is needed for carboxylic acids.
