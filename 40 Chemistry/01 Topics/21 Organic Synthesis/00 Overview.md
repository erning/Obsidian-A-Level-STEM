# 21 Organic Synthesis

## Core Idea

Organic synthesis brings the AS organic topics together. The aim is to recognise functional groups, predict reactions and design or analyse short multi-step routes.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 21 Organic synthesis.
- 21.1 Organic synthesis.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Identify organic functional groups using reactions in the AS syllabus.
- Predict properties and reactions of molecules containing several functional groups.
- Devise multi-step synthetic routes using known AS reactions.
- Analyse a given synthetic route in terms of reaction type, reagents and possible by-products.

## How to Study

- Build one reaction map that links all AS functional groups.
- Work backwards from the target molecule to likely precursors, then check if each step is in the syllabus.
- For every step, write reagent, condition, reaction type and product.

## Practice Directions

- Identify functional groups in unfamiliar molecules.
- Plan two-step or three-step routes between AS functional groups.
- Annotate a given synthesis with reaction types, reagents and by-products.

## Common Traps

- Planning a route with reactions outside the AS syllabus when the question expects AS chemistry.
- Giving reagents without conditions.
- Ignoring possible by-products in a proposed route.
