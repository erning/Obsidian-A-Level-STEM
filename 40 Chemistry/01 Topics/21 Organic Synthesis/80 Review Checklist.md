# 21 Organic Synthesis - Review Checklist

## Functional Group Recognition

- I can identify alkanes, alkenes, halogenoalkanes, alcohols, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, esters, primary amines, nitriles, and hydroxynitriles.
- I can use aqueous bromine as evidence for $\ce{C=C}$.
- I can use 2,4-DNPH, Fehling's reagent, and Tollens' reagent to distinguish carbonyl compounds.
- I can use carbonate reactions as evidence for carboxylic acids.
- I can connect infrared and mass-spectrum evidence to structure when it is provided.

## Route Planning

- I can count carbon atoms before choosing a route.
- I can identify which functional group changes in each step.
- I can choose substitution, elimination, addition, oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis, condensation, or polymerisation as needed.
- I can insert intermediates when no one-step AS route exists.
- I can use nitriles to increase carbon chain length by one.

## Reagents and Conditions

- I can distinguish $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ and heat from ethanolic $\ce{NaOH}$ and heat.
- I can distinguish oxidation by distillation from oxidation by reflux.
- I can choose $\ce{NaBH4}$ or $\ce{LiAlH4}$ for aldehydes and ketones, but $\ce{LiAlH4}$ for carboxylic acids.
- I can give $\ce{KCN}$ in ethanol and heat for nitrile formation.
- I can give $\ce{NH3}$ in ethanol heated under pressure for primary amine formation.
- I can give concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$ as catalyst for esterification.

## Route Analysis

- I can state the reaction type for each step of a route.
- I can identify possible by-products when they are chemically relevant.
- I can explain substitution versus elimination competition in halogenoalkanes.
- I can explain why free-radical substitution can give mixtures.
- I can identify water as a condensation by-product in esterification.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Do not use reactions outside the AS syllabus unless the question provides them.
- Do not omit conditions such as heat, reflux, ethanol, pressure, catalyst, or acidification.
- Do not forget carbon count changes when $\ce{CN-}$ is introduced.
- Do not use $\ce{NaBH4}$ for carboxylic acids.
- Do not ignore other functional groups in the molecule.
- Do not call a route complete if it lacks reagents and conditions.

## Ready for Analysis When

- I can explain a multi-step route in words, equations, reagents, conditions, and reaction types.
- I can use qualitative tests and spectroscopy as evidence for the product of a synthesis.
- I can check that a proposed product fits both the reaction chemistry and the analytical data.
