# 21 Organic Synthesis - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. Convert chloroethane into ethanol. Give the reagent, condition, and reaction type.

2. Convert chloroethane into ethene. Give the reagent, condition, and reaction type.

3. Devise a two-step route from 1-bromoethane to propanoic acid. Include reagents, conditions, and reaction types.

4. Devise a route from ethene to ethyl ethanoate using AS reactions.

5. A compound gives an orange precipitate with 2,4-DNPH reagent but no silver mirror with Tollens' reagent. What functional group is suggested?

6. A compound decolourises aqueous bromine and forms a diol with cold dilute acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$. What functional group is suggested?

7. Choose suitable conditions to convert propan-1-ol into propanal. Explain why these conditions differ from those used to make propanoic acid.

8. Analyse this step: $\ce{CH3CH2Br ->[NH3,\ ethanol,\ heat,\ pressure] CH3CH2NH2}$. State the reaction type and any inorganic by-product in a balanced equation.

9. A route uses $\ce{CH3CH2Br ->[KCN] CH3CH2CN ->[hydrolysis] CH3CH2COOH}$. Explain why the product has three carbon atoms.

10. For each transformation, state a suitable reagent and condition: aldehyde to primary alcohol; ketone to secondary alcohol; carboxylic acid to primary alcohol.

11. A student proposes using $\ce{NaBH4}$ to convert ethanoic acid into ethanol. Explain the problem and give the correct reducing agent.

12. A molecule contains both $\ce{C=C}$ and $\ce{-COOH}$. Suggest one reaction that identifies the alkene group and one reaction that identifies the carboxylic acid group.
