# 26 Reaction Kinetics - Key Practice Problems

Answers are intentionally excluded. Use [[40 Chemistry/01 Topics/26 Reaction Kinetics/31 Key Practice Solutions|Key Practice Solutions]] after attempting the questions.

## 1. Definitions

Define rate equation, order of reaction, overall order, rate constant, half-life, rate-determining step, and intermediate.

## 2. Initial Rates

For a reaction involving $\ce{X}$ and $\ce{Y}$:

| Experiment | $[\ce{X}]$ / $\text{mol dm}^{-3}$ | $[\ce{Y}]$ / $\text{mol dm}^{-3}$ | Initial rate / $\text{mol dm}^{-3}\text{ s}^{-1}$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 0.100 | 0.200 | $1.60 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| 2 | 0.200 | 0.200 | $3.20 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| 3 | 0.100 | 0.400 | $6.40 \times 10^{-3}$ |

Deduce the rate equation and overall order.

## 3. Rate Constant

Using the data in Question 2, calculate the value and units of the rate constant.

## 4. Half-Life

A first-order reaction has a half-life of $240\ \text{s}$.

(a) Calculate $k$.

(b) If the initial concentration is $0.640\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$, calculate the concentration after $720\ \text{s}$.

## 5. Graph Interpretation

Describe the expected shape of a graph of rate against concentration for:

(a) a zero-order reaction

(b) a first-order reaction

(c) a second-order reaction

## 6. Mechanism And Rate Equation

The overall reaction is:

$$
\ce{2NO(g) + 2H2(g) -> N2(g) + 2H2O(g)}
$$

A proposed mechanism includes the slow step:

$$
\ce{2NO(g) + H2(g) -> N2O(g) + H2O(g)}
$$

followed by a fast step:

$$
\ce{N2O(g) + H2(g) -> N2(g) + H2O(g)}
$$

(a) Show that the steps add to the overall equation.

(b) Deduce the rate equation predicted by the slow step.

(c) Identify any intermediate.

## 7. Temperature Effect

Explain qualitatively how increasing temperature changes the rate constant and the rate of reaction.

## 8. Catalysts

(a) Explain the mode of action of a heterogeneous catalyst, using iron in the Haber process as the example.

(b) Explain why $\ce{Fe^{2+}}/\ce{Fe^{3+}}$ in the reaction between $\ce{I-}$ and $\ce{S2O8^{2-}}$ is a homogeneous catalyst.
