# 26 Reaction Kinetics

## Core Idea

A Level reaction kinetics explains rate data with rate equations, orders of reaction, rate constants, mechanisms, and catalysts. It links measured rates to a possible rate-determining step.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 26 Reaction kinetics
- 26.1 Simple rate equations, orders of reaction and rate constants
- 26.2 Homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysts

## What to Learn

- Use rate equation, order of reaction, overall order, rate constant, half-life, rate-determining step, and intermediate.
- Deduce orders from initial rates, half-life data, and graphs.
- Calculate rate constants and use the units implied by the overall order.
- Relate a proposed mechanism to the observed rate equation.
- Explain homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysis in suitable examples.

## How to Study

- Let experimental data determine the rate equation; do not copy coefficients from the overall equation.
- Compare one concentration change at a time in initial-rate tables.
- Use graph shapes and half-life behaviour to identify first-order reactions.
- For mechanisms, check that the steps add to the overall reaction and that the slow step matches the rate equation.

## Practice Directions

- Deduce rate equations from initial-rate data.
- Calculate a rate constant from a rate equation or first-order half-life.
- Interpret concentration-time and rate-concentration graphs.
- Identify intermediates, catalysts, and possible rate-determining steps in mechanisms.

## Common Traps

- Assuming reaction orders from stoichiometric coefficients.
- Giving units for $k$ without checking the overall order.
- Treating half-life as concentration-independent for every reaction.
- Calling a species a catalyst when it is not regenerated later in the mechanism.
