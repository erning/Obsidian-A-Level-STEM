# 13 An Introduction to AS Level Organic Chemistry - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Define hydrocarbon, functional group, homologous series, saturated and unsaturated.
- State what $\ce{R}$, $\ce{R'}$ and $\ce{X}$ represent in organic formulae.
- Recognise alkene, halogenoalkane, alcohol, aldehyde, ketone, carboxylic acid, ester, primary amine and nitrile functional groups.
- Explain molecular, empirical, general, structural, displayed and skeletal formulae.
- Define homolytic fission, heterolytic fission, free radical, nucleophile and electrophile.

## Skills

- Name simple aliphatic molecules up to six carbon atoms using CAIE AS functional groups.
- Convert between structural, displayed and skeletal representations.
- Deduce molecular and empirical formulae from structural, displayed or skeletal formulae.
- Classify reactions as addition, substitution, elimination, hydrolysis, condensation, oxidation or reduction.
- Use curly arrows from a bond or lone pair to show electron-pair movement.
- Describe local shapes around $\mathrm{sp}$, $\mathrm{sp^2}$ and $\mathrm{sp^3}$ atoms.
- Identify chain, positional and functional group isomerism.
- Identify cis/trans isomerism and chiral centres.

## Common Errors

- Treating alkanes as if they have a functional group.
- Forgetting that the nitrile carbon is part of the named carbon chain.
- Starting a curly arrow at a positive charge instead of an electron pair.
- Calling every alkene geometrical just because it contains $\ce{C=C}$.
- Missing a chiral centre because the structure is written as a condensed formula.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can look at a formula and immediately locate the functional group.
- You can explain why a reaction is a particular reaction type.
- You can sketch the electron movement expected in an AS mechanism.
- You can list plausible isomers systematically without counting the same structure twice.
