# 13 An Introduction to AS Level Organic Chemistry

## Core Idea

This topic gives the language of AS organic chemistry: functional groups, formulae, naming, reaction types, mechanisms, molecular shape and isomerism.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 13 An introduction to AS Level organic chemistry.
- 13.1 Formulas, functional groups and the naming of organic compounds.
- 13.2 Characteristic organic reactions.
- 13.3 Shapes of organic molecules; sigma and pi bonds.
- 13.4 Isomerism: structural isomerism and stereoisomerism.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Interpret and use general, structural, displayed and skeletal formulae.
- Name simple aliphatic organic molecules with the functional groups in the AS syllabus.
- Use core organic vocabulary: homologous series, saturated, unsaturated, nucleophile, electrophile, addition, substitution, elimination, hydrolysis, condensation, oxidation and reduction.
- Understand curly-arrow mechanisms for the AS reaction types.
- Describe molecular shape, sigma and pi bonding, structural isomerism and stereoisomerism.

## How to Study

- Build a functional group map before studying individual reaction families.
- Draw displayed and skeletal formulae side by side until the same molecule is recognisable in either form.
- For mechanisms, make the electron-pair movement explicit and check that charges are conserved.

## Practice Directions

- Name and draw organic molecules from different formula representations.
- Identify functional groups, reaction types and possible isomers.
- Add curly arrows to simple AS mechanisms and explain what each arrow means.

## Common Traps

- Treating functional groups as labels without connecting them to reactions.
- Drawing curly arrows from atoms instead of from bonds or lone pairs.
- Confusing structural isomerism with stereoisomerism.
