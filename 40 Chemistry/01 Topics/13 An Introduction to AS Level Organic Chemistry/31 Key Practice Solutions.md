# 13 An Introduction to AS Level Organic Chemistry - Key Practice Solutions

1. $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2OH}$ contains $\ce{C4H10O}$, so the molecular formula is $\ce{C4H10O}$. The ratio $4:10:1$ cannot be simplified while keeping whole numbers, so the empirical formula is also $\ce{C4H10O}$.

2. Names:
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br}$: 1-bromopropane.
   - $\ce{CH3COCH2CH3}$: butan-2-one.
   - $\ce{CH3CH2COOH}$: propanoic acid.
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CN}$: propanenitrile. The nitrile carbon is included in the parent chain.

3. Structural formulae:
   - pent-2-ene: $\ce{CH3CH=CHCH2CH3}$.
   - 2-chlorobutane: $\ce{CH3CHClCH2CH3}$.
   - propylamine: $\ce{CH3CH2CH2NH2}$.
   - ethyl ethanoate: $\ce{CH3COOCH2CH3}$.

4. Reaction types:
   - Addition, because $\ce{Br2}$ adds across $\ce{C=C}$.
   - Substitution, more specifically nucleophilic substitution.
   - Elimination, because water is removed and $\ce{C=C}$ forms.
   - Condensation, because two molecules join and water is eliminated; it is also esterification.

5. A nucleophile donates an electron pair to an electron-deficient atom. An electrophile accepts an electron pair. In reaction with a halogenoalkane, $\ce{OH-}$ is the nucleophile because it has a lone pair and a negative charge.

6. Homolytic fission splits a covalent bond so each atom receives one bonding electron, forming radicals. Heterolytic fission gives both bonding electrons to one atom, forming ions.

7. The aldehyde is propanal, $\ce{CH3CH2CHO}$. The ketone is propanone, $\ce{CH3COCH3}$. These are functional group isomers because aldehyde and ketone are different functional groups.

8. Isomerism decisions:
   - But-2-ene can show cis/trans isomerism because each double-bond carbon has $\ce{H}$ and $\ce{CH3}$.
   - 2-methylpropene cannot, because one double-bond carbon has two identical $\ce{CH3}$ groups and the other has two identical $\ce{H}$ atoms.
   - A disubstituted cyclobutane can show cis/trans isomerism if the substituents can be on the same side or opposite sides of the ring. Ring bonding restricts rotation.

9. In $\ce{CH3CHClCH2CH3}$, carbon 2 is chiral because it is attached to $\ce{H}$, $\ce{Cl}$, $\ce{CH3}$ and $\ce{CH2CH3}$.

10. A curly arrow should begin at a bond or a lone pair of electrons. It shows movement of an electron pair.
