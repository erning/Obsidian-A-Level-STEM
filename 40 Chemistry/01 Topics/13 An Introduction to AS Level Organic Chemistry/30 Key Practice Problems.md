# 13 An Introduction to AS Level Organic Chemistry - Key Practice Problems

1. Give the molecular formula and empirical formula of $\ce{CH3CH2CH2CH2OH}$.

2. Name each compound:
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CH2Br}$
   - $\ce{CH3COCH2CH3}$
   - $\ce{CH3CH2COOH}$
   - $\ce{CH3CH2CN}$

3. Draw or describe the structural formula for each compound:
   - pent-2-ene
   - 2-chlorobutane
   - propylamine
   - ethyl ethanoate

4. For each reaction, state the reaction type.
   - $\ce{CH2=CH2 + Br2 -> CH2BrCH2Br}$
   - $\ce{CH3CH2Br + OH- -> CH3CH2OH + Br-}$
   - $\ce{CH3CH2OH -> CH2=CH2 + H2O}$
   - $\ce{CH3COOH + CH3OH -> CH3COOCH3 + H2O}$

5. Define nucleophile and electrophile, then identify the nucleophile in $\ce{OH-}$ reacting with a halogenoalkane.

6. State the difference between homolytic fission and heterolytic fission.

7. Deduce the structural isomers of $\ce{C3H6O}$ that are an aldehyde or a ketone.

8. Decide whether each compound can show cis/trans isomerism, and explain briefly:
   - but-2-ene
   - 2-methylpropene
   - cyclobutane with two adjacent different substituents

9. Identify any chiral centre in $\ce{CH3CHClCH2CH3}$.

10. In a curly-arrow mechanism, where should a curly arrow begin?
