# 01 Atomic Structure

## Core Idea

Atomic structure explains how protons, neutrons and electrons determine the identity of an element, the behaviour of ions and isotopes, and the patterns seen in electronic configuration and ionisation energy.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Physical chemistry: 1 Atomic structure.
- 1.1 Particles in the atom and atomic radius.
- 1.2 Isotopes.
- 1.3 Electrons, energy levels and atomic orbitals.
- 1.4 Ionisation energy.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Describe the relative charge, relative mass and position of protons, neutrons and electrons.
- Use atomic number, mass number and charge to determine particles in atoms and ions.
- Explain isotopes and why isotopes of the same element have the same chemical properties but can have different physical properties.
- Write electronic configurations using shells, sub-shells, orbitals and electrons-in-boxes notation.
- Interpret first and successive ionisation energies using nuclear charge, atomic radius, shielding and electron repulsion.

## How to Study

- Draw the atom as a model with a tiny dense nucleus and electron shells, then connect the model to measured evidence.
- Practise writing configurations from proton number until the order of sub-shell filling becomes automatic.
- Treat ionisation energy graphs as evidence: look for big jumps and explain them using shell structure.

## Practice Directions

- Find numbers of protons, neutrons and electrons for neutral atoms and ions.
- Write full and shorthand electronic configurations for atoms and common ions.
- Use successive ionisation energy data to deduce group, period and electronic structure.

## Common Traps

- Confusing atomic number with mass number.
- Writing electron configurations without adjusting for ionic charge.
- Describing ionisation energy trends without naming the factor that changes.
