# Atomic Structure - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. Deduce the number of protons, neutrons and electrons in each species:
   - $^{27}_{13}\ce{Al^{3+}}$
   - $^{79}_{35}\ce{Br-}$
   - $^{40}_{18}\ce{Ar}$

2. Describe what happens to beams of protons, neutrons and electrons moving at the same velocity through an electric field. Explain both direction and relative amount of deflection.

3. Explain why isotopes of magnesium have the same chemical properties but can have different densities.

4. Write full electronic configurations for:
   - $\ce{P}$
   - $\ce{Ca}$
   - $\ce{Cl-}$

5. Write shorthand electronic configurations for:
   - $\ce{Br}$
   - $\ce{Ni}$
   - $\ce{Ni^{2+}}$

6. State the number of orbitals and maximum number of electrons in $s$, $p$ and $d$ sub-shells.

7. Sketch or describe the shapes of $s$ and $p$ orbitals.

8. Define first ionisation energy and write an equation for the first ionisation energy of aluminium.

9. Explain why first ionisation energy decreases down Group 1.

10. Explain why the first ionisation energy of aluminium is lower than that of magnesium.

11. The successive ionisation energies of an element show a large jump between the third and fourth ionisation energies. Deduce the group of the element and explain your reasoning.

12. Arrange $\ce{O^{2-}}$, $\ce{F-}$, $\ce{Na+}$ and $\ce{Mg^{2+}}$ in order of decreasing ionic radius. Explain the trend.
