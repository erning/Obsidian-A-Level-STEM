# Atomic Structure - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. $^{27}_{13}\ce{Al^{3+}}$: $13$ protons, $27 - 13 = 14$ neutrons, $13 - 3 = 10$ electrons.

   $^{79}_{35}\ce{Br-}$: $35$ protons, $79 - 35 = 44$ neutrons, $35 + 1 = 36$ electrons.

   $^{40}_{18}\ce{Ar}$: $18$ protons, $40 - 18 = 22$ neutrons, $18$ electrons.

2. Protons are deflected towards the negative plate. Electrons are deflected towards the positive plate. Neutrons are not deflected. Electrons are deflected more than protons because they have a much smaller mass for the same magnitude of charge.

3. Isotopes of magnesium have the same number of protons and the same electron arrangement, so they have the same chemical properties. They have different numbers of neutrons, so their masses differ. A sample containing more of the heavier isotope can have a different density.

4. Phosphorus has $15$ electrons:

   $$
   \ce{P}: 1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^3
   $$

   Calcium has $20$ electrons:

   $$
   \ce{Ca}: 1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^6 4s^2
   $$

   $\ce{Cl-}$ has $18$ electrons:

   $$
   \ce{Cl-}: 1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^6
   $$

5. Bromine has $35$ electrons:

   $$
   \ce{Br}: [\ce{Ar}] 3d^{10} 4s^2 4p^5
   $$

   Nickel has $28$ electrons:

   $$
   \ce{Ni}: [\ce{Ar}] 3d^8 4s^2
   $$

   For $\ce{Ni^{2+}}$, remove the two $4s$ electrons first:

   $$
   \ce{Ni^{2+}}: [\ce{Ar}] 3d^8
   $$

6. An $s$ sub-shell has $1$ orbital and holds up to $2$ electrons. A $p$ sub-shell has $3$ orbitals and holds up to $6$ electrons. A $d$ sub-shell has $5$ orbitals and holds up to $10$ electrons.

7. An $s$ orbital is spherical. A $p$ orbital is dumb-bell shaped. The three $p$ orbitals in a $p$ sub-shell are arranged at right angles to one another.

8. First ionisation energy is the energy required to remove one electron from each atom in one mole of gaseous atoms to form one mole of gaseous $1+$ ions.

   $$
   \ce{Al(g) -> Al+(g) + e-}
   $$

9. Down Group 1, the outer electron is in a shell further from the nucleus and is more shielded by inner shells. The attraction between the nucleus and the outer electron is weaker, so less energy is needed to remove it.

10. Magnesium loses a $3s$ electron, while aluminium loses a $3p$ electron. The $3p$ electron is in a higher-energy sub-shell and is more shielded than the $3s$ electron, so it is easier to remove. Therefore aluminium has the lower first ionisation energy.

11. A large jump between the third and fourth ionisation energies means that three electrons are removed before an inner-shell electron would be removed. The atom has three outer-shell electrons, so it is in Group 13.

12. The order of decreasing ionic radius is:

   $$
   \ce{O^{2-} > F- > Na+ > Mg^{2+}}
   $$

   All four ions are isoelectronic with $10$ electrons. Across the series, proton number increases from oxygen to magnesium. Greater nuclear charge pulls the same number of electrons closer, so ionic radius decreases.
