# Chemical Bonding - Key Practice Problems

## Problems

1. Define electronegativity and explain why electronegativity generally increases across a period.

2. Use electronegativity to explain why $\ce{MgO}$ is ionic and why $\ce{HCl}$ has a polar covalent bond.

3. Define ionic bonding and describe the bonding in calcium fluoride, $\ce{CaF2}$.

4. Define metallic bonding and explain why metals conduct electricity.

5. Define covalent bonding and coordinate bonding.

6. Describe how $\ce{NH4+}$ forms from $\ce{NH3}$ and $\ce{H+}$.

7. For each molecule, state the number of $\sigma$ and $\pi$ bonds:
   - $\ce{H2}$
   - $\ce{C2H6}$
   - $\ce{C2H4}$
   - $\ce{N2}$

8. Use VSEPR theory to predict the shape and bond angle of:
   - $\ce{BF3}$
   - $\ce{CH4}$
   - $\ce{NH3}$
   - $\ce{H2O}$
   - $\ce{PF5}$

9. Explain why $\ce{NH3}$ has a smaller bond angle than $\ce{CH4}$.

10. Explain why $\ce{CO2}$ is non-polar even though each $\ce{C=O}$ bond is polar.

11. Describe instantaneous dipole-induced dipole forces and state one factor that increases their strength.

12. Explain why water has relatively high surface tension.

13. State why ice is less dense than liquid water.

14. A simple molecular substance melts at a low temperature. Explain what is overcome during melting and what is not broken.
