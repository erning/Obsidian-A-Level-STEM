# 03 Chemical Bonding

## Core Idea

Chemical bonding explains how particles are held together and why substances have different shapes, polarities, structures and physical properties.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Physical chemistry: 3 Chemical bonding.
- 3.1 Electronegativity and bonding.
- 3.2 Ionic bonding.
- 3.3 Metallic bonding.
- 3.4 Covalent bonding and coordinate (dative covalent) bonding.
- 3.5 Shapes of molecules.
- 3.6 Intermolecular forces, electronegativity and bond properties.
- 3.7 Dot-and-cross diagrams.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Use electronegativity to predict bond type, bond polarity and molecular polarity.
- Describe ionic, metallic, covalent and coordinate bonding using electrostatic attraction.
- Use orbital overlap to describe sigma and pi bonds, and use hybridisation where required.
- Apply VSEPR theory to predict molecular shapes and bond angles.
- Explain physical properties using bonding, structure and intermolecular forces.

## How to Study

- Build from electrons first: dot-and-cross diagrams, then bond type, then shape and polarity.
- Separate bonds within particles from intermolecular forces between particles.
- Use named examples to test each explanation: simple molecular, giant ionic, giant metallic and giant covalent structures.

## Practice Directions

- Draw dot-and-cross diagrams for specified ionic, covalent and coordinate species.
- Predict shapes, bond angles and molecular polarity for common molecules and ions.
- Explain melting point, boiling point, solubility and conductivity from structure and bonding.

## Common Traps

- Calling all attractions between molecules "bonds".
- Predicting shape from the number of atoms only, rather than electron pairs.
- Forgetting that molecular polarity depends on both bond polarity and shape.
