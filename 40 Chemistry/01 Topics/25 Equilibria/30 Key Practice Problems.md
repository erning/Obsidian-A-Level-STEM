# 25 Equilibria - Key Practice Problems

Answers are intentionally excluded. Use [Key Practice Solutions](31%20Key%20Practice%20Solutions.md) after attempting the questions.

## 1. Conjugate Acid-Base Pairs

For each equation, identify the conjugate acid-base pairs.

(a)

$$
\ce{HCO3-(aq) + H2O(l) <=> CO3^{2-}(aq) + H3O+(aq)}
$$

(b)

$$
\ce{NH3(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NH4+(aq) + Cl-(aq)}
$$

## 2. Strong Acid And Strong Alkali pH

(a) Calculate the pH of $0.00400\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{HNO3(aq)}$.

(b) Calculate the pH of $0.0200\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{NaOH(aq)}$ at $298\ \text{K}$.

## 3. Weak Acid pH

Calculate the pH of $0.0500\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ methanoic acid, $\ce{HCOOH}$, using $K_a = 1.78 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$.

## 4. Buffer pH

A buffer is made from $25.0\ \text{cm}^3$ of $0.300\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{CH3COOH}$ and $75.0\ \text{cm}^3$ of $0.100\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{CH3COONa}$. Use $K_a = 1.74 \times 10^{-5}$ to calculate the pH.

## 5. Buffer Response To Alkali

The buffer in Question 4 is treated with $1.50 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{mol}$ of $\ce{OH-}$. Calculate the new pH and write the equation showing how the alkali is removed.

## 6. Solubility Product

For:

$$
\ce{CaF2(s) <=> Ca^{2+}(aq) + 2F-(aq)}
$$

$K_{sp} = 3.9 \times 10^{-11}$. Calculate the molar solubility of $\ce{CaF2}$ in pure water.

## 7. Common Ion Effect

For $\ce{PbCl2}$:

$$
\ce{PbCl2(s) <=> Pb^{2+}(aq) + 2Cl-(aq)}
$$

$K_{sp} = 1.7 \times 10^{-5}$. Estimate $[\ce{Pb^{2+}}]$ in $0.100\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{NaCl(aq)}$.

## 8. Partition Coefficient

At equilibrium:

$$
K_{pc} = \frac{[\text{solute in hexane}]}{[\text{solute in water}]} = 5.5
$$

$0.0800\ \text{mol}$ of solute is shaken with $40.0\ \text{cm}^3$ of hexane and $80.0\ \text{cm}^3$ of water. Calculate the moles of solute in the hexane layer at equilibrium.
