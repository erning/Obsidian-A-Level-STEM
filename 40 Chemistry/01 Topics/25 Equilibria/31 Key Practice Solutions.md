# 25 Equilibria - Key Practice Solutions

## 1. Conjugate Acid-Base Pairs

(a)

$$
\ce{HCO3-(aq) + H2O(l) <=> CO3^{2-}(aq) + H3O+(aq)}
$$

$\ce{HCO3-/CO3^{2-}}$ is one conjugate pair, and $\ce{H3O+/H2O}$ is the other.

(b)

$$
\ce{NH3(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NH4+(aq) + Cl-(aq)}
$$

$\ce{NH4+/NH3}$ is one conjugate pair, and $\ce{HCl/Cl-}$ is the other.

## 2. Strong Acid And Strong Alkali pH

(a) $\ce{HNO3}$ is a strong monoprotic acid:

$$
[\ce{H+}] = 0.00400\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}
$$

$$
\text{pH} = -\log_{10}(0.00400) = 2.40
$$

(b) $\ce{NaOH}$ is a strong alkali:

$$
[\ce{OH-}] = 0.0200\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}
$$

$$
\text{pOH} = -\log_{10}(0.0200) = 1.70
$$

$$
\text{pH} = 14.00 - 1.70 = 12.30
$$

## 3. Weak Acid pH

For:

$$
\ce{HCOOH(aq) <=> H+(aq) + HCOO-(aq)}
$$

Assuming small dissociation:

$$
[\ce{H+}] \approx \sqrt{K_a c}
$$

$$
[\ce{H+}] = \sqrt{(1.78 \times 10^{-4})(0.0500)}
= 2.98 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}
$$

$$
\text{pH} = -\log_{10}(2.98 \times 10^{-3}) = 2.53
$$

The dissociation is about $5.96\%$, so the approximation is acceptable for typical A Level work unless greater precision is requested.

## 4. Buffer pH

Moles of acid:

$$
n(\ce{CH3COOH}) = 0.300 \times \frac{25.0}{1000} = 0.00750\ \text{mol}
$$

Moles of conjugate base:

$$
n(\ce{CH3COO-}) = 0.100 \times \frac{75.0}{1000} = 0.00750\ \text{mol}
$$

Since the moles are equal:

$$
\text{pH} = \text{p}K_a
$$

$$
\text{p}K_a = -\log_{10}(1.74 \times 10^{-5}) = 4.76
$$

So the buffer pH is $4.76$.

## 5. Buffer Response To Alkali

The alkali is removed by:

$$
\ce{CH3COOH(aq) + OH-(aq) -> CH3COO-(aq) + H2O(l)}
$$

New moles:

$$
n(\ce{CH3COOH}) = 0.00750 - 0.00150 = 0.00600\ \text{mol}
$$

$$
n(\ce{CH3COO-}) = 0.00750 + 0.00150 = 0.00900\ \text{mol}
$$

$$
\text{pH} = 4.76 + \log_{10}\frac{0.00900}{0.00600}
$$

$$
\text{pH} = 4.76 + \log_{10}(1.50) = 4.94
$$

## 6. Solubility Product

Let the molar solubility be $s$.

$$
[\ce{Ca^{2+}}] = s,\quad [\ce{F-}] = 2s
$$

$$
K_{sp} = [\ce{Ca^{2+}}][\ce{F-}]^2 = s(2s)^2 = 4s^3
$$

$$
s = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3.9 \times 10^{-11}}{4}}
= 2.14 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}
$$

## 7. Common Ion Effect

In $0.100\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{NaCl}$, assume $[\ce{Cl-}] \approx 0.100\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}$.

$$
K_{sp} = [\ce{Pb^{2+}}][\ce{Cl-}]^2
$$

$$
[\ce{Pb^{2+}}] = \frac{1.7 \times 10^{-5}}{(0.100)^2}
= 1.7 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{mol dm}^{-3}
$$

## 8. Partition Coefficient

Let $x$ be the moles in hexane. Then $0.0800-x$ is in water.

$$
\frac{x/0.0400}{(0.0800-x)/0.0800} = 5.5
$$

$$
\frac{25.0x}{1.00 - 12.5x} = 5.5
$$

$$
25.0x = 5.50 - 68.75x
$$

$$
93.75x = 5.50
$$

$$
x = 0.0587\ \text{mol}
$$

So $0.0587\ \text{mol}$ is in the hexane layer at equilibrium.
