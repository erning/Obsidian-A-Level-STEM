# 25 Equilibria

## Core Idea

A Level equilibria deepens AS reversible-reaction ideas through acid-base systems, buffers, solubility equilibria, and partition between solvents. The focus is on using equilibrium constants to explain and calculate chemical behaviour.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, A Level subject content.
- 25 Equilibria
- 25.1 Acids and bases
- 25.2 Partition coefficients

## What to Learn

- Use conjugate acid-base pairs and the terms pH, $K_a$, $pK_a$, and $K_w$.
- Calculate pH for strong acids, strong alkalis, weak acids, and buffer solutions.
- Explain how buffer solutions control pH.
- Use solubility product, $K_{sp}$, including common ion effects.
- Use partition coefficient, $K_{pc}$, and explain it in terms of solute and solvent polarity.

## How to Study

- Begin each calculation by deciding whether the system is strong, weak, buffered, sparingly soluble, or partitioned.
- Write the equilibrium expression before substituting numbers.
- Keep approximation steps visible, especially for weak acids and low-solubility salts.
- Use chemical equations to support buffer explanations, not just prose.

## Practice Directions

- Calculate pH from concentration and acid dissociation data.
- Construct buffer calculations from weak acid/conjugate base mixtures.
- Calculate $K_{sp}$ or ion concentrations, including a common ion.
- Use partition coefficient data to compare distribution between two solvents.

## Common Traps

- Confusing strong acid with concentrated acid.
- Using the wrong equilibrium expression for $K_a$, $K_w$, $K_{sp}$, or $K_{pc}$.
- Forgetting powers in $K_{sp}$ expressions.
- Describing a buffer without showing how added acid or alkali is removed.
