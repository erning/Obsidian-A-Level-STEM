# 28 Chemistry of Transition Elements - Key Practice Solutions

## Solutions

1. A transition element is a d-block element which forms one or more stable ions with incomplete d orbitals.

2. The topic focuses on the first row from $\ce{Ti}$ to $\ce{Cu}$. These elements form stable ions with incomplete d orbitals. $\ce{Zn}$ forms $\ce{Zn^{2+}}$, which has a full $3d^{10}$ subshell, so zinc does not meet the strict CAIE definition of a transition element.

3. A $3d_{xy}$ orbital has four lobes in the $xy$ plane, between the $x$ and $y$ axes. A $3d_{z^2}$ orbital has two main lobes along the $z$ axis and a ring of electron density around the middle.

4. The $3d$ and $4s$ subshells are similar in energy. Different numbers of electrons can be removed or used in bonding while still forming stable ions, so transition elements can have variable oxidation states.

5. Transition elements can behave as catalysts because they can change between more than one stable oxidation state during a reaction and then be regenerated. They also have energetically accessible vacant d orbitals that can form dative covalent bonds with ligands or reactant molecules.

6. A ligand is a species with a lone pair of electrons that forms a dative covalent bond to a central metal atom or ion. A complex is a molecule or ion formed by a central metal atom or ion surrounded by one or more ligands.

7. The classifications are:

| Ligand | Type |
|---|---|
| $\ce{H2O}$ | monodentate |
| $\ce{NH3}$ | monodentate |
| $\ce{Cl-}$ | monodentate |
| $\ce{CN-}$ | monodentate |
| $\ce{H2NCH2CH2NH2}$ | bidentate |
| $\ce{C2O4^2-}$ | bidentate |
| $\ce{EDTA^4-}$ | polydentate |

8. Four $\ce{Cl-}$ ligands give total ligand charge $-4$. With $\ce{Co^{2+}}$, the overall charge is $-2$. The complex is:

$$
\ce{[CoCl4]^2-}
$$

It is usually tetrahedral.

9. en is bidentate, so three en ligands form six dative covalent bonds. The ligand is neutral, so the complex charge stays $2+$. The formula is:

$$
\ce{[Ni(en)3]^2+}
$$

The coordination number is 6.

10. The ligand exchange equation is:

$$
\ce{[Cu(H2O)6]^2+(aq) + 4NH3(aq) <=> [Cu(NH3)4(H2O)2]^2+(aq) + 4H2O(l)}
$$

The colour changes because $\ce{NH3}$ ligands replace some $\ce{H2O}$ ligands. The new ligand environment changes d-orbital splitting, $\Delta E$, so a different frequency of light is absorbed and a different complementary colour is observed.

11. The ligand exchange equation is:

$$
\ce{[Co(H2O)6]^2+(aq) + 4Cl-(aq) <=> [CoCl4]^2-(aq) + 6H2O(l)}
$$

Concentrated chloride ions favour the blue $\ce{[CoCl4]^2-}$ complex. Adding water increases the amount of water ligand and shifts the equilibrium back towards pink $\ce{[Co(H2O)6]^2+}$.

12. The balanced ionic equation is:

$$
\ce{2MnO4- + 16H+ + 5C2O4^2- -> 2Mn^{2+} + 8H2O + 10CO2}
$$

13. The balanced equation for iron(II) is:

$$
\ce{MnO4- + 8H+ + 5Fe^{2+} -> Mn^{2+} + 4H2O + 5Fe^{3+}}
$$

Moles of $\ce{MnO4-}$:

$$
0.0150 \times \frac{16.40}{1000}
= 2.46 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

Moles of $\ce{Fe^{2+}}$:

$$
5 \times 2.46 \times 10^{-4}
= 1.23 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}
$$

Concentration of $\ce{Fe^{2+}}$:

$$
\frac{1.23 \times 10^{-3}}{20.00/1000}
= 0.0615\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}
$$

14. In a free ion, d orbitals are degenerate. In a complex, ligands split them into non-degenerate sets. An electron can absorb light and move from a lower d orbital to a higher d orbital. The energy gap is $\Delta E$, and $\Delta E = h\nu$, so the gap determines the frequency absorbed. The colour observed is complementary to the colour absorbed.

15. In octahedral complexes, two d orbitals are higher in energy and three are lower. In tetrahedral complexes, three d orbitals are higher in energy and two are lower.

16. $\ce{[Pt(NH3)2Cl2]}$ is square planar. The two $\ce{Cl-}$ ligands can be adjacent in the cis isomer or opposite in the trans isomer. These arrangements are different in space and cannot be interconverted by rotation.

17. $\ce{[Ni(en)3]^2+}$ contains three bidentate en ligands in an octahedral arrangement. The chelate rings can arrange as non-superimposable mirror images, so the complex can show optical isomerism.

18. The expression is:

$$
K_\mathrm{stab} =
\frac{[\ce{[M(EDTA)]^2-}]}{[\ce{M^{2+}}][\ce{EDTA^4-}]}
$$

19. The expression is:

$$
K_\mathrm{stab} =
\frac{[\ce{[Cu(NH3)4]^2+}]}{[\ce{Cu^{2+}}][\ce{NH3}]^4}
$$

Water is the solvent and its concentration is effectively constant, so it is not included in the expression.

20. A very large $K_\mathrm{stab}$ means the equilibrium lies far to the right. The product complex is much more stable relative to the separated metal ion and ligands, so the ligand exchange strongly favours the product complex.
