# 28 Chemistry of Transition Elements - Key Practice Problems

## Questions

1. State the CAIE definition of a transition element.

2. Explain why $\ce{Ti}$ to $\ce{Cu}$ are treated as the first-row transition elements in this topic, but $\ce{Zn}$ is not normally included by the strict definition.

3. Describe the shapes of a $3d_{xy}$ orbital and a $3d_{z^2}$ orbital.

4. Explain why transition elements show variable oxidation states.

5. Give two reasons why transition elements or their compounds can behave as catalysts.

6. Define ligand and complex.

7. Classify each ligand as monodentate, bidentate, or polydentate: $\ce{H2O}$, $\ce{NH3}$, $\ce{Cl-}$, $\ce{CN-}$, $\ce{H2NCH2CH2NH2}$, $\ce{C2O4^2-}$, $\ce{EDTA^4-}$.

8. Write the formula and charge of the complex formed from $\ce{Co^{2+}}$ and four $\ce{Cl-}$ ligands. State its likely shape.

9. A complex contains $\ce{Ni^{2+}}$ and three bidentate en ligands. Write its formula and state its coordination number.

10. The pale blue ion $\ce{[Cu(H2O)6]^2+}$ forms a deep blue solution with excess $\ce{NH3}$. Write a ligand exchange equation and explain the colour change.

11. The pink ion $\ce{[Co(H2O)6]^2+}$ forms a blue solution in concentrated chloride ions. Write a ligand exchange equation and explain why adding water reverses the change.

12. Acidified $\ce{MnO4-}$ reacts with $\ce{C2O4^2-}$. Write the balanced ionic equation.

13. $20.00\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of $\ce{Fe^{2+}}$ solution reacts exactly with $16.40\ \mathrm{cm^3}$ of $0.0150\ \mathrm{mol\ dm^{-3}}$ acidified $\ce{MnO4-}$. Calculate the concentration of $\ce{Fe^{2+}}$.

14. Explain why transition element complexes are coloured, using the terms degenerate, non-degenerate, $\Delta E$, frequency, and complementary colour.

15. State which two sets of d orbitals are formed in octahedral and tetrahedral complexes.

16. Explain why $\ce{[Pt(NH3)2Cl2]}$ can show cis/trans isomerism.

17. Explain why $\ce{[Ni(en)3]^2+}$ can show optical isomerism.

18. Write a $K_\mathrm{stab}$ expression for:

$$
\ce{M^{2+}(aq) + EDTA^4-(aq) <=> [M(EDTA)]^2-(aq)}
$$

19. For:

$$
\ce{Cu^{2+}(aq) + 4NH3(aq) <=> [Cu(NH3)4]^2+(aq)}
$$

write the $K_\mathrm{stab}$ expression and state why water should not be included.

20. A ligand exchange equilibrium has a very large $K_\mathrm{stab}$ for the product complex. What does this imply about the position of equilibrium and the stability of the product complex?
