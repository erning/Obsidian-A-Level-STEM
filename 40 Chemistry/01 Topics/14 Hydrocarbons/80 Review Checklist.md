# 14 Hydrocarbons - Review Checklist

## Recall

- Define alkane, alkene, saturated and unsaturated.
- State conditions for alkene hydrogenation and alkane cracking.
- State conditions for alkane free-radical substitution with $\ce{Cl2}$ or $\ce{Br2}$.
- State routes to alkenes from halogenoalkanes, alcohols and cracking.
- State the alkene reactions with $\ce{H2}$, steam, $\ce{HX}$, $\ce{X2}$, cold dilute acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ and hot concentrated acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$.

## Skills

- Balance complete and incomplete combustion equations.
- Write initiation, propagation and termination steps for free-radical substitution.
- Explain alkane unreactivity using bond strength and low polarity.
- Predict major products of electrophilic addition to unsymmetrical alkenes.
- Use carbocation stability and inductive effects to explain Markovnikov addition.
- Use aqueous bromine as evidence for $\ce{C=C}$.
- Deduce alkene positions from oxidative cleavage products.
- Write repeat units for addition polymers.

## Common Errors

- Using ultraviolet light for alkene bromination when simple addition is intended.
- Forgetting that cracking produces alkenes as well as smaller alkanes.
- Calling combustion of alkanes substitution.
- Predicting the minor hydrogen halide addition product as the major product.
- Confusing cold dilute and hot concentrated acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$.

## Ready To Move On When

- You can decide from the reagent whether an alkane or alkene reaction is likely.
- You can explain mechanisms using radicals for alkanes and electrophiles for alkenes.
- You can connect hydrocarbons to later functional group routes: alkenes to halogenoalkanes and alcohols, and halogenoalkanes or alcohols back to alkenes.
