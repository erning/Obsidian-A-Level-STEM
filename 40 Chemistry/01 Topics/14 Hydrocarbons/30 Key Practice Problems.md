# 14 Hydrocarbons - Key Practice Problems

1. Write a balanced equation for the complete combustion of butane.

2. Explain why alkanes are generally unreactive towards polar reagents.

3. State the reagents and conditions for converting ethene to ethane.

4. Write initiation, two propagation steps and one termination step for bromination of ethane.

5. A longer-chain alkane cracks to give $\ce{C5H12}$ and ethene. Deduce the formula of the starting alkane.

6. State reagents and conditions for producing ethene from:
   - bromoethane
   - ethanol

7. Predict the major product when but-1-ene reacts with $\ce{HBr}$ at room temperature, and explain the role of carbocation stability.

8. Describe the observation when aqueous bromine is added to cyclohexene.

9. Predict the product of reacting ethene with cold dilute acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$.

10. Predict the organic products from hot concentrated acidified $\ce{KMnO4}$ oxidation of:
   - propene
   - 2-methylpropene

11. Draw or write the repeat unit of poly(ethene).
