# 14 Hydrocarbons - Key Practice Solutions

1. Butane is $\ce{C4H10}$.

$$
\ce{2C4H10 + 13O2 -> 8CO2 + 10H2O}
$$

2. Alkanes contain strong $\ce{C-C}$ and $\ce{C-H}$ bonds. These bonds are almost non-polar, so alkanes do not strongly attract nucleophiles or electrophiles.

3. Ethene is converted to ethane by hydrogenation using $\ce{H2(g)}$ with a Pt or Ni catalyst and heat.

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 + H2 -> CH3CH3}
$$

4. Bromination of ethane:

Initiation:

$$
\ce{Br2 ->[uv] 2Br^.}
$$

Propagation:

$$
\ce{Br^. + CH3CH3 -> HBr + CH3CH2^.}
$$

$$
\ce{CH3CH2^. + Br2 -> CH3CH2Br + Br^.}
$$

One termination step:

$$
\ce{CH3CH2^. + Br^. -> CH3CH2Br}
$$

5. Products are $\ce{C5H12}$ and $\ce{C2H4}$. Add atoms: starting alkane is $\ce{C7H16}$.

$$
\ce{C7H16 -> C5H12 + C2H4}
$$

6. Routes to ethene:
   - From bromoethane: ethanolic $\ce{NaOH}$ and heat.
   - From ethanol: dehydration using heated $\ce{Al2O3}$ or concentrated $\ce{H2SO4}$.

7. But-1-ene forms mainly 2-bromobutane:

$$
\ce{CH2=CHCH2CH3 + HBr -> CH3CHBrCH2CH3}
$$

The major route forms a secondary carbocation rather than a primary carbocation. Alkyl groups stabilise the positive charge by inductive effects.

8. Aqueous bromine is decolourised from orange/brown to colourless because bromine adds across the $\ce{C=C}$ bond.

9. Ethene forms ethane-1,2-diol:

$$
\ce{CH2=CH2 + [O] + H2O -> HOCH2CH2OH}
$$

10. Oxidative cleavage:
   - Propene, $\ce{CH3CH=CH2}$, gives ethanoic acid and carbon dioxide.
   - 2-methylpropene, $\ce{(CH3)2C=CH2}$, gives propanone and carbon dioxide.

11. Poly(ethene) repeat unit:

$$
\ce{[-CH2-CH2-]_{n}}
$$
