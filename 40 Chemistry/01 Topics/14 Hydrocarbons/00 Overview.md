# 14 Hydrocarbons

## Core Idea

Hydrocarbons introduce the contrast between relatively unreactive alkanes and reactive alkenes, using combustion, substitution, addition, oxidation and cracking.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Chemistry 9701, AS Level subject content.
- Organic chemistry: 14 Hydrocarbons.
- 14.1 Alkanes.
- 14.2 Alkenes.
- Theory route: Paper 1 Multiple Choice and Paper 2 AS Level Structured Questions.

## What to Learn

- Describe production and reactions of alkanes, including combustion and free-radical substitution.
- Explain the general unreactivity of alkanes using bond strength and low polarity.
- Describe production and reactions of alkenes, including electrophilic addition, oxidation and addition polymerisation.
- Use aqueous bromine as a test for a carbon-carbon double bond.
- Explain electrophilic addition and Markovnikov addition at AS level.

## How to Study

- Separate alkane substitution from alkene addition before comparing them.
- For each reaction, learn reagent, condition, organic product and reaction type together.
- Use mechanisms to explain selectivity rather than memorising isolated products.

## Practice Directions

- Write equations for combustion, cracking, hydrogenation and halogenation examples.
- Draw free-radical substitution steps for an alkane example.
- Predict products from electrophilic addition to alkenes.

## Common Traps

- Confusing cracking with combustion.
- Omitting ultraviolet light for free-radical substitution with halogens.
- Applying Markovnikov addition without considering the carbocation stability.
