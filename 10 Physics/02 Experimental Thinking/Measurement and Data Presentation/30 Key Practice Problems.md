# Measurement and Data Presentation Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Treat every numerical answer
as incomplete until it has a unit, sensible significant figures, and an
uncertainty where the question asks for one.

## A. Measurement, Tables, and Significant Figures

1. A wire has diameter about $0.40\ \mathrm{mm}$. Choose a suitable measuring
   instrument and explain why a metre rule is unsuitable.

2. A digital voltmeter reads $2.34\ \mathrm{V}$ and has resolution
   $0.01\ \mathrm{V}$. State a suitable absolute uncertainty and write the
   reading with uncertainty.

3. A stop-watch reads to $0.01\ \mathrm{s}$, but a student starts and stops it
   by hand. Explain why an uncertainty of $0.01\ \mathrm{s}$ is usually too
   small.

4. Five readings of a period are $1.83\ \mathrm{s}$, $1.87\ \mathrm{s}$,
   $1.85\ \mathrm{s}$, $1.84\ \mathrm{s}$, and $1.86\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the
   mean and half-range uncertainty.

5. A length is recorded as $(2.40\pm0.05)\ \mathrm{cm}$. Find the percentage
   uncertainty.

6. A column of raw length readings is taken with the same millimetre rule. Two
   entries are written as $4.3\ \mathrm{cm}$ and $5.27\ \mathrm{cm}$. Explain
   what is wrong.

7. A potential difference is measured to $2$ significant figures and a current
   to $4$ significant figures. How many significant figures are suitable for a
   resistance calculated from them?

8. Write suitable table headings for measured current in milliamperes, measured
   potential difference in volts, and calculated resistance in ohms.

## B. Uncertainty Calculations

9. A distance is found from $d=x_2-x_1$, where
   $x_2=(84.0\pm0.5)\ \mathrm{cm}$ and
   $x_1=(12.0\pm0.5)\ \mathrm{cm}$. Find $d$ and its absolute uncertainty.

10. A resistor has $V=(2.40\pm0.05)\ \mathrm{V}$ and
    $I=(0.520\pm0.005)\ \mathrm{A}$. Find $R=V/I$ with a percentage and
    absolute uncertainty.

11. A density is found from $\rho=m/V$, where
    $m=(42.0\pm0.1)\ \mathrm{g}$ and
    $V=(15.0\pm0.5)\ \mathrm{cm^3}$. Find the percentage uncertainty in
    $\rho$.

12. A period is $T=(1.85\pm0.02)\ \mathrm{s}$. Find $T^2$ and the percentage
    uncertainty in $T^2$.

13. A force has percentage uncertainty $4.0\%$ and a length has percentage
    uncertainty $1.5\%$. Which measurement dominates the uncertainty in
    $F/l$?

14. A calculated gradient is $3.46\ \mathrm{N\ m^{-1}}$ with absolute
    uncertainty $0.18\ \mathrm{N\ m^{-1}}$. Quote the result sensibly.

## C. Graphs and Linearisation

15. State three features of a well-plotted graph layout.

16. Explain why a line of best fit is normally drawn instead of joining the
    data points.

17. A straight line passes through two well-separated points on the best-fit
    line: $(0.20\ \mathrm{m},0.95\ \mathrm{N})$ and
    $(0.80\ \mathrm{m},3.35\ \mathrm{N})$. Find the gradient and its unit.

18. A graph of $V$ against $I$ has gradient $2.50\ \mathrm{V\ A^{-1}}$ and
    passes through $(0.30\ \mathrm{A},1.10\ \mathrm{V})$. Find the
    $V$-intercept.

19. For a mass-spring oscillator,
    $T=2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$. A graph of $T^2$ against $m$ has gradient
    $1.60\ \mathrm{s^2\ kg^{-1}}$. Find $k$.

20. A best-fit line has gradient $2.40\ \mathrm{N\ m^{-1}}$. The worst
    acceptable line has gradient $2.15\ \mathrm{N\ m^{-1}}$. Estimate the
    gradient uncertainty and quote the gradient.

21. A relationship has the form $y=ax^n$. State what graph should be plotted
    to determine $n$ and $a$, and state what the gradient and intercept mean.

22. A relationship has the form $y=ae^{kx}$. State what graph should be plotted
    to determine $k$ and $a$.

23. The equation for a discharging capacitor is
    $V=V_0e^{-t/RC}$. State what graph should be plotted to find $RC$ from the
    gradient.

24. One plotted point is far from the trend of the other points. State how it
    should be treated on the graph and in the analysis.
