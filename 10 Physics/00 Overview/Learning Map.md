# Learning Map

This map uses CAIE Physics 9702 as a coherent route through pre-university physics. The syllabus gives the official scope and topic wording; the learning goal is deeper than examination technique: explain, model, calculate, measure, and connect.

## Route

1. Foundations: physical quantities, SI units, errors, uncertainties, scalars, vectors, graphs, and mathematical tools.
2. Mechanics: kinematics, Newton's laws, momentum, equilibrium, pressure, work, energy, materials, circular motion, and gravitation.
3. Waves and oscillations: progressive waves, superposition, diffraction, interference, stationary waves, SHM, damping, and resonance.
4. Electricity and fields: current, potential difference, resistance, D.C. circuits, electric fields, capacitance, magnetic fields, induction, and alternating currents.
5. Thermal and particle models: temperature, ideal gases, thermodynamics, atomic structure, fundamental particles, quantum physics, and nuclear physics.
6. Applications and scale: medical imaging, astronomy, standard candles, stellar radii, redshift, and Hubble's law.
7. Experimental thinking: practical skills, data presentation, uncertainty, linearisation, and evaluation run through every topic.

## Learning Questions

Use the same questions for every topic:

- What phenomenon is being described?
- What idealised model is being used?
- What quantities are measurable, and with what uncertainty?
- What equation or graph expresses the relationship?
- What evidence supports the model?
- Where does the model fail?
- Which previous topic or mathematical tool does it depend on?

## Three Passes

First pass: build the map. Read lightly and write only the core idea, source anchors, and key quantities for each topic.

Second pass: build the models. Work through coursebook examples, derive or justify formulae, and add graph meanings and assumptions.

Third pass: build connections. Link circular motion to gravitation and magnetic fields, SHM to waves, electric potential to gravitational potential, and exponential decay to capacitor discharge and radioactive decay.

## Expected Outcome

By the end, you should be able to translate between words, diagrams, equations, units, graphs, and experimental data. That translation ability is the real learning target.
