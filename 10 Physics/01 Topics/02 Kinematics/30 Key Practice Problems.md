# Kinematics Key Practice Problems

Work these without the solution note first. Use SI units, clear signs, and
labelled graph features throughout.

Take $g=9.81\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where needed.

## A. Motion Language and Graphs

1. A cyclist rides $420\ \mathrm{m}$ east, then $160\ \mathrm{m}$ west, in a
   total time of $145\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the total distance, resultant
   displacement, average speed, and average velocity.

2. A car changes speed from $36\ \mathrm{km\ h^{-1}}$ to
   $90\ \mathrm{km\ h^{-1}}$ in $12\ \mathrm{s}$. Calculate its average
   acceleration in $\mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$.

3. A displacement-time graph consists of straight sections through the points
   $(0,0)$, $(3.0,9.0)$, $(5.0,9.0)$, and $(9.0,-3.0)$, where time is in
   seconds and displacement is in metres.

   a. Find the velocity in each section.
   b. State during which section the object is stationary.
   c. State during which section the object moves in the negative direction.

4. A velocity-time graph has these straight sections:

   - from $0$ to $4.0\ \mathrm{s}$, velocity increases from $2.0$ to
     $10.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$;
   - from $4.0$ to $8.0\ \mathrm{s}$, velocity remains
     $10.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$;
   - from $8.0$ to $12.0\ \mathrm{s}$, velocity decreases to
     $-2.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

   Find the acceleration in the first and final sections, the total
   displacement, and the total distance travelled.

5. At one instant on a curved velocity-time graph, the tangent passes through
   the points $(2.0\ \mathrm{s},4.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}})$ and
   $(8.0\ \mathrm{s},16.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}})$. Find the instantaneous
   acceleration at that instant and explain why the constant-acceleration
   equations cannot automatically be used over the whole curve.

## B. Uniform Acceleration in a Straight Line

6. A sprinter starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at
   $3.2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ for $4.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the final velocity and
   the displacement during this time.

7. A train accelerates uniformly from $12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to
   $28\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ over a distance of $400\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the
   acceleration and the time taken.

8. A car slows uniformly from $30\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to
   $12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in $9.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Find its acceleration and
   displacement.

9. A ball rolls up a straight slope with initial velocity
   $5.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Its acceleration is
   $-0.80\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$, taking up the slope as positive. Find the time
   taken to stop and the distance travelled up the slope before stopping.

10. Starting from the definition of acceleration and the area under a
    velocity-time graph, derive

    $$
    s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2
    $$

    for motion with constant acceleration.

## C. Free Fall and Determining $g$

11. A stone is dropped from rest from a bridge $65\ \mathrm{m}$ above a river.
    Ignore air resistance. Find the time to reach the water and the impact
    speed.

12. A ball is thrown vertically upwards from a balcony with initial velocity
    $18\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The balcony is $12\ \mathrm{m}$ above the ground.
    Taking upward as positive, find:

    a. the maximum height above the balcony;
    b. the time taken to hit the ground;
    c. the velocity just before it hits the ground.

13. In an experiment to determine $g$, a ball is released from rest through a
    height

    $$
    h=(0.800\pm0.005)\ \mathrm{m}
    $$

    and the measured time is

    $$
    t=(0.405\pm0.005)\ \mathrm{s}.
    $$

    Calculate $g$ using $g=2h/t^2$ and estimate its percentage uncertainty.

14. Describe a laboratory experiment using a falling object and an electronic
    timer to determine $g$. Your answer should include the measurements taken,
    the graph plotted, how $g$ is obtained, and one systematic effect that could
    make the value too small.

## D. Projectile Motion

15. A ball rolls horizontally off a table $1.25\ \mathrm{m}$ high with speed
    $3.2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Ignore air resistance. Find the time of flight
    and the horizontal range.

16. A projectile is launched from level ground with speed
    $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ at $40^\circ$ above the horizontal. It lands at the
    same height. Find the time of flight, the range, and the maximum height.

17. A stone is projected horizontally from a cliff $30\ \mathrm{m}$ high. It
    lands $42\ \mathrm{m}$ from the base of the cliff. Find its initial
    horizontal speed and the angle of its velocity below the horizontal just
    before impact.

18. A ball is kicked from ground level at $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and
    $30^\circ$ above the horizontal. Find the time to reach its highest point
    and the velocity vector at that point.

## E. Mixed Review

19. A velocity-time graph for a trolley has these straight sections:

    - $0$ to $6.0\ \mathrm{s}$: velocity increases from $4.0$ to
      $16\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$;
    - $6.0$ to $10.0\ \mathrm{s}$: velocity is constant at
      $16\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$;
    - $10.0$ to $14.0\ \mathrm{s}$: velocity decreases uniformly to zero.

    Find the acceleration in each section and the total displacement.

20. Car A starts from rest with constant acceleration
    $2.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$. At the same instant, car B passes the same point
    moving at constant velocity $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in the same direction.

    a. Find the displacement of each car after $8.0\ \mathrm{s}$.
    b. Find the time when car A catches car B.
    c. Find the displacement from the start when this happens.

21. A student uses $s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2$ for a car whose velocity-time graph is
    curved. Explain why this may be invalid, and state what graph methods could
    still be used to find instantaneous acceleration and displacement.

22. A projectile is launched horizontally at $9.5\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ from a
    height of $18\ \mathrm{m}$. At the same instant, a second ball is dropped
    from rest from the same height. Ignoring air resistance, compare their times
    to hit the ground and explain the result.
