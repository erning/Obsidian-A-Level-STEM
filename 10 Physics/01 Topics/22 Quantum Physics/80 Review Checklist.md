# Quantum Physics Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. Most calculation errors in
this topic come from mixing joules and electronvolts or forgetting which model
the evidence supports.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Electromagnetic radiation has a particulate nature as well as wave
      behaviour.
- [ ] A photon is a quantum of electromagnetic energy.
- [ ] Photon energy is $E=hf$ and $E=hc/\lambda$.
- [ ] One electronvolt is $1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{J}$.
- [ ] A photon has momentum $p=E/c=h/\lambda$.
- [ ] Photoelectric emission can occur when radiation illuminates a metal
      surface.
- [ ] Work function is the minimum energy needed for an electron to escape from
      the metal.
- [ ] Threshold frequency is a minimum frequency; threshold wavelength is a
      maximum wavelength.
- [ ] Increasing intensity above threshold increases photoelectric current but
      not maximum kinetic energy.
- [ ] Interference and diffraction support the wave model of electromagnetic
      radiation.
- [ ] The photoelectric effect supports the particulate model of
      electromagnetic radiation.
- [ ] Electron diffraction is evidence for the wave nature of particles.
- [ ] A moving particle has de Broglie wavelength $\lambda=h/p$.
- [ ] Isolated atoms have discrete electron energy levels.
- [ ] Emission and absorption line spectra come from transitions between
      allowed energy levels.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Calculate photon energy from frequency or wavelength.
- [ ] Convert energy between joules and electronvolts.
- [ ] Calculate photon momentum using $p=E/c$ or $p=h/\lambda$.
- [ ] Use $hf_0=\Phi$ to find threshold frequency.
- [ ] Use $\lambda_0=hc/\Phi$ or $\lambda_0=c/f_0$ for threshold wavelength.
- [ ] Use $hf=\Phi+\frac{1}{2}mv_{\max}^2$ for photoelectric emission.
- [ ] Interpret a $K_{\max}$ against $f$ graph: gradient, intercepts, and work
      function.
- [ ] Use stopping potential through $eV_s=K_{\max}$.
- [ ] Use $\lambda=h/p$ for matter waves.
- [ ] Estimate an electron de Broglie wavelength from accelerating voltage.
- [ ] Use $hf=E_{\text{high}}-E_{\text{low}}$ for spectral lines.
- [ ] Explain emission and absorption spectra using energy-level transitions.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Explaining photoelectric emission with intensity alone.
- [ ] Treating intensity as energy per photon.
- [ ] Saying high intensity below threshold eventually releases electrons.
- [ ] Confusing threshold frequency with threshold wavelength.
- [ ] Dropping the work function in photoelectric calculations.
- [ ] Using electronvolts directly in SI equations without converting to
      joules.
- [ ] Using the wrong sign when subtracting negative energy levels.
- [ ] Treating wave-particle duality as two classical pictures happening
      literally side by side.
- [ ] Explaining electron diffraction with a particle-only model.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can calculate photon energy, wavelength, and momentum.
- [ ] I can explain the photoelectric observations using photons and work
      function.
- [ ] I can use threshold frequency, threshold wavelength, and stopping
      potential correctly.
- [ ] I can interpret the slope and intercepts of a photoelectric graph.
- [ ] I can explain electron diffraction using de Broglie wavelength.
- [ ] I can calculate wavelengths from atomic energy-level transitions.
- [ ] I can distinguish emission and absorption line spectra.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [[10 Physics/01 Topics/08 Superposition/00 Overview|Superposition]]
      and [[10 Physics/01 Topics/23 Nuclear Physics/00 Overview|Nuclear Physics]].
