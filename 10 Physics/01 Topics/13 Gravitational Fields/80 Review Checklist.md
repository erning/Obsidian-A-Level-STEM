# Gravitational Fields Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. If a calculation feels unclear,
first decide whether the target quantity is force, field strength, potential,
potential energy, orbital speed, or orbital period.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] A gravitational field is a field of force around a mass.
- [ ] Gravitational field strength is force per unit mass:
      $g=\frac{F}{m}$.
- [ ] Gravitational field lines point in the direction of the force on a test
      mass.
- [ ] A uniform sphere may be treated as a point mass at its centre for points
      outside the sphere.
- [ ] Newton's law of gravitation is
      $F=\frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2}$.
- [ ] The gravitational field strength due to a point mass is
      $g=\frac{GM}{r^2}$.
- [ ] Force and field strength follow inverse-square dependence.
- [ ] Gravitational potential is work done per unit mass in bringing a small
      test mass from infinity to the point.
- [ ] For a point mass, gravitational potential is
      $\phi=-\frac{GM}{r}$.
- [ ] Gravitational potential energy for two point masses is
      $E_p=-\frac{GMm}{r}$.
- [ ] In a circular orbit, gravity supplies the centripetal force.
- [ ] A geostationary satellite must have a $24\ \mathrm{h}$ period, orbit
      west to east, and be directly above the Equator.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Use $F=mg$ when field strength is given.
- [ ] Use centre-to-centre distance in Newton's law of gravitation.
- [ ] Derive $g=\frac{GM}{r^2}$ from
      $F=\frac{GMm}{r^2}$ and $g=\frac{F}{m}$.
- [ ] Compare field strength at different distances using inverse-square
      ratios.
- [ ] Calculate gravitational potential and keep the negative sign.
- [ ] Calculate gravitational potential energy using
      $E_p=-\frac{GMm}{r}$.
- [ ] Find changes in potential energy between two radii.
- [ ] Derive and use $v=\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ for a circular orbit.
- [ ] Use $T=\frac{2\pi r}{v}$ or
      $T^2=\frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GM}$ for orbital period.
- [ ] Distinguish orbital radius from height above a surface.
- [ ] Interpret $g$ and $\phi$ graphs against distance from a point mass.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Confusing gravitational field strength with gravitational potential.
- [ ] Forgetting that gravitational potential is negative when zero is chosen
      at infinity.
- [ ] Measuring satellite radius from the surface instead of from the centre.
- [ ] Applying $mg\Delta h$ over a very large height where $g$ changes
      significantly.
- [ ] Treating any $24\ \mathrm{h}$ orbit as geostationary.
- [ ] Forgetting that a satellite in orbit still experiences gravity.
- [ ] Using inverse-square reasoning for potential. Potential is proportional
      to $\frac{1}{r}$, not $\frac{1}{r^2}$.
- [ ] Dropping the satellite mass cancellation in orbital speed derivations.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can explain the difference between force, field strength, potential,
      and potential energy.
- [ ] I can derive $g=\frac{GM}{r^2}$ and
      $v=\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ without memorising every step.
- [ ] I can decide when a spherical mass can be treated as a point mass.
- [ ] I can calculate orbital speed, period, and geostationary radius using
      centre-to-centre distance.
- [ ] I can explain why gravitational potential approaches zero from below as
      distance increases.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [[10 Physics/01 Topics/12 Motion in a Circle/00 Overview|Motion in a Circle]]
      and [[10 Physics/01 Topics/18 Electric Fields/00 Overview|Electric Fields]].
