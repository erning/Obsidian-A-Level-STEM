# Gravitational Fields Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Decide whether the question
is about force, field strength, potential, potential energy, or orbit before
choosing a formula.

Use

$$
G=6.67 \times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{N\ m^2\ kg^{-2}},
\quad
M_{\mathrm{E}}=5.97 \times 10^{24}\ \mathrm{kg},
\quad
R_{\mathrm{E}}=6.37 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}
$$

where needed.

## A. Field Strength, Field Lines, and Newton's Law

1. Define gravitational field and gravitational field strength. State the unit
   of gravitational field strength.

2. A $2.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ mass is placed in a gravitational field of strength
   $3.7\ \mathrm{N\ kg^{-1}}$. Find the gravitational force on it.

3. A $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ satellite is at distance
   $7.0 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}$ from the centre of a planet of mass
   $6.0 \times 10^{24}\ \mathrm{kg}$. Calculate the gravitational force on the
   satellite.

4. Explain why the distance used in Newton's law of gravitation for a satellite
   is measured from the centre of the planet, not from the surface.

5. Use $g=\frac{GM_{\mathrm{E}}}{R_{\mathrm{E}}^2}$ to calculate the
   gravitational field strength at the Earth's surface.

## B. Radial Fields, Potential, and Potential Energy

6. Derive $g=\frac{GM}{r^2}$ from Newton's law of gravitation and the
   definition of gravitational field strength.

7. At distance $R$ from a planet's centre, the gravitational field strength is
   $9.8\ \mathrm{N\ kg^{-1}}$. Find the field strength at distances $2R$ and
   $3R$ from the centre.

8. At what height above a planet's surface is the gravitational field strength
   one quarter of its surface value? Let the planet's radius be $R$.

9. At distance $r$ from a point mass, the gravitational field strength is $g$
   and the gravitational potential is $\phi$. State the field strength and
   potential at distance $2r$.

10. The Moon has mass $7.35 \times 10^{22}\ \mathrm{kg}$ and radius
    $1.74 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}$. Calculate the gravitational potential at
    its surface.

11. Calculate the gravitational potential energy of a
    $250\ \mathrm{kg}$ spacecraft at distance
    $2.0 \times 10^7\ \mathrm{m}$ from the Earth's centre.

12. A $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ satellite is moved from
    $7.0 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}$ to
    $1.40 \times 10^7\ \mathrm{m}$ from the Earth's centre. Find the change in
    gravitational potential energy.

## C. Circular Orbits and Geostationary Satellites

13. Derive $v=\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ for a satellite in a circular orbit around
    a planet of mass $M$.

14. A satellite orbits the Earth at radius $7.0 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}$.
    Calculate its orbital speed and period.

15. Use $T=24.0\ \mathrm{h}$ to estimate the radius of a geostationary orbit
    around the Earth. Then find the height above the Earth's surface.

16. State all the conditions needed for a satellite to be geostationary.

17. Explain why astronauts in a low Earth circular orbit are still in a
    gravitational field even though they appear weightless.

## D. Interpretation and Approximation

18. Describe the shapes of graphs of gravitational field strength $g$ and
    gravitational potential $\phi$ against distance $r$ from a point mass.
    Include sign and limiting behaviour as $r$ becomes very large.

19. Explain why $\Delta E_p=mg\Delta h$ may be used for small height changes
    near the Earth's surface, but not for very large changes in height.

20. A $500\ \mathrm{kg}$ spacecraft is moved from the Earth's surface to a
    distance $2R_{\mathrm{E}}$ from the Earth's centre. Find the increase in
    gravitational potential energy using
    $E_p=-\frac{GM_{\mathrm{E}}m}{r}$.
