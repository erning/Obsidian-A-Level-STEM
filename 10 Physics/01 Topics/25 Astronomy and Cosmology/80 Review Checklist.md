# Astronomy and Cosmology Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. Keep separate what the source
emits, what reaches the observer, and what is inferred from the observation.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Luminosity is the total power of radiation emitted by a star.
- [ ] Radiant flux intensity is received power per unit area.
- [ ] The inverse square law is $F=L/(4\pi d^2)$.
- [ ] A standard candle is an object of known luminosity.
- [ ] Standard candles allow distances to galaxies to be estimated.
- [ ] Wien's displacement law links peak wavelength and surface temperature.
- [ ] A hotter star has a shorter peak wavelength.
- [ ] The Stefan-Boltzmann law is $L=4\pi\sigma r^2T^4$.
- [ ] A cool star can be highly luminous if its radius is large.
- [ ] Redshift means spectral lines are observed at longer wavelengths than
      their laboratory values.
- [ ] For small redshift, $\Delta\lambda/\lambda\approx v/c$.
- [ ] Hubble's law is $v\approx H_0d$.
- [ ] Hubble's law supports expansion of space, not Earth as the centre.
- [ ] Tracing expansion backwards leads to the Big Bang model.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Calculate distance from luminosity and radiant flux intensity.
- [ ] Explain how a standard candle is used as a distance indicator.
- [ ] Use Wien's displacement law to estimate stellar surface temperature.
- [ ] Use the Stefan-Boltzmann law to estimate luminosity or radius.
- [ ] Calculate recession speed from wavelength redshift.
- [ ] Use frequency redshift in magnitude when required.
- [ ] Use Hubble's law with SI units.
- [ ] Estimate a Hubble time from $1/H_0$.
- [ ] Explain why redshift and Hubble's law support an expanding Universe.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Calling an object bright without distinguishing luminosity from flux.
- [ ] Forgetting the $4\pi d^2$ surface area in the inverse square law.
- [ ] Treating a standard candle as merely an object that appears bright.
- [ ] Using Celsius instead of kelvin in Wien's law or Stefan-Boltzmann law.
- [ ] Forgetting that $T$ is raised to the fourth power in
      $L=4\pi\sigma r^2T^4$.
- [ ] Thinking redshift means every spectral line becomes visibly red.
- [ ] Applying the simple redshift formula without recognising the small-speed
      approximation.
- [ ] Interpreting Hubble's law as expansion away from Earth rather than
      expansion of space.
- [ ] Mixing non-SI distance units with $H_0$ in $\mathrm{s^{-1}}$.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can distinguish luminosity from radiant flux intensity in words and
      equations.
- [ ] I can calculate astronomical distances from standard candles.
- [ ] I can estimate stellar temperature from peak wavelength.
- [ ] I can estimate stellar radius from luminosity and temperature.
- [ ] I can calculate recession speed from redshift.
- [ ] I can use Hubble's law to estimate distance, speed, or Hubble time.
- [ ] I can explain how redshift and Hubble's law lead to the Big Bang model.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [Waves](../07%20Waves/00%20Overview.md)
      and [Quantum Physics](../22%20Quantum%20Physics/00%20Overview.md).
