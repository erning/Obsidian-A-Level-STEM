# Temperature Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. If an item feels uncertain,
identify whether the process is temperature measurement, temperature
conversion, temperature change, change of state, or experimental loss.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Thermal energy is transferred from higher temperature to lower
      temperature.
- [ ] Thermal equilibrium means equal temperature and no net transfer of
      thermal energy.
- [ ] A thermometer must reach thermal equilibrium with the object being
      measured.
- [ ] A thermometric property is a physical property that varies with
      temperature.
- [ ] Thermodynamic temperature does not depend on the property of a particular
      substance.
- [ ] Absolute zero is $0\ \mathrm{K}$, the lowest possible temperature on the
      thermodynamic scale.
- [ ] Celsius and kelvin intervals have the same size, but absolute
      thermodynamic temperatures must be in kelvin.
- [ ] Specific heat capacity is energy per unit mass per unit temperature
      rise.
- [ ] Specific latent heat is energy per unit mass for change of state at
      constant temperature.
- [ ] Latent heat of fusion concerns solid-liquid changes; latent heat of
      vaporisation concerns liquid-gas changes.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Convert temperatures using
      $T\ (\mathrm{K})=\theta\ (^\circ\mathrm{C})+273.15$.
- [ ] Use linear interpolation for a calibrated thermometric property.
- [ ] Use $E=mc\Delta\theta$ for a temperature change within one state.
- [ ] Use $E=mL$ for a change of state at constant temperature.
- [ ] Use $E=Pt$ or $E=VIt$ for electrical heating.
- [ ] Split combined heating processes into separate temperature-change and
      state-change stages.
- [ ] Interpret sloping and flat sections of a heating curve.
- [ ] Use graph gradient with constant power to find specific heat capacity.
- [ ] Explain how heat loss affects measured values of $c$ or $L$.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Treating temperature as the same thing as internal energy.
- [ ] Saying thermal energy flows from larger mass to smaller mass instead of
      hotter to cooler.
- [ ] Taking a thermometer reading before thermal equilibrium is reached.
- [ ] Writing degree kelvin. The unit is kelvin.
- [ ] Using degrees Celsius as an absolute temperature in thermodynamic
      formulae.
- [ ] Assuming supplied energy always raises temperature.
- [ ] Using $E=mc\Delta\theta$ during a change of state.
- [ ] Using $E=mL$ during a temperature rise in one state.
- [ ] Forgetting to convert grams to kilograms.
- [ ] Ignoring energy lost to the surroundings in heating experiments.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can explain temperature measurement using thermal equilibrium.
- [ ] I can name suitable thermometric properties and describe calibration.
- [ ] I can convert between Celsius and kelvin and distinguish values from
      temperature changes.
- [ ] I can choose correctly between $E=mc\Delta\theta$ and $E=mL$.
- [ ] I can read a heating curve as particle kinetic energy changes and
      particle potential energy changes.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [[10 Physics/01 Topics/15 Ideal Gases/00 Overview|Ideal Gases]]
      and [[10 Physics/01 Topics/16 Thermodynamics/00 Overview|Thermodynamics]].
