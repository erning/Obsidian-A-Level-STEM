# Temperature Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Decide whether energy is
changing temperature, changing state, or being lost to the surroundings before
choosing an equation.

## A. Thermal Equilibrium and Temperature Scales

1. Explain the direction in which thermal energy is transferred between two
   objects at different temperatures. State what is meant by thermal
   equilibrium.

2. Explain why a thermometer must be left in contact with an object before its
   reading is taken. Name four possible thermometric properties.

3. A resistance thermometer has resistance $100.0\ \Omega$ at
   $0.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ and $138.5\ \Omega$ at
   $100.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Assuming linear variation, find the temperature
   when the resistance is $127.0\ \Omega$.

4. Convert these temperatures: $-273.15\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to kelvin,
   $25.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to kelvin, and $350\ \mathrm{K}$ to degrees
   Celsius.

5. Explain why the thermodynamic temperature scale does not depend on the
   property of one particular substance. State what is meant by absolute zero.

## B. Specific Heat Capacity

6. Define specific heat capacity and state its SI unit.

7. Calculate the energy needed to heat $0.75\ \mathrm{kg}$ of water from
   $20\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ to $85\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Use
   $c=4200\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}\ K^{-1}}$.

8. A $1.00\ \mathrm{kg}$ metal block is heated by a $12.0\ \mathrm{V}$,
   $2.00\ \mathrm{A}$ heater for $300\ \mathrm{s}$. Its temperature rises by
   $8.0\ \mathrm{K}$. Estimate the specific heat capacity of the metal.

9. A $0.20\ \mathrm{kg}$ copper sample receives
   $3.9 \times 10^3\ \mathrm{J}$ of energy. Use
   $c=390\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}\ K^{-1}}$ to find its temperature rise.

10. A $0.40\ \mathrm{kg}$ sample is heated at constant power
    $60\ \mathrm{W}$. The temperature-time graph has gradient
    $0.050\ \mathrm{K\ s^{-1}}$. Find the specific heat capacity.

## C. Specific Latent Heat

11. Define specific latent heat. Distinguish between specific latent heat of
    fusion and specific latent heat of vaporisation.

12. Calculate the energy needed to melt $0.15\ \mathrm{kg}$ of ice at
    $0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Use
    $L_f=3.34 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}}$.

13. Calculate the energy needed to vaporise $0.025\ \mathrm{kg}$ of water at
    its boiling point. Use $L_v=2.26 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}}$.

14. A heater supplies $9.0\ \mathrm{V}$ at $2.0\ \mathrm{A}$ for
    $600\ \mathrm{s}$ to a boiling liquid. The mass lost is
    $4.8 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{kg}$. Estimate the specific latent heat of
    vaporisation.

15. State two differences between the equations $E=mc\Delta\theta$ and
    $E=mL$.

## D. Heating Curves and Experimental Checks

16. A $0.10\ \mathrm{kg}$ sample of ice at $-20\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ is heated
    to ice at $0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ and then melted. Use
    $c_{\text{ice}}=2100\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}\ K^{-1}}$ and
    $L_f=3.34 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}}$ to find the total energy
    needed.

17. A $0.20\ \mathrm{kg}$ sample of water at $20\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ is heated
    to steam at $100\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Use
    $c_{\text{water}}=4200\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}\ K^{-1}}$ and
    $L_v=2.26 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}}$.

18. On a heating curve, explain what is happening to particle kinetic energy
    and potential energy during a sloping section and during a flat section.

19. In an electrical experiment to find specific heat capacity of a metal
    block, some energy is lost to the surroundings. If the student assumes all
    electrical energy heats the block, explain whether the calculated value of
    $c$ is too high or too low.

20. A $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ metal block is heated by a $30\ \mathrm{W}$ heater
    for $200\ \mathrm{s}$. Its temperature rises by $10\ \mathrm{K}$. If the
    true specific heat capacity is $900\ \mathrm{J\ kg^{-1}\ K^{-1}}$, find
    the useful energy gained by the block, the energy lost, and the efficiency
    of the heating.
