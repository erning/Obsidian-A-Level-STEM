# Work, Energy and Power Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. In each numerical problem,
write the energy transfer or work done statement before substituting values.

Take $g=9.81\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where needed.

## A. Work Done

1. A constant force of $75\ \mathrm{N}$ pushes a box $3.2\ \mathrm{m}$ in the
   direction of the force. Calculate the work done.

2. A rope pulls a crate with force $150\ \mathrm{N}$ at $25^\circ$ above the
   horizontal. The crate moves $6.0\ \mathrm{m}$ horizontally. Calculate the
   work done by the rope.

3. A block slides $8.0\ \mathrm{m}$ along a horizontal surface while friction
   of $40\ \mathrm{N}$ acts opposite the motion. Calculate the work done by
   friction.

4. A stone moves in a horizontal circle at constant speed. The tension in the
   string is directed toward the centre of the circle. Explain why the tension
   does no work on the stone.

5. A force-displacement graph has force increasing uniformly from $0$ to
   $60\ \mathrm{N}$ over the first $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$, then remaining at
   $60\ \mathrm{N}$ from $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$ to $1.20\ \mathrm{m}$. Calculate
   the work done.

## B. Gravitational Potential Energy and Kinetic Energy

6. A $12\ \mathrm{kg}$ load is lifted vertically through $2.5\ \mathrm{m}$ at
   constant speed. Calculate the increase in gravitational potential energy.

7. A $0.25\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball travels at $18\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Calculate
   its kinetic energy.

8. A $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ car speeds up from $12\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to
   $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Calculate the increase in kinetic energy.

9. A stone is dropped from rest through a vertical height of $15\ \mathrm{m}$.
   Find its speed just before impact, ignoring air resistance.

10. A ball is thrown vertically upwards at $14\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Use energy
    to find its maximum height above the launch point, ignoring air resistance.

## C. Conservation of Energy and Losses

11. A $0.60\ \mathrm{kg}$ object slides down a track through a vertical drop of
    $1.8\ \mathrm{m}$. It starts from rest and reaches the bottom at
    $5.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Calculate the energy transferred to internal
    energy and sound, and the percentage efficiency of transfer to kinetic
    energy.

12. A pendulum bob is released from rest at a point
    $0.45\ \mathrm{m}$ above the lowest point of its swing. Calculate its speed
    at the lowest point, ignoring air resistance.

13. A roller-coaster car starts from rest at a height of $25\ \mathrm{m}$ and
    later reaches a point of height $10\ \mathrm{m}$. Find its speed at the
    lower point, assuming no energy losses.

14. A $1200\ \mathrm{kg}$ car travelling at $22\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ is brought
    to rest by a constant average braking force of $4800\ \mathrm{N}$. Estimate
    the stopping distance.

15. A vehicle engine does useful work against resistive forces of
    $800\ \mathrm{N}$ while the vehicle moves $500\ \mathrm{m}$ at steady speed.
    The fuel supplies $8.0\times 10^5\ \mathrm{J}$ of energy during this
    journey. Calculate the useful work done and the efficiency.

## D. Power and Mixed Review

16. A lift motor raises a $500\ \mathrm{kg}$ lift car through
    $18\ \mathrm{m}$ in $12\ \mathrm{s}$. Calculate the useful output power
    against gravity.

17. A car engine supplies $20\ \mathrm{kW}$ of useful power at a steady speed
    of $16\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the total resistive force on the car.

18. A runner of weight $600\ \mathrm{N}$ climbs a vertical height of
    $4.0\ \mathrm{m}$ in $6.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Calculate the useful output power
    against gravity. If the runner's body is $25\%$ efficient for this task,
    find the input power from chemical energy.

19. Derive $P=Fv$ from $P=W/t$ for a constant force acting in the direction of
    motion.

20. An object falls from rest through $5.0\ \mathrm{m}$ with negligible air
    resistance. Use both an energy method and a force/kinematics method to find
    the impact speed, and explain why the two methods agree.
