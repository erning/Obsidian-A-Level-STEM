# Work, Energy and Power Review Checklist

Use this after the worked examples and practice problems. Any unchecked item is
a cue to return to the lecture notes and redo one relevant calculation.

## Core Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Work done is energy transferred by a force moving through a displacement.
- [ ] Work done by a constant force is $W=Fs\cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is the
  angle between the force and the displacement.
- [ ] The joule is the unit of work and energy, with
  $1\ \mathrm{J}=1\ \mathrm{N\ m}$.
- [ ] The area under a force-displacement graph gives the work done.
- [ ] In a uniform gravitational field, $\Delta E_p=mg\Delta h$.
- [ ] Kinetic energy is $E_k=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
- [ ] The principle of conservation of energy applies to a closed system.
- [ ] Efficiency is useful output divided by total input, using either energy
  values or power values consistently.
- [ ] Power is the rate of work done or rate of energy transfer:
  $P=W/t$.
- [ ] For a constant force in the direction of motion, $P=Fv$.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Decide whether a force does positive, negative, or zero work.
- [ ] Resolve an angled force before calculating work done.
- [ ] Find work done from the area under a force-displacement graph.
- [ ] Derive and use $\Delta E_p=mg\Delta h$ from $W=Fs$.
- [ ] Derive and use $E_k=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$ from equations of motion.
- [ ] Write an energy equation with initial energy, final energy, inputs, and
  losses.
- [ ] Use efficiency to find useful output, total input, or wasted energy.
- [ ] Use $P=W/t$ for timed transfers and $P=Fv$ for steady motion.
- [ ] Compare an energy method with a dynamics or kinematics method.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Using $W=Fs$ when the force is not in the direction of displacement.
- [ ] Treating a force at $90^\circ$ to the displacement as doing work.
- [ ] Forgetting the negative sign for work done by a resistive force.
- [ ] Using path length instead of vertical height in
  $\Delta E_p=mg\Delta h$.
- [ ] Calculating $\Delta E_k$ by squaring the change in speed.
- [ ] Assuming kinetic energy is conserved whenever momentum is conserved.
- [ ] Treating energy lost from mechanical stores as destroyed.
- [ ] Mixing energy efficiency with power efficiency in the same ratio.
- [ ] Confusing the symbol $W$ for work done with the unit $\mathrm{W}$ for
  watt.

## Quick Self-Checks

- [ ] I can explain why holding a stationary load does no mechanical work on
  the load.
- [ ] I can use a force-displacement graph without assuming the force is
  constant.
- [ ] I can show why $m$ cancels in simple falling problems with no air
  resistance.
- [ ] I can state where the missing mechanical energy goes in a system with
  friction or drag.
- [ ] I can derive $P=Fv$ from $P=W/t$ and $W=Fs$.
