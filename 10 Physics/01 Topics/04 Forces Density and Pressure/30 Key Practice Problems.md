# Forces, Density and Pressure Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw force lines of action
and pressure surfaces before choosing an equation.

Take $g=9.81\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where needed.

## A. Moments, Couples, and Equilibrium

1. A $45\ \mathrm{N}$ force acts at the end of a lever
   $0.28\ \mathrm{m}$ from a pivot. Find the moment about the pivot when:

   a. the force is perpendicular to the lever;
   b. the force is at $40^\circ$ to the lever.

2. A child of weight $350\ \mathrm{N}$ sits $1.2\ \mathrm{m}$ from the pivot of
   a seesaw. A second child has weight $420\ \mathrm{N}$. How far from the pivot
   should the second child sit on the other side for equilibrium?

3. A uniform beam of length $2.4\ \mathrm{m}$ and weight $60\ \mathrm{N}$ is
   supported at both ends. A $90\ \mathrm{N}$ load is placed
   $1.8\ \mathrm{m}$ from the left support. Find the upward force at each
   support.

4. Two equal and opposite forces of $25\ \mathrm{N}$ form a couple. The
   perpendicular separation of their lines of action is $0.18\ \mathrm{m}$.
   Find the torque of the couple.

5. A sign of weight $40\ \mathrm{N}$ is supported by two strings. The left
   string is at $45^\circ$ to the horizontal and the right string is at
   $30^\circ$ to the horizontal. Find the tension in each string.

6. Explain how a plumb line can be used to find the centre of gravity of a thin
   irregular lamina.

## B. Density and Pressure

7. A rectangular metal block has mass $0.840\ \mathrm{kg}$ and dimensions
   $4.0\ \mathrm{cm}\times 3.0\ \mathrm{cm}\times 2.0\ \mathrm{cm}$. Find its
   density in $\mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$.

8. Convert $1.25\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}$ to $\mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$.

9. A normal force of $180\ \mathrm{N}$ acts on an area of
   $0.015\ \mathrm{m^2}$. Find the pressure.

10. In a hydraulic system, a force of $80\ \mathrm{N}$ is applied to a small
    piston of area $4.0\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{m^2}$. The pressure is transmitted
    to a large piston of area $0.020\ \mathrm{m^2}$. Find the force on the large
    piston, assuming no losses.

## C. Hydrostatic Pressure and Upthrust

11. Find the pressure difference between the surface of water and a point
    $2.5\ \mathrm{m}$ below the surface. Use
    $\rho_{\text{water}}=1000\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$.

12. Oil of density $850\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$ fills a tank to a depth of
    $6.0\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the pressure difference between the surface and the
    bottom.

13. A rectangular block is fully submerged in water. Its vertical height is
    $0.12\ \mathrm{m}$ and its horizontal cross-sectional area is
    $0.030\ \mathrm{m^2}$. Find the pressure difference between its lower and
    upper faces and hence the upthrust. Check the answer using
    $F=\rho gV$.

14. A block has mass $1.8\ \mathrm{kg}$ and volume
    $2.0\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{m^3}$. It is fully submerged in water. Find its
    upthrust and apparent weight.

15. A wooden block of density $600\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$ and volume
    $0.020\ \mathrm{m^3}$ floats in water. Find the fraction of the volume
    submerged and the upthrust.

16. A ferry has horizontal cross-sectional area
    $600\ \mathrm{m^2}$ at the water line. Twelve cars of average mass
    $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$ drive onto the ferry. Find the extra depth that the
    ferry sinks in water of density $1000\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$.

## D. Mixed Review

17. Derive

    $$
    \Delta p=\rho g\Delta h
    $$

    using a vertical column of fluid of cross-sectional area $A$ and height
    $\Delta h$.

18. Two forces of $10\ \mathrm{N}$ act vertically on a horizontal rod. The left
    force acts upwards and the right force acts downwards. Their lines of action
    are $0.50\ \mathrm{m}$ apart. Explain why the resultant force is zero but
    the rod is not in rotational equilibrium.

19. An object of density $750\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$ is placed in oil of density
    $900\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$. State whether it floats or sinks, and find the
    fraction of its volume submerged if it floats.

20. A door handle is $0.32\ \mathrm{m}$ from the hinge. A force of
    $60\ \mathrm{N}$ is applied at $70^\circ$ to the door. Find the moment about
    the hinge.
