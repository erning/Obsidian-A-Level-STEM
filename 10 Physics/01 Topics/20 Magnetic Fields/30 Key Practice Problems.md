# Magnetic Fields Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw the direction of the
field, current, velocity, or force before calculating, because most errors in
this topic are direction errors disguised as formula errors.

## A. Field Patterns and Conductor Forces

1. State what is meant by a magnetic field and explain what magnetic field
   lines represent.

2. Sketch the magnetic field pattern for each of the following:
   a long straight wire, a flat circular coil, and a long solenoid. State the
   rule used to find the direction in each case.

3. A long solenoid carries a steady current. Explain the effect of placing a
   ferrous core inside the solenoid.

4. A wire of length $0.15\ \mathrm{m}$ carries a current of $4.0\ \mathrm{A}$
   at right angles to a magnetic field of flux density $0.28\ \mathrm{T}$.
   Find the force on the wire.

5. The wire in question 4 is turned so that the angle between the wire and the
   magnetic field is $35^\circ$. Find the new force.

6. A wire of length $0.12\ \mathrm{m}$ is placed at right angles to a magnetic
   field. It carries a current of $2.0\ \mathrm{A}$ and experiences a force of
   $0.048\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the magnetic flux density.

7. A current-carrying wire is parallel to a uniform magnetic field. Explain why
   the magnetic force on the wire is zero.

## B. Moving Charges, Hall Voltage, and Selection

8. A positive ion moves to the right in a magnetic field directed into the
   page. State the direction of the magnetic force on the ion. State what would
   change if the ion were negatively charged.

9. A proton moves at $2.5 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ perpendicular to a
   uniform magnetic field of flux density $0.18\ \mathrm{T}$. Find the
   magnetic force on the proton.

10. The proton in question 9 instead moves at $40^\circ$ to the magnetic
    field. Find the magnetic force.

11. Explain why a magnetic force can make a charged particle move in a circle
    without increasing its speed.

12. A Hall probe has charge-carrier number density
    $6.0 \times 10^{22}\ \mathrm{m^{-3}}$ and thickness
    $0.40\ \mathrm{mm}$. It carries a current of $30\ \mathrm{mA}$ in a
    magnetic field of flux density $0.40\ \mathrm{T}$. Calculate the Hall
    voltage using $q=1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$.

13. State how the orientation of a Hall probe affects its reading.

14. A proton of mass $1.67 \times 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg}$ and charge
    $1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$ enters a uniform magnetic field of flux
    density $0.16\ \mathrm{T}$ at right angles with speed
    $2.4 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the radius of its circular
    path.

15. An electron and a proton enter the same uniform magnetic field with the
    same speed at right angles to the field. Compare the radii and directions
    of curvature of their paths.

16. Crossed electric and magnetic fields are used as a velocity selector. The
    electric field strength is $2.7 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{V\ m^{-1}}$ and the
    magnetic flux density is $0.090\ \mathrm{T}$. Find the speed of undeflected
    particles.

## C. Currents, Flux, and Induction

17. Two long parallel wires carry currents. Explain why currents in the same
    direction attract and currents in opposite directions repel.

18. A uniform magnetic field of flux density $0.45\ \mathrm{T}$ passes
    perpendicular to a flat coil of area $3.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^2}$.
    Find the magnetic flux through the coil.

19. The field in question 18 makes an angle of $60^\circ$ with the normal to
    the coil. Find the magnetic flux.

20. A coil has $240$ turns and area $2.5 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^2}$. The
    magnetic field is perpendicular to the plane of the coil. The flux density
    decreases uniformly from $0.50\ \mathrm{T}$ to $0.10\ \mathrm{T}$ in
    $0.80\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the magnitude of the induced e.m.f. and state how
    Lenz's law fixes its direction.

21. A conducting rod of length $0.20\ \mathrm{m}$ moves at
    $4.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ perpendicular to a magnetic field of flux
    density $0.50\ \mathrm{T}$. The circuit resistance is $5.0\ \Omega$. Find
    the induced e.m.f. and current.

22. State three changes that can increase the magnitude of the induced e.m.f.
    in a coil.

23. A north pole of a magnet is pushed towards a coil connected to a
    galvanometer. Explain the direction of the induced effect using Lenz's
    law.

24. A student says, "Lenz's law means the induced current always opposes the
    magnetic field." Correct this statement.
