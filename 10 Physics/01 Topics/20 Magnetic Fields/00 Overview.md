# 20 Magnetic Fields

## Core Idea

Magnetic fields act on moving charges and currents, and changing magnetic flux induces e.m.f.

## Source Alignment

- Primary syllabus source: CAIE Physics 9702.
- 20 Magnetic fields
- 20.1 Concept of a magnetic field
- 20.2 Force on a current-carrying conductor
- 20.3 Force on a moving charge
- 20.4 Magnetic fields due to currents
- 20.5 Electromagnetic induction
- Coursebook route: Physics Coursebook Chapters 24-26: Magnetic fields, charged-particle motion, Hall effect, and electromagnetic induction.

## What to Learn

- Use magnetic flux density and field-line representations.
- Find the force on a current-carrying conductor and on a moving charge in a magnetic field.
- Analyse circular motion of charged particles in a magnetic field.
- Use magnetic fields due to currents and the force between current-carrying conductors.
- Use magnetic flux, flux linkage, Faraday's law, and Lenz's law for electromagnetic induction.

## How to Study

- Practise direction rules slowly and check with energy or Lenz's law.
- Connect magnetic force to circular motion whenever the force is perpendicular to velocity.
- Treat induction as a changing-flux story before writing equations.

## Practice Directions

- Calculate magnetic force and flux density.
- Solve charged-particle circular-motion problems.
- Use induced e.m.f. and flux-linkage rates of change.

## Common Traps

- Applying magnetic force to stationary charges.
- Forgetting the angle between motion/current and field.
- Using Lenz's law as a memorised direction instead of an energy-conservation check.
