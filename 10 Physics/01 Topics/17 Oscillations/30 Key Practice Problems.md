# Oscillations Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Measure displacement from
equilibrium, use radians for phase, and check whether acceleration is directed
towards equilibrium.

## A. Describing SHM

1. Define displacement, amplitude, period, frequency, angular frequency, and
   phase difference for an oscillation.

2. An oscillator has period $0.40\ \mathrm{s}$. Find its frequency and angular
   frequency.

3. Two identical oscillations have time separation $0.15\ \mathrm{s}$ between
   corresponding maxima. Their period is $0.60\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the phase
   difference in radians and degrees.

4. A body has acceleration $a=-16x$, with SI units. Show that the motion is
   SHM and find $\omega$ and $T$.

5. An $a$ against $x$ graph is a straight line through the origin with gradient
   $-36\ \mathrm{s^{-2}}$. Find the angular frequency and period.

## B. SHM Equations and Graphs

6. An oscillator has displacement

   $$
   x=0.030\sin(10t),
   $$

   where $x$ is in metres and $t$ is in seconds. Find $x_0$, $\omega$, $T$,
   and $x$ at $t=0.157\ \mathrm{s}$.

7. A body oscillates with amplitude $0.060\ \mathrm{m}$ and angular frequency
   $8.0\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Find its maximum speed and maximum
   acceleration.

8. The same body as in Problem 7 is at displacement $0.020\ \mathrm{m}$. Find
   its speed.

9. In SHM, a body is at negative displacement and moving towards equilibrium.
   State the direction of acceleration and whether its speed is increasing or
   decreasing.

10. At maximum positive displacement in SHM, state the velocity, acceleration,
    kinetic energy, and potential energy.

## C. Energy in SHM

11. A $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ oscillator has amplitude $0.040\ \mathrm{m}$ and
    angular frequency $6.0\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Find the total energy.

12. For the oscillator in Problem 11, find the potential energy and kinetic
    energy at $x=0.020\ \mathrm{m}$.

13. If the amplitude of an undamped oscillator is doubled while $m$ and
    $\omega$ stay the same, by what factor does the total energy change?

14. A mass on a spring has restoring force $F=-kx$. Show that its motion can
    be SHM and state the relationship between $\omega$, $k$, and $m$.

15. A pendulum is described as approximately SHM only for small angles. Explain
    why the word "approximately" is needed.

## D. Damping, Forced Oscillation, and Resonance

16. Describe how the displacement-time graph changes for light damping,
    critical damping, and heavy damping.

17. Define forced oscillation and resonance.

18. Describe how increasing damping changes a resonance curve.

19. Give one example where resonance is useful and one example where resonance
    may be damaging. In each case, mention energy transfer.

20. A student says, "The speed is greatest at maximum displacement because the
    displacement is greatest there." Correct the statement using the energy
    exchange in SHM.
