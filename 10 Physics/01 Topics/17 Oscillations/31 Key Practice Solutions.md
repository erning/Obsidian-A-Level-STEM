# Oscillations Key Practice Solutions

Use these solutions to check the physical picture as well as the calculation.
In SHM, the acceleration always points back towards equilibrium.

## A. Describing SHM

1. Displacement is the signed distance from equilibrium. Amplitude is the
   maximum magnitude of displacement. Period is the time for one complete
   oscillation. Frequency is the number of oscillations per second. Angular
   frequency is the phase change per second, in $\mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Phase
   difference measures how far one oscillation is ahead of another in a cycle.

2. Frequency:

   $$
   f=\frac{1}{T}=\frac{1}{0.40}=2.5\ \mathrm{Hz}.
   $$

   Angular frequency:

   $$
   \omega=2\pi f=2\pi(2.5)=15.7\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

3. Phase difference:

   $$
   \phi=2\pi\frac{\Delta t}{T}
   =
   2\pi\frac{0.15}{0.60}
   =
   \frac{\pi}{2}\ \mathrm{rad}.
   $$

   In degrees,

   $$
   360^\circ\frac{0.15}{0.60}=90^\circ.
   $$

4. SHM has the form

   $$
   a=-\omega^2x.
   $$

   Since $a=-16x$, the acceleration is proportional to displacement and
   opposite in direction. Therefore the motion is SHM. Also

   $$
   \omega^2=16,
   $$

   so

   $$
   \omega=4.0\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

   The period is

   $$
   T=\frac{2\pi}{\omega}
   =
   \frac{2\pi}{4.0}
   =
   1.57\ \mathrm{s}.
   $$

5. For an $a$ against $x$ graph,

   $$
   \text{gradient}=-\omega^2.
   $$

   Thus

   $$
   \omega=\sqrt{36}=6.0\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

   The period is

   $$
   T=\frac{2\pi}{6.0}=1.05\ \mathrm{s}.
   $$

## B. SHM Equations and Graphs

6. Compare

   $$
   x=0.030\sin(10t)
   $$

   with $x=x_0\sin\omega t$. Therefore

   $$
   x_0=0.030\ \mathrm{m},
   \qquad
   \omega=10\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

   The period is

   $$
   T=\frac{2\pi}{10}=0.628\ \mathrm{s}.
   $$

   At $t=0.157\ \mathrm{s}$,

   $$
   10t=1.57\ \mathrm{rad}\approx\frac{\pi}{2},
   $$

   so

   $$
   x\approx 0.030\ \mathrm{m}.
   $$

7. Maximum speed:

   $$
   v_0=\omega x_0=(8.0)(0.060)=0.48\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

   Maximum acceleration:

   $$
   a_{\text{max}}=\omega^2x_0=(8.0)^2(0.060)
   =
   3.84\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}.
   $$

8. Use

   $$
   |v|=\omega\sqrt{x_0^2-x^2}.
   $$

   Hence

   $$
   |v|
   =
   8.0\sqrt{(0.060)^2-(0.020)^2}
   =
   0.45\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}.
   $$

9. Negative displacement means the body is on the negative side of
   equilibrium. Since $a=-\omega^2x$, acceleration is positive, towards
   equilibrium. If it is moving towards equilibrium, acceleration and velocity
   are in the same direction, so its speed is increasing.

10. At maximum positive displacement, velocity is zero. Acceleration is
    maximum in magnitude and negative, towards equilibrium. Kinetic energy is
    zero and potential energy is maximum.

## C. Energy in SHM

11. Total energy:

    $$
    E=\frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x_0^2.
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    E=
    \frac{1}{2}(0.50)(6.0)^2(0.040)^2
    =
    1.44 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{J}.
    $$

12. Potential energy at $x=0.020\ \mathrm{m}$:

    $$
    E_p=
    \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x^2
    =
    \frac{1}{2}(0.50)(6.0)^2(0.020)^2
    =
    3.60 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{J}.
    $$

    Kinetic energy:

    $$
    E_k=E-E_p
    =
    1.44 \times 10^{-2}-3.60 \times 10^{-3}
    =
    1.08 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{J}.
    $$

13. Since

    $$
    E=\frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x_0^2,
    $$

    total energy is proportional to amplitude squared. Doubling amplitude
    multiplies the energy by $2^2=4$.

14. Newton's second law gives

    $$
    ma=-kx.
    $$

    Hence

    $$
    a=-\frac{k}{m}x.
    $$

    This has the SHM form $a=-\omega^2x$, so

    $$
    \omega^2=\frac{k}{m}.
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    \omega=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}.
    $$

15. For a pendulum, the restoring effect is only proportional to displacement
    when the angle is small, so $\sin\theta\approx\theta$ in radians. At large
    angles, this approximation fails, so the motion is not exactly SHM.

## D. Damping, Forced Oscillation, and Resonance

16. With light damping, the graph continues to oscillate while the amplitude
    gradually decreases. With critical damping, the graph returns to equilibrium
    in the shortest time without crossing and oscillating. With heavy damping,
    it returns to equilibrium without oscillating but more slowly.

17. A forced oscillation is an oscillation maintained by an external periodic
    driving force. Resonance occurs when the driving frequency equals the
    natural frequency of the system, giving maximum amplitude because energy is
    transferred most efficiently.

18. Increasing damping lowers the maximum amplitude of the resonance peak and
    makes the peak broader. The frequency of maximum response may shift
    slightly lower.

19. Useful resonance: a radio tuning circuit responds strongly to the selected
    signal frequency, so energy transfer from that signal is greatest. Harmful
    resonance: a bridge or building may receive energy from wind, footsteps, or
    earthquake motion near its natural frequency, producing large oscillations.

20. The speed is zero at maximum displacement because the oscillator is turning
    around at an extreme. At that instant, energy is stored as potential energy.
    Speed is greatest at equilibrium, where potential energy is minimum and
    kinetic energy is maximum.
