# Superposition Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw the path difference,
fringe spacing, grating angle, or node pattern before substituting values.

## A. Superposition and Interference Conditions

1. At one point, three waves have displacements $+3.0\ \mathrm{mm}$,
   $-1.2\ \mathrm{mm}$, and $+0.5\ \mathrm{mm}$. Find the resultant
   displacement.

2. Two waves of amplitude $4.0\ \mathrm{mm}$ meet. Find the resultant amplitude
   when they are in phase and when they are exactly in antiphase. For the
   in-phase case, state the intensity relative to one wave alone.

3. Two coherent waves of wavelength $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$ arrive at a point with
   path difference $2.4\ \mathrm{m}$. State whether the interference is
   constructive or destructive, and find the phase difference in degrees.

4. Two coherent waves of wavelength $0.60\ \mathrm{m}$ arrive with path
   difference $0.90\ \mathrm{m}$. State whether the interference is
   constructive or destructive.

5. State the meaning of coherent sources and give two conditions needed for a
   stable two-source interference pattern.

## B. Diffraction and Double-Slit Interference

6. Plane water waves pass through a gap. Explain how the diffraction changes
   when the gap width is much larger than the wavelength, then when it is about
   equal to the wavelength.

7. Light of wavelength $630\ \mathrm{nm}$ passes through a double slit with
   slit separation $0.40\ \mathrm{mm}$. The screen is $2.0\ \mathrm{m}$ from
   the slits. Find the fringe spacing.

8. In a double-slit experiment, the distance from the first bright fringe to
   the eleventh bright fringe is $24.0\ \mathrm{mm}$. The slit separation is
   $0.25\ \mathrm{mm}$ and the screen is $1.2\ \mathrm{m}$ away. Find the
   wavelength of the light.

9. Use $x=\lambda D/a$ to state how the fringe spacing changes if:
   a. the wavelength is increased;
   b. the screen distance is increased;
   c. the slit separation is increased.

10. In a microwave two-source interference experiment, the path difference at a
    detector is $4.2\ \mathrm{cm}$. The wavelength is $2.8\ \mathrm{cm}$. State
    whether the detector is at a maximum or a minimum.

## C. Diffraction Gratings

11. Monochromatic light of wavelength $500\ \mathrm{nm}$ is incident normally
    on a grating with $600$ lines per millimetre. Find the angle of the
    first-order maximum.

12. For the grating and light in Problem 11, find the largest possible order.

13. A diffraction grating has slit spacing $2.00\times 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{m}$.
    The second-order maximum is observed at $30.0^\circ$. Find the wavelength.

14. White light is incident normally on a diffraction grating. Explain why the
    zero-order maximum is white and why red light is at a larger angle than
    violet light in the same non-zero order.

15. A grating has slit spacing $1.25\times 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the line
    density in lines per metre and in lines per millimetre.

## D. Stationary Waves

16. Adjacent nodes in a stationary wave are $12\ \mathrm{cm}$ apart. The wave
    speed is $48\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the wavelength and frequency.

17. A string fixed at both ends has length $1.2\ \mathrm{m}$. It vibrates with
    four loops at frequency $180\ \mathrm{Hz}$. Find the wavelength and wave
    speed on the string.

18. In a microwave stationary wave experiment, the distance from the first
    minimum to the fifth minimum is $6.0\ \mathrm{cm}$. Find the wavelength and
    frequency of the microwaves. Use $c=3.0\times 10^8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

19. An air column open at both ends has length $0.85\ \mathrm{m}$. For its
    simplest stationary wave pattern, find the wavelength and frequency of the
    sound. Use $v=340\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

20. Explain how a stationary wave is formed from two progressive waves. State
    the phase relationship between points in the same loop and points in
    adjacent loops.
