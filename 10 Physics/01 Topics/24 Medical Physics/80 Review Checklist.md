# Medical Physics Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. For every method, name the
source, contrast mechanism, detector signal, reconstruction method, and main
risk or limitation.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] A piezoelectric crystal changes shape when a p.d. is applied and
      generates an e.m.f. when its shape changes.
- [ ] A piezoelectric transducer can both emit and detect ultrasound.
- [ ] Ultrasound reflections at tissue boundaries give diagnostic information.
- [ ] Echo depth uses return time, so $d=v\Delta t/2$.
- [ ] Specific acoustic impedance is $Z=\rho v$.
- [ ] A larger acoustic impedance mismatch gives stronger reflection.
- [ ] Ultrasound gel reduces reflection at the transducer-skin boundary.
- [ ] Ultrasound attenuation follows $I=I_0e^{-\mu x}$.
- [ ] Higher ultrasound frequency improves resolution but reduces penetration.
- [ ] X-rays are produced when accelerated electrons hit and decelerate in a
      metal target.
- [ ] The minimum X-ray wavelength comes from $eV=hc/\lambda_{\min}$.
- [ ] X-ray contrast comes from different attenuation in different tissues.
- [ ] X-ray attenuation follows $I=I_0e^{-\mu x}$.
- [ ] CT reconstructs slices from many X-ray measurements at different angles.
- [ ] A PET tracer contains radioactive nuclei absorbed by the tissue being
      studied.
- [ ] PET uses a beta-plus emitting tracer, positron-electron annihilation, and
      gamma-ray coincidence detection.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Explain ultrasound production and detection using the piezoelectric
      effect.
- [ ] Calculate echo depth from ultrasound return time.
- [ ] Calculate specific acoustic impedance and reflection coefficient.
- [ ] Use exponential attenuation with compatible units.
- [ ] Calculate the minimum wavelength from an X-ray tube accelerating p.d.
- [ ] Explain X-ray image contrast and the purpose of filters or contrast
      media.
- [ ] Explain how CT reconstructs a three-dimensional image.
- [ ] Calculate the energy of PET annihilation photons using $E_\gamma=m_ec^2$.
- [ ] Explain PET line-of-response reconstruction from arrival times.
- [ ] Compare ultrasound, X-ray/CT, and PET by source, contrast, risk, and
      information produced.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Forgetting the factor of 2 in ultrasound echo distance.
- [ ] Treating acoustic impedance as density alone.
- [ ] Using the reflection coefficient formula when normal incidence is not
      stated or justified.
- [ ] Mixing the speed of sound in tissue with the speed of light in vacuum.
- [ ] Using metres with $\mu$ given in $\mathrm{cm^{-1}}$, or centimetres with
      $\mu$ given in $\mathrm{m^{-1}}$.
- [ ] Thinking CT is one sharper X-ray photograph.
- [ ] Forgetting that X-rays and PET involve ionising radiation.
- [ ] Placing positron-electron annihilation in the detector instead of in the
      patient's tissue.
- [ ] Treating PET as an ordinary photograph of the tracer.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can explain how ultrasound pulses are produced, reflected, detected,
      and converted into A-scan or B-scan information.
- [ ] I can calculate ultrasound depth, reflection coefficient, and attenuation.
- [ ] I can derive and use the minimum X-ray wavelength equation.
- [ ] I can explain X-ray contrast and CT reconstruction.
- [ ] I can describe PET from tracer injection to positron emission,
      annihilation, gamma detection, and image reconstruction.
- [ ] I can compare the three methods by information gained and radiation risk.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [Waves](../07%20Waves/00%20Overview.md)
      and [Nuclear Physics](../23%20Nuclear%20Physics/00%20Overview.md).
