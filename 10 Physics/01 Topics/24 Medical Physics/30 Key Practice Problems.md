# Medical Physics Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. For each method, identify
the source, the interaction that creates contrast, what is detected, and how
the image is reconstructed.

Use these constants unless stated otherwise:

$$
h=6.63 \times 10^{-34}\ \mathrm{J\ s},\quad
c=3.00 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}},\quad
e=1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}.
$$

## A. Ultrasound

1. Explain how the piezoelectric effect allows one ultrasound transducer to
   transmit and receive ultrasound.

2. Ultrasound travels through soft tissue at
   $1540\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. An echo returns
   $52\ \mu\mathrm{s}$ after the pulse is emitted. Find the depth of the
   reflecting boundary.

3. Explain the difference between an A-scan and a B-scan.

4. A tissue has density $1050\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$ and ultrasound speed
   $1540\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Calculate its specific acoustic impedance.

5. At normal incidence, ultrasound meets a boundary where
   $Z_1=1.62 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-2}\ s^{-1}}$ and
   $Z_2=1.48 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{kg\ m^{-2}\ s^{-1}}$. Calculate the
   intensity reflection coefficient.

6. Explain why ultrasound gel is used between the transducer and the skin.

7. Ultrasound of initial intensity $5.0\ \mathrm{W\ m^{-2}}$ travels
   $6.0\ \mathrm{cm}$ through tissue with attenuation coefficient
   $25\ \mathrm{m^{-1}}$. Find the transmitted intensity.

8. Explain the tradeoff between ultrasound frequency, resolution, and
   penetration.

## B. X-Rays and CT

9. Explain how X-rays are produced in an X-ray tube.

10. Electrons in an X-ray tube are accelerated through $60\ \mathrm{kV}$.
    Calculate the minimum wavelength of the X-rays produced.

11. Explain why aluminium filters may be used in an X-ray beam.

12. X-rays of initial intensity $100\ \mathrm{W\ m^{-2}}$ pass through
    $4.0\ \mathrm{cm}$ of tissue with attenuation coefficient
    $0.35\ \mathrm{cm^{-1}}$. Find the transmitted intensity.

13. Explain the term contrast in X-ray imaging.

14. Explain how CT scanning forms a three-dimensional image rather than a
    single shadow image.

15. State one benefit and one risk of X-ray or CT imaging.

## C. PET and Method Comparison

16. State what a tracer is and why a beta-plus emitting tracer is used in PET
    scanning.

17. Describe what happens when a positron emitted by the tracer meets an
    electron in the tissue.

18. Calculate the energy of each gamma-ray photon emitted in electron-positron
    annihilation. Use $m_e=9.11 \times 10^{-31}\ \mathrm{kg}$.

19. Explain how coincidence detection gives a line of response in PET.

20. Explain why a PET image shows tracer concentration rather than a simple
    surface photograph.

21. Compare the main contrast mechanism in ultrasound, X-ray imaging, and PET.

22. For each case, choose a suitable imaging method and give a physics reason:
    foetal imaging, imaging a broken bone, and mapping active tissue uptake.

23. A student says that PET works by photographing the radioactive tracer
    directly. Correct this statement.

24. Compare the ionising-radiation risk of ultrasound, X-ray/CT, and PET.
