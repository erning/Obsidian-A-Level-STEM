# Capacitance Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Identify series and
parallel groups before using $Q=CV$, and convert microfarads to farads in
energy or time-constant calculations.

## A. Capacitance and Networks

1. Define capacitance and state the unit farad in base circuit quantities.

2. A $100\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor is charged to $6.0\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the
   charge stored.

3. A capacitor stores $3.6 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{C}$ at a p.d. of
   $12\ \mathrm{V}$. Find its capacitance in farads and microfarads.

4. Capacitors $2.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$, $3.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$, and
   $5.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ are in parallel. Find the equivalent capacitance.

5. Capacitors $2.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ and $3.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ are in series.
   Find the equivalent capacitance.

6. A $6.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor and a $3.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor are
   in parallel. This group is in series with a $2.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor.
   Find the total capacitance.

7. Capacitors $4.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ and $8.0\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ are in series
   across $12\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the charge on each capacitor and the p.d.
   across each.

## B. Energy Stored

8. A $220\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor is charged to $15\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the
   stored energy.

9. A capacitor has charge $2.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{C}$ at p.d.
   $50\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the stored energy using $W=\frac{1}{2}QV$.

10. A $V$-$Q$ graph for a capacitor is a straight line from the origin to
    $Q=5.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{C}$ and $V=80\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the area
    under the graph and state its meaning.

11. If the p.d. across a capacitor is doubled while capacitance stays constant,
    by what factor does the stored energy change?

12. A $10\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor initially at $20\ \mathrm{V}$ is
    discharged completely. How much energy is transferred from the capacitor?

## C. Discharge Through a Resistor

13. A $470\ \mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor discharges through a
    $2.0\ \mathrm{k\Omega}$ resistor. Find the time constant.

14. A capacitor starts at $9.0\ \mathrm{V}$ and discharges with time constant
    $3.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Find its p.d. after $6.0\ \mathrm{s}$.

15. A capacitor has initial charge $2.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{C}$ and time
    constant $4.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the charge after one time constant and
    after $8.0\ \mathrm{s}$.

16. A $12\ \mathrm{V}$ capacitor discharges through a $4.0\ \mathrm{k\Omega}$
    resistor. Find the initial current magnitude.

17. A graph of $\ln V$ against time has gradient
    $-0.50\ \mathrm{s^{-1}}$. Find $RC$.

## D. Graphs and Interpretation

18. Describe the shapes of $Q$-$t$, $V$-$t$, and current magnitude-$t$ graphs
    for a discharging capacitor.

19. Explain why the current in a capacitor discharge decreases with time.

20. A student says a capacitor is fully discharged after one time constant.
    Correct this statement quantitatively.
