# D.C. Circuits Key Practice Solutions

These solutions correspond to [[10 Physics/01 Topics/10 DC Circuits/30 Key Practice Problems|Key Practice Problems]].

## A. Practical Circuits and Kirchhoff's Laws

1. An ammeter is connected in series with the component or branch whose current
   is being measured. An ideal ammeter has zero resistance. A voltmeter is
   connected in parallel across the component or section whose p.d. is being
   measured. An ideal voltmeter has infinite resistance.

2. Kirchhoff's first law gives

   $$
   6.0=2.5+1.4+I.
   $$

   Therefore

   $$
   I=2.1\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

3. Total resistance:

   $$
   R=4.0+8.0=12\ \Omega.
   $$

   Current:

   $$
   I=\frac{9.0}{12}=0.75\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   P.d. across $4.0\ \Omega$:

   $$
   V=(0.75)(4.0)=3.0\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

   P.d. across $8.0\ \Omega$:

   $$
   V=(0.75)(8.0)=6.0\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

   The p.d.s add to $9.0\ \mathrm{V}$.

4. Net e.m.f.:

   $$
   12-2.0=10\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

   Current:

   $$
   I=\frac{10}{5.0}
   =2.0\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

5. In parallel, each resistor has p.d. $12\ \mathrm{V}$.

   Current in $6.0\ \Omega$ branch:

   $$
   I=\frac{12}{6.0}=2.0\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   Current in $3.0\ \Omega$ branch:

   $$
   I=\frac{12}{3.0}=4.0\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   Total current:

   $$
   I_{\text{total}}=2.0+4.0=6.0\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

## B. Series, Parallel, and Mixed Networks

6. Total resistance:

   $$
   R=20+30+50=100\ \Omega.
   $$

   Current:

   $$
   I=\frac{10}{100}=0.10\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   P.d.s:

   $$
   V_{20}=(0.10)(20)=2.0\ \mathrm{V},
   $$

   $$
   V_{30}=(0.10)(30)=3.0\ \mathrm{V},
   $$

   $$
   V_{50}=(0.10)(50)=5.0\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

7. For parallel resistors,

   $$
   \frac{1}{R}
   =\frac{1}{20}+\frac{1}{30}+\frac{1}{60}
   =0.100.
   $$

   Therefore

   $$
   R=10\ \Omega.
   $$

8. Parallel group:

   $$
   \frac{1}{R_p}=\frac{1}{8.0}+\frac{1}{12}
   =0.2083,
   $$

   so

   $$
   R_p=4.8\ \Omega.
   $$

   Total resistance:

   $$
   R_{\text{total}}=4.8+5.2=10.0\ \Omega.
   $$

   Total current:

   $$
   I=\frac{20}{10.0}=2.0\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   P.d. across the series $5.2\ \Omega$ resistor:

   $$
   V=(2.0)(5.2)=10.4\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

   P.d. across the parallel group:

   $$
   20-10.4=9.6\ \mathrm{V}.
   $$

   Branch currents:

   $$
   I_{8\Omega}=\frac{9.6}{8.0}=1.2\ \mathrm{A},
   $$

   $$
   I_{12\Omega}=\frac{9.6}{12}=0.80\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

9. Let the unknown resistor be $R$:

   $$
   \frac{1}{8.0}=\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{R}.
   $$

   Hence

   $$
   \frac{1}{R}=\frac{1}{8.0}-\frac{1}{12}
   =\frac{1}{24}.
   $$

   Therefore

   $$
   R=24\ \Omega.
   $$

10. From Problem 5, the total current is $6.0\ \mathrm{A}$. Total power:

    $$
    P=VI=(12)(6.0)=72\ \mathrm{W}.
    $$

## C. Internal Resistance

11. Total resistance:

    $$
    R+r=11+1.0=12\ \Omega.
    $$

    Current:

    $$
    I=\frac{6.0}{12}=0.50\ \mathrm{A}.
    $$

    Lost volts:

    $$
    Ir=(0.50)(1.0)=0.50\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    Terminal p.d.:

    $$
    V=E-Ir=6.0-0.50=5.5\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

12. Current through the external resistor:

    $$
    I=\frac{2.8}{10}=0.28\ \mathrm{A}.
    $$

    Lost volts:

    $$
    E-V=3.0-2.8=0.20\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    Since lost volts is $Ir$,

    $$
    r=\frac{0.20}{0.28}
    =0.71\ \Omega.
    $$

13. For a source,

    $$
    V=E-Ir.
    $$

    On a $V$ against $I$ graph, the intercept is $E$ and the gradient is
    $-r$. Therefore

    $$
    E=4.5\ \mathrm{V},
    \qquad
    r=0.75\ \Omega.
    $$

14. Terminal p.d.:

    $$
    V=E-Ir
    =12-(80)(0.040)
    =8.8\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

15. For a short circuit, the external resistance is approximately zero, so the
    current is limited mainly by internal resistance:

    $$
    I=\frac{E}{r}
    =\frac{3.0}{0.50}
    =6.0\ \mathrm{A}.
    $$

## D. Potential Dividers and Potentiometers

16. Output across $R_2$:

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}
    =V_{\text{in}}\frac{R_2}{R_1+R_2}
    =9.0\frac{2.0}{1.0+2.0}
    =6.0\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

17. Use

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}=V_{\text{in}}\frac{R_2}{R_1+R_2}.
    $$

    Substitute:

    $$
    3.0=12\frac{R_2}{3.0+R_2}.
    $$

    Solve for $R_2$:

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    3.0(3.0+R_2)&=12R_2,\\
    9.0+3.0R_2&=12R_2,\\
    9.0&=9.0R_2.
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    R_2=1.0\ \mathrm{k\Omega}.
    $$

18. Dark:

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}
    =6.0\frac{10}{50+10}
    =1.0\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    Bright:

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}
    =6.0\frac{10}{5.0+10}
    =4.0\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    The output across the fixed resistor increases as light intensity increases.

19. Cold:

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}
    =10\frac{15}{5.0+15}
    =7.5\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    Hot:

    $$
    V_{\text{out}}
    =10\frac{5.0}{5.0+5.0}
    =5.0\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    Because the output is across the thermistor, the output p.d. decreases as
    temperature rises.

20. The p.d. per metre along the potentiometer wire is

    $$
    \frac{4.0}{1.00}=4.0\ \mathrm{V\ m^{-1}}.
    $$

    A balance length of $0.420\ \mathrm{m}$ corresponds to

    $$
    E=(4.0)(0.420)
    =1.68\ \mathrm{V}.
    $$

    A null galvanometer reading means no current flows through the
    galvanometer branch, so the p.d. along the selected wire length equals the
    cell e.m.f.
