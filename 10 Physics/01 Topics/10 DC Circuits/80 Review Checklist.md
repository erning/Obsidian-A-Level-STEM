# D.C. Circuits Review Checklist

Use this after the worked examples and practice problems. Any unchecked item is
a cue to return to the lecture notes and redraw the circuit with junctions,
loops, and output terminals labelled.

## Core Ideas to Recall

- [ ] An ammeter is connected in series and should have very low resistance.
- [ ] A voltmeter is connected in parallel and should have very high resistance.
- [ ] E.m.f. is energy transferred per unit charge in driving charge around a
  complete circuit.
- [ ] P.d. is energy transferred per unit charge in a component.
- [ ] Kirchhoff's first law is conservation of charge at a junction.
- [ ] Kirchhoff's second law is conservation of energy around a closed loop.
- [ ] Series components have the same current.
- [ ] Parallel components have the same p.d.
- [ ] The combined resistance of parallel branches is less than the smallest
  branch resistance.
- [ ] A source delivering current has terminal p.d. $V=E-Ir$.
- [ ] On a graph of terminal p.d. against current, intercept is $E$ and
  gradient is $-r$.
- [ ] A potential divider shares an input p.d. between series components.
- [ ] A potentiometer balance is a null method with zero galvanometer current.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Apply Kirchhoff's first law to a junction.
- [ ] Apply Kirchhoff's second law to a loop, including opposing sources.
- [ ] Derive and use the combined resistance formula for series resistors.
- [ ] Derive and use the combined resistance formula for parallel resistors.
- [ ] Reduce mixed resistor networks in stages.
- [ ] Work back through a reduced network to find branch currents and p.d.s.
- [ ] Calculate current, terminal p.d., lost volts, e.m.f., or internal
  resistance.
- [ ] Use $V_{\text{out}}=V_{\text{in}}R_2/(R_1+R_2)$ only after identifying
  which component the output is across.
- [ ] Explain how LDR and thermistor dividers produce light- or
  temperature-dependent output p.d.s.
- [ ] Use potentiometer balance lengths to compare p.d.s or e.m.f.s.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Treating components as series just because they are drawn in a row.
- [ ] Treating components as parallel without checking that both ends share the
  same two nodes.
- [ ] Adding parallel resistances directly.
- [ ] Forgetting that a parallel group reduces the total resistance.
- [ ] Ignoring the sign of an opposing source in a loop.
- [ ] Treating e.m.f. and terminal p.d. as always equal.
- [ ] Using the potential divider formula without checking the output terminals.
- [ ] Ignoring loading when the output device has resistance comparable with the
  divider resistance.
- [ ] Thinking a null galvanometer reading means zero p.d. everywhere.

## Quick Self-Checks

- [ ] I can explain why Kirchhoff's first law follows from conservation of
  charge.
- [ ] I can explain why Kirchhoff's second law follows from conservation of
  energy.
- [ ] I can derive the series and parallel resistor formulae from Kirchhoff's
  laws.
- [ ] I can predict how terminal p.d. changes when current drawn from a cell
  increases.
- [ ] I can choose the correct output side of an LDR or thermistor divider for
  the desired sensor response.
- [ ] I can explain why a potentiometer does not draw current from the cell
  being measured at balance.
