# Physical Quantities and Units Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Keep units, uncertainties,
and directions visible throughout your working.

## A. Quantities, SI Units, and Prefixes

1. A student writes only this result in a notebook:

   $$
   18
   $$

   Explain why this is not a complete physical quantity. Give two different
   complete physical quantities that could have the numerical magnitude $18$.

2. A resting pulse rate is about $70\ \text{beats per minute}$. Estimate the
   number of heartbeats in one day. Give your answer to one significant figure.

3. Convert each quantity to SI units.

   a. $4.8\ \mathrm{km}$
   b. $75\ \mu\mathrm{s}$
   c. $0.032\ \mathrm{G}\Omega$
   d. $6.0\ \mathrm{cm^3}$

4. Express each derived unit in SI base units.

   a. newton, $\mathrm{N}$
   b. pascal, $\mathrm{Pa}$
   c. coulomb, $\mathrm{C}$
   d. volt, $\mathrm{V}$

5. The density of a liquid is $0.92\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}$. Convert this density
   to $\mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$.

6. Use SI base units to decide whether each equation is homogeneous.

   a. $v^2=u^2+2as$
   b. $v=u+as$

   In each expression, $u$ and $v$ are speeds, $a$ is acceleration, and $s$ is
   displacement.

## B. Errors, Accuracy, Precision, and Uncertainty

7. A length is measured four times, giving these readings in centimetres:

   $$
   81.2,\quad 81.4,\quad 81.3,\quad 81.1.
   $$

   Find the mean value and estimate the uncertainty using half the range.

8. A resistor has potential difference

   $$
   V=(4.82\pm0.01)\ \mathrm{V}
   $$

   and current

   $$
   I=(0.620\pm0.005)\ \mathrm{A}.
   $$

   Find the resistance $R=V/I$ and its absolute uncertainty.

9. A micrometer reads $0.56\ \mathrm{mm}$ when measuring a wire. When the jaws
   are fully closed, it reads $+0.03\ \mathrm{mm}$. Find the corrected wire
   diameter and identify the type of error involved.

10. A rectangular card has

    $$
    L=(8.0\pm0.1)\ \mathrm{cm},
    \qquad
    W=(5.0\pm0.1)\ \mathrm{cm}.
    $$

    Find the perimeter and area with uncertainties.

11. A balance always reads $0.40\ \mathrm{g}$ when nothing is on it. A student
    uses the balance to measure the mass of five identical washers and obtains
    closely grouped readings. Explain which part of this situation is about
    precision and which part is about accuracy.

## C. Scalars and Vectors

12. Classify these quantities as scalar or vector:

    distance, displacement, speed, velocity, mass, force, energy, acceleration,
    temperature, electric field strength.

13. A student walks $120\ \mathrm{m}$ north and then $50\ \mathrm{m}$ south.
    Find the total distance travelled and the resultant displacement.

14. A drone flies $60\ \mathrm{m}$ east and then $80\ \mathrm{m}$ north. Find
    the magnitude and direction of its resultant displacement.

15. A force of $35\ \mathrm{N}$ acts at $40^\circ$ above the horizontal. Find
    its horizontal and vertical components.

16. Two forces act on a small object. One is $12\ \mathrm{N}$ east. The other
    is $18\ \mathrm{N}$ at $120^\circ$ measured anticlockwise from east. Find
    the resultant force as a magnitude and direction.

17. A boat can move at $3.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ due north relative to the
    water. The river current is $1.2\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ due east. Find the
    boat's resultant velocity relative to the river bank.

18. Vector $\mathbf{A}$ is $9.0\ \mathrm{N}$ east. Vector $\mathbf{B}$ is
    $5.0\ \mathrm{N}$ north. Find $\mathbf{A}-\mathbf{B}$ as a magnitude and
    direction.

## D. Mixed Review

19. A trolley moves along a straight track. Its position changes from

    $$
    x_1=(12.4\pm0.1)\ \mathrm{cm}
    $$

    to

    $$
    x_2=(48.9\pm0.1)\ \mathrm{cm}
    $$

    in a time

    $$
    t=(0.120\pm0.002)\ \mathrm{s}.
    $$

    Find the average speed and its absolute uncertainty.

20. A metal cube has side length

    $$
    a=(2.50\pm0.02)\ \mathrm{cm}
    $$

    and mass

    $$
    m=(125\pm1)\ \mathrm{g}.
    $$

    Find its density in $\mathrm{kg\ m^{-3}}$ with an uncertainty.

21. For a mass-spring oscillator, the period is suggested to be

    $$
    T=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}},
    $$

    where $m$ is mass and the spring constant $k$ has unit
    $\mathrm{N\ m^{-1}}$. Show that the equation is homogeneous.

22. An experiment suggests that a resistive force $F$ on a sphere moving
    through a fluid is related to speed $v$ by

    $$
    F=Kv^2.
    $$

    Find the SI base units of $K$.
