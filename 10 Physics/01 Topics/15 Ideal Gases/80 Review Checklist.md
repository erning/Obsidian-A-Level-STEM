# Ideal Gases Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. If a calculation feels
uncertain, check first whether the question is asking for $n$, $N$, $p$, $V$,
$T$, $\langle c^2\rangle$, $c_{\mathrm{r.m.s.}}$, or molecular kinetic
energy.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Amount of substance is an SI base quantity with unit mole.
- [ ] One mole contains $N_\mathrm{A}$ particles.
- [ ] An ideal gas obeys $pV\propto T$ for a fixed amount of gas, where
      $T$ is thermodynamic temperature.
- [ ] The ideal gas equation can be written as $pV=nRT$ or $pV=NkT$.
- [ ] The Boltzmann constant is $k=\frac{R}{N_\mathrm{A}}$.
- [ ] Gas pressure is caused by molecular collisions with container walls.
- [ ] Kinetic theory assumes random molecular motion, elastic collisions,
      negligible molecular volume, and negligible intermolecular forces except
      during collisions.
- [ ] The kinetic theory pressure equation is
      $pV=\frac{1}{3}Nm\langle c^2\rangle$.
- [ ] The root-mean-square speed is
      $c_{\mathrm{r.m.s.}}=\sqrt{\langle c^2\rangle}$.
- [ ] Mean translational kinetic energy is
      $\langle E_k\rangle=\frac{3}{2}kT$.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Convert degrees Celsius to kelvin before using a gas equation.
- [ ] Convert pressure to pascals and volume to cubic metres.
- [ ] Convert between $n$ and $N$ using $N=nN_\mathrm{A}$.
- [ ] Use molar mass consistently with mass units.
- [ ] Use $pV=nRT$ for mole-based ideal-gas calculations.
- [ ] Use $pV=NkT$ for molecule-number calculations.
- [ ] Use $\frac{pV}{T}=\text{constant}$ for two states of a fixed gas sample.
- [ ] Explain pressure using momentum change and collision rate.
- [ ] Use $pV=\frac{1}{3}Nm\langle c^2\rangle$ to find pressure or r.m.s.
      speed.
- [ ] Use $\langle E_k\rangle=\frac{3}{2}kT$ to connect temperature with
      molecular kinetic energy.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Using degrees Celsius in $pV=nRT$ or $pV=NkT$.
- [ ] Treating $n$ and $N$ as the same quantity.
- [ ] Forgetting that $m$ in the kinetic theory pressure equation is the mass
      of one molecule.
- [ ] Mixing grams with kilograms when using molar mass.
- [ ] Confusing mean speed, mean-square speed, and r.m.s. speed.
- [ ] Treating ideal gas behaviour as exact for high-pressure or low-temperature
      real gases.
- [ ] Forgetting that doubling thermodynamic temperature doubles mean
      translational kinetic energy, not r.m.s. speed.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can solve ideal-gas state problems with correct SI units.
- [ ] I can choose between $pV=nRT$ and $pV=NkT$ from the information given.
- [ ] I can state the kinetic theory assumptions and explain why each matters.
- [ ] I can explain gas pressure through molecular momentum change.
- [ ] I can use the pressure equation to connect pressure with
      $\langle c^2\rangle$.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [[10 Physics/01 Topics/14 Temperature/00 Overview|Temperature]]
      and [[10 Physics/01 Topics/16 Thermodynamics/00 Overview|Thermodynamics]].
