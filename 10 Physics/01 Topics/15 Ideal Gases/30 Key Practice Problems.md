# Ideal Gases Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Convert temperature to
kelvin, pressure to pascals, volume to cubic metres, and decide whether the
question is about moles, molecules, or molecular speeds.

Use

$$
N_\mathrm{A}=6.02 \times 10^{23}\ \mathrm{mol^{-1}},
\quad
R=8.31\ \mathrm{J\ mol^{-1}\ K^{-1}},
\quad
k=1.38 \times 10^{-23}\ \mathrm{J\ K^{-1}}
$$

where needed.

## A. Mole, Particles, and Constants

1. Define amount of substance and the mole. State the meaning of Avogadro's
   constant.

2. Find the number of molecules in $0.50\ \mathrm{mol}$ of gas. Then find the
   amount of substance in a sample containing $1.20 \times 10^{24}$ molecules.

3. Oxygen has molar mass $32.0\ \mathrm{g\ mol^{-1}}$. Find the mass of
   $2.00\ \mathrm{mol}$ of oxygen in grams and in kilograms.

4. Use $k=\frac{R}{N_\mathrm{A}}$ to calculate the Boltzmann constant from
   $R$ and $N_\mathrm{A}$.

## B. Ideal Gas Equation

5. Find the volume of $1.00\ \mathrm{mol}$ of ideal gas at
   $1.01 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$ and $20.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$.

6. A gas has pressure $2.00 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$, volume
   $1.00 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m^3}$, and temperature $300\ \mathrm{K}$.
   Find the amount of substance.

7. A fixed volume of gas has pressure $1.00 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$ at
   $20.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$. Find its pressure at
   $100.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$ if the amount of gas is unchanged.

8. A fixed mass of ideal gas changes from
   $p_1=1.50 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$, $V_1=2.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^3}$,
   and $T_1=300\ \mathrm{K}$ to
   $V_2=3.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^3}$ and $T_2=450\ \mathrm{K}$. Find
   $p_2$.

9. A gas occupies $5.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^3}$ at pressure
   $1.2 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$ and temperature $280\ \mathrm{K}$. Find the
   number of molecules.

10. A $0.120\ \mathrm{kg}$ sample of carbon dioxide has molar mass
    $0.0440\ \mathrm{kg\ mol^{-1}}$. It occupies
    $6.00 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m^3}$ at $300\ \mathrm{K}$. Estimate its
    pressure assuming ideal behaviour.

## C. Kinetic Theory

11. State the basic assumptions of the kinetic theory of gases used to derive
    the pressure equation.

12. Explain qualitatively how molecular motion causes gas pressure on a
    container wall.

13. Outline the derivation of
    $pV=\frac{1}{3}Nm\langle c^2\rangle$, explaining the origin of the factor
    $\frac{1}{3}$.

14. A gas contains $2.5 \times 10^{22}$ molecules in a volume
    $1.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{m^3}$. Each molecule has mass
    $4.65 \times 10^{-26}\ \mathrm{kg}$ and
    $c_{\mathrm{r.m.s.}}=500\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Use
    $pV=\frac{1}{3}Nm\langle c^2\rangle$ to find the pressure.

15. A helium gas sample has pressure $1.0 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$, volume
    $2.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m^3}$, and
    $5.0 \times 10^{23}$ atoms. The mass of one helium atom is
    $6.64 \times 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg}$. Find
    $c_{\mathrm{r.m.s.}}$.

16. Find the mean translational kinetic energy of one ideal-gas molecule at
    $27.0\ ^\circ\mathrm{C}$.

17. An ideal gas has thermodynamic temperature doubled while remaining ideal.
    State what happens to the mean translational kinetic energy and to the
    r.m.s. speed.

## D. Interpretation and Limits

18. A student uses $20$ instead of $293$ for the temperature in
    $pV=nRT$. Explain the error and how it affects the calculated value of
    $n$.

19. Explain why a real gas is expected to deviate from ideal behaviour at high
    pressure or low temperature.

20. Distinguish between mean speed, mean-square speed, and root-mean-square
    speed.
