# Electric Fields Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw charge signs before
choosing a direction, and decide whether the field is uniform or radial.

Use

$$
\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}=8.99 \times 10^9\ \mathrm{N\ m^2\ C^{-2}}
$$

where needed.

## A. Field Strength and Uniform Fields

1. Define electric field and electric field strength. State the unit of
   electric field strength.

2. A uniform electric field has strength
   $3.0 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{N\ C^{-1}}$ to the right. Find the force on a
   $+2.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ charge and on a $-2.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ charge.
   Include directions.

3. Describe the field-line pattern for a uniform electric field and for the
   radial field around an isolated positive point charge.

4. Parallel plates are separated by $2.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m}$ and have
   potential difference $500\ \mathrm{V}$. Find the electric field strength
   between the plates.

5. An alpha particle of charge $+3.20 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$ and mass
   $6.64 \times 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg}$ is in a uniform field of strength
   $1.0 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{N\ C^{-1}}$. Find its acceleration.

6. An electron enters a uniform electric field with velocity initially
   perpendicular to the field. Describe the shape of its path and the direction
   of the electric force relative to the field.

## B. Point Charges and Radial Fields

7. Charges $+4.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ and $+1.5\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ are separated by
   $0.30\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the force magnitude and state whether it is
   attractive or repulsive.

8. Two point charges are separated by distance $r$. If the separation is
   doubled, what happens to the force magnitude?

9. Explain why the distance in Coulomb's law for a point outside a charged
   spherical conductor is measured from the centre of the sphere.

10. Find the electric field strength at a point $0.25\ \mathrm{m}$ from a
    charge $-5.0\ \mathrm{nC}$. State the field direction.

11. Two equal positive charges are placed symmetrically on either side of a
    point. State the resultant electric field at the midpoint, and explain why.

## C. Electric Potential and Potential Energy

12. Define electric potential at a point.

13. Find the electric potential at a point $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$ from a charge
    $+6.0\ \mathrm{nC}$.

14. Charges $+6.0\ \mathrm{nC}$ and $-2.0\ \mathrm{nC}$ are each
    $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$ from point P. Find the total potential at P.

15. A charge $-3.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ is placed at a point where the electric
    potential is $+135\ \mathrm{V}$. Find its electric potential energy.

16. Two charges $+2.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ and $-3.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ are separated
    by $0.20\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the electric potential energy of the pair.

## D. Potential Gradient, Equipotentials, and Interpretation

17. Electric potential decreases uniformly from $120\ \mathrm{V}$ to
    $20\ \mathrm{V}$ over $5.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the electric
    field strength and state its direction.

18. Explain why no work is done in moving a charge along an equipotential.

19. Compare electric fields with gravitational fields. State one similarity
    and one important difference.

20. A student uses $E=\frac{\Delta V}{\Delta d}$ to calculate the electric
    field near a point charge over a large distance. Explain why this may be
    invalid and what should be used instead.
