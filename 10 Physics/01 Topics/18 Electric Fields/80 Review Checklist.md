# Electric Fields Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the practice problems. If a result has the wrong
direction or sign, redraw the charge signs and the positive-test-charge field
direction.

## Ideas to Recall

- [ ] Electric field direction is the force direction on a positive test
      charge.
- [ ] Electric field strength is force per unit positive charge:
      $E=\frac{F}{q}$.
- [ ] A positive charge is forced along the field; a negative charge is forced
      opposite to the field.
- [ ] Uniform fields have parallel, equally spaced field lines.
- [ ] Point charges produce radial fields.
- [ ] Between parallel plates, $E=\frac{\Delta V}{\Delta d}$.
- [ ] Coulomb's law for point charges is
      $F=\frac{Q_1Q_2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0r^2}$.
- [ ] The field due to a point charge is
      $E=\frac{Q}{4\pi\varepsilon_0r^2}$.
- [ ] Electric potential is work done per unit positive charge from infinity.
- [ ] Point-charge potential is $V=\frac{Q}{4\pi\varepsilon_0r}$.
- [ ] Electric field is the negative potential gradient.

## Skills to Perform

- [ ] Use $F=qE$ and decide direction from the sign of $q$.
- [ ] Calculate uniform field strength between parallel plates.
- [ ] Describe charged-particle motion in a uniform electric field.
- [ ] Use Coulomb's law with centre-to-centre separation.
- [ ] Apply inverse-square reasoning to point-charge forces and fields.
- [ ] Add electric field vectors when directions differ.
- [ ] Add electric potentials algebraically because potential is scalar.
- [ ] Use $E_p=qV$ and
      $E_p=\frac{Qq}{4\pi\varepsilon_0r}$ with signs.
- [ ] Find field strength from potential gradient.
- [ ] Explain why equipotential motion requires no work.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- [ ] Forgetting that field direction is defined using a positive charge.
- [ ] Giving the force on an electron in the field direction.
- [ ] Confusing potential $V$ with potential energy $E_p$.
- [ ] Adding field magnitudes when field directions oppose.
- [ ] Using uniform-field equations over a large region of a radial field.
- [ ] Measuring distance from the surface of a charged sphere instead of from
      its centre.
- [ ] Dropping negative signs in potential and potential energy.

## Ready to Move On When

- [ ] I can draw field lines for uniform and radial electric fields.
- [ ] I can calculate forces, fields, potentials, and potential energies with
      correct signs.
- [ ] I can describe how charged particles move in a uniform electric field.
- [ ] I can use Coulomb's law and point-charge field expressions.
- [ ] I can explain potential gradient and equipotentials.
- [ ] I can connect this topic to [Gravitational Fields](../13%20Gravitational%20Fields/00%20Overview.md)
      and [Capacitance](../19%20Capacitance/00%20Overview.md).
