# Motion in a Circle Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw the circle, mark the
centre, draw velocity tangentially, and draw the resultant force towards the
centre before choosing equations.

## A. Radians, Period, Frequency, and Angular Speed

1. Define one radian. Use your definition to explain why a complete revolution
   is $2\pi\ \mathrm{rad}$.

2. An object moves along an arc of length $1.8\ \mathrm{m}$ on a circle of
   radius $0.60\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the angular displacement in radians.

3. Convert $45^\circ$, $120^\circ$, and $315^\circ$ into radians, giving exact
   answers as multiples of $\pi$.

4. A wheel completes one revolution every $0.25\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the
   frequency and angular speed.

5. A motor shaft rotates at $1800\ \mathrm{rev\ min^{-1}}$. Convert this to
   frequency and angular speed in $\mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$.

6. A rigid disc rotates with angular speed $8.0\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Find
   the linear speed of points at radii $0.050\ \mathrm{m}$ and
   $0.20\ \mathrm{m}$.

## B. Centripetal Acceleration and Force

7. A car of mass $1200\ \mathrm{kg}$ travels at $18\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   around a bend of radius $60\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the centripetal acceleration
   and the inward resultant force.

8. A $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ object moves in a circle of radius
   $0.25\ \mathrm{m}$ with angular speed $12\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Find its
   linear speed, centripetal acceleration, and inward force.

9. A model aircraft flies in a horizontal circle of radius $15\ \mathrm{m}$
   with period $3.0\ \mathrm{s}$. Find $\omega$, $v$, and $a$.

10. A particle has speed $6.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and centripetal
    acceleration $24\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$. Find the radius of its circular
    path.

11. A force of constant magnitude acts perpendicular to an object's velocity
    at every instant. Explain why this can produce circular motion with
    constant speed.

## C. Real Forces Supplying the Inward Resultant

12. A $0.30\ \mathrm{kg}$ stone moves in a horizontal circle of radius
    $0.75\ \mathrm{m}$ at speed $5.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Assuming the string
    tension is horizontal, find the tension.

13. A car travels around a flat bend of radius $45\ \mathrm{m}$. The maximum
    frictional force available is $0.50mg$. Find the maximum safe speed.

14. A conical pendulum has radius $0.80\ \mathrm{m}$ and the string makes an
    angle of $30^\circ$ with the vertical. Use
    $\tan\theta=\frac{v^2}{rg}$ to find the speed of the mass.

15. A $0.25\ \mathrm{kg}$ mass moves at constant speed
    $3.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in a vertical circle of radius
    $0.45\ \mathrm{m}$. Find the string tension at the top and at the bottom.

## D. Interpretation and Connected Contexts

16. A satellite moves in a circular orbit of radius $7.0 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}$
    with period $5.9 \times 10^3\ \mathrm{s}$. Find its speed and centripetal
    acceleration. State the real force that provides the inward resultant.

17. A charged particle of mass $9.1 \times 10^{-31}\ \mathrm{kg}$ moves at
    $2.0 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in a circle. The magnetic force on
    it is $1.6 \times 10^{-14}\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the radius of the path.

18. Two riders are on the same carousel. Rider A is $1.5\ \mathrm{m}$ from the
    centre and Rider B is $3.0\ \mathrm{m}$ from the centre. The carousel has
    angular speed $1.2\ \mathrm{rad\ s^{-1}}$. Compare their linear speeds
    and centripetal accelerations.

19. A student says, "The object has constant speed, so acceleration is zero.
    The centripetal force acts outwards." Identify both errors and correct
    them.

20. An amusement ride of diameter $6.0\ \mathrm{m}$ rotates at
    $45\ \mathrm{rev\ min^{-1}}$. A rider has mass $70\ \mathrm{kg}$. Find
    $\omega$, $v$, $a$, and the inward resultant force on the rider.
