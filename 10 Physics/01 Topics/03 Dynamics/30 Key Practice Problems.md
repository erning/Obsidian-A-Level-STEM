# Dynamics Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Draw the system, force
diagram, or before-and-after momentum diagram before calculating.

Take $g=9.81\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-2}}$ where needed.

## A. Forces and Newton's Laws

1. A van of mass $1250\ \mathrm{kg}$ has a driving force of $2600\ \mathrm{N}$
   forwards and resistive forces of $850\ \mathrm{N}$ backwards. Find the
   resultant force and acceleration.

2. A crate of mass $8.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ is pulled along horizontal ground by a
   $50\ \mathrm{N}$ force at $25^\circ$ above the horizontal. Friction is
   $14\ \mathrm{N}$. Find the horizontal acceleration.

3. A $65\ \mathrm{kg}$ astronaut is on Earth and then on the Moon, where
   $g=1.6\ \mathrm{N\ kg^{-1}}$. Find the astronaut's weight in each place and
   explain what happens to the astronaut's mass and inertia.

4. A book rests on a table. Identify the forces acting on the book. Then state
   why the weight of the book and the normal contact force from the table are
   not a Newton's third-law pair.

5. A $0.40\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball is moving horizontally. A constant resultant force
   of $6.0\ \mathrm{N}$ acts in the direction of motion for $0.30\ \mathrm{s}$.
   Find the acceleration and the change in velocity.

## B. Resistive Forces and Terminal Velocity

6. A small ball falls through oil from rest. Describe how its weight, drag,
   resultant force, acceleration, and velocity change from release until
   terminal velocity is reached.

7. A car engine provides a maximum driving force of $900\ \mathrm{N}$. Its drag
   force is modelled by

   $$
   D=0.36v^2,
   $$

   with $D$ in newtons and $v$ in $\mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the top speed. If
   the car mass is $1000\ \mathrm{kg}$, find its acceleration when
   $v=30\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

## C. Momentum, Impulse, and Newton's Second Law

8. Calculate the momentum of each object.

   a. A $0.20\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball moving at $18\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ east.
   b. A $1500\ \mathrm{kg}$ car moving at $22\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ west.
   c. A $7.5\times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{kg}$ pellet moving at
      $240\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

9. A $0.060\ \mathrm{kg}$ ball hits a wall at $20\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$
   perpendicular to the wall and rebounds along the same line at
   $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The contact time is $0.010\ \mathrm{s}$. Taking
   motion towards the wall as positive, find the change in momentum and the
   average force on the ball.

10. A $900\ \mathrm{kg}$ car accelerates from $5.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ to
    $24\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in $12\ \mathrm{s}$. Find the change in momentum
    and the average resultant force.

11. A $70\ \mathrm{kg}$ passenger travelling at $15\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ is
    brought to rest in a collision. Compare the average force if the stopping
    time is $0.020\ \mathrm{s}$ with the average force if an airbag increases
    the stopping time to $0.15\ \mathrm{s}$.

12. A machine fires pellets of mass $0.012\ \mathrm{kg}$ at
    $600\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. It fires $8$ pellets each second. Find the rate
    of change of momentum of the pellets and the average recoil force on the
    machine.

## D. Collisions and Explosions

13. A $0.80\ \mathrm{kg}$ trolley moving at $3.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ collides
    with a stationary $1.60\ \mathrm{kg}$ trolley. They stick together. Find the
    common velocity and the kinetic energy lost.

14. A $2.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ trolley moving at $4.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ collides
    head-on with a $3.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ trolley moving at
    $1.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ in the opposite direction. They stick together.
    Find their common velocity and the kinetic energy lost.

15. A $1.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ cart moving at $3.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ collides
    elastically in one dimension with a stationary $2.0\ \mathrm{kg}$ cart.
    Use momentum conservation and the relative-speed condition to find the
    final velocities.

16. Two identical balls collide elastically. Before the collision, one ball
    moves at $0.60\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and the other is stationary. After the
    collision, the balls move off at equal speeds, each at $45^\circ$ to the
    original direction. Find the speed of each ball after the collision and show
    that kinetic energy is conserved.

17. A particle initially has momentum $5.0\ \mathrm{kg\ m\ s^{-1}}$ east. After
    a collision, one object has momentum $3.0\ \mathrm{kg\ m\ s^{-1}}$ at
    $30^\circ$ north of east. Find the momentum of the other object as a
    magnitude and direction.

18. A stationary object explodes into two fragments. A $0.12\ \mathrm{kg}$
    fragment moves east at $40\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. The other fragment has
    mass $0.18\ \mathrm{kg}$. Find the velocity of the second fragment and
    explain why kinetic energy can increase.

## E. Mixed Review

19. A ball of mass $0.50\ \mathrm{kg}$ strikes a wall at
    $8.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ and rebounds at $6.0\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$ along
    the same line. Explain whether momentum is conserved for the ball alone and
    for the wider ball-wall-Earth system.

20. Two skaters are initially at rest on frictionless ice. Skater A has mass
    $50\ \mathrm{kg}$ and pushes skater B of mass $70\ \mathrm{kg}$. After the
    push, skater A moves left at $1.4\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find skater B's
    velocity, and explain why the total momentum of the two-skater system is
    conserved.
