# Waves Key Practice Problems

Work these without looking at the solutions first. Always decide whether a
graph is displacement-position or displacement-time before reading wavelength
or period.

## A. Wave Quantities and Phase

1. A displacement-position graph has amplitude $0.025\ \mathrm{m}$ and adjacent
   crests separated by $0.40\ \mathrm{m}$. If the frequency is
   $8.0\ \mathrm{Hz}$, find the wavelength and wave speed.

2. A displacement-time graph for one point on a wave has period
   $0.020\ \mathrm{s}$ and amplitude $6.0\ \mathrm{mm}$. Find the frequency
   and state what the amplitude means physically.

3. Two points on a progressive wave are separated by $0.15\ \mathrm{m}$. The
   wavelength is $0.60\ \mathrm{m}$. Find their phase difference in degrees
   and radians.

4. Two oscillations of the same wave are separated in time by
   $2.0\ \mathrm{ms}$. The period is $8.0\ \mathrm{ms}$. Find their phase
   difference in degrees.

5. Derive $v=f\lambda$ using the definitions of wave speed, wavelength, period,
   and frequency.

## B. CRO Traces, Wave Speed, and Intensity

6. A CRO trace has one cycle occupying $5.0$ horizontal divisions. The time-base
   is $0.20\ \mathrm{ms\ div^{-1}}$. The crest-to-trough height is
   $3.0$ vertical divisions and the y-gain is
   $0.50\ \mathrm{V\ div^{-1}}$. Find the period, frequency, and voltage
   amplitude.

7. A sound wave in air has frequency $256\ \mathrm{Hz}$ and speed
   $330\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find its wavelength.

8. Ripples on water have wavelength $0.12\ \mathrm{m}$ and frequency
   $2.5\ \mathrm{Hz}$. Find their speed.

9. A progressive wave transmits power $48\ \mathrm{W}$ through an area
   $12\ \mathrm{m^2}$. Find the intensity. If the amplitude is then doubled in
   the same medium, find the new intensity.

10. The amplitude of a progressive wave is reduced to $0.40$ of its original
    value. Find the ratio of the new intensity to the original intensity.

## C. Wave Types and Electromagnetic Waves

11. Compare transverse and longitudinal waves. Include the direction of
    oscillation relative to wave travel and one example of each.

12. A longitudinal sound wave has adjacent compressions separated by
    $0.75\ \mathrm{m}$. The frequency is $440\ \mathrm{Hz}$. Find the wave
    speed and explain what the compressions represent.

13. Write the main regions of the electromagnetic spectrum in order of
    increasing frequency, from radio waves to $\gamma$-rays. State the
    approximate wavelength range of visible light in free space.

14. Classify each electromagnetic wavelength: $2.0\ \mathrm{m}$,
    $3.0\ \mathrm{cm}$, $1.0\times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{m}$,
    $500\ \mathrm{nm}$, and $1.0\times 10^{-9}\ \mathrm{m}$.

15. An electromagnetic wave in free space has frequency
    $6.0\times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{Hz}$. Find its wavelength and state whether it
    lies in the visible range.

## D. Doppler Effect and Polarisation

16. A stationary observer hears a moving sound source. Explain qualitatively
    why the observed frequency is higher when the source approaches and lower
    when it recedes. State whether the speed of sound in the air changes.

17. A source emits sound of frequency $500\ \mathrm{Hz}$. The speed of sound is
    $340\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$. Find the observed frequency for a stationary
    observer when the source approaches at $25\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$, and when
    it recedes at $25\ \mathrm{m\ s^{-1}}$.

18. Plane-polarised light of intensity $8.0\ \mathrm{W\ m^{-2}}$ is incident on
    a polarising filter. The angle between the plane of polarisation and the
    transmission axis is $30^\circ$. Find the transmitted intensity.

19. Plane-polarised light passes through a polarising filter. The transmitted
    intensity is $25\%$ of the incident intensity. Find the angle between the
    incident plane of polarisation and the transmission axis.

20. Plane-polarised light of intensity $16\ \mathrm{W\ m^{-2}}$ passes through
    two polarising filters. The first filter is at $45^\circ$ to the incident
    plane of polarisation. The second filter is at $30^\circ$ to the first
    filter. Find the final transmitted intensity.
